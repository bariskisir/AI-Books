# Book 7: The Salt Parliament - Outline

## Synopsis

In 2436, the Orison Shoals fracture over the obligations created by the Pelagos Reef Injunction, the Lumen Silence Protocol, and the spread of human legal habits into Orison memory waters. The Salt Parliament, an Orison deliberative body that votes by temporary body exchange, summons Ren Okoye, a human interpreter trained in treaty-science but distrusted by every faction, to witness a dispute humans keep mistranslating as a policy debate.

Third-Voice-of-Reef, an Orison speaker whose body carries incompatible factional memories, argues that the Shoals must honor human treaties without becoming human law's mirror. Lael Shun, a Compact treaty advocate, arrives to protect route access and prevent Orison withdrawal from Meridian coordination. But the Parliament's crisis is older and stranger than diplomacy: voting now changes bodies permanently, minority positions are being metabolized out of existence, and human observers may be accelerating the collapse by insisting on individual signatures.

Ren must learn to interpret consensus without reducing it to sameness. As the Pale Arc anomalies and Dock Seven identity doctrines ripple outward, the Orison must decide whether treaty obligations bind a people whose bodies, memories, and political selves are always in motion. The ending establishes a new Shoals compact: relation can bind without ownership, dissent must remain embodied, and alien consensus cannot be made safe by translating it into human unanimity.

## Protagonist Arc

Ren Okoye begins as a gifted but cautious human interpreter who believes ethical translation means making Orison meaning legible enough for human institutions to respect. Ren has inherited Mira Cael's treaty-science methods but fears becoming another human who mistakes witness for authority.

The Parliament forces Ren beyond legibility. They must accept that some Orison political acts are not concepts to translate but living processes to protect from premature translation. By the end, Ren stops trying to produce one clean treaty sentence and instead builds a multi-state witness practice where dissent, embodiment, delay, and partial understanding remain part of the record.

## Supporting Cast

- **Third-Voice-of-Reef**: Orison parliament speaker formed from three reef lineages, carrying a deliberate internal disagreement. Their body is a political compromise under strain.
- **Lael Shun**: Compact treaty advocate tasked with preserving human-Orison route obligations. Skilled, sincere, and dangerous when she treats process stability as peace.
- **Keeps-Water-Calm**: Returning Orison diplomat from Pelagos, now older in relation rather than years, wary of humans who learned the right words after damage.
- **Oru-Kel-Under-Quiet**: Orison acoustic mathematician from the Lumen observation effort, arguing that Pale Arc boundary rhythms require Shoals-wide response.
- **Mira Cael**: Remote treaty-science adviser whose earlier work gives Ren tools but not permission to repeat old methods.
- **Salt-Before-Shell**: Radical Orison faction speaker advocating withdrawal from all human treaties until humans stop making Orison continuity into legal categories.
- **Aven Rusk**: Routeholder envoy who sees Orison treaty uncertainty as a threat to convoy schedules and private gate access.

## Antagonist

The antagonist is not one villain but the pressure to make alien consensus administratively convenient. Human institutions want signatures, deadlines, stable parties, and enforceable obligations. Some Orison factions want purity through withdrawal. Others want unity so badly they are willing to metabolize dissent into silent tissue. The Parliament's own voting technology becomes dangerous when it turns temporary political embodiment into permanent erasure.

## Setting

The Orison Shoals are a chain of ocean moons, brine habitats, living reef chambers, treaty pools, and migration currents connected by slow meridian routes and Orison acoustic memory practices. The Salt Parliament meets in the Parliament Basin, a vast living chamber where delegates enter exchange bodies, trade chemical memory, and vote by taking temporary responsibility for another faction's felt position.

## Technology and Culture Notes

- Orison voting is embodied: a delegate temporarily carries another faction's sensory and memory state before casting a position.
- Orison consensus means sustained relation under acknowledged difference, not unanimity.
- Human treaty law requires stable parties; Orison political identity can bud, merge, split, and return changed.
- The Pelagos Reef Injunction protects memory ecologies but also pressures Orison factions to present themselves in human-legible categories.
- Pale Arc boundary rhythms from the Lumen investigation suggest older nonhuman constraints around route disturbance.
- Dock Seven's mirror refusal doctrine influences Ren's thinking about consent, identity, and the right not to be made evidence.

## Ending State

The Salt Parliament does not become unified in human terms. Instead, it creates a Shoals Compact that preserves embodied dissent while maintaining limited treaty obligations for humanitarian and route-safety purposes. Lael Shun accepts censure risk for refusing a false stable-party signature. Aven Rusk's bypass attempt exposes the danger of private route simplification near Pale Arc boundary systems. Ren becomes a witness of unfinished relation rather than a translator of clean answers.
