### Synopsis

Margot Delacroix, a widowed rose grower in Provence, discovers that a rare rose her late husband Étienne was cultivating has produced a single perfect bloom. She enters the prestigious Grand Prize of Provence competition, where she faces Laurent Vasseur, a cold-hearted three-time champion whose obsession with winning hides a grief of his own — estrangement from his young daughter, Camille.

Through the long summer of cultivation, sabotage, drought, and blight, Margot and Laurent clash and then gradually connect. A shared remedy saves Laurent's roses; a shared humanity bridges their solitude. Laurent writes to his daughter and begins to rebuild that broken bond. Meanwhile, the competition judge Madame Fontaine probes both growers to reveal the true meaning behind their roses.

The competition ends in an unprecedented tie. Madame Fontaine explains: "I cannot choose between a rose grown from love and a rose grown from longing." Margot and Laurent, no longer rivals, begin a new chapter together. Camille joins them. The garden grows.
