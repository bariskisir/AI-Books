# The Mona Lisa Smile — Outline

## Premise

Jean-Paul Dubois, a middle-aged security guard at the Louvre Museum, has spent twelve years watching over the Mona Lisa. He knows every brushstroke, every crack in the varnish, every nuance of her smile. One quiet Tuesday morning, he notices something wrong — the smile is fractionally wider, the shadows beneath her cheekbones softer. He dismisses it at first, but the doubt gnaws at him. When he secretly compares old photographs to the painting on the wall, he realizes the truth: the Mona Lisa hanging in the Louvre is a perfect forgery. The real painting has been stolen. But when he reports his discovery, no one believes him. The museum's director calls it impossible. The security footage shows nothing. The alarms never triggered. Jean-Paul must risk his career, his freedom, and ultimately his life to prove the painting is a fake, uncover the forger, and find the real Mona Lisa before the greatest art theft in history goes undiscovered forever.

## Core Cast

- **Jean-Paul Dubois** — A 58-year-old Louvre security guard. Quiet, observant, methodical. Twelve years in the Salle des Etats. Divorced. Lives alone with his cat. Has a photographer's eye for detail.
- **Dr. Simone Moreau** — The Louvre's chief curator of Renaissance painting. Elegant, sharp, politically astute. She has spent thirty years protecting the museum's reputation. She does not want to hear bad news.
- **Viktor Orlov** — The forger. A Russian expatriate painter of extraordinary skill. Once celebrated, now forgotten. He lives in a cramped studio in Montmartre and nurses a bitter grudge against the art world that rejected him.
- **Captain Luc Roussel** — Paris police art-crimes investigator. Skeptical, weary, but competent. He has seen too many false alarms to get excited.
- **Anouk** — Jean-Paul's daughter. A graduate student in art history at the Sorbonne. She believes her father.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Observation (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Morning Shift** — Jean-Paul arrives at the Louvre before dawn. His routine. The empty galleries. He takes his position in the Salle des Etats.
2. **The Usual Watchers** — The morning crowd files past. Jean-Paul watches the watchers. A young woman lingers too long. He notes her face.
3. **Something Off** — After lunch, Jean-Paul looks at the painting and feels a twist in his gut. The smile is different. He cannot say how.
4. **The Photograph** — Jean-Paul takes out his phone and compares the painting to a photo he took years ago. The difference is real.
5. **Doubt** — He tells himself he is imagining things. Old age. Fatigue. He tries to forget it.
6. **The Second Look** — Before the museum closes, he returns. The forgery is undeniable. The shadows are wrong.
7. **The Night Guard** — Jean-Paul cannot sleep. He drives back to the Louvre at midnight. Stands in the dark gallery. The painting glows under the security lights. It is not the same.
8. **The Call** — He calls his daughter Anouk. She listens. She tells him to document everything.
9. **The Evidence** — Jean-Paul spends the night comparing every detail. Proportions. Crack patterns. The background landscape. He builds his case.
10. **The Report** — He writes a formal report and emails it to Dr. Moreau before he can lose his nerve.

### Part II: The Cover-Up (Chapters 11-20)

11. **Moreau's Office** — Dr. Moreau reads the report. She calls Jean-Paul in. Her tone is cold. She asks how he could know this.
12. **The Connoisseur** — Moreau brings in an outside expert who examines the painting and pronounces it authentic. Jean-Paul sees the expert hesitate for half a second.
13. **The Threat** — Moreau warns Jean-Paul that making false claims could cost him his pension, his job, his reputation.
14. **The Photographs** — Jean-Paul shows Anouk his comparison photos. She sees it too. The left eye has shifted 2 millimeters.
15. **The Science** — Anouk brings her professor, a pigment specialist, to analyze the photographs. The chemical signature is wrong.
16. **Silence** — Jean-Paul is reassigned to the Egyptian wing. Far from the Mona Lisa. He is being buried.
17. **The Forger's Name** — Anouk's professor recalls a painter expelled from the Academy twenty years ago. Viktor Orlov. A master of imitation.
18. **Montmartre** — Jean-Paul finds Viktor Orlov's studio. He watches from across the street. A man with paint-stained hands.
19. **The Conversation** — Jean-Paul approaches Orlov. The forger is surprised. He does not deny his skill, but he denies the theft.
20. **The Invitation** — Orlov invites Jean-Paul inside. Shows him his work. His copies are flawless. Jean-Paul knows he has found the right man.

### Part III: The Investigation (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Deal** — Jean-Paul convinces Orlov to help. The forger wants a gallery show. Jean-Paul promises nothing but asks everything.
22. **The Technique** — Orlov explains how he makes his forgeries. Layers. Aging. Cracking. The patience of months.
23. **The Commission** — Orlov admits a wealthy collector approached him six months ago. Paid him half a million euros. Gave him access to high-resolution photographs.
24. **The Client** — Orlov never met the client face to face. Emails. Dead drops. A Swiss bank account. Professional.
25. **The Timeline** — The forgery was delivered three months ago. That means the real painting was swapped then. Jean-Paul pulls the old security footage.
26. **The Gap** — The footage shows nothing. But Jean-Paul notices a 47-second gap between camera feeds. Someone knows the system.
27. **The Insider** — Someone inside the Louvre helped. Jean-Paul makes a list of everyone with access to the security system. Twenty-three names.
28. **The Leak** — Someone warns Moreau that Jean-Paul is still investigating. She calls him to her office again. She is angry now.
29. **The Suspension** — Jean-Paul is suspended without pay. His badge is taken. He is escorted from the building.
30. **The Breaking Point** — Anouk convinces Jean-Paul to go to the police. They walk into the art-crimes division together.

### Part IV: The Chase (Chapters 31-40)

31. **Captain Roussel** — Captain Roussel listens. He has heard similar stories. But Jean-Paul's photographs are convincing. He opens a file.
32. **The Investigation Begins** — Roussel's team examines the painting with portable spectrometers. The results are damning. The painting is modern.
33. **The Leak Traced** — Roussel traces the security footage gap to a junior technician named Benoit Lefevre. He is missing.
34. **The Technician** — Lefevre's apartment is empty. He left in a hurry. Roussel finds a burner phone and a train ticket to Geneva.
35. **The Collector** — Roussel identifies the Swiss bank account. It belongs to a shadowy Russian oligarch named Dmitri Volkov.
36. **The Connection** — Volkov collects stolen masterpieces. He has a private vault in Geneva. The Mona Lisa is likely there.
37. **The Warrant** — Roussel works with Swiss authorities. A warrant is issued. But Volkov has lawyers. The process takes days.
38. **The Raid** — The Geneva vault is opened. Inside are dozens of paintings. But not the Mona Lisa. They are too late.
39. **The Trail** — Volkov's private jet left for Dubai three hours before the raid. The painting is gone.
40. **The Betrayal** — Orlov disappears. His studio is empty. Jean-Paul realizes the forger was playing both sides.

### Part V: The Resolution (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Letter** — Orlov sends Jean-Paul a letter. He apologizes. Volkov threatened his daughter. He had to run. But he left a clue.
42. **The Clue** — The clue is a shipping manifest. A crate sent to a private address in Marrakech. Volkov's Moroccan estate.
43. **The Plan** — Roussel cannot get a warrant for Morocco. But Jean-Paul has an idea. He will go himself. Anouk insists on coming.
44. **Marrakech** — Father and daughter arrive in Morocco. They pose as tourists. They find the estate outside the city.
45. **The Estate** — The estate is a fortress. Walls. Guards. Cameras. The Mona Lisa is somewhere inside.
46. **The Way In** — Anouk charms a groundskeeper. She learns the estate has a weak point — a delivery entrance used by local vendors.
47. **The Photograph** — Jean-Paul climbs a wall and photographs the main hall through a window. He sees the Mona Lisa on a wall. The frame is unmistakable.
48. **The Arrest** — The photographs are enough. Roussel arranges an Interpol operation. Volkov is arrested at the estate. The painting is recovered.
49. **The Return** — The Mona Lisa comes back to the Louvre. The world media descends. Jean-Paul is called a hero. Moreau offers him his job back.
50. **The Quiet** — Jean-Paul refuses the job. He visits the painting one last time, now behind stronger glass. Her smile is the same as it always was. He walks out into the Paris sun.
