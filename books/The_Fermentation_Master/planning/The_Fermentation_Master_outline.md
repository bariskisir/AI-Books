# The Fermentation Master — Outline

## Premise

In 1972 Jeonju, South Korea, Park Soonja — 67, keeper of a doenjang and kimchi fermentation studio her family has operated since 1890 — faces twin threats to everything she has built. A government hygiene official arrives to assess whether her clay onggi jars and hand-harvested salt methods meet modern standards, while her daughter Miyeon presses her to sell to a food company whose representative, Baek Seunghyun, circles with contracts and flattery. Soonja's young apprentice Jinho urges modernization. But Soonja is already executing a plan none of them know: she has been recording every fermentation formula the family has held for eighty years — not for commerce, not for surrender, but for memory itself — and she intends to finish the record before the official returns with his verdict. The novel traces ten days in autumn 1972, interweaving the sensory world of the studio with pressures of industrialization, family tension, and the quiet determination of a woman who understands that some things must be written down before they vanish.

## Core Cast

- **Park Soonja, 67** — The fermentation master. Precise, unhurried, privately fierce. She speaks little and observes everything. Her hands know temperatures no thermometer captures.
- **Jinho, 24** — Her male apprentice. Bright, restless, genuinely talented, but hungry for speed. He respects Soonja yet believes the old methods are holding them back.
- **Park Miyeon, 41** — Soonja's daughter. A schoolteacher in Jeonju city. Practical to the point of coldness, worn down by years of watching her mother refuse to modernize.
- **Oh Jungsik, 44** — The government hygiene official. Meticulous, not unkind, caught between genuine concern for public health and pressure from superiors to clear studios for industrial alternatives.
- **Elder Kim, 78** — A neighboring grandmother who ran her own kimchi studio before arthritis stopped her. She remembers methods even Soonja has only heard described.
- **Baek Seunghyun, 35** — Food company representative. Polished, patient, not villainous — he believes industrial fermentation is simply the future, and he is not entirely wrong.
- **Dohee, 28** — Miyeon's daughter, Soonja's granddaughter. An art teacher in Seoul, visiting for the first time in three years. She arrives dismissive and leaves changed.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Arrival (Chapters 1–10)

1. Soonja opens the studio at dawn, checking onggi jars by hand; we learn the studio's history and her daily rhythm.
2. Jinho arrives early and proposes buying stainless steel vats; Soonja declines without explaining.
3. A letter arrives from the Provincial Hygiene Bureau — an inspection is scheduled for ten days hence.
4. Miyeon drives from Jeonju city to deliver the news in person; she and Soonja argue in the kitchen.
5. Oh Jungsik makes an unannounced preliminary visit to the studio, observing silently, taking notes.
6. Soonja shows Jungsik the oldest onggi jars; he is polite but noncommittal about their compliance status.
7. Baek Seunghyun arrives the same evening with a folder of acquisition documents; Miyeon is unsurprised.
8. Jinho and Baek have a long conversation in the courtyard; Jinho returns troubled, neither convinced nor unconvinced.
9. Soonja, alone at midnight, retrieves a leather-bound ledger from under her sleeping mat and begins writing.
10. Elder Kim visits the studio the next morning and identifies the salt in a new batch by smell alone.

### Act II: The Record (Chapters 11–20)

11. Soonja asks Jinho to gather specific measurements — he does not yet know why.
12. Miyeon returns and finds Baek still in town; she shares a meal with him that unsettles her assumptions.
13. The studio receives its autumn delivery of Gochang sea salt; Soonja inspects each bag by hand.
14. Jungsik returns for a formal walk-through; he notices the ledger on Soonja's worktable but says nothing.
15. Dohee arrives unexpectedly from Seoul, drawn by a vague worry she cannot name.
16. Dohee and Jinho work together during a night salting session; their dynamic is careful, slightly wary.
17. Soonja teaches Dohee one task: how to judge the temperature of brine by feel alone.
18. Elder Kim tells Soonja about a formula that predates the studio — a summer doenjang her own mother made in the mountains.
19. Soonja writes the mountain formula into the ledger from Elder Kim's spoken memory; Dohee watches.
20. Jungsik meets Baek in a teahouse; Jungsik leaves the meeting uncomfortable with what was implied.

### Act III: The Pressures (Chapters 21–30)

21. Miyeon confronts Soonja about the ledger, believing it to be a legal document — a will.
22. Soonja corrects her: it is a recipe archive, not property distribution.
23. Baek makes a revised offer — the company will keep the Soonja brand name and employ Jinho as head technician.
24. Jinho tells Soonja about the offer that evening; she listens without interrupting.
25. The studio's biggest autumn kimchi batch begins: three days of work, all hands needed.
26. During the batch, Dohee begins noticing small details — the specific fold of a cabbage leaf, the salt-to-weight ratios.
27. Jungsik returns for a surprise mid-process inspection; he witnesses the three-day batch in progress.
28. Soonja shows Jungsik a page of the ledger — formulas, temperatures, timing notations — without comment.
29. Jungsik reads the page carefully and does not speak for a long time.
30. Baek leaves Jeonju after Miyeon tells him, firmly and finally, that the studio is not for sale this year.

### Act IV: The Record Completed (Chapters 31–40)

31. Miyeon asks Soonja why she changed her mind about selling; Soonja says she never agreed.
32. Elder Kim falls ill; Soonja spends an afternoon at her house, continuing to record the old woman's memories.
33. The kimchi batch is finished; the onggi lids are sealed and the jars are left in the courtyard.
34. Dohee stays an extra week; she begins sketching the jars and the courtyard in a small notebook.
35. Jinho asks Soonja directly what she plans to do with the ledger.
36. Soonja tells him: the ledger goes to the National Folklore Archive in Seoul when it is complete.
37. Jinho is startled — not by the destination but by the scale of what she has been doing for years.
38. Dohee asks to read the ledger; Soonja sets it in front of her without a word.
39. Dohee reads for two hours, then asks a question about a formula from the 1910s; Soonja answers in detail.
40. Miyeon reads one page at her mother's table and sits for a long time afterward.

### Act V: The Verdict (Chapters 41–50)

41. The night before the formal inspection, Soonja cleans every surface of the studio by herself.
42. Jungsik arrives at eight in the morning with a colleague; the formal inspection begins.
43. Jungsik's colleague raises objections about the clay vessels and the absence of temperature-logging equipment.
44. Jungsik overrules three of his colleague's objections, citing the historical practices provision in the hygiene code.
45. The inspection concludes; Jungsik will file his report within fourteen days.
46. Baek sends a final letter; Miyeon reads it and hands it to Soonja unopened, then watches her set it aside.
47. Jinho tells Soonja he wants to stay for another three years; she nods and says they will need to hire someone for the salt work.
48. Dohee calls from Seoul to say she has spoken to someone at the National Folklore Archive.
49. Elder Kim recovers enough to walk to the studio; she stands at the courtyard edge and looks at the sealed jars.
50. Soonja finishes the last entry in the ledger in the evening, seals it, and sets it on the worktable for morning.
