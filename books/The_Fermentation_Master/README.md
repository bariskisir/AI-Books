# The Fermentation Master

## Premise

1972, Jeonju, South Korea. Park Soonja, 67, is the keeper of a doenjang and kimchi fermentation studio that her family has operated since 1890, using the same clay onggi jars, the same hand-harvested salt from the Gochang flats, and the same recipe logic her grandmother passed down in fragments. As South Korea's food industry begins mass-producing fermented products using chemical shortcuts, a government food hygiene official arrives to assess whether Soonja's traditional studio meets modern standards. Her young apprentice Jinho wants her to modernize; her daughter Miyeon wants her to sell to a food company. Soonja has other plans — she has been preparing something no one expected: a formal record of every fermentation formula the family has held for eighty years, not for commerce but for preservation, and she intends to finish it before the official returns with his verdict.

## Structure

Fifty chapters across five acts, spanning twenty-three days in October 1972. The novel interweaves the physical work of the fermentation studio — the autumn kimchi batch, the winter doenjang turning, the salt delivery and its quality reading — with the bureaucratic and commercial pressure on the studio, and with Soonja's secret project: a ninety-two-formula archive destined for the National Folklore Archive in Seoul.
