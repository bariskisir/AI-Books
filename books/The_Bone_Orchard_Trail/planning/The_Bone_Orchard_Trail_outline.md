# The Bone Orchard Trail — Outline

## Premise

Eli Crane, a former lawman who hung up his star after a hanging that went wrong, rides into the high-desert town of Quartz Hollow to settle an old debt with a dead man's widow. He finds a town that buries its dead beneath a grove of pale, twisted trees the locals call the bone orchard — and finds that the dead do not stay buried there. The cattle baron Absalom Reede has struck a bargain with something older than the territory to keep his herd fat and his land green while the rest of the basin withers. The price is paid in the bodies of the orchard. Bound by his debt and his own buried guilt, Eli must learn what Reede has promised, what walks the orchard at night, and whether a man can put the risen back into the ground without becoming one of them.

## Core Cast

- **Eli Crane** — Former territorial marshal, mid-fifties, quiet and weather-worn. Quit the law after he hanged the wrong man. Rides a sorrel mare named Juno. Carries the debt of a friend's death.
- **Naomi Hale** — Widow of Eli's dead friend Tom Hale. Runs the failing way-station and keeps the town's burial ledger. Sharp, unsentimental, brave.
- **Absalom Reede** — Cattle baron of the Reede ranch. Old, courtly, terrifyingly calm. Made the bargain a generation ago and has paid it ever since.
- **Doc Sennett** — The town's drunk physician, keeper of uncomfortable knowledge about what comes up out of the orchard.
- **Pastor Wells** — A frightened preacher who long ago stopped reading over the graves.
- **Cole Reede** — Absalom's son, weak and resentful, who wants the bargain ended.
- **The Sexton** — Mute old man who tends the orchard and knows its rules.
- **Tom Hale** — Eli's dead friend, whose grave lies in the orchard. The debt that drew Eli west.
- **The Thing in the Ground** — An ancient presence the Spanish called *el sediento*, the thirsty one, fed by Reede.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part One — The Town That Buries Its Dead (1–12)

1. **Dust and Salt** — Eli rides into Quartz Hollow at blood-dusk and sees the pale grove on the ridge for the first time.
2. **The Way-Station** — He finds Naomi Hale and learns Tom is two years dead and buried in the orchard.
3. **The Ledger** — Naomi shows Eli the burial book; more names go in than the town's deaths account for.
4. **Doc's Bottle** — Eli drinks with Doc Sennett, who warns him to ride out before the next dark of the moon.
5. **The Green Acre** — Eli sees Reede's land alone is green in a basin of dust and asks how.
6. **A Star in a Drawer** — Naomi finds Eli's old marshal's badge and asks why a lawman quit.
7. **The Sexton's Rows** — Eli walks the orchard and meets the mute sexton tending fresh-turned earth.
8. **Reede's Invitation** — A rider summons Eli to supper at the Reede ranch.
9. **Supper with the Baron** — Absalom Reede is courteous, knows Eli's history, and offers him work.
10. **What the Dog Won't Cross** — Eli's mare and the town dogs refuse to go near the orchard fence at night.
11. **The First Walking** — Eli wakes to see a figure standing in the orchard, watching the town, gone by dawn.
12. **Tom's Stone** — Eli visits Tom's grave; the earth above it looks recently disturbed.

### Part Two — The Bargain (13–25)

13. **The Hanging He Carries** — Flashback frame: why Eli left the law, the man he hanged who was innocent.
14. **Pastor Without Words** — Pastor Wells admits he stopped saying graveside rites years ago, and why.
15. **The Dry Years** — Naomi tells Eli the basin's drought and how Reede's land alone survived.
16. **Counting the Missing** — Eli and Naomi tally names: drifters, debtors, the friendless — all gone to the orchard.
17. **The Spanish Word** — Doc shows Eli a journal: a priest's account of *el sediento* and the first bargain.
18. **Cole's Fear** — Reede's son Cole corners Eli, says his father is feeding something and he wants it stopped.
19. **Night Ride to the Grove** — Eli watches the orchard and sees the sexton lead a shrouded shape into a row.
20. **The Risen Hand** — Eli digs at a fresh grave and finds it empty but for a print of a hand pressed up from below.
21. **Reede's Offer Again** — Absalom offers Eli a deputy's wage to keep order and look away.
22. **What Keeps the Cattle Fat** — Eli learns Reede's herd never sickens because the orchard feeds the land.
23. **The Debt Named** — Naomi reveals Tom died owing Reede money — and his body was the payment.
24. **A Light in the Big House** — Eli sees Absalom kneeling nightly at a dry well behind the ranch.
25. **The Thing at the Well** — Eli looks down the well and hears a voice that is not water.

### Part Three — Digging Up the Past (26–37)

26. **The Empty Coffins** — Eli and Doc exhume three graves by lantern; all are empty.
27. **Marks on the Trees** — The pale trunks bear handprints in a dark sap that smells of iron.
28. **The Sexton Speaks Once** — The mute sexton writes a single word in the dust: *more*.
29. **Reede's Long Life** — Doc realizes Absalom is far older than any living memory should allow.
30. **The Widow's Plan** — Naomi proposes burning the orchard; Eli warns fire may free what's bound.
31. **Cole's Betrayal** — Cole tells his father that Eli has been digging.
32. **The Warning** — Reede's men beat Doc and leave him at the way-station door.
33. **The Marshal Returns** — Eli pins on the old badge again to face Reede with the law behind him.
34. **No Law Out Here** — Reede laughs at the badge; the nearest court is two hundred miles of dust away.
35. **The Drought Breaks Wrong** — A black rain falls only over the orchard; the trees drink it.
36. **Tom's Voice** — At Tom's grave Eli hears his dead friend speak, begging to be let stay down.
37. **The Price of the Bargain** — Reede confesses to Eli: he must feed the orchard a body each dark moon or his land dies and the thing comes for him.

### Part Four — The Dark of the Moon (38–50)

38. **Three Days to Dark** — The next dark moon nears; the town knows a name will be chosen.
39. **The Chosen Name** — Reede's men come for Naomi as the next payment.
40. **The Rescue** — Eli takes Naomi from the wagon at gunpoint and rides into the hills.
41. **Cornered at the Way-Station** — Reede's riders besiege the way-station through a long night.
42. **Doc's Last Counsel** — Dying Doc tells Eli the orchard can be closed if the first bargainer is given to it.
43. **The First Bargainer** — Eli understands: Absalom himself was the first body promised, and has cheated death by feeding others.
44. **Into the Orchard** — Eli and Naomi cross the fence at dusk to face the well.
45. **The Risen Rows** — The dead of the orchard rise around them, slow and reaching, not cruel but hungry.
46. **Tom Among Them** — Tom's risen form blocks the path but turns aside, remembering Eli.
47. **The Well's Bargain** — At the dry well the voice offers Eli his own dead back if he will keep feeding it.
48. **Reede's End** — Eli refuses; he forces Absalom to the well and the thing takes its first promised body at last.
49. **The Closing** — With the first debt paid, the orchard stills; the risen lie down; the pale trees crack and die.
50. **The Long Ride Out** — Rain comes clean over the whole basin; Eli buries Tom proper, returns the badge to a drawer, and rides on with Naomi watching the road.
