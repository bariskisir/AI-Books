# The Petrified Sea

## Overview

A paleontological historical novel set in the chalk country of western Kansas, 1876–1879. Two famous eastern scientists are at war over the fossil sea-reptiles of the Niobrara chalk — bribing telegraph operators, salting each other's quarries, naming species by wire. Eleanor "Nell" Granger, a widowed schoolteacher who taught herself anatomy from a borrowed book and draws fossils with a surveyor's precision, is hired into one camp as a field illustrator — and discovers what neither general has: a complete giant mosasaur in a homesteader's bluff, its jaws closed on the paddle of its last meal.

## The Story Engines

The novel braids two races. The first is scientific: a fictionalized Bone Wars, in which priority of naming is the whole law and the feud between Professor Hiram Ordway (cold, rich, methodical) and Dr. Lucius Pratt (brilliant, hasty, once publicly humiliated for mounting a skull on the wrong end of a skeleton) has turned the floor of a vanished ocean into a courtroom. The second is legal: the bones lie on a quarter-section of forfeited railroad checkerboard, and whoever holds the ground when the General Land Office fires its gun holds everything. Nell wins the second race the way the era allowed a widow to win it — actual settlement, a chalk-block house built on June evenings, and first place in line at the Hays land office.

## Key Themes

**Who gets to write the label:** Nell's plates are published as the work of "Mr. E. Granger"; the museum's gallery card says "Collected by the Museum's party." The novel ends on a four-by-six porcelain label that reads small and correct.

**Deep time and small honesty:** The field journal — dated, measured, witnessed — is the only honest record in a war of liars, and it decides everything. "Draw what is there, not what ought to be there."

**Institutions and neighbors:** The brownstone museum with nineteen hundred unopened crates against the county-built Bone House where the children of the district go in free, forever.

**Faith and the fossil record:** Reverend Mott's three-year journey from fear of deep time to his porch-step sermon: "His sixth day is longer than we counted."

## Characters

**Eleanor "Nell" Granger** — Widowed schoolteacher, self-taught anatomist, exacting illustrator. Refuses both generals and publishes *Tylosaurus marthae* under her own full name.

**Silas Decker** — Ordway's field foreman, a former buffalo hunter who tries every plaster bung himself. Quits Connecticut to build the Kansas hall.

**Professor Hiram Ordway** — The cold collector; offers Nell institutional immortality at the price of her journal, and is refused.

**Dr. Lucius Pratt** — The wounded genius; names the great find sight-unseen off spy sketches, mistaking its prey's paddle for its own limb — then rides through snow to ask Nell to correct him in print, clean, so he can be free.

**Dane Hollis** — The hailed-out homesteader whose land holds the bluff; the species is named for his late wife Martha, who kept fossil shells in a jar and called them "the first folks."
