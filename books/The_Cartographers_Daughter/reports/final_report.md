# The Cartographer's Daughter — Final Report

## Summary

The Cartographer's Daughter is an adventure novel set in 1785, following Ada Thorne as she stows away on a mapping expedition to an uncharted island where the laws of geography and physics break down. The story is a literary adventure about the limits of human knowledge, the nature of reality, and the maps we draw to make sense of what cannot be measured.

## Characters

- **Ada Thorne** — Cartographer's daughter, stowaway, self-taught navigator
- **Captain Jeremiah Blackwood** — Commander of HMS Tenacity, carrying a debt to Ada's father
- **First Mate Kenji Saito** — Japanese navigator, former assistant to Elias Thorne

## Story Arc

- **Part I (Ch 1–10):** Ada stows away on the HMS Tenacity, is discovered, and the voyage to the uncharted island begins.
- **Part II (Ch 11–20):** The crew arrives on the island. Compasses deviate, landmarks shift, and a sailor vanishes.
- **Part III (Ch 21–30):** Ada deciphers the ancient cartographic language on a stone archway and learns the island's secret.
- **Part IV (Ch 31–40):** The expedition reaches the pivot point — a crystal chamber where Ada finds her father alive beyond a mirror.
- **Part V (Ch 41–50):** The island shifts violently. Ada chooses to return home, leads the crew across water, and returns to London to continue her father's work.
