# The Last Voyage of the Mermaid — Outline

## Premise

A deep-sea salvage crew tows a derelict luxury yacht from international waters back to port, intending to claim a healthy salvage fee. But the yacht — the *Mermaid* — is not empty. Its passengers are still aboard, preserved in the party attire they wore on the night the ship lost power three years ago. As the tow line pulls them toward land, the salvage crew discovers that the dead are not resting, and the *Mermaid* has one last voyage to complete — with a new crew.

## Core Cast

- **Captain Elias Shaw** — Veteran salvage captain, pragmatic, weathered. He commands the tug *Sea Serpent* and believes in charts and steel, not ghosts.
- **Jess Corrigan** — Rigger and deckhand, late twenties. Sharp-eyed, cynical, the first to notice something is wrong aboard the yacht.
- **The Yacht's Log** — The *Mermaid's* voyage data recorder and captain's journal, recovered in pieces. Its entries reveal what happened to the ship and its passengers.
- **First Mate Soloman Voss** — Shaw's second-in-command. Superstitious, uneasy from the start. Wants to cut the line and leave.
- **Engineer Pete Harlow** — The tug's engineer, practical and quiet. Keeps the engines running and his mouth shut.
- **Deckhand Ana Reyes** — Youngest crew member, eager to prove herself. She sees things she cannot explain.
- **The Passengers** — Thirty-four souls preserved below decks on the *Mermaid*, frozen in a moment of celebration. They begin to move.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Catch (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Signal** — The *Sea Serpent* receives a salvage alert. A luxury yacht, the *Mermaid*, is adrift in the North Atlantic. Captain Shaw takes the contract.
2. **The Tow** — The crew reaches the *Mermaid*. She floats dark and silent. Shaw orders the line attached.
3. **First Look** — Jess boards the yacht through a shattered hatch. The interior is pristine. Place settings wait on tables.
4. **The Galley** — Ana finds the galley fully stocked, food still fresh. A coffee cup sits warm on the counter.
5. **The Staterooms** — Jess checks the cabins. Beds are made. Personal effects are arranged neatly. No sign of struggle.
6. **The Ballroom** — Voss forces open the ballroom doors. The passengers are there — standing, smiling, dressed for a party that never ended.
7. **The Captain's Table** — Shaw reviews the *Mermaid's* manifest. Thirty-four passengers and crew. None accounted for alive.
8. **The Log Begins** — Shaw finds the captain's tablet in the bridge. The last entry is dated three years ago.
9. **The Line Tightens** — The tow begins. The *Mermaid* groans as the sea serpent takes her weight. They head for port.
10. **Voices Below** — Ana hears music from inside the yacht. Voss dismisses it as settling metal. But he crosses himself.

### Part II: The Crossing (Chapters 11–20)

11. **The First Night** — The convoy moves slowly. The sea is calm. Jess cannot sleep. She watches the *Mermaid* through binoculars.
12. **Lights** — A light flickers in a porthole. Then another. The crew watches as the yacht begins to glow from within.
13. **The Watch** — On second watch, Voss sees a figure on the deck of the *Mermaid*. He radios the bridge. No one else is aboard.
14. **The Journal Entry** — Shaw reads the first full entry. The yacht was celebrating a record passage. Morale was high.
15. **Strange Course** — The *Mermaid* begins to drift starboard. The tow line strains. Shaw corrects course, but the yacht resists.
16. **The Second Entry** — Passengers complained of a smell. The crew checked the vents. Something was wrong with the air circulation.
17. **Music** — Ana hears piano music across the water. A waltz. The notes are clear. They come from the ballroom.
18. **Harlow's Discovery** — The engineer checks the tow cable and finds it scored — as if something tried to cut it from the other side.
19. **The Third Entry** — The captain of the *Mermaid* noted electrical failures. Systems failed one by one. Communications went dark.
20. **The Figure Returns** — Jess sees the figure again, closer now. It is a woman in a blue dress. She stands at the railing, watching the tug.

### Part III: The Unraveling (Chapters 21–30)

21. **No Power** — The *Mermaid* never had a generator failure. Harlow confirms the batteries are fully charged. The yacht could have called for help.
22. **They Chose Not To** — Shaw realizes: no distress call was ever sent. The passengers knew they were dying and did nothing.
23. **The Fourth Entry** — The yacht's captain recorded a change in the passengers. They became calm. Eerily serene. No one wanted to leave the ballroom.
24. **The Dance** — Ana watches through a porthole. The passengers are dancing inside the ballroom. Slow, deliberate movements.
25. **Voss Breaks** — First Mate Voss refuses to continue the watch. He locks himself in his cabin. Shaw relieves him.
26. **The Fifth Entry** — The *Mermaid's* captain stopped making entries. The last words are incomplete. "They are all — "
27. **The Engine Room** — Harlow hears knocking from below deck on the *Mermaid*. Rhythmic. Deliberate. Like someone signaling.
28. **Jess Goes Alone** — Jess boards the *Mermaid* alone to investigate. The interior is warm. Too warm. The smell of perfume is overwhelming.
29. **The Ballroom Door** — Jess opens the ballroom door a crack. The dancers stop. Every head turns toward her.
30. **Escape** — Jess runs. She crosses the deck, slips on the wet surface, nearly goes overboard. The tow line catches her.

### Part IV: The Resistance (Chapters 31–40)

31. **Aftermath** — Jess is pulled back aboard the tug. She refuses to describe what she saw. Shaw orders the crew to arm themselves.
32. **The Decision** — Shaw wants to cut the line. But maritime law requires they deliver the vessel or forfeit salvage. The money is too good.
33. **Cut** — Voss takes matters into his own hands. He grabs an axe and heads for the tow line. Harlow stops him.
34. **The Sixth Entry** — A final fragment of the log surfaces. The captain wrote that the passengers began to change. Their skin grew cold. Their eyes went dark. But they kept smiling.
35. **The Serenade** — Music plays from the *Mermaid* all night. A different song now. Slower. A requiem.
36. **Ana's Vigil** — Ana keeps watch. She speaks to the passengers through the radio. No one answers. But the lights blink in response.
37. **The Storm** — Weather turns. Waves build. The tow line strains. The *Mermaid* rides the swells like she is alive.
38. **Cracks** — A crack appears in the hull of the *Sea Serpent*. Not from the storm. From something striking from below.
39. **Shaw's Reckoning** — Shaw reads the full log. The truth dawns on him. The passengers are not dead. They are waiting. They have been waiting for new company.
40. **The Bargain** — Shaw makes a decision. He will not cut the line. He will deliver the *Mermaid* to port — but on his terms.

### Part V: The Last Voyage (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Ritual** — The crew notices the *Mermaid* is listing. Not from damage. From the passengers gathering on one side. All facing the tug.
42. **Voices** — Shaw hears them now. Not through the air. In his head. They are speaking. They want to come home.
43. **The Crossing Guard** — Coast Guard hails them. The *Mermaid* is not on any registry. No vessel by that name exists. Shaw continues anyway.
44. **The Fever** — Harlow falls ill. His skin is cold. His eyes are dark. He speaks in a voice that is not his own.
45. **Containment** — The crew locks Harlow in the engine room. He pounds on the door. The rhythm matches the music from the yacht.
46. **The Last Dance** — Ana sees them all on deck. All thirty-four. Standing in rows. Facing the tug. Their mouths move in silent song.
47. **Shaw's Confession** — Shaw confesses: he knew the *Mermaid* was cursed when he took the contract. He has salvaged haunted ships before. He thought he could handle it.
48. **The Line Breaks** — The tow cable snaps. Not from strain. From something cutting it from within the water. The *Mermaid* drifts free.
49. **The Choice** — Jess has a choice. Stay with the tug and survive. Or board the *Mermaid* and learn what they want. She boards.
50. **Homecoming** — The *Mermaid* sails on without a tow line, toward a destination that does not appear on any chart. Jess stands at the railing in a blue dress. The last passenger has joined the party.
