# The Broken Hour — Outline

## Premise

Felix Voss is a watchmaker in a small city, methodical and solitary, who keeps his own fractured sense of time at bay by repairing the broken clocks of strangers. One afternoon at 4:14 PM, he looks up from his workbench and sees a woman step into the street at the wrong moment — and a dark sedan mount the curb. Before he can move, the world resets. He is back at his bench, the hands of the wall clock reading 4:00 PM exactly. The hour plays again, identical in every detail, until he changes something. Felix quickly realizes he is trapped in a single hour — 4:00 to 5:00 PM — and that the disaster he glimpsed from his window is not random. Each loop teaches him more about the people who walk past his shop, the customer who brings in a broken watch, and the man in the coat who watches from the corner. But every attempt to stop the crash reveals new layers — and the deeper Felix digs, the more he suspects the broken hour is not a punishment. It is a mechanism. And he may be the one who wound it.

## Core Cast

- **Felix Voss** — A watchmaker in his mid-forties. Quiet, precise, haunted by a loss he has never named. He lives alone above his shop. He notices everything and says little.
- **Anna** — A woman in her late thirties who brings a vintage pocket watch to Felix for repair on the first iteration. She is nervous, evasive, and connected to the crash in ways Felix does not immediately understand.
- **The Man in the Coat** — A tall figure in a grey coat who stands at the corner of Felix's street every iteration, watching. He never enters the shop. He never speaks. His presence is the first clue that the loop is not random.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Fracture (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Workbench** — Felix Voss begins his afternoon at 4:00 PM, alone in his shop. His hands move with practiced precision over a customer's clock.
2. **Anna's Watch** — A woman enters the shop. She carries a gold pocket watch, stopped at 4:14. Felix notices her hands shake.
3. **The Man in the Coat** — Through the window, Felix sees a figure in a grey coat take position at the corner of Ash and Elm. He has never seen him before.
4. **Fourteen Minutes** — At 4:14, Felix looks up from the watch. A sedan rounds the corner too fast. A woman — Anna — steps into the street. Felix lunges.
5. **The Crash** — The sedan mounts the curb. Impact. Glass breaks. Felix feels a shock in his chest.
6. **Darkness** — Everything goes black. Felix feels himself falling through cold space.
7. **4:00** — Felix opens his eyes. He is at his workbench. The wall clock reads 4:00 PM. The bell over the door has not rung yet.
8. **Deja Vu** — Anna enters. She says the same words. The man in the coat takes his position. Felix watches, frozen.
9. **The Second Crash** — At 4:14, it happens again. Felix tries to shout. The bell drowns him out. The sedan hits.
10. **The Pattern** — Third iteration. Felix begins to take notes. He records every detail. The loop is real.

### Part II: The Map (Chapters 11–20)

11. **Small Changes** — Felix tries altering one thing: he closes the shop early. The crash still happens. The man in the coat shifts position.
12. **Anna's Secret** — Felix keeps Anna in the shop past 4:14 by pretending the watch needs immediate work. The sedan still comes. But Anna is not in the street.
13. **The Wrong Victim** — Without Anna in the street, someone else steps into the path. A boy with a bicycle. Felix did not see him coming.
14. **The Bystander's Name** — Felix learns the boy's name: Leo. He is twelve. His mother owns the bakery. Felix carries this knowledge into the next loop.
15. **The Sedan** — Felix studies the car. A black sedan, driver obscured. The license plate reads a single word: TEMPUS.
16. **The Man Watches** — Felix exits the shop early and approaches the man in the coat. The man turns and walks away. Felix follows. The loop resets before he catches him.
17. **Felix's Notebook** — Felix starts keeping a written log of each iteration. He fills pages with diagrams, times, trajectories.
18. **The Phone Call** — Felix tries calling the police before 4:14. The line is dead. Every time.
19. **The Broken Phone** — Felix checks his landline. It has been cut — the wire cleanly severed. The loop is not natural.
20. **Seventeen Iterations** — Felix counts seventeen resets. He has not slept. He has not eaten. But the hour is always fresh.

### Part III: The Mechanism (Chapters 21–30)

21. **Anna's Watch Repaired** — Felix fixes Anna's pocket watch on loop twenty-one. The hands move. She smiles. The crash still comes.
22. **The Watch Changes Things** — With the watch running, Anna's behavior shifts. She walks faster. She is trying to intercept someone.
23. **The Letter** — Felix finds a sealed envelope in Anna's coat pocket when she drops it. It is addressed to a name he does not recognize: Elias.
24. **Reading the Letter** — Felix opens the letter. It is a confession. Anna was paid to distract the driver of the sedan.
25. **The Conspiracy** — Felix realizes the crash is not an accident. It is a targeted event. Someone wants the sedan to stop at that corner.
26. **The Man in the Coat Speaks** — On iteration twenty-six, Felix corners the man. The man says three words: "Fix the hour."
27. **The Meaning** — Felix tries to understand. The man is a warden of time. Felix's shop sits on a fault line where hours fracture.
28. **Felix's Past** — The man reveals that Felix was once a chronologist. He chose to forget. He broke something twenty years ago.
29. **The Memory** — Felix recalls a night in a basement lab. A device. A woman's face. He pulled a lever he should not have touched.
30. **The Prison** — Felix understands: the broken hour is a prison he built for himself. The crash is a symptom. The real crime is in his past.

### Part IV: The Repairs (Chapters 31–40)

31. **Anna's Role** — Felix asks Anna directly what she knows. She was hired by a man named Voss — Felix's own surname. His estranged brother.
32. **The Brother** — Felix has a brother, Simon, who never forgave him for the accident in the lab. Simon wants Felix trapped in the hour forever.
33. **The Driver** — The sedan driver is Simon's agent. The crash is meant to kill Anna before she delivers a package to the courthouse.
34. **The Package** — Anna carries evidence of Simon's financial crimes. If the package reaches the judge by 5:00 PM, Simon goes to prison.
35. **Felix Chooses** — Felix must decide: stop the crash and free himself, or let Anna die and stay trapped. The man in the coat watches.
36. **Simon's Visit** — In one iteration, Simon enters the shop. He is older, harder. He tells Felix the loop will never end unless Felix gives up the watch.
37. **The Watch Secret** — Anna's pocket watch contains a microfilm. It is the evidence. Felix holds the key to his brother's freedom or ruin.
38. **The Hour's Weakness** — The man in the coat reveals that the loop can be broken if Felix lets the crash happen exactly as it did the first time — but with himself in the car's path.
39. **The Trade** — Felix realizes he can take Anna's place in the street. He can end the loop by absorbing the impact himself. But does he want to die?
40. **The Count** — Felix has forty-eight hours of lived experience compressed into forty loops. He is exhausted. He knows every face on the street. He is ready.

### Part V: The Escape (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Final Decision** — Felix decides. He will not let Anna die. He will not let his brother win. He will change the hour from within.
42. **The Plan** — Felix draws up a final strategy. He must intercept the package, confront the driver, and speak to the man in the coat one last time.
43. **Anna's Trust** — Felix tells Anna everything. She does not believe him at first. Then she watches the clock hit 4:14 and reset. She believes.
44. **The Sedan Stopped** — Felix places himself between the sedan and Anna. The driver swerves. The car hits the lamppost instead.
45. **The Brother Watches** — Simon appears from the crowd. He is furious. The loop tries to pull Felix back, but the hour holds.
46. **The Man's Gift** — The man in the coat steps forward. He touches Felix's chest. "You remembered. The hour is yours again."
47. **The Watch Runs** — Anna's pocket watch, fully repaired, ticks past 4:14 for the first time in the real world. The loop does not reset.
48. **5:00 PM** — The hour ends. Felix stands at his shop door. The street is whole. The man in the coat is gone. Anna delivers her package.
49. **Simon's Arrest** — Two days later, Felix reads the news. Simon Voss has been arrested for fraud and attempted murder. Felix is free.
50. **The Workbench** — Felix sits at his bench. He picks up a broken clock. He begins to repair it. The hour moves forward, one second at a time. He is grateful for each one.
