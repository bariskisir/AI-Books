# Paper Tigers

**Genre:** Contemporary Fiction | **Status:** Complete

## About

Three generations of Chinese-American women in San Francisco confront the secrets that have shaped them during one Lunar New Year.

Lily Chen fled Mao's China at eighteen with nothing but a jade bracelet and a photograph of her father. Now seventy-eight, she rules her family with silence and judgment. Her daughter June has spent forty years building the perfect American life, only to find that her own daughter is a stranger. When June's daughter Mei returns home for Lunar New Year, she carries a truth she has been hiding for years.

Set against the lantern-lit streets of San Francisco's Chinatown, *Paper Tigers* is a novel about inheritance, identity, and the courage it takes to break a family's oldest silences.

## Characters

- **Lily Chen** — The grandmother. Born in Guangzhou, 1948. Fled China in 1966. Owns a shop in Chinatown.
- **June Chen-Lin** — The mother. A senior accountant, married to David. Has spent her life building stability and avoiding emotion.
- **Mei Lin** — The daughter. A graphic designer in Brooklyn. Returns for Lunar New Year with a secret she can no longer keep.
- **David Lin** — June's husband. A software engineer. Patient and quietly supportive.
- **Zoe** — Mei's girlfriend. A nurse from Brooklyn. Her grandmother is Korean.
- **Auntie Grace** — June's sister. Free-spirited. Moves to Seattle during the novel.
