## Premise

Three generations of Chinese-American women in San Francisco confront the secrets that have shaped them during one Lunar New Year. Lily, the grandmother who fled Mao's China with nothing, now rules her family with silence and judgment. June, her daughter, has spent forty years trying to be American enough while raising a daughter who seems to reject everything she sacrificed for. Mei, the youngest, returns from New York carrying a revelation that will shatter the careful distances they have all maintained.

## Characters

- **Lily Chen** (78) — Grandmother. Born Guangzhou 1948, fled in 1966. Shop owner in Chinatown.
- **June Chen-Lin** (54) — Mother. Senior accountant. Married to David Lin.
- **Mei Lin** (28) — Daughter. Graphic designer in Brooklyn.
- **David Lin** — June's husband. Software engineer.
- **Zoe** — Mei's girlfriend. Nurse in Brooklyn.
- **Auntie Grace** — June's sister.
- **Kevin** — Grace's boyfriend.

## Part Structure

1. **Part I: The Kitchen God Reports** (Chapters 1-10) — The week before New Year's Eve, preparations begin, tensions simmer.
2. **Part II: The Reunion Dinner** (Chapters 11-20) — New Year's Eve, the family gathers, old fault lines open.
3. **Part III: The Breaking** (Chapters 21-30) — The days after New Year, the surface cracks.
4. **Part IV: The Reckoning** (Chapters 31-40) — Five days before the Lantern Festival, the deeper work.
5. **Part V: The Lantern Festival** (Chapters 41-50) — The final night, release and renewal.

## Notes

- The title "Paper Tigers" references the Chinese concept of a hollow threat — the novel's central metaphor for fear that appears real until confronted.
- The jade bracelet functions as a physical symbol of inheritance and connection across generations.
- The novel is structured around the Lunar New Year celebration, using the traditional 15-day festival as a timeline.
