# Paper Tigers — Outline

**Genre:** Contemporary Fiction
**Setting:** San Francisco, present day
**Timeline:** The two weeks before, during, and after Lunar New Year

## Premise

Three generations of Chinese-American women gather for Lunar New Year in San Francisco, each carrying secrets that have shaped their lives. Lily, the grandmother who fled Mao's China with nothing, now rules her family with silence and judgment. June, her daughter, has spent forty years trying to be American enough while raising a daughter who seems to reject everything she sacrificed for. Mei, the youngest, returns from New York carrying a revelation that will shatter the careful distances they have all maintained. Over the course of two weeks, the Lunar New Year preparations force them into proximity, and the secrets they have kept from one another finally surface, revealing that the threats they feared were never as real as the love they refused to name.

## Core Cast

- **Lily Chen** (grandmother, 78) — Born in Guangzhou, 1948. Fled to Hong Kong in 1966 at the start of the Cultural Revolution. Worked in a garment factory for twenty years before opening her own shop in Chinatown. Widowed. Speaks Cantonese and fractured English. Keeps a jade bracelet her mother pressed into her hand the day she left. Has never spoken of what happened to her father.

- **June Chen-Lin** (mother, 54) — Born in Hong Kong, raised in San Francisco from age seven. A senior accountant at a mid-sized firm. Married to David Lin, a software engineer, for twenty-eight years. She has spent her life building stability — the mortgage, the 401(k), the right school districts. She speaks fluent English with no accent and resents when people ask where she is from.

- **Mei Lin** (daughter, 28) — Born in San Francisco. Graduated from Columbia. Works as a graphic designer in Brooklyn. Has a girlfriend she has not told her family about. She visits twice a year and spends every visit feeling like a stranger in her own childhood home.

## Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Kitchen God Reports (Chapters 1-10)
*The week before New Year's Eve — preparations begin, tensions simmer.*

1. **The Flight** — Mei lands at SFO. The fog is so thick the plane circles twice. She texts her girlfriend, Zoe: *I am already drowning.* Zoe writes back: *You are not even there yet.*
2. **The Apartment on Waverly** — June scrubs the kitchen floor on her hands and knees. It is already clean. She cannot stop. David watches from the doorway and says nothing.
3. **The Old Country** — Lily sorts through dried mushrooms in her kitchen above the shop. A customer asks about her plans for New Year. Lily says the same thing she says every year: *The same as always.*
4. **The Food** — June makes a list. Sticky rice, lotus root, dried scallops, black moss, sea cucumber, abalone. Mei walks in and says: *Mom, there are two of us eating.*
5. **The Argument That Is Not an Argument** — Mei offers to help with the cooking. June says no. Mei asks why. June says because. The silence that follows is older than Mei.
6. **The Shop** — Mei visits her grandmother's shop for the first time in a year. Lily is helping a young couple pick a tea set. She is warm with them. With Mei, she is formal.
7. **The Bracelet** — Lily catches Mei staring at the jade bracelet. She does not offer to tell its story. She does not need to. The absence of the story is itself the story.
8. **The Invitation** — June's sister, Auntie Grace, calls to say she is bringing her new boyfriend to the reunion dinner. June asks if he is Chinese. Grace laughs and hangs up.
9. **The Question** — David asks Mei if she is seeing anyone. Mei says no. She watches her mother's shoulders relax. The lie sits on the table between them like an untouched dish.
10. **The Ghost** — Lily wakes at 3 a.m. from a dream about her father. She has been having this dream for sixty years. She gets up and makes tea. The kitchen is dark. The city is quiet. She does not turn on the light.

### Part II: The Reunion Dinner (Chapters 11-20)
*New Year's Eve — the family gathers, and old fault lines open.*

11. **The Table** — June sets the table for twelve. There will be eight. She sets it for twelve anyway, the way her mother always did. Hope and superstition, indistinguishable.
12. **The Arrival** — Auntie Grace arrives with Kevin, who is white, loud, and earnest. He shakes everyone's hand too hard. Lily stares at him for a long moment and then speaks to him in Cantonese, knowing he will not understand.
13. **The Offering** — Mei brings a bottle of wine. June puts it aside and opens the one she bought. Mei says nothing. She has learned this.
14. **The Toasts** — David raises his glass. He says the right things. Health, happiness, prosperity. Lily mutters something under her breath. June pretends not to hear.
15. **The Dumpling** — Lily finds the coin dumpling. She has found it every year for thirty years. She slips it to Mei's plate when no one is looking.
16. **The Question of the Future** — Auntie Grace announces she is moving to Seattle. June's face goes still. Grace says: *I did not ask your permission.*
17. **The Phone Call** — Mei excuses herself to the bathroom and calls Zoe. She whispers: *I have to tell them.* Zoe says: *Then tell them.* Mei says: *You do not understand.*
18. **The Red Envelopes** — Lily hands out lai see. She gives each adult the same amount. But she holds Mei's hand an extra second when she passes hers over. June notices.
19. **The Dishes** — June and Grace wash dishes together. They do not look at each other. Grace says: *You think I am running away.* June says: *I think you always do.* Grace laughs. It is not a kind laugh.
20. **The Departure** — The guests leave. The apartment is quiet. Lily sits alone at the empty table. She picks up a stray grain of rice and eats it.

### Part III: The Breaking (Chapters 21-30)
*The days after New Year — the surface cracks.*

21. **The Walk** — Mei walks through Chinatown alone. The streets are still loud with firecracker wrappers and celebration. She feels invisible in plain sight.
22. **The Confession** — Mei tells her mother she is gay. They are in the kitchen. June is chopping vegetables. The knife does not stop. June says: *You do not have to decide that now.*
23. **The Denial** — June leaves the kitchen. She goes to the bedroom and closes the door. She sits on the edge of the bed and stares at the wall. She does not cry. She has trained herself not to.
24. **The Silence** — An entire day passes without a word between them. David moves between them like a diplomat in a war zone. Mei packs her bag.
25. **The Grandmother** — Mei goes to Lily's shop. She does not tell her. She just sits on the stool by the counter. Lily watches her for an hour. Then she says: *You look like your mother when she is afraid.*
26. **The Story of the Bracelet** — Lily tells Mei about the jade bracelet for the first time. Her mother gave it to her the night before she left Guangzhou. She never saw her mother again. *Some goodbyes are silent,* Lily says. *That does not make them unloving.*
27. **The Fight** — June finds Mei at Lily's shop. The three generations stand in the narrow aisle between shelves of porcelain. June says: *You told your grandmother before you told me.* Mei says: *I told you first. You walked away.*
28. **The Father** — David takes June to their bedroom and closes the door. He speaks quietly. He says: *She is our daughter. She has always been our daughter.* June says: *I know.* She says it like it is the worst thing she knows.
29. **The Letter** — Lily goes to her bedroom and takes a box from the back of her closet. Inside is a letter her father wrote in 1966, the last year he was free. She has never shown it to anyone. She reads it again.
30. **The Reaching** — June knocks on Mei's door at midnight. She says: *I do not understand. But I want to.* It is not enough. It is a start.

### Part IV: The Reckoning (Chapters 31-40)
*Five days before the Lantern Festival — the deeper work.*

31. **The History** — Lily tells June about the letter from her grandfather. A university professor accused of bourgeois thinking. Arrested in 1966. Never seen again. Lily was eighteen. She never spoke of it because speaking did not save him.
32. **The Inheritance** — June realizes her silence is not her own. It is her mother's. And her mother's before that. A line of women who learned that safety lived in stillness.
33. **The Parallel** — June remembers 1989. She was in college. She watched the news from Tiananmen Square. She did not call her mother. She did not know how. She still does not.
34. **The Visit** — Mei brings Zoe to San Francisco. She introduces her as her roommate. June looks at Zoe. Zoe looks back. Neither of them blinks.
35. **The Recognition** — At dinner, Zoe mentions her own grandmother in Seoul. Lily listens. After dinner, Lily says to June in Cantonese: *She has kind eyes.* June pretends not to understand.
36. **The Walk Together** — Lily, June, Mei, and Zoe walk through the lantern displays in Portsmouth Square. They move in a loose formation, never quite in step. But they move together.
37. **The Photograph** — David takes a picture of the four of them under the lanterns. Later, June looks at it alone. She zooms in on Mei's face. Mei is smiling. A real smile. June has not seen it in years.
38. **The Gift** — Lily takes the jade bracelet off her wrist and holds it out to Mei. Mei says: *I cannot take this.* Lily says: *It is not for you. It is for her. When you are ready.*
39. **The Night Before** — The night before the Lantern Festival. Mei and Zoe share the guest room. June passes the door and hears them laughing. She stands in the hallway for a long time.
40. **The Decision** — June tells David she wants to meet Zoe properly. Not as a roommate. As her daughter's partner. David kisses her forehead. He says: *That is my girl.*

### Part V: The Lantern Festival (Chapters 41-50)
*The final night — release and renewal.*

41. **The Invitation** — June invites Zoe to the Lantern Festival dinner. She says: *As family.* Mei cries. She has not cried until now.
42. **The Preparation** — They cook together. June, Mei, Zoe. Lily watches from her chair, offering occasional instructions that no one follows. It is the first time they have cooked together without arguing.
43. **The Table Again** — June sets the table. Twelve settings again. This time, Zoe sits beside Mei. Lily sits at the head. She nods at Zoe. A small thing. Everything.
44. **The Toast** — David gives a toast. He talks about family. About how family is not who you were born to. It is who you choose to feed. Lily snorts. But she does not disagree.
45. **The Story** — Lily tells a story about crossing the border from China to Hong Kong in 1966. She walked for three days. She carried nothing but the bracelet and a photograph of her father. She does not tell them she still has the photograph.
46. **The Question** — Mei asks Lily if she was afraid. Lily is quiet for a long time. Then she says: *Every day. But fear is a paper tiger. It looks real until you walk through it.*
47. **The Understanding** — June realizes her mother has been telling her this her whole life, just in different languages. She reaches under the table and takes Mei's hand. Mei squeezes back.
48. **The Lanterns** — They go to the festival. The sky fills with light. Mei releases a lantern with a wish written on it. She does not tell anyone what she wrote. But she looks at Zoe when she lets go.
49. **The Photograph, Again** — David takes another picture. All of them this time. Lily is almost smiling. June's hand is on Mei's shoulder. Mei's hand is in Zoe's. The lanterns blur in the background like falling stars.
50. **The Morning After** — Mei and Zoe fly back to New York. June stands at the window and watches the plane arc over the Pacific. Lily sits in her kitchen above the shop. She takes out her father's photograph and holds it to the light. The city hums below her. Somewhere, the last lanterns drift out over the bay. The new year has begun.
