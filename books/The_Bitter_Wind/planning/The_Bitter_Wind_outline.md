# The Bitter Wind — Outline

## Premise

After a small passenger plane crashes on the Greenland ice cap during a whiteout, seven strangers survive the impact but face an even crueler trial: only one emergency ration kit remains intact among the wreckage. The kit contains enough food and medical supplies for one person to survive a week — maybe long enough for rescue. Dr. Singh, a trauma surgeon with a broken moral compass, argues for triage logic. The Pilot, whose error caused the crash, sees it as his burden to carry. The Soldier, a veteran of cold-weather operations, knows the ice waits for no one's conscience. And The Child, a twelve-year-old stowaway, forces every adult to confront the question none of them wants to answer. As the bitter wind strips away warmth, civility, and hope, the survivors must decide not just who lives — but what kind of person they are willing to become.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Arun Singh** — A trauma surgeon from Delhi, haunted by a malpractice case he left unfinished. Methodical, cold, logical. He sees the ration problem as a clinical triage decision. But the ice has a way of thawing buried guilt.

- **The Pilot (Erik Larsson)** — Swedish, mid-forties. A bush pilot with a spotty record. The crash was his fault — he ignored weather warnings to make up time. He carries the weight of seven lives and a flask he pretends is water.

- **The Soldier (Sergeant Maya Chen)** — U.S. Army Arctic veteran, on leave after a disastrous patrol in Norway. She knows survival protocols cold. But knowing how to survive and wanting to survive are different things.

- **The Child (Levi)** — Twelve years old. Not on the manifest. He stowed away in the cargo hold to escape a group home in Nuuk. He says little, observes everything, and understands more than the adults want him to.

- **The Businessman (Richard Mercer)** — CEO of a mining corporation, on his way to negotiate drilling rights. He is used to buying what he needs. He offers cash for the ration kit.

- **The Anthropologist (Dr. Freja Lindholm)** — Danish, specializing in Greenlandic Inuit survival traditions. She knows the old ways — how to read ice, find water, endure. Her knowledge is the group's best asset, if they will listen.

- **The Wounded Man (Henrik)** — A Danish construction worker, leg crushed in the crash. He cannot walk. He cannot contribute. He is the ration problem made flesh.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. The plane enters the whiteout — a wall of nothing that swallows the world.
2. Impact — the fuselage tears across the ice, metal screaming.
3. Silence — then the wind. Survivors emerge from the wreckage, bleeding and disoriented.
4. Dr. Singh takes charge of triage, assessing injuries with clinical detachment.
5. The Pilot stands apart, staring at the hole they came through. He does not meet anyone's eyes.
6. Sergeant Chen finds Levi in the cargo compartment, curled between luggage.
7. Richard Mercer demands to use the satellite phone. It is dead.
8. Freja Lindholm takes bearings. She knows this ice. She knows how bad it is.
9. Henrik is pinned under a structural beam. They free him, but his leg is ruined.
10. First night falls. The cold is a living thing, pressing through the ruptured skin of the fuselage.
11. Morning inventory: the emergency supplies are scattered across half a kilometer of ice.
12. Levi finds the ration kit wedged in a crevasse, untouched. It is the only intact one.
13. The group gathers. Mercer asks who gets the kit.
14. Dr. Singh proposes triage: allocate resources based on survival probability.
15. Sergeant Chen refuses the framework. She has seen triage before. It never ends cleanly.
16. Freja suggests they share everything equally until rescue comes. She knows it will not be enough.
17. The Pilot speaks for the first time. He will take nothing for himself.
18. Levi asks a simple question: "What happens when the food runs out?"
19. Henrik, feverish, offers himself as a sacrifice. Remove the weakest variable.
20. Mercer makes a direct offer: five thousand dollars for the ration kit. Cash in his wallet.
21. Dr. Singh intervenes. Money means nothing on the ice. He takes custody of the kit.
22. The wound on Henrik's leg turns septic. Dr. Singh operates with a pocketknife and a bottle of duty-free vodka.
23. Freja builds an igloo-style windbreak. She teaches them to pack snow for insulation.
24. Sergeant Chen leads a trek to recover more scattered supplies. They find a melted radio.
25. The Pilot goes alone to the ridge. He returns with nothing. His flask is empty now.
26. Levi finds a signal flare gun. He does not tell anyone. He keeps it hidden.
27. Day four. Hunger sharpens everything — words, tempers, desires.
28. Mercer tries to take the ration kit by force. Sergeant Chen disarms him without speaking.
29. Dr. Singh gives a portion of the kit's food to Henrik against his better medical judgment.
30. Freja tells stories of Greenlandic survival — the old way, the shared burden.
31. The Pilot admits to Freja that he was drinking before the flight. He has not said it aloud before.
32. Sergeant Chen's frostbitten fingers worsen. She hides the extent of the damage.
33. A storm pins the group inside the wreckage for thirty-six hours.
34. Levi shows Freja the flare gun. She tells him to keep it hidden a little longer.
35. Henrik's fever breaks. Dr. Singh is surprised. He calculated differently.
36. The satellite phone — Mercer has been quietly charging it from the plane's battery. It gets one bar.
37. The call connects. A rescue coordinator says a weather window opens in four days.
38. Four days. The ration kit will feed one person for seven. Or seven people for one.
39. Dr. Singh divides the remaining food. Each person gets precisely calculated portions.
40. Mercer refuses his portion. He has been eating his emergency chocolate bars in secret. Levi saw him.
41. Sergeant Chen confronts Mercer. The group turns on him. He is no longer one of them.
42. The Pilot rigs the wreckage's wiring to drain the remaining battery for heat. One last night of warmth.
43. Freja sings an old Greenlandic song about a hunter who shares his seal with a stranger.
44. Levi asks Dr. Singh if he has children. The question lands like a blade.
45. Dr. Singh tells Levi about the patient he lost — the one he walked away from.
46. The rescue plane approaches but cannot land. The whiteout has returned.
47. Hope collapses. The remaining rations are gone. They have nothing left.
48. Sergeant Chen makes a decision. She will hike out to find help. It is a one-way walk.
49. The wind breaks at dawn. The rescue plane returns. They are spotted.
50. On the tarmac in Nuuk, the seven survivors stand together. They do not speak. They do not need to. They have been changed by the ice, and they carry its weight in silence.
