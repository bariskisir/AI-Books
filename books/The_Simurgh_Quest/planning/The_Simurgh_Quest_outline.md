# The Simurgh Quest — Outline

## Premise

When the Shahanshah of Persia is struck by a mysterious wasting curse, the court astrologers declare that only a feather from the Simurgh — the primordial bird of Persian myth — can save him. The task falls to Rostam Karimi, a disgraced royal cartographer who once mapped the empire's lost borders and now seeks redemption after a failed rebellion cost him his rank and his family. He is joined by Parisa Daneshvar, a warrior woman of the Jeyan tribe whose village was burned on the Shah's orders, and who sees the quest as a path to a different kind of justice. Guided by the cryptic predictions of the Zoroastrian priest Darius Kamgar, the pair must cross the Seven Valleys of Persian mysticism — each a literal and spiritual trial — while the Shah's ambitious vizier, Asmodeus, dispatches assassins to ensure the Simurgh never heals the king he intends to replace. The quest becomes a confrontation with their own past sins, the nature of power, and the truth behind the myth.

## Core Cast

- **Rostam Karimi** (Protagonist): A 37-year-old former royal cartographer who once charted the empire's farthest reaches. Tall and deliberate, with a mapmaker's precise hands and a scholar's hollowed eyes. He was disgraced and his wife and son taken as collateral after he failed to prevent a border rebellion. Guilt drives him; he believes finding the Simurgh will earn him the Shah's pardon and restore his family.

- **Parisa Daneshvar** (Co-Protagonist): A 32-year-old warrior of the Jeyan tribe, wiry and quick, with burn scars across her left shoulder from the night her village was razed. She is a master archer and tracker, fluent in the mountain dialects of the northern provinces. She carries a deep hatred for the Shah but agrees to the quest after the priest Darius reveals the Simurgh can revive her dead clan.

- **Darius Kamgar** (Mentor): A 55-year-old Zoroastrian mobad (high priest) from the Temple of Adur Gushnasp. He wears white robes perpetually stained with saffron and ash. Blind in one eye from a childhood ritual accident, he speaks in riddles and reads signs in smoke, water, and the flight of birds. He knows more about the Simurgh than he reveals, and his motives are not purely altruistic.

- **Asmodeus** (Antagonist): The Shah's ambitious vizier, a silver-tongued courtier who has secretly been poisoning the Shah for years. He sees the Simurgh quest as a threat to his plan to seize the throne, and dispatches assassins to stop the party.

- **Shahanshah Khosrow** (Ruler): The aging King of Kings, struck by a curse that may or may not be natural. His rule has been long and sometimes cruel.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Curse (Chapters 1-6)
1. **The Shah's Dream** — Shahanshah Khosrow dreams of a great bird consuming the sun; wakes with a black mark spreading on his chest.
2. **The Curse Made Visible** — The court physicians are baffled; the curse spreads; panic grips Ctesiphon.
3. **The Mapmaker's Cell** — Rostam Karimi languishes in a debtor's cell, learning of the Shah's illness from a guard.
4. **The Warrior's Debt** — Parisa Daneshvar is arrested in a tavern brawl; in the dungeons she meets Darius Kamgar.
5. **The Priest's Confession** — Darius reveals to the captives that the Simurgh is real and the quest has been foreseen.
6. **The Shah's Decree** — The Shah declares a royal pardon for whoever brings the Simurgh's feather; Rostam volunteers.

### Part II: The Road of Trials (Chapters 7-13)
7. **The First Step West** — Rostam, Parisa, and Darius depart Ctesiphon into the Zagros foothills.
8. **The Village of Ashes** — They find a burned village; Parisa recognizes the work of the Shah's soldiers.
9. **The First Ambush** — Asmodeus's hired swords attack the party at a river crossing.
10. **The Valley of Quest** — They enter the First Valley: Valley of Quest, where the road becomes a maze.
11. **The Wind That Remembers** — A magical wind in the valley shows them visions of their past failures.
12. **The Caravanserai of Ghosts** — They shelter in an abandoned inn where the dead still walk.
13. **The Well of Truth** — Darius forces Rostam and Parisa to drink from a well that reveals hidden truths.

### Part III: The Shadow of Court (Chapters 14-20)
14. **Asmodeus at Court** — Asmodeus tightens his hold on the Shah, accelerating his poison.
15. **The Fire Temple** — The party visits the Temple of Adur Gushnasp, where Darius receives a troubling omen.
16. **The Map of Shadows** — Rostam's old maps reveal a hidden route through the Alborz mountains.
17. **The Snow Pass** — They cross a treacherous mountain pass; Parisa saves Rostam from a fall.
18. **The Valley of Love** — The Second Valley: they encounter a village under an enchantment of paralyzing love.
19. **The Rose Garden Trap** — A beautiful garden hides a div's trap; Parisa's arrows break the spell.
20. **The Div's Bargain** — A div offers them safe passage in exchange for a memory.

### Part IV: The Desert of the Soul (Chapters 21-27)
21. **The Village of the Blind** — A village whose inhabitants were blinded by a false prophet.
22. **The Second Ambush** — More of Asmodeus's assassins strike, this time with poisoned darts.
23. **The Salt Desert** — They cross the Dasht-e Kavir; salt plains stretch to every horizon.
24. **The Mirage City** — A city appears that isn't there; Rostam nearly dies of thirst chasing it.
25. **The Valley of Insight** — The Third Valley: they see the world as it truly is, not as they wish it.
26. **The Truth About the Rebellion** — Parisa learns that Rostam's cartography inadvertently enabled the raid on her village.
27. **Parisa's Trial** — Parisa must choose between vengeance and purpose.

### Part V: The Depths (Chapters 28-34)
28. **Darius's Shadow** — Darius reveals his true reason for the quest: he once failed the Simurgh and seeks atonement.
29. **The Caravan of the Dead** — A ghost caravan crosses the desert; the dead offer cryptic warnings.
30. **The Lake of Night** — A subterranean lake beneath the Alborz where time moves differently.
31. **The Valley of Detachment** — The Fourth Valley: they must surrender their worldly attachments.
32. **The Nameless City** — Ruins of a pre-Persian civilization where the Simurgh was once worshipped.
33. **The Hall of Cages** — A chamber where past versions of themselves are imprisoned.
34. **The Div of the Cages** — The div who guards the hall offers to free them if they admit their worst sin.

### Part VI: The Approach (Chapters 35-41)
35. **The Second Trial of Letting Go** — Rostam must let go of his hope of seeing his family again.
36. **The Valley of Unity** — The Fifth Valley: all distinctions dissolve; they see the universe as one.
37. **The Mirror Plain** — A plain of mirrors that reflects their innermost selves.
38. **The Choice at the Mirror** — Parisa must choose between the person she is and the person she could be.
39. **The Simurgh's Feather Fall** — A single feather falls from the sky, marking the path to the final valleys.
40. **The Valley of Bewilderment** — The Sixth Valley: reason fails; the world makes no sense.
41. **The Stairway to Qaf** — A stair carved into the sky; they climb toward Mount Qaf.

### Part VII: The Summit (Chapters 42-50)
42. **The Gardens of Qaf** — The cosmic mountain's gardens, where time and space fold.
43. **The Nest of the Simurgh** — They find the Simurgh's nest — but the bird is not there.
44. **The Simurgh Revealed** — The Simurgh appears as a being of thirty colors, each a different truth.
45. **The Question of Worth** — The Simurgh asks each of them why they deserve what they seek.
46. **The Priest's Plea** — Darius begs the Simurgh to take his life instead of granting their wishes.
47. **Rostam's Bargain** — Rostam must choose between saving the Shah and freeing his family.
48. **The Feather of Thirty Colors** — The Simurgh grants a single feather, but warns of its price.
49. **The Return Journey** — The party descends from Mount Qaf; Asmodeus makes his final play.
50. **The Mapmaker's Choice** — Rostam returns to Ctesiphon and makes a choice that redefines his life.
