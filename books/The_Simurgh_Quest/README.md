# The Simurgh Quest

**Genre:** Mythological Fantasy  
**Setting:** Ancient Persia (Sassanian Era)  
**Chapters:** 50  
**Word Count:** 26,554  

## Synopsis

When Shahanshah Khosrow of Persia is struck by a mysterious wasting curse, the court astrologers declare that only a feather from the Simurgh — the primordial bird of Persian myth — can save him. The task falls to Rostam Karimi, a disgraced royal cartographer who once mapped the empire's lost borders and now seeks redemption after a failed rebellion cost him his rank and his family. He is joined by Parisa Daneshvar, a warrior woman of the Jeyan tribe whose village was burned on the Shah's orders, and who sees the quest as a path to a different kind of justice. Guided by the cryptic predictions of the Zoroastrian priest Darius Kamgar, the pair must cross the Seven Valleys of Persian mysticism — each a literal and spiritual trial — while the Shah's ambitious vizier, Asmodeus, dispatches assassins to ensure the Simurgh never heals the king he intends to replace.

## Characters

- **Rostam Karimi** — Disgraced royal cartographer, seeking redemption
- **Parisa Daneshvar** — Warrior of the Jeyan tribe, master archer
- **Darius Kamgar** — Zoroastrian high priest, blind in one eye
- **Asmodeus** — The Shah's ambitious vizier
- **Shahanshah Khosrow** — The dying King of Kings
