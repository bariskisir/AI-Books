# The Hireling — Outline

## Premise

Remy Deschamps is a fixer in Casablanca, 1941 — a man who finds things, loses things, and makes problems disappear for the right price. When a French expatriate named Lucien Moreau hires him to find a missing woman named Leila Bensoussan, Remy expects a routine case of a husband or a debt. But Leila was last seen near the old medina carrying documents that could fracture the fragile power balance of Vichy-controlled Morocco. As Remy pulls threads through the city's smoke-filled cafes, back-alley safe houses, and the fortress-like police prefecture, he discovers that Leila is no ordinary missing person — she is a Courier for the Resistance, and half of Casablanca wants her silenced. Inspector Roux, a Vichy officer with his own secret calculus, shadows every move. The deeper Remy digs, the more the case closes around him, and the question becomes not whether he will find Leila, but whether he will survive what he finds.

## Core Cast

- **Remy Deschamps** — A fixer in Casablanca. Former soldier, former lover, current cynic. He takes jobs that other men refuse and asks no questions — until this one.
- **Leila Bensoussan** — The missing woman. A Jewish-Algerian schoolteacher turned Resistance Courier. Intelligent, careful, and carrying a secret that could collapse the Vichy administration in Morocco.
- **Inspector Marcel Roux** — Vichy police inspector. A man of rules and rationed morality. He watches Remy, but his loyalties are a locked room.
- **Lucien Moreau** — The man who hires Remy. A French wine merchant with a missing sister-in-law, or so he claims. His grief is a performance.
- **Simone Bensoussan** — Leila's mother. An aging woman in a cramped apartment who waits for news that will not come.
- **Youssef Benali** — A smuggler who knows every tunnel under the medina. Remy's oldest contact and the closest thing he has to a friend.
- **Captain Dietrich** — A German liaison officer attached to the Vichy prefecture. He does not raise his voice, and that is what makes him dangerous.
- **Marguerite Duvall** — Owner of the Café de l'Oasis. She sees everything from behind the bar and sells information like coffee.
- **Father Antoine** — A priest at the Cathedral of the Sacred Heart. His confessional is a dead drop.
- **Kamel** — A street boy who runs messages for Remy. Quick, invisible, and smarter than anyone knows.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Summons (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Fixer** — Remy sits in the Café de l'Oasis nursing a coffee. A man approaches with an envelope of francs and a name: Leila Bensoussan. The job is simple — find her.
2. **The Client** — Lucien Moreau tells Remy that Leila is his wife's sister, missing for six days. The police will not help. Remy takes the money.
3. **The Mother** — Remy visits Simone Bensoussan in her dark apartment. She gives him a photograph, a letter, and a lie that he does not catch until later.
4. **The Last Sighting** — A shopkeeper in the medina remembers Leila buying bread on Tuesday. She was nervous. A man in a grey suit followed her out.
5. **The Inspector** — Inspector Roux intercepts Remy on the street. A quiet warning: this case is closed. Walk away. Remy does not walk.
6. **The Ledger** — Youssef Benali, in his cave-like shop beneath the medina, reveals that Leila bought a forged travel pass three weeks ago. Destination: Lisbon.
7. **The Room** — Remy searches Leila's rented room. Under a loose floorboard, he finds a railroad timetable and a charred fragment of a letter in German.
8. **The German** — Captain Dietrich visits the prefecture. Remy watches from a cafe across the street. Dietrich emerges carrying a folder marked with Leila's name.
9. **The Confession** — Father Antoine tells Remy that Leila came to him frightened. She said she was carrying something that belonged to a dead man. She would not say what.
10. **The Café** — Marguerite Duvall gives Remy a name: the grey-suited man from the medina. His name is Vernet. He works for the prefecture.

### Part II: The Thread (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Tail** — Remy follows Vernet through the European quarter. Vernet meets a man in a fez outside the post office. They exchange a leather satchel.
12. **The Break-In** — Remy breaks into Vernet's office at the prefecture. He finds a dossier on Leila with photographs and a list of names — all Jewish intellectuals.
13. **The Confrontation** — Vernet corners Remy in an alley. There is a fight. Remy wins but takes a knife wound to the ribs. Vernet escapes.
14. **The Doctor** — A Resistance-aligned medic stitches Remy in a basement. He says the name of the dead man Leila spoke of: Antoine Bensoussan — her father.
15. **The Archive** — Remy learns from Simone that her husband Antoine was a historian who documented Vichy collaboration in Morocco. He died in custody six months ago.
16. **The German Request** — Captain Dietrich formally requests Remy's cooperation. Find Leila and deliver her to the prefecture. The offer is not optional.
17. **The Compromise** — Remy agrees to work for Dietrich while secretly feeding information to Youssef. He becomes a double agent in his own case.
18. **The List** — Youssef deciphers the railroad timetable. It maps a transport route for political prisoners to a port south of Casablanca.
19. **The Telegram** — A coded wire arrives at the Café de l'Oasis. It is from Leila. She is alive, hiding in the Atlas foothills, and she needs help.
20. **The Departure** — Remy leaves Casablanca by night, riding in a farmer's truck toward the mountains. Roux watches from the gate but does not stop him.

### Part III: The Courier (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Mountain** — Remy finds Leila in a stone shepherd's hut. She is thinner than her photograph, and her eyes are older. She trusts no one.
22. **The Document** — Leila shows Remy what she carries: her father's final testimony — a ledger of names, dates, and shipments. Proof of Vichy complicity in the Holocaust.
23. **The Story** — Leila tells Remy how she escaped. Her father was arrested. The night he died, a guard slipped her his notebook. She has been running since.
24. **The Hunter** — Dietrich has sent a team after them. Remy and Leila move deeper into the hills. They sleep in shifts and speak in whispers.
25. **The Village** — A Berber village takes them in for a night. An old woman reads Leila's palm and warns of a man with two faces. Remy thinks of Roux.
26. **The Trap** — Dietrich's men catch up. A firefight in a dry riverbed. Remy is wounded again. Leila drags him to safety through a cave system.
27. **The Pact** — In the dark of the cave, Remy and Leila strike a deal. He will get her to the British consulate in Tangier. She will give him the ledger to copy.
28. **The Return** — They make their way back toward Casablanca. Leila will hide with Youssef until passage can be arranged.
29. **The Betrayal** — When they reach the city, Youssef's shop is burned. Youssef is dead. A note in Vernet's handwriting: "You should have walked away."
30. **The Grief** — Remy buries what he can find of Youssef. Leila weeps for the first time. Remy realizes he has crossed a line — he is no longer a hireling. He is involved.

### Part IV: The Reckoning (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Proposal** — Marguerite offers to smuggle Leila out on a Portuguese freighter. Cost: the original ledger. Remy refuses. He does not trust her price.
32. **The Priest's Secret** — Father Antoine reveals that he has been passing messages for the Resistance. He can get a message to Tangier. Remy gives him a coded letter.
33. **The Double Game** — Remy meets with Dietrich, pretending to work the case. Dietrich shows him a photograph of Leila as a child. A warning: he knows everything.
34. **Roux's Choice** — Inspector Roux arrests Remy in the street. But instead of the prefecture, he takes him to his apartment. Roux is Resistance. He has been watching to see whose side Remy would choose.
35. **The Network** — Roux explains: the Resistance cell in Casablanca was shattered. Leila's father was its leader. The ledger is the only remaining copy of their intelligence.
36. **The Plan** — A coordinated operation: Remy will deliver a fake ledger to Dietrich while Leila escapes on the freighter with the real one. Roux will look the other way.
37. **The Preparation** — Remy forges a ledger using Youssef's old ledgers and the information Leila has memorized. It must look real enough to buy forty-eight hours.
38. **The Vigil** — The night before the operation. Remy and Leila sit on the roof of Youssef's ruined shop. She tells him about her father's garden. He tells her about the war he lost.
39. **The Handoff** — Remy delivers the forged ledger to Dietrich at the prefecture. Dietrich opens it, reads a page, and smiles. "You almost had me."
40. **The Collapse** — Dietrich knows. Someone talked. The freighter is watched. The safe house is compromised. Roux goes silent. Remy has hours.

### Part V: The Price (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Hunt** — Dietrich's men sweep the medina. Remy moves Leila through the tunnels Youssef showed him. They surface near the port, surrounded.
42. **The Sacrifice** — Roux appears. He creates a diversion, drawing the soldiers away. A burst of German gunfire. Roux falls. Remy and Leila run.
43. **The Boat** — Marguerite's freighter is still in port. But the price has changed: the real ledger, or Leila stays. Remy hands over the document he copied from Leila's original.
44. **The Chase** — Dietrich himself pursues them onto the docks. A standoff. Leila boards the freighter. Dietrich raises his pistol.
45. **The Truth** — Remy tells Dietrich that the ledger he delivered was a forgery, but the real one is already on its way to the British consulate. A copy was made.
46. **The Shot** — Dietrich fires. The bullet hits Remy in the chest. He falls into the harbor. The freighter leaves. Leila watches from the rail as Remy sinks.
47. **The Float** — Remy does not die. The bullet passed through. Youssef's old smuggling partner fishes him from the water. He wakes in a fisherman's shack, wrapped in bandages.
48. **The Aftermath** — The ledger reaches Tangier. The British publish its contents. Vichy officials flee Casablanca. Dietrich is recalled to Berlin. The network scatters.
49. **The Return** — Remy walks through Casablanca a month later. The city is different. Marguerite gives him a letter. Leila made it to London. She is alive.
50. **The Hireling** — Remy sits at the Café de l'Oasis. A man approaches with an envelope. A new job. Remy looks at the money. He thinks of Leila, of Roux, of Youssef. He pushes the envelope back. The fixer is done fixing.
