# The Beekeeper's War — Outline

## Premise

Sgt. Thomas Bell, a village beekeeper from Sussex, serves on the Western Front in 1917. Before the war he kept thirty hives on the South Downs. Now he tends a small apiary behind the reserve lines, producing honey for the wounded and sending jars home to his wife, Clara. But Clara has stopped writing back. Between bombardments, gas attacks, and the daily horror of the trenches, Bell tends his bees and writes letters that go unanswered, haunted by the silence from home while the war devours everything around him.

## Core Cast

- **Sgt. Thomas Bell** — A beekeeper turned soldier, 38 years old. Quiet, observant, patient. Finds solace in the rhythm of the hives.
- **Lt. Philip Graves** — A young officer, 22. University-educated, idealistic at first, then ground down by the war. Bell's company commander.
- **Clara Bell** — Thomas's wife. We see her only through letters and memory.
- **The Bees** — Three hives that survive the winter of 1916-17. They become a lens for Bell's inner life.
- **Cpl. Jim Hartley** — A farmer's son from Yorkshire, Bell's friend in the trenches.
- **CSM Walter Finch** — The company sergeant major, a regular soldier from the old army.
- **Private Eli Cohen** — A young Jewish soldier from London's East End.
- **Private Danny O'Mara** — A drummer boy, only 17, who lies about his age.

## Chapter Plan

### Part I: Spring 1917 — The Hives

1. **The Bees at Dawn** — Bell checks his hives at first light behind the lines at Arras. Bees fly out over the mud.
2. **The Letter** — Bell writes to Clara, describing the apple blossom he found near the shattered orchard.
3. **Lt. Graves** — Graves arrives fresh from officer training. Bell teaches him about bees to calm the young man's nerves.
4. **Relief** — The company rotates out of the line. Bell finds a jar of honey he saved for Clara.
5. **The Wounded** — Bell brings honey to the field hospital for a boy with a burned throat.
6. **Silence** — Bell realizes Clara's last letter was three weeks ago. He writes again.
7. **Drumfire** — A German bombardment. Bell worries about his hives.
8. **Hartley's Joke** — Hartley teases Bell about talking to his bees. Later they share a smoke in the dark.
9. **The Orchard** — Graves comes with Bell to the orchard. They find a shattered apple tree still blooming.
10. **No Reply** — Bell receives letters from his mother, from a neighbor, but nothing from Clara.

### Part II: Summer 1917 — The Long Offensive

11. **The Push** — The company moves up for the Third Battle of Ypres. Bell must leave the hives.
12. **Mud** — Endless rain. The guns. Men sink in the mud. Bell thinks of the bees' industry, their order.
13. **Cohen's Fear** — Cohen confides he has never held a living thing before the army. He is afraid to die.
14. **Graves's Shell** — A close explosion buries Graves in mud. Bell digs him out with his hands.
15. **The Queen Cell** — Bell dreams of the bees swarming, of finding the queen on his pillow.
16. **Packs of Mail** — Letters arrive for everyone. Nothing for Bell. Hartley shares a letter from his wife.
17. **Gas** — A gas alarm in the night. Bell helps Cohen with his mask. One man dies.
18. **The Advance** — Over the top. Machine guns. Bell runs through smoke and does not stop.
19. **O'Mara** — Danny O'Mara is hit. Bell carries him back. The boy dies in the aid post.
20. **Honey and Ashes** — Bell returns to the hives behind the lines. One hive is dead. He cleans it out.

### Part III: Autumn 1917 — The Silence Deepens

21. **A New Queen** — Bell splits the surviving hives. Work keeps the grief at bay.
22. **Graves's Change** — Graves is harder now, colder. He drinks. He does not speak of the bees.
23. **The Deserter** — A man from B Company runs. They catch him. Graves must command the firing squad.
24. **The Execution** — Bell watches. He thinks of the bees, who kill their drones without hesitation.
25. **Clara's Mother** — A letter from Clara's mother. Clara is alive but will not write. Something is wrong.
26. **The Leaves** — Autumn comes to the ruined landscape. Finch talks of his pre-war life as a gamekeeper.
27. **The Wound** — A sniper's bullet grazes Bell's arm. He is sent to a casualty clearing station.
28. **The Hospital Tent** — Bell shares a ward with a man who talks only of his wife. Bell listens, says nothing.
29. **Pen and Paper** — Bell writes Clara a different letter. He asks her to tell him the truth.
30. **Return** — Back to the hives. The bees have survived a German bombing. Bell rebuilds.

### Part IV: Winter 1917-18 — The Cold

31. **First Snow** — Snow covers the trenches. The bees cluster for warmth. Bell wraps the hives.
32. **Graves Confides** — Graves is drunk. He tells Bell about the girl in England who stopped writing to him.
33. **The Ruined Church** — The company rests in a broken church. Bell finds a beeswax candle stub.
34. **Christmas** — A truce in one sector. Not here. Bell shares his honey ration with the men.
35. **Clara's Letter** — A letter arrives. Clara is pregnant. The child is not Bell's.
36. **The Truth** — Bell reads the letter again in the dark. He does not tell anyone.
37. **Rats** — The trenches are thick with rats. Bell thinks of the cleanliness of the hive.
38. **Pneumonia** — Cohen falls ill. Bell sits with him through the night.
39. **The New Year** — 1918. Bell writes a letter he will not send. He tells Clara he understands.
40. **Stillness** — A quiet week. No attacks. Bell tends the bees and feels something break inside him.

### Part V: Spring 1918 — The Last Season

41. **The Kaiserschlacht** — The German Spring Offensive begins. The company is thrown into the line.
42. **The Retreat** — The British line collapses. Bell must abandon the hives.
43. **Hartley Falls** — Hartley is killed by a shell. Bell does not stop. He cannot.
44. **The Rearguard** — Graves holds a bridge with twenty men. Bell fights beside him.
45. **The Hives Destroyed** — Bell returns through the chaos to find the apiary shelled, the hives burning.
46. **The Road** — The long retreat. Exhaustion. Bell carries nothing but a frame of comb he saved.
47. **Graves's End** — Graves is shot leading a counterattack. Bell holds him as he dies.
48. **The Armistice** — November 11, 1918. Silence. Bell stands in the mud and does not know what to do.
49. **The Return** — Bell goes home to Sussex. The house is empty. Clara is gone.
50. **The New Hive** — Bell builds a new hive. He catches a swarm from the wild. The bees begin again.
