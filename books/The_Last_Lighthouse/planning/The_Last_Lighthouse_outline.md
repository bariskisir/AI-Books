# The Last Lighthouse — Outline

## Premise

In a drowned world where sea levels have risen over a hundred feet and civilization has collapsed into scattered coastal gangs, Jonah Thorne refuses to abandon his post as keeper of the Sentinal Lighthouse. The beacon still turns, powered by solar panels he has maintained for a decade. The government that employed him is gone. The coast is claimed by Warlord Cain's reavers. But Jonah believes the light matters — that somewhere out on the dark water, someone needs it to find their way home. When a mysterious voice crackles over the radio calling itself "The Navigator," Jonah must decide whether hope is a delusion or the only thing worth dying for.

## Core Cast

- **Jonah Thorne** — The last lighthouse keeper. Former Navy signalman. Stubborn, solitary, quietly broken by loss. He keeps the light burning because he promised someone he would.

- **The Navigator** — A voice on the emergency radio band. Claims to be guiding a ship full of refugees toward the light. Never gives their real name. May be real, may be a ghost, may be something else.

- **Warlord Cain** — Leader of the coastal reavers. Ruthless, pragmatic, and patient. He wants the lighthouse for its strategic position and comms tower. He does not want Jonah dead unless necessary.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Light (Chapters 1-16)

1. **The Last Keeper** — Jonah winds the clockwork mechanism that still turns the lamp. Dawn reveals a flooded world from the gallery deck.
2. **The Morning Watch** — Jonah catalogs supplies, checks the solar array, spots a skiff on the horizon. Routine as ritual.
3. **The Radio** — A voice cuts through static at dusk. "Sentinal Light, this is Navigator. Do you read?" Jonah's first contact in eight months.
4. **The Promise** — Flashback to the day Jonah's wife Mira put the lighthouse key in his hand and made him swear to keep the light burning.
5. **The Reavers** — Cain's scouts circle the lighthouse island. Jonah watches from the lantern room, rifle ready.
6. **The Navigator's Story** — The voice returns. A ship called *Arcadia* carries three hundred survivors from the drowned southern cities. They need the light.
7. **The Storm Cellar** — Jonah retreats below as a nor'easter pounds the coast. He reads Mira's old logbooks.
8. **The Signal** — During a lull, Jonah climbs to repair a damaged antenna. The wind nearly takes him.
9. **The Offer** — Cain arrives by launch under a white flag. He offers Jonah safe passage inland in exchange for the lighthouse. Jonah refuses.
10. **The Navigator's Doubt** — Static and silence. Jonah begins to wonder if the Navigator is real, or if solitude has cracked his mind.
11. **The Cache** — Jonah discovers a hidden arms locker Mira had stashed years ago. Old Navy flares, a signal pistol, ammunition.
12. **The Siege Begins** — Cain's men cut the underwater cable to the mainland. Jonah is now truly isolated.
13. **The Lighthouse Ghosts** — Jonah dreams of the previous keepers — men who served here since 1873. He feels their presence in the turning mechanism.
14. **The Navigator Returns** — The voice comes back, weaker now. "Still here," Jonah says. "Still burning."
15. **The Cost** — A reaver skiff attempts a night landing. Jonah fires warning shots. One man is wounded, pulled back.
16. **The Light Endures** — End of Act I. Jonah stands watch, the beam sweeping the dark water. The beacon turns.

### Act II: The Storm (Chapters 17-33)

17. **The Wound** — Jonah takes stock after the night attack. He is running low on ammunition.
18. **The Navigator's Secret** — The voice admits the *Arcadia* may not make it. There are sick aboard. Children. They are running out of fuel.
19. **The Trade** — Cain sends a second envoy. A deal: one month of peace while Jonah considers. Jonah buys time.
20. **The Repairs** — The lamp mechanism is failing. Jonah strips parts from the old generator room to keep it turning.
21. **The Memory of Mira** — Flashback: Mira taught Jonah the lighthouse's rhythms. She knew every bolt and bearing.
22. **The Message** — Jonah finds an old military satellite phone in a sealed locker. He gets a single bar of signal long enough to send a distress ping.
23. **The Waiting** — Days pass. No response to the ping. The Navigator checks in each night at midnight.
24. **The Hunger** — Supplies are running low. Jonah fishes from the rocks. He catches just enough.
25. **The Visitor** — A woman appears on the shore, washed up from a wrecked boat. Exhausted, feverish. Jonah brings her inside.
26. **The Story of the South** — The woman, Elara, tells of cities swallowed by the sea. The *Arcadia* is real. She was on it. She fell overboard in the storm.
27. **The Fever** — Elara worsens. Jonah burns precious medical supplies to keep her alive.
28. **The Two Lights** — Cain's men build a bonfire on the mainland shore, mocking the lighthouse. Jonah ignores it.
29. **The Choice** — Elara wakes. She tells Jonah the *Arcadia* will reach the lighthouse in three days. He must decide whether to signal them or send them away to safety.
30. **The Navigator's Voice** — The Navigator reveals they are Jonah's daughter, Lena. Mira sent her away before the floods came. She has been trying to reach him for years.
31. **The Betrayal** — Elara is a spy for Cain. She radios his position in exchange for her life. Jonah discovers her true purpose.
32. **The Confrontation** — Jonah holds Elara at gunpoint but cannot pull the trigger. He locks her in the cellar.
33. **The Eve of Battle** — The *Arcadia* is twelve hours out. Cain prepares a full assault. Jonah arms every flare, every weapon.

### Act III: The Dawn (Chapters 34-50)

34. **The Last Watch** — Jonah climbs to the lantern room. The beacon turns one more revolution.
35. **The Assault** — Cain's reavers come in three launches, engines screaming across the dark water.
36. **The Flare** — Jonah fires the signal pistol. A flare blooms over the bay, blinding the attackers.
37. **The Navigator's Light** — Lena guides the *Arcadia* using the lighthouse beam. She sings a lullaby Mira used to sing. Jonah hears it over the radio.
38. **The Stand** — Jonah fights from the gallery deck with a rifle. He picks off two reavers trying to scale the tower.
39. **The Fire** — A Molotov cocktail strikes the base of the lighthouse. The old paint catches. Smoke rises.
40. **The Descent** — Jonah climbs down through smoke and flame to the cellar to free Elara. She does not deserve to burn.
41. **The Reckoning** — Elara, seeing Jonah's mercy, turns on Cain. She leads him into a trap.
42. **The Wounded Light** — The lamp mechanism is damaged by gunfire. Jonah must climb the exterior ladder to realign the reflector.
43. **The Fall** — Cain reaches the lantern room. He and Jonah face each other among the prisms and gears.
44. **The Choice** — Jonah can either fight Cain or keep the light turning. He cannot do both. He chooses the light.
45. **The Arrival** — The *Arcadia* appears out of the mist. The refugees see the beacon. Cheers erupt from the deck.
46. **The Fall of Cain** — Lena, watching from the ship, sees Cain raise his weapon. She fires a flare gun from the deck, striking Cain's position. He falls from the gallery.
47. **The Reunion** — Jonah and Lena embrace on the landing dock. She is grown now. She has his eyes. She kept the promise Mira made him swear.
48. **The New World** — The refugees disembark. They will rebuild on the lighthouse island — the only high ground for miles.
49. **The Keeper's Return** — Jonah hands the lighthouse key to Lena. The light will pass to a new generation.
50. **The Beacon** — Jonah and Lena stand together in the lantern room as dawn breaks. The beam still turns. The light never went out.
