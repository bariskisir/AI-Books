## Description

A fisherman's late testament gives an island basalt quarry and common shore to a luxury developer. The paper looks proper, the signature looks plausible, and the probate clock is moving fast.

Nora Vale notices that the attached stone witness sample comes from the wrong lava flow. With a geologist, a harbor clerk, a quarry mason, a parish priest, and a village doctor, she follows basalt fractures, ash in a paper fold, tide entries, and funeral records to prove the document was made after death.
