## Narrative Notes

The book follows Dr. Sonia Park, a clinical psychologist who investigates a corrupt neurological research protocol at the Harmon Institute. The novel is structured in five parts across 50 chapters, tracking her investigation from initial suspicion through full exposure and aftermath.

The cover depicts the book's central metaphor: a one-way mirror showing a room full of identical calm faces looking directly at the viewer, rendered in cold institutional white and blue fluorescent light.

Key themes: epistemological uncertainty, the ethics of clinical research, the fragility of the self/other boundary, institutional complicity, and the cost of acting on incomplete certainty.
