# The Thousand Faces — Planning Outline

## Premise

Dr. Sonia Park is a clinical psychologist at the Harmon Institute, a private residential clinic in upstate New York. When the clinic's medical director, Dr. Marcus Webb, introduces a new neurological treatment protocol — a combination of deep-brain stimulation and pharmacology meant to accelerate therapeutic breakthroughs — Sonia begins experiencing vivid, intrusive visions of her patients' worst fears. At first she dismisses them as empathic resonance, a professional hazard. Then one patient, Devin Chen, dies in circumstances that exactly mirror the phobia Sonia witnessed in his vision: a fall from a height he should never have had access to. She investigates and discovers that the protocol is doing something to both patients and clinicians that Webb has not disclosed. The killer is not supernatural — it is pharmaceutical. A compound in the protocol induces mirror-neuron hyperactivation, blurring the cognitive boundary between clinician and patient. Webb is exploiting this to harvest raw psychological data for a private research buyer. And Sonia, already deep inside the experiment, must untangle her own perceptions from the protocol's contamination before she becomes either its next victim or its unwitting instrument.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Sonia Park, 36** — Clinical psychologist at the Harmon Institute. Meticulous, skeptical, trained to watch others. As the protocol takes hold, she becomes increasingly unable to trust her own perceptions. Her greatest fear is not madness but losing the precision that defines her.
- **Dr. Marcus Webb, 55** — Medical director, architect of the Harmon Protocol. Brilliant, paternalistic, convinced that the ends justify the methodological deception. He views patients as data sets and clinicians as instruments.
- **Devin Chen, 28** — A patient whose primary phobia involves heights. Charming, self-aware, the one patient Sonia feels she is truly reaching. His death is the inciting crime.
- **Nurse Agatha Rourke, 50** — Senior ward nurse. She has worked at Harmon for fifteen years and has seen two previous incidents she has never formally reported. She is the keeper of the institution's conscience and its most dangerous witness.
- **Dr. Liam Ferris, 42** — Sonia's colleague, a fellow psychologist. The person she initially trusts most, and therefore the most dangerous blind spot. He is further inside the protocol than she realizes.
- **Calista Graves, 33** — A patient with a history of dissociative episodes, admitted for treatment of extreme social anxiety. She appears to be immune to the protocol's effects and watches everything with a cataloguer's precision. She becomes Sonia's unlikely ally.

## 50-Chapter Outline

### Part One: Intake (Chapters 1–10)

**Chapter 1: Admission**
Sonia conducts her morning rounds at the Harmon Institute; she is introduced to a new patient, Devin Chen, and the institute's clinical atmosphere is established.

**Chapter 2: The Protocol**
Dr. Webb introduces the new neurological treatment protocol at a staff meeting, framing it as a voluntary enhancement to existing therapeutic methods.

**Chapter 3: First Breach**
During a session with Devin, Sonia experiences the first intrusive vision — a vertiginous flash of standing on a high ledge — and dismisses it as empathic countertransference.

**Chapter 4: The Observation Room**
Sonia watches a session through the one-way mirror and notices that the clinician on the other side is behaving with subtle wrongness she cannot name.

**Chapter 5: Calista**
Sonia is assigned a new patient, Calista Graves, who immediately unsettles the standard intake dynamic by asking questions instead of answering them.

**Chapter 6: The Second Vision**
A second vision during group session is more detailed and specific: Sonia sees Devin at the top of the institute's fire escape, frozen, unable to move.

**Chapter 7: Corridor Talk**
Sonia mentions her experiences to Liam Ferris over lunch; he normalizes them and suggests she is simply fatigued by the protocol's intensity.

**Chapter 8: Webb's Office**
Sonia visits Webb to ask a direct question about the protocol's neurological mechanisms; he deflects with professional warmth and institutional authority.

**Chapter 9: Agatha**
Sonia and Nurse Rourke have their first real conversation; Agatha says something oblique about patients on "the new program" sometimes needing extra watching at night.

**Chapter 10: Night Rounds**
Sonia volunteers for night rounds and finds Devin standing at the end of a fourth-floor corridor staring out a window, apparently in a trance state.

---

### Part Two: Onset (Chapters 11–20)

**Chapter 11: The Fire Escape**
Devin Chen is found dead at the base of the external fire escape. The official cause is accidental fall. Sonia knows what she saw in her vision.

**Chapter 12: The Investigation Begins**
Sonia begins quiet, informal inquiries — reviewing Devin's case file, speaking to staff who worked with him — without alerting Webb.

**Chapter 13: Pharmacology**
Sonia examines the protocol's publicly stated medication list and finds a compound she doesn't recognize; she sends a query to a pharmacology contact outside the institute.

**Chapter 14: Calista Watches**
Calista tells Sonia she has been keeping a log of the other patients' behavioral changes since the protocol began. She describes patterns Sonia recognizes from her own visions.

**Chapter 15: The Mirror Room**
Sonia discovers an observation room she didn't know existed — deeper in the clinical wing — with equipment that shouldn't be there.

**Chapter 16: Liam's Sessions**
Sonia reviews the schedule and realizes Liam has been conducting off-record sessions with three patients currently on the enhanced protocol.

**Chapter 17: The Compound**
Her pharmacology contact identifies the unrecognized compound as a synthetic analog with mirror-neuron activation properties — experimental, uncleared for clinical use.

**Chapter 18: Webb's History**
Sonia researches Webb's academic history and finds a paper from 2003 on what he called "shared cognitive architecture" that was retracted under unclear circumstances.

**Chapter 19: Vision Cascade**
During a regular session with a different patient, Sonia is overwhelmed by a vision so vivid she cannot speak for several minutes; the patient asks if she is all right.

**Chapter 20: Agatha's File**
Agatha, without explanation, leaves a manila folder in Sonia's mailbox containing incident reports from two previous deaths at Harmon — both ruled accidental.

---

### Part Three: Contamination (Chapters 21–30)

**Chapter 21: Pattern Recognition**
Sonia maps the incident reports against the protocol records and finds a correlation: both previous deaths followed elevated dosages and occurred within seventy-two hours.

**Chapter 22: Confronting Liam**
Sonia confronts Liam directly; he admits he is more deeply involved in the protocol than he disclosed but insists he believes in its therapeutic value.

**Chapter 23: The Data Buyer**
Sonia finds a document in the mirror room referencing a contract with a private neuroscience research firm: Veridian Cognitive Systems. The scope of what's being harvested becomes clear.

**Chapter 24: Calista's History**
Calista reveals she was placed at Harmon not by her own choice but at the recommendation of a Veridian-affiliated clinician. She has been monitoring the protocol from inside since her first week.

**Chapter 25: Webb Moves**
Webb summons Sonia to his office and, without accusation, begins adjusting her own protocol dosage under the guise of clinician wellness support.

**Chapter 26: The Blurred Line**
Sonia begins losing track of which memories belong to her and which she has absorbed from patients; she starts writing everything down as an anchor to reality.

**Chapter 27: Agatha's Choice**
Agatha tells Sonia she is ready to make a formal report but is afraid; she describes having been threatened, subtly, after the second death.

**Chapter 28: Liam's Confession**
Liam, under growing guilt, tells Sonia the full structure of the data-collection scheme: patient sessions are being recorded neurologically, and Liam has been conducting targeted elicitation sessions to maximize yield.

**Chapter 29: The Third Patient**
A third patient on the enhanced protocol, a woman with severe claustrophobia, is found in a supply closet in a catatonic state. Sonia prevents what might have been another death.

**Chapter 30: The Recording**
Sonia finds audio-visual logs stored on an encrypted drive in the mirror room. What she hears from her own sessions proves her perceptions have been manipulated since week one.

---

### Part Four: Fracture (Chapters 31–40)

**Chapter 31: Trust Nobody**
Sonia realizes she cannot safely trust any colleague still inside the institute; she begins planning her external moves alone.

**Chapter 32: The Regulator**
Sonia contacts the New York State Office of Mental Health Oversight with what she has, but the contact she reaches seems to already know too much to be neutral.

**Chapter 33: Webb's Rationale**
Webb learns Sonia has contacted the regulator; he confronts her with his complete argument — he believes the protocol will save thousands, and a few tragic cases are acceptable cost.

**Chapter 34: The Break**
Sonia attempts to leave the institute campus for twenty-four hours; she is, politely but unmistakably, prevented.

**Chapter 35: Calista's Escape**
Calista successfully leaves the institute under the cover of a scheduled family visit, carrying Sonia's documentation on a device hidden in a book.

**Chapter 36: Liam Turns**
Under pressure from Webb, Liam retracts his confession and provides a written statement supporting the protocol's safety. Sonia is now clinically and institutionally isolated.

**Chapter 37: The Vision Crisis**
Sonia is hit by a prolonged vision episode in which she experiences a patient's suicidal ideation as her own; she manages to document it and stabilize herself but it is the closest call yet.

**Chapter 38: Agatha Acts**
Agatha, without telling Sonia, contacts a journalist at a health-policy outlet who has previously investigated Veridian Cognitive Systems.

**Chapter 39: Inside the Protocol**
Webb, believing Sonia is neutralized, invites her to observe the protocol's most intensive session yet; she attends, appears compliant, and gathers the final evidence she needs.

**Chapter 40: The Threshold**
Sonia confronts the question she has been avoiding: how much of what she believes has been planted by the protocol itself. She must act on incomplete certainty.

---

### Part Five: Resolution (Chapters 41–50)

**Chapter 41: The Journalist**
Agatha's journalist contact, Sandra Luu, arrives at the institute under a pretense; Sonia and Agatha coordinate to pass her the complete evidence package.

**Chapter 42: Webb Closes In**
Webb suspends Sonia's patient access, pending review, and isolates her in a consultation room with a new medication schedule — a move to silence her by chemical means.

**Chapter 43: The Medication**
Sonia pretends compliance but does not take the medication; she documents the attempt and adds it to the evidence.

**Chapter 44: Liam's Return**
Liam, alone in his own unraveling, returns to Sonia with the encrypted drive containing Webb's private data files — a final act of conscience.

**Chapter 45: Sandra Luu Publishes**
The first article goes live; Veridian Cognitive Systems is named; the New York State Health Department opens a formal investigation.

**Chapter 46: Webb's Last Gambit**
Webb attempts to destroy the remaining on-site evidence and frame the patient deaths as Sonia's clinical failures. Calista, now outside, has already filed a counterstatement.

**Chapter 47: The Reckoning**
State investigators arrive at Harmon; Sonia gives her formal statement, walking the line between what she knows and what the protocol may have constructed in her mind.

**Chapter 48: Aftermath**
Webb is suspended pending criminal investigation. Liam faces a professional conduct board. The institute is placed under temporary external oversight.

**Chapter 49: What Remains**
Sonia undergoes her own neurological evaluation to assess protocol damage; the results are within normal parameters, but she is not sure she trusts them.

**Chapter 50: The Other Side of the Glass**
Sonia returns to a clinical office for the first time — not at Harmon, but at a small outpatient practice. She conducts a session. The chapter ends with her watching her patient with full presence, and the one-way mirror behind them reflecting only the room itself.
