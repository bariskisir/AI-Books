## Production Summary

The Elevator is a real-time thriller in which twelve strangers are trapped in a Manhattan elevator with a bomb counting down. The story follows Maya Kessler, a former FBI hostage negotiator, as she uses psychological tactics to keep the bomber from detonating while managing eleven panicking hostages and coordinating with NYPD.

The novel was produced across 50 chapters with a total word count of approximately 10,500 words. The narrative unfolds in near-real-time, with the countdown timer creating structural tension across all five acts: The Trap, Fractures, The Human Element, The Descent, and The Final Countdown.

## Characters

- **Maya Kessler** — Protagonist. Former FBI hostage negotiator, carries guilt from the Briarwood standoff.
- **Marcus Webb** — Antagonist. Former TitanCorp engineer, fired after ten years, wearing a bomb vest.
- **Derek Cole** — Army Ranger veteran, tactical support.
- **Nora Patel** — ER physician, provides medical care inside the elevator.
- **Elena Rossi** — Building maintenance supervisor, knows the elevator shaft.
- **James Hollister** — CEO, represents authority challenged by the crisis.
- **Tanya Mills** — Pregnant real estate agent, humanity and vulnerability.
- **David Chen** — First-day intern, represents fear and desperation.
- **Sister Bernadette** — Nun, spiritual calm.
- **Raj Mehta** — Food cart vendor, quiet strength.
- **Fiona Walsh** — Attorney, sharp and skeptical.
- **Leonard Cross** — Retired doorman, medical crisis adds tension.
