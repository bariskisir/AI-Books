# The Train in the Snow — Outline

## Premise

A luxury express train bound for St. Moritz becomes trapped by an avalanche high in the Swiss Alps. As the passengers wait for rescue, one of them is found dead — killed in a locked compartment. Inspector Brandt, traveling incognito, must identify the killer among a group of strangers, all of whom have perfect alibis. The victim was a blackmailer. Every passenger had motive. But nobody could have done it.

## Core Cast

- **Inspector Felix Brandt** — A weary, sharp-eyed detective returning from a failed case in Zurich. Traveling under a false name. He notices everything and says little.

- **The Widow (Clara Voss)** — A striking woman in her forties, recently widowed. Her husband died under suspicious circumstances six months ago. She is traveling to St. Moritz to "clear her head." Calm, calculating, always watching.

- **The Doctor (Dr. Markus Engel)** — A respected physician from Bern. On his way to a medical conference. Precise, authoritative, hiding a tremor in his hands. He carried a locked medical bag.

- **The Conductor (Hans Meier)** — A career railway man approaching retirement. He knows every inch of the train. Dutiful, nervous, protective of his record. He was in the dining car serving coffee when the murder happened.

- **Stefan Gross** — The victim. A journalist and professional blackmailer. Found in his compartment with a single stab wound to the chest. The door was locked from inside.

- **Greta Lind** — The stewardess. Young, eager, terrified. She discovered the body.

- **Herr Obermann** — A businessman traveling first class. Loud, defensive, claims he saw nothing. Has a secret deal going wrong in Zurich.

- **Frau Obermann** — His wife. Quiet, bruised, watches her husband with fear and hatred.

## Chapter Plan

### PART ONE: THE JOURNEY

1. **The Glacier Express** — Inspector Brandt boards the train in Chur. Snow falls heavily. The train is nearly full. Brandt notes the passengers with idle professional habit.

2. **First Class Passengers** — Introduces the cast through Brandt's observations in the first-class carriage: the Widow, the Doctor, the Obermanns, Stefan Gross.

3. **The Widow's Silence** — Clara Voss sits alone, refusing conversation. Brandt notices she watches Stefan Gross with particular attention.

4. **The Doctor's Bag** — Dr. Engel snaps at a stewardess who brushes his medical bag. He apologizes too quickly. Brandt files the moment.

5. **The Conductor's Routine** — Hans Meier makes his rounds. He seems distracted, checks his pocket watch repeatedly. The train climbs higher into the mountains.

6. **Snowfall Intensifies** — The weather worsens. Passengers grow restless. The train slows. Whispers of an avalanche warning reach the dining car.

7. **Stefan Gross at Dinner** — Gross holds court in the dining car, drinking heavily, making veiled remarks that make other passengers uncomfortable. He is enjoying himself.

8. **The Avalanche** — A deep rumble. The train shudders to a halt. Snow buries the tracks ahead and behind. The train is trapped. Panic in the dining car.

9. **Waiting for Rescue** — The Conductor announces rescue crews have been notified but roads are impassable. Passengers are asked to remain calm. Brandt feels his instincts prickling.

10. **Last Call** — The dining car serves a final round. Passengers retire to their compartments. Gross is the last to leave, smirking. Brandt notices the clock: 11:47 PM.

### PART TWO: THE CRIME

11. **The Body** — Greta Lind screams. Gross's compartment door is locked. The Conductor forces it open. Gross lies on the floor, blood pooling, a letter opener in his chest.

12. **Locked Room** — The window is sealed shut, the door was bolted from inside. No weapon visible except the letter opener. No signs of struggle. Impossible.

13. **Inspector Revealed** — Brandt identifies himself to the Conductor. He takes charge of the scene. The passengers are assembled in the dining car.

14. **The Alibis Begin** — Brandt takes preliminary statements. The Widow was in her compartment reading. The Doctor was in his compartment asleep. The Conductor was in the dining car with Greta.

15. **The Widow's Alibi** — Greta confirms she brought Clara Voss tea at 11:30 and collected the cup at 12:15. The cup was empty. Clara could not have left.

16. **The Doctor's Alibi** — Dr. Engel claims he was asleep. His compartment adjoins Gross's. Brandt notes the thin walls. The Doctor says he heard nothing. But the medical bag is now missing.

17. **The Conductor's Alibi** — Greta confirms Meier was with her in the dining car from 11:20 until 12:05, preparing for the morning service. He never left.

18. **The Impossible Window** — Brandt examines Gross's compartment. The window is frosted shut, sealed by ice. No one entered or left through it. He searches for secret passages.

19. **The Missing Bag** — Brandt notices the Doctor's medical bag is missing from its place. Dr. Engel claims he moved it to his compartment for safekeeping. But Brandt saw it in the dining car at dinner.

20. **Motive Emerges** — Brandt searches Gross's belongings. He finds letters, photographs, bank records. Gross was a blackmailer. Every passenger had reason to want him dead.

### PART THREE: THE PUZZLE

21. **The Photographs** — Brandt finds photographs of the Widow with a man who is not her husband. The dates are recent. Her husband's death was ruled accidental.

22. **The Doctor's Secret** — Among Gross's papers, a letter referencing "the Engel matter" and a payment schedule. Dr. Engel was paying Gross for silence about what?

23. **The Obermann Connection** — A photograph of Herr Obermann meeting a man in Zurich. A note: "Obermann's little insurance scheme." Gross had evidence of fraud.

24. **The Conductor's Debt** — Brandt finds a promissory note signed by Hans Meier. The Conductor owed Gross a significant sum. Meier had not mentioned knowing the victim.

25. **Second Interrogation: The Widow** — Brandt confronts Clara Voss. She admits Gross was blackmailing her. The photographs were from an affair, but her husband died of a heart attack. She has an alibi — the tea.

26. **Second Interrogation: The Doctor** — Dr. Engel admits he was being blackmailed. The "Engel matter" was a misdiagnosis years ago that led to a patient's death. He hid it. His career would be ruined. But he was asleep when Gross died.

27. **Second Interrogation: The Conductor** — Meier admits he borrowed money from Gross to cover a gambling debt. He was repaying it slowly. He was in the dining car at the time of death.

28. **The Stewardess** — Greta Lind is questioned again. She is nervous, evasive. She admits she liked Gross, that he was kind to her. The other passengers mock her for it.

29. **A Second Search** — Brandt searches the train more thoroughly. In the baggage car, he finds the Doctor's medical bag, empty. Why would Engel hide it?

30. **The Missing Scalpel** — Brandt opens the medical bag. Inside, a surgical kit. One scalpel is missing. The letter opener in Gross's chest was a plant. The real weapon is gone.

31. **The Doctor's Explanation** — Dr. Engel says he put the bag in the baggage car because he feared being accused. He denies the missing scalpel means anything. He has many scalpels.

32. **The Time Puzzle** — Brandt maps the timeline. The murder happened between 11:50 and 12:05. The Widow was in her compartment. The Doctor was supposedly asleep. The Conductor was with Greta. All airtight.

33. **The Ventilation Grille** — Brandt notices a ventilation grille in Gross's compartment. It is loose. He removes it. The shaft is narrow but connects to the adjacent compartment — the Doctor's.

34. **The Impossible Route** — Even through the ventilation shaft, the killer would have to be small, agile. Dr. Engel is large and stiff from an old injury. The Widow is slight but was seen. The Conductor is broad-shouldered.

35. **A Break in the Weather** — The storm eases. Rescue crews are making progress. Brandt has hours, not days. He gathers everyone in the dining car for a final confrontation.

### PART FOUR: THE SOLUTION

36. **The Gathering** — All passengers assemble. Brandt stands at the head of the dining car. He lays out the evidence: the blackmail, the motives, the locked room.

37. **The Impossible Alibi** — Brandt explains why each alibi is perfect. The Widow was served tea. The Doctor was in a locked room. The Conductor had witnesses. And yet, someone is lying.

38. **The Conductor's Error** — Brandt reveals the flaw. Greta said Meier was with her from 11:20 to 12:05. But the coffee service would have taken only ten minutes. Meier had forty-five minutes unaccounted for within that window.

39. **Greta's Confession** — Greta breaks down. She lied for the Conductor because she trusted him. He told her he was checking the heating system. She believed him. She was wrong.

40. **The Conductor's Story** — Meier admits he left the dining car. He went to confront Gross about the debt. But he only argued with him. Gross was alive when he left. He went to the baggage car to calm down.

41. **The Missing Time** — Brandt does the math. Meier had fifteen minutes unaccounted for, not enough to have done it and cleaned up. The timeline still doesn't work.

42. **The Doctor's Bag Revisited** — Brandt returns to the missing scalpel. He examines the bag more closely. Inside the lining, he finds a bloodstained handkerchief. Not Meier's. Not the Doctor's.

43. **The Widow's Handkerchief** — The handkerchief is monogrammed. "C.V." Clara Voss. The Widow was in the Doctor's compartment after all. She took the scalpel. She used the ventilation shaft.

44. **The Widow's Confession** — Clara Voss confesses. She killed Gross because he was going to expose her affair to the police investigating her husband's death. They would have reopened the case. She would have been tried for murder — her husband's death was no accident.

45. **The Impossible Murder** — Brandt reconstructs it. Clara used the ventilation shaft to crawl from the Doctor's empty compartment (the Doctor was in the dining car briefly, not asleep) to Gross's. She stabbed him with the Doctor's scalpel, planted the letter opener she found in Gross's compartment, and crawled back. She counted on the Doctor hiding his bag.

### PART FIVE: THE AFTERMATH

46. **The Widow's Story** — Clara Voss tells everything. Her husband abused her for years. She poisoned him slowly. Gross figured it out. She came on this trip intending to kill Gross, to silence him forever.

47. **The Doctor's Shame** — Dr. Engel admits he was not asleep. He was in the dining car retrieving his bag during the critical window. He lied because his career was already fragile. His lie became Clara's alibi.

48. **The Morning After** — Rescue arrives. The snow is cleared. The passengers are evacuated. Brandt watches Clara Voss being led away. Justice is never clean.

49. **Brandt's Reflection** — Brandt sits alone in the dining car as the last passengers depart. He solved the case. But Clara Voss was right — her husband was a monster. The law made no room for that.

50. **The Train in the Snow** — The engine starts. The train lurches forward. Brandt looks out at the white landscape, the snow covering everything, erasing all tracks. The train moves on. The snow will remember.
