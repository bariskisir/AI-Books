## Story Summary

Inspector Felix Brandt boards the Glacier Express in Chur, heading for St. Moritz. An avalanche traps the train in the Swiss Alps. Stefan Gross, a journalist and blackmailer, is found dead in a locked compartment — stabbed with a letter opener. Brandt reveals his identity and investigates.

All passengers had motive: Gross was blackmailing the Widow (Clara Voss) about her husband's suspicious death and an affair; Dr. Engel about a fatal medical error; Conductor Meier about a gambling debt and workplace accident; Herr Obermann about insurance fraud.

All alibis appear perfect: The Widow was served tea in her compartment. The Doctor was supposedly asleep. The Conductor was in the dining car with the stewardess.

Brandt discovers a loose ventilation grille connecting Gross's compartment to the Doctor's. A missing scalpel. A bloodstained handkerchief. He reconstructs the murder: Clara Voss used the ventilation shaft, taking the Doctor's scalpel, stabbing Gross, and planting the letter opener to confuse the investigation.

## Key Findings

- The locked room was breached via a ventilation shaft
- The murder weapon was a scalpel, not a letter opener
- The killer manipulated evidence to implicate the Doctor
- All alibis were technically true — the killer exploited the gaps between them
