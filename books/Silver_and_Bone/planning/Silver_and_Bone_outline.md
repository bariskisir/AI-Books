# Silver and Bone — Outline

## Premise

Mira Black is a Chicago private detective with an unusual ability: she can see through the glamour that hides the fae from mortal eyes. When a series of brutal murders begins targeting half-fae hybrids across the city, Mira is pulled into a hidden war between the Summer Court, ruled by the iron-fisted Queen Mab, and the insurgent Autumn Court, led by her exiled son. With the help of Finn, a double-agent fae with his own agenda, Mira must navigate a world of ancient loyalties and deadly bargains before the conflict spills into the mortal realm — and before she becomes a pawn in a game far older than she imagined.

## Core Cast

- **Mira Black** — Chicago PI, mid-thirties. A childhood trauma gifted her the sight to pierce fae glamour. Wary, pragmatic, stubborn.
- **Finn** — A fae of the Summer Court who feeds information to Mira. Quick-tongued, morally flexible, nursing a private grievance against Queen Mab.
- **Queen Mab** — Ruler of the Summer Court. Ancient, cold, strategic. Believes mortal and fae worlds must remain strictly separate.
- **Prince Rowan** — Mab's exiled son, leader of the Autumn Court rebellion. Believes fae must integrate with mortals to survive.
- **Detective Sergeant Cole Hart** — Chicago PD, works the murder cases. Mortal, skeptical, becomes Mira's reluctant ally.
- **The Morrigan** — A ancient fae entity, neutral observer and information broker. Takes the form of a crow.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Sight (Chapters 1-10)

1. Mira Black takes a photograph of a client's cheating spouse and sees something impossible behind the man's face.
2. A half-fae woman is found dead in an alley, her body marked with silver burns — Mira is called in by Detective Hart.
3. Mira visits Finn at a dive bar that serves as neutral ground between fae factions.
4. Finn explains the Summer and Autumn Courts are on the verge of open war.
5. A second murder, different method — the killer is sending a message.
6. Prince Rowan appears at Mira's office, offering a deal she does not want.
7. Queen Mab summons Mira to the Summer Court's hidden embassy beneath the Chicago River.
8. Mab reveals she knows about Mira's sight and has been watching her for years.
9. Finn is caught passing information and is branded with iron — a punishment that could kill him.
10. Mira realizes she is being used as a message-bearer between both courts.

### Part II: The Bargain (Chapters 11-20)

11. Mira agrees to work for neither court — but to investigate the murders on her own terms.
12. Hart shares crime scene photos: the symbols carved into the victims match pre-Christian Celtic bindings.
13. Mira discovers the victims were all half-fae who refused to choose a side.
14. Finn, recovering from his brand, warns Mira that the Morrigan has taken an interest in her.
15. The Morrigan visits Mira in the form of a crow through her office window.
16. Mira learns the murders are tied to a weapon — a blade forged from unalloyed iron and bone.
17. Queen Mab's spymaster makes an attempt on Mira's life in a parking garage.
18. Rowan saves Mira, proving the Autumn Court wants her alive.
19. Mira and Hart compare notes: the blade's next target is the leader of the neutral faction.
20. The neutral leader is killed before Mira can reach them.

### Part III: The Hunt (Chapters 21-30)

21. Mira follows the blade's trail to an underground market beneath Wicker Park.
22. She finds the weapon-smith — an exiled Summer Court craftsman working in iron.
23. The smith tells Mira the blade was commissioned by someone inside both courts.
24. Finn disappears. His apartment is cleaned out, no trace of fae or mortal.
25. Hart pulls records: Finn was arrested under a mortal alias five years ago and released the same night.
26. Mira finds Finn in a Summer Court prison cell, beaten and glamour-stripped.
27. Finn tells Mira he was never a spy — he was bait, placed by Mab to draw her in.
28. Mira breaks Finn out of the Summer Court prison using the one thing fae cannot abide: cold iron.
29. On the run, Mira and Finn cross into Autumn Court territory in the South Side.
30. Rowan shelters them but demands Mira's sight as payment — she refuses.

### Part IV: The War (Chapters 31-40)

31. The Morrigan offers Mira a third path: find the blade's wielder before the courts destroy each other.
32. Mira learns the weapon is not a fae blade — it was forged by a mortal who learned fae secrets.
33. Hart is pulled off the case by his superiors, who have been paid off by Summer Court agents.
34. Mira tracks the mortal smith to an abandoned steel mill in Gary, Indiana.
35. The smith reveals he is the son of a fae and a mortal — the blade is his revenge against both worlds.
36. Mira realizes the smith is the killer — but he is also dying of iron poisoning from his own work.
37. The smith attacks Mira with the blade; she disarms him using her knowledge of fae weakness.
38. Queen Mab and Prince Rowan both arrive, demanding the blade and the smith.
39. Mira destroys the blade by dropping it into molten steel, ending the weapon's power.
40. The smith dies from his wounds, taking the killer's identity to the grave.

### Part V: The Reckoning (Chapters 41-50)

41. With the blade gone, the fae courts have no reason to keep fighting — but neither will back down.
42. Mira brokers a meeting between Mab and Rowan on neutral ground at the dive bar.
43. The negotiations fail. Mab demands Rowan's unconditional surrender.
44. Finn reveals his true loyalty: he serves neither court, but the fae who want peace.
45. The Morrigan declares a geis — a binding oath — forcing both courts to negotiate or face her wrath.
46. Mab and Rowan agree to a truce: the borders between mortal and fae worlds remain sealed.
47. Half-fae are granted safe passage between worlds — no more executions for refusing allegiances.
48. Mira is offered a place as the mortal liaison to both courts. She declines.
49. In the final scene, Mira watches a crow circle overhead and knows her work is not done.
50. Mira walks home through Chicago at dawn. The city looks the same, but she knows nothing will ever be.
