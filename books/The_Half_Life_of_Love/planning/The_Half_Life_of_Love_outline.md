# The Half-Life of Love

## Premise

Dr. Vera Vesna, a Serbian particle physicist working on entanglement experiments at CERN, meets Luca Moretti, an Italian potter in Geneva for an artist-in-residence program. As their relationship deepens, Vera realizes their dynamic mirrors the strange behavior of quantum-entangled particles — instant connection across distance, irreducible uncertainty, and the terrifying way observation collapses possibility into fixed reality. When Vera's experiment at the Large Hadron Collider hits a critical juncture, she must decide whether love is something that can be measured at all.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Vera Vesna** — 34, experimental particle physicist, Serbian, pragmatic and brilliant, secretly lonely
- **Luca Moretti** — 36, ceramic artist, Italian from Umbria, intuitive and patient, recovering from divorce
- **The Collider** — The LHC itself; Vera's experiment, a character in its own right
- **Dr. Simone Dubois** — Vera's French supervisor, supportive but career-focused
- **Ingrid** — Vera's lab technician and closest friend
- **Marco** — Luca's brother, runs the family pottery studio back in Italy
- **Professor Yutaka Tanaka** — A visiting physicist from KEK in Japan, a mentor figure

## 50-Chapter Plan

### ACT I: COLLISION (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Beam Line** — Vera calibrates her detector in the LHC tunnel before dawn. She thinks about spin states.

2. **Clay and Water** — Luca unloads his kiln at the Geneva atelier. His hands remember the shape of bowls he has not yet made.

3. **The First Meeting** — They meet at a cafe near the Swiss-French border. Vera's phone keeps buzzing with alerts from the control room.

4. **Entanglement States** — Vera gives a public lecture on quantum entanglement at the CERN visitor center. Luca attends, understanding almost nothing but watching her hands.

5. **A Potter's Hands** — Luca invites Vera to his studio. She watches him throw a pot and sees wave functions in the spinning clay.

6. **The Control Room** — Vera's run at the LHC begins. She monitors collision data through the night.

7. **Umbrian Terracotta** — Luca explains the chemistry of glazes. Vera talks about particle decay. They find the same vocabulary.

8. **Correlation Without Communication** — Vera and Luca kiss for the first time. She immediately thinks about Bell's theorem.

9. **Background Noise** — Ingrid warns Vera that she is distracted. Vera insists she can separate work from feeling.

10. **The First Firing** — Luca shows Vera his finished pieces. She touches the fired clay, amazed that water can become stone.

### ACT II: ENTANGLEMENT (Chapters 11-20)

11. **Superposition** — Vera tries to explain that in quantum mechanics, a particle can be in multiple states at once. Luca says love is like that.

12. **The Glaze Formula** — Luca struggles with a new glaze. Vera suggests thinking of it as a chemical reaction, not magic.

13. **Neutrinos Pass Through Everything** — Vera's experiment detects anomalous neutrino activity. She spends three days in the control room without seeing Luca.

14. **Waiting for Firing** — Luca waits for Vera outside the lab. He understands the waiting. Pottery is waiting.

15. **The Alpine Weekend** — They take a train to the mountains. Vera feels the distance from CERN like a particle escaping a field.

16. **Broken Pot** — Vera accidentally breaks one of Luca's unfired pieces. He says it does not matter. She does not believe him.

17. **The Uncertainty Principle** — Vera realizes that knowing Luca better changes what she sees in him. Heisenberg applies to people too.

18. **Letter from Italy** — Marco writes asking when Luca is coming home. Luca does not show Vera the letter.

19. **Dark Matter** — Vera receives preliminary data. Something unexpected is happening in the calorimeter. She cannot look away.

20. **Kiln Gods** — Luca tells Vera about the old belief that kilns have spirits. She says physics has gods too — conservation laws.

### ACT III: DECOHERENCE (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Anomaly** — Vera's data shows a pattern that contradicts the Standard Model. Her team is electrified and terrified.

22. **Luca's Exhibition** — Luca's residency show opens. Vera is not there — she is in a closed-door meeting with CERN management.

23. **Broken Symmetry** — Luca is hurt she missed the opening. Vera cannot explain why the data matters more than his pottery. She does not want to explain.

24. **The Review Panel** — External reviewers scrutinize Vera's results. She prepares for weeks. Luca fades into background noise.

25. **Empty Studio** — Luca visits his studio but does not touch the clay. He texts Vera. She replies twelve hours later.

26. **Spin Measurement** — Vera's team must decide which variable to measure. Choosing one axis collapses all others. She thinks about choices.

27. **Marco Arrives** — Luca's brother visits from Umbria. He sees how unhappy Luca is. He says come home.

28. **The Copenhagen Interpretation** — Vera tells Ingrid that observing changes the observed. Ingrid asks if she is talking about particles or Luca.

29. **Luca Decides** — Luca tells Vera he is going back to Italy. Not forever. Just to think. She nods like a physicist accepting data.

30. **The Last Night** — They spend one more night together. Vera watches him sleep and wonders if entanglement requires proximity.

### ACT IV: OBSERVATION (Chapters 31-40)

31. **Geneva Without Luca** — Vera returns to the lab. The anomaly is still there. She feels nothing. That is not true.

32. **Umbria in Winter** — Luca works in his brother's studio. The clay feels wrong. His hands know Vera was the last thing they shaped.

33. **Professor Tanaka** — The visiting physicist reviews Vera's data. He tells her the anomaly could be the discovery of a lifetime. She should be happy.

34. **The Half-Glazed Bowl** — Luca finds a bowl he started the week he met Vera. Half-glazed. He finishes it with her in mind.

35. **Simone's Warning** — Vera's supervisor tells her that CERN is considering pulling resources from her experiment if the anomaly does not resolve. Vera works harder.

36. **Postcards** — Luca sends Vera a postcard of a Umbrian hillside. She pins it above her terminal. Ingrid notices.

37. **The Hidden Variable** — Vera presents her hypothesis: the anomaly might be a new particle. The room goes silent. She thinks of Luca.

38. **Luca's Firing** — Luca fires the half-glazed bowl. It comes out perfectly. He decides to go back to Geneva.

39. **The Observer Effect** — Vera realizes she has been avoiding the question of Luca the same way she avoided looking directly at her data. Both changed when observed.

40. **Collision Course** — Luca returns to Geneva. He does not tell Vera. He stands outside CERN and watches the buildings glow.

### ACT V: SUPERPOSITION (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Note** — Vera finds a note from Luca at her apartment door. Two words: "I'm here."

42. **Restarting the Beam** — Vera asks Luca to meet her at the cafe where they first met. He comes.

43. **The Measurement Problem** — They talk. Vera says she does not know if the relationship works. Luca says knowing is not the point.

44. **Bell Test** — Vera explains that Bell's theorem proved reality is non-local. Luca says he knew that — he has always felt her across distance.

45. **The Grant Decision** — CERN approves continued funding for Vera's experiment. Simone congratulates her. Vera feels hollow.

46. **Throwing Together** — Luca asks Vera to make something with him. She sits at the wheel. Her first pot is ugly. He calls it beautiful.

47. **The Data Run** — Vera's final data run of the season. She watches the collision counters. Every collision is a meeting.

48. **Quantum Erasure** — Vera reads about the quantum eraser experiment — information can be erased after measurement. She wonders about forgiveness.

49. **The Firing** — Vera and Luca fire a pot together. The kiln heat strips everything away. What remains is what was always there.

50. **Half-Life** — Vera understands that love, like particles, decays. But some decays produce new particles. She and Luca are a collision that created something new. She tells him. He holds her. The Collider hums underground.
