# The Oracle's Lament — Outline

## Premise

Cassandra, daughter of King Priam and Queen Hecuba of Troy, is cursed by Apollo to speak true prophecies that no one will ever believe. After refusing the god's advances, she received the gift of foresight — but the curse ensures her warnings fall on deaf ears. As the Greek fleet amasses across the Aegean and war drums sound, Cassandra alone sees the fire that will consume her city. This is the story of her desperate, lonely struggle to save Troy from a fate everyone else embraces blindly — and what becomes of a prophet who must choose between her city and her sanity.

## Core Cast

- **Cassandra** — Trojan princess and prophetess, cursed by Apollo
- **Prince Paris** — Cassandra's brother, whose abduction of Helen triggers the war
- **Andromache** — Wife of Hector, a clear-eyed ally who sometimes believes
- **The Priestess** — Theano, high priestess of Athena at Troy

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Cassandra dreams of fire and wakes screaming — no one comes.
2. She walks the citadel walls and sees the Greek fleet three years before it sails.
3. Paris returns from Sparta with Helen — Cassandra sees the ships burning.
4. The Trojan council celebrates — Cassandra's warnings are laughed away.
5. She confronts her father Priam in private — he calls her madness the god's punishment.
6. Andromache finds Cassandra weeping at the Scaean Gate and asks the truth.
7. Cassandra describes the horse she sees in visions — a wooden beast full of armed men.
8. Hector dismisses Cassandra at a war council — Troy's best warrior trusts strategy over omens.
9. Theano the priestess performs a sacrifice — the entrails are clean but Cassandra smells rot.
10. Paris defends his love for Helen — Cassandra tells him she will die before the war ends.
11. First Greek ships sighted off Tenedos — panic in the lower city.
12. Cassandra walks the market — merchants mock her as they pack their goods.
13. She visits the temple of Apollo and feels the god's presence like a hot blade.
14. Andromache confesses she half-believes — Cassandra begs her to convince Hector.
15. The Greeks land on the beach — Achilles cuts down the first Trojan scout.
16. Cassandra watches the first battle from the walls — her brothers die in the surf.
17. She tends wounded soldiers in the citadel — they call her witch and turn away.
18. Paris consults Cassandra in secret — she tells him Helen will outlive him.
19. Hector returns victorious — Cassandra sees his funeral in the same breath.
20. The plague strikes the Greek camp — Cassandra knows Apollo's hand but Troy does not listen.
21. A truce is called — Cassandra walks the plain and meets a Greek herald who flinches at her words.
22. Andromache gives birth to Astyanax — Cassandra sees the boy thrown from the wall.
23. Hector refuses to retreat behind the walls — Cassandra screams prophecy from the gate.
24. Patroclus dies — the Greek camp mourns and Cassandra knows what comes next.
25. Achilles returns to battle — Cassandra sees him drag Hector's body around the city.
26. Hector and Achilles meet — Cassandra watches from the wall as her brother falls.
27. Priam goes to ransom Hector's body — Cassandra begs him not to go alone.
28. The funeral of Hector — Andromache's grief confirms every prediction Cassandra made.
29. Penthesilea and the Amazons arrive — Cassandra tells the queen she dies at Achilles' hand.
30. The Amazon battle — Cassandra sees the pattern of the war shift toward Troy's end.
31. Memnon arrives with his Ethiopians — another ally Cassandra buries with her eyes.
32. Paris kills Achilles at the Scaean Gate — Cassandra sees the arrow before it flies.
33. The Greeks feign retreat — Cassandra knows the ruse, but no one asks her.
34. The wooden horse is built — Cassandra sees it from her window and laughs until she weeps.
35. Sinon the spy is brought before Priam — Cassandra denounces him, he charms the court.
36. Laocoon thrusts his spear into the horse — Cassandra watches the serpents rise from the sea.
37. The Trojans drag the horse through the gates — Cassandra throws herself before the wheels.
38. Night falls — Cassandra walks the deserted streets and hears the horse creak.
39. The Greek fleet returns — Cassandra lights a beacon no one understands.
40. The horse opens — armed men pour into the citadel.
41. Cassandra runs to the temple of Athena — Theano bars the door with the other women.
42. Greeks breach the temple — Ajax the Lesser drags Cassandra from the altar.
43. The city burns — Cassandra sees every fire from every prophecy converge.
44. Priam is killed at the altar of Zeus — Cassandra hears his cry from across the city.
45. Andromache is taken — Cassandra sees her led away with Astyanax in her arms.
46. Cassandra is bound and led to Agamemnon's ship — she speaks his death aloud.
47. The fleet sails — Cassandra watches the smoke of Troy fade on the horizon.
48. Clytemnestra receives Cassandra at Mycenae — the queen's smile tells everything.
49. Cassandra walks into the palace knowing she will not walk out.
50. The final vision — Cassandra sees her death and, beyond it, a city rebuilt.
