## Summary

Daniel Rourke accepts a six-month posting as lighthouse keeper at Cape Ash, the most remote station on the coast. He arrives to find the lighthouse operating perfectly but the previous keeper, Edwin Marsh, dead in the cistern, killed months earlier. A diary documents Marsh's slow unraveling -- the hum in the tower, the light that changes color at 2:47 AM, the city beneath the waves. As Daniel reads, he experiences the same phenomena. The lighthouse is not a machine. It is a living threshold. And every keeper eventually steps through.

The story is told in literary spare prose across five parts:

1. **Arrival (Chapters 1-10):** Daniel arrives, finds Marsh's body, begins reading the diary.
2. **The Diary (Chapters 11-20):** The diary entries grow stranger. The coast guard denies Cape Ash exists.
3. **Unraveling (Chapters 21-30):** Daniel experiences the same descent. The city beneath the water becomes visible.
4. **The Descent (Chapters 31-40):** Daniel opens the trapdoor, descends into the city of light.
5. **The End (Chapters 41-50):** The cycle continues. Sarah Chen arrives. The lighthouse is patient.
