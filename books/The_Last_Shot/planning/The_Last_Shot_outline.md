# The Last Shot — Outline

## Premise

Sloane Keegan was a tennis prodigy who made the Wimbledon semifinals at seventeen and seemed destined for greatness. But the pressure broke her. She spent fifteen years as a solid top-fifty player, never winning the major everyone predicted. At thirty-five, she lost in the third round to a fifteen-year-old wild card named Anika Voss — a loss so complete it ended her career. Five years later, Sloane is forty, teaching at a suburban tennis club, when an unexpected wild card invitation arrives. She has one more chance at Wimbledon. Standing in her way is Anika, now twenty, the defending champion and youngest world number one in history. The draw puts them against each other in the quarterfinals. Sloane has five weeks to prepare a body and mind that have been retired for half a decade. One last shot.

## Core Cast

- **Sloane Keegan** — Former prodigy, now forty. Teaching pro at a club in Connecticut. Divorced, one daughter. Never won Wimbledon.
- **Anika Voss** — Twenty-year-old world number one. Defending Wimbledon champion. A phenom who has never known failure.
- **Coach Ray** — Ray Matsuda, Sloane's childhood coach, now seventy-two. He never stopped believing.
- **Maya Keegan** — Sloane's sixteen-year-old daughter. Lives with her father. Wary of her mother's return to the tour.
- **Diana Keegan** — Sloane's mother, who pushed her into tennis and still lives vicariously through her.
- **Petra Novak** — Anika's coach, a former champion herself. Cold, calculating, protective.
- **Tom Breslin** — ESPN commentator, former rival of Sloane's. Now covering the tournament.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Invitation (Chapters 1–12)

1. **The Envelope** — Sloane opens the Wimbledon wild card letter at her kitchen table in Connecticut.
2. **The Club** — A day teaching spoiled teens at the tennis club. Sloane's life now.
3. **The First Hit** — Sloane steps onto a court alone for the first time in years. The ball feels foreign.
4. **Coach Ray** — Sloane drives to see Ray at a nursing home. He is frail but sharp. He says yes.
5. **The Trophy Case** — Sloane visits her mother's house. Diana still has every clipping. The pressure returns.
6. **The Champion** — Across the Atlantic, Anika wins another title in Rome. Flawless. Untouchable.
7. **The Body** — Sloane's first real training session. Her knees swell. Her back locks. Ray adjusts everything.
8. **The Daughter** — Maya visits. She does not understand why her mother would do this. Tension.
9. **The Draw** — The Wimbledon draw is announced. Sloane is in Anika's quarter. The headlines explode.
10. **The Spotlight** — Media calls, interview requests. Sloane is suddenly relevant again.
11. **The Doubt** — After a terrible practice, Sloane nearly calls Wimbledon to withdraw.
12. **The Decision** — Maya shows up to watch Sloane train. She says nothing. Her presence is enough.

### Act II: Arrival (Chapters 13–22)

13. **London** — Sloane lands at Heathrow. The city smells like memory.
14. **The Grounds** — Walking through the Wimbledon gates. Everything is the same. Everything is different.
15. **The Practice** — First session on grass. The ball skids low. Sloane adjusts.
16. **The Rival** — Sloane sees Anika practicing through the fence. She is faster, stronger, younger.
17. **Media Day** — Sloane faces the press. They ask about Anika. They ask about age. She handles it.
18. **The Old Guard** — Tom Breslin finds Sloane in the players' lounge. An honest conversation about fear.
19. **First Round** — Sloane wins her first match in straight sets. The crowd remembers her.
20. **Second Round** — A three-set battle against a Russian qualifier. Sloane's legs survive.
21. **Third Round** — Sloane faces a promising eighteen-year-old. Experience beats youth. Into the second week.
22. **The Fourth Round** — Sloane defeats a former top-ten player in two tiebreaks. Anika awaits.

### Act III: The Threshold (Chapters 23–32)

23. **The Scout** — Ray breaks down Anika's game on a whiteboard. Weaknesses are theoretical.
24. **Anika's Burden** — Anika in her own space. The pressure of perfection. A phone call to her mother reveals cracks.
25. **The Body Breaks** — Sloane's knee gives out in practice. Ice, anti-inflammatories, denial.
26. **The Last Time** — Sloane remembers their 2021 match. Anika's forehand winner down the line. The sound of her own career ending.
27. **Anika Speaks** — Anika's pre-match press conference. She calls Sloane a legend. She means it. She is terrified.
28. **Rest Day** — Sloane does nothing. Reads a book. Calls Maya. Maya says she is watching.
29. **Match Day** — Sloane wakes at 5 AM. The body hurts. The mind is clear.
30. **The Walk** — Walking through the tunnel to Centre Court. The roar of the crowd.
31. **The Warm-Up** — Sloane and Anika on court together. The generation gap visible.
32. **First Blood** — The first game. Anika holds serve at love. The match is underway.

### Act IV: The Battle (Chapters 33–42)

33. **The Exchange** — The first five games. Both players holding. Quality is high.
34. **The Break** — Sloane breaks Anika's serve for the first time. The crowd erupts.
35. **Set One** — Sloane takes the first set 7-5. Fifteen years of want in one set.
36. **The Response** — Anika storms through the second set 6-1. She adjusts. She dominates.
37. **The Body Talks** — Third set. Sloane's movement slows. Anika smells blood.
38. **The Threshold** — Down two breaks. Sloane considers letting go. Ray's voice from the box.
39. **The Crowd** — The crowd wills Sloane back. She breaks. She holds. The set slips to a tiebreak.
40. **The Tiebreak** — Sloane wins the tiebreak 7-4. Two sets to one.
41. **The Fourth Set** — Anika levels the match. Two sets all. A fifth set to decide.
42. **The Fifth** — The final set. Both women exhausted. Tennis stripped to its bones.

### Act V: The Last Point (Chapters 43–50)

43. **Match Point** — Sloane has match point at 7-6 in the fifth. She serves for the match.
44. **The Miss** — Sloane hits the net. Deuce. Anika breaks back. The moment is gone.
45. **The End** — Anika wins 9-7 in the fifth. The handshake at the net. Anika holds Sloane's hand an extra second.
46. **The Interview** — Sloane's on-court interview. She does not cry. She thanks the crowd. She thanks Ray.
47. **The Locker Room** — Sloane sits alone in the locker room. Ray finds her. They do not speak for a long time.
48. **The Call** — Maya calls. She says she is proud. Sloane cries for the first time.
49. **The Press** — Final press conference. A journalist asks if she will try again next year. Sloane smiles. No.
50. **The Exit** — Sloane walks out of Wimbledon. Through the gates. She does not look back.
