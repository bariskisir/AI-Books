# The Marble Census - Outline

## Premise

In the old city of Merrow, every public statue is counted once a decade because marble remembers civic promises better than officials do. Census registrar Elian Voss discovers that several statues have changed posture overnight, and each change corresponds to a living citizen removed from municipal rolls. As council renovation plans threaten to quarry the old civic quarter into luxury stone, Elian follows plinth numbers, mason marks, vanished addresses, and whispered testimony from the statues themselves to prove that the census is not ceremonial. It is a compact between the city and the people it would rather forget.

## Core Cast

- **Elian Voss** - Adult census registrar assigned to count public statues, precise, tired, and stubborn about names.
- **Mara Quill** - Adult stone conservator who reads tool marks and weathering on civic monuments.
- **Jonas Reed** - Adult night watchman in the civic quarter who has heard statues move.
- **Councilor Irena Vale** - Adult redevelopment chair turning the old quarter into luxury arcades.
- **Nadia Senn** - Adult legal aid worker whose clients disappear from official address rolls.
- **Orren Pike** - Adult quarry broker buying condemned municipal stone.
- **Talia Morse** - Adult newspaper engraver documenting statue changes.
- **Bram Hesk** - Adult retired mason who carved hidden witness marks into the monuments.
- **Magistrate Leon Arda** - Adult civic magistrate who must decide whether the census has legal force.
- **The Meridian Figure** - A weathered marble statue that becomes the case's first speaking witness.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Changed Stone
1. **First Count** - Elian begins the marble census and finds a statue's hand moved.
2. **Missing Address** - The changed statue corresponds to a woman erased from address rolls.
3. **Night Watch** - Jonas admits he heard stone step after midnight.
4. **Tool Marks** - Mara proves the movement was not vandalism or repair.
5. **Redevelopment Map** - Irena presents a plan to remove old statues for arcade stone.
6. **Plinth Number** - Elian finds a census number hidden under black grime.
7. **Legal Aid List** - Nadia shows a pattern of missing tenants and missing statues.
8. **Engraver's Proof** - Talia compares old engravings to the current posture.
9. **Quarry Contract** - Orren's contract prices condemned monuments by weight.
10. **First Whisper** - The Meridian Figure whispers a name.

### Part II: Civic Memory
11. **Name in Marble** - Elian matches the whispered name to a vanished tenant.
12. **Bram's Mark** - Bram explains the old masons carved witness marks into public stone.
13. **Deleted Rolls** - Address deletions coincide with monument condemnation.
14. **Council Hearing** - Irena calls the census ceremonial and nonbinding.
15. **Cold Square** - More statues shift toward empty apartments.
16. **Stone Dust** - Mara finds fresh dust inside sealed plinth joints.
17. **Tenants' Ledger** - Nadia recovers rent receipts from people declared moved.
18. **Arcade Model** - The luxury model uses marble from named civic figures.
19. **Second Voice** - A statue speaks only when rain touches its mouth.
20. **The Compact** - Elian finds the original charter of the marble census.

### Part III: The Count Refuses
21. **Uncounted** - Elian refuses to certify the census as complete.
22. **Suspension Notice** - Irena has him suspended from the registry.
23. **Talia Prints** - Talia publishes before-and-after engravings.
24. **Stone Night** - Citizens gather to watch the statues after dark.
25. **The Removed Plinth** - A missing statue leaves a warm foundation stone.
26. **Quarry Yard** - Mara and Jonas find civic marble already cut for sale.
27. **Bram's Confession** - Bram admits witness marks were meant for corrupt times.
28. **Nadia Files** - Nadia files for emergency protection of the erased tenants.
29. **Irena's Defense** - Irena argues that empty housing records prove legal vacancy.
30. **Marble Testimony** - Elian asks the magistrate to accept the census as evidence.

### Part IV: Public Roll
31. **Magistrate Arda** - The court orders all statues and address rolls preserved.
32. **Weight Tickets** - Quarry tickets match missing monument fragments.
33. **Rain Session** - The court hears a rain-activated statue speak one name.
34. **Vacancy Scheme** - Nadia proves tenants were rerouted to invalid addresses.
35. **Irena's Map** - Talia overlays redevelopment parcels with erased citizens.
36. **Stone Choir** - Multiple statues turn toward the courthouse.
37. **Orren's Ledger** - Orren reveals council instructions to price civic stone early.
38. **Old Charter** - Elian reads the compact: no citizen may be erased while their monument remembers.
39. **Council Split** - The council fractures over legal and supernatural evidence.
40. **The Count Opens** - Citizens bring names to the square for public counting.

### Part V: Names Restored
41. **Returned Addresses** - The registry restores the first erased tenants.
42. **Broken Arcade** - The luxury project loses its stone supply and financing.
43. **Bram's Repair** - Bram teaches apprentices to uncover witness marks.
44. **Irena Removed** - Irena is removed from redevelopment authority.
45. **Quarry Halt** - The court stops sale of civic marble.
46. **Full Census** - Elian completes the count with citizens present.
47. **The Meridian Name** - The first whispered name is engraved back into public rolls.
48. **Living Square** - The statues settle as people return to the quarter.
49. **New Charter** - The city rewrites the census law with public access.
50. **The Marble Census** - Elian begins the next ledger knowing stone is not silent.
