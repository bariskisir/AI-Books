# The Marble Census

## Overview

In Merrow, public statues are counted once a decade because marble remembers civic promises better than officials do. Registrar Elian Voss finds the Meridian Figure pointing toward a tenant erased from the address rolls, and a ceremonial census becomes a fight over law, memory, and stone.

**Genre:** Civic Gothic / Architectural Magical Realism
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~7600
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

Changed statues, hidden witness marks, deleted address rolls, and quarry contracts reveal a redevelopment scheme that converts citizens and monuments into absence.

## Key Themes

- Public memory requires maintenance.
- Redevelopment language can hide erasure.
- Old laws survive when people keep reading them.
- Counting can be an act of restoration.

## Characters

**Elian Voss** - Adult census registrar assigned to count public statues.

**Mara Quill** - Adult stone conservator reading impossible material changes.

**Jonas Reed** - Adult night watchman who hears statues move.

**Councilor Irena Vale** - Adult redevelopment chair pushing the arcade plan.

**Nadia Senn** - Adult legal aid worker defending erased tenants.

**Talia Morse** - Adult newspaper engraver documenting changed stone.

**Bram Hesk** - Adult retired mason who knows the hidden witness marks.
