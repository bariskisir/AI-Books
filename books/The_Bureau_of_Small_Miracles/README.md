# The Bureau of Small Miracles

## Overview

In Lumen, small miracles are real, rationed, and processed through a tired municipal office. Senior clerk Mira Thale notices that ordinary petitions for oven heat, one extra hour, a working light, or a final word are being denied while donor districts receive decorative rain and banquet glow. With help from a janitor, an auditor, and a street registrar, Mira traces forged priority codes through the Bureau of Small Miracles and forces the city to ask who wonder is meant to serve.

**Genre:** Bureaucratic Fantasy / Civic Magical Realism
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~9500
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

A city bureau diverts small acts of mercy into public spectacle, and a suspended clerk uses ledgers, district clocks, and bottled sparks to restore need-first allocation.

## Key Themes

- Mercy fails when public systems value visibility over need.
- Small miracles matter most when they arrive on time.
- Dashboards can hide harm as easily as reveal it.
- Public ledgers inconvenience theft.
