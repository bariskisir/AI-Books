# The Bureau of Small Miracles - Outline

## Premise

In the city of Lumen, small miracles are real, rationed, and administered through a tired municipal office. Mira Thale, a senior clerk in the Bureau of Small Miracles, notices that ordinary petitions for minor grace--lost keys found, a final rain for a garden, one extra hour before a debt call--are being denied while luxury districts receive decorative wonders. With help from an after-hours janitor, a disillusioned miracle auditor, and a street registrar, Mira traces diverted miracle allotments through forged priority codes and must decide whether to expose the bureau or quietly restore the city's supply of mercy.

## Core Cast

- **Mira Thale** - Adult senior clerk who has processed miracle petitions for fifteen years and remembers when the work mattered.
- **Jon Bell** - Adult night janitor who hears miracles hum in locked cabinets.
- **Rhea Voss** - Adult miracle auditor assigned to efficiency metrics but tired of elegant falsifications.
- **Pavel Orin** - Adult street registrar who collects petitions from markets, hospitals, and debt offices.
- **Director Senn Vale** - Adult bureau director who believes public faith is best managed through spectacle.
- **Iris Cal** - Adult council liaison pressuring the bureau to favor donor districts.
- **Noma Reed** - Adult bakery owner whose denied oven miracle starts Mira's suspicion.
- **Ari Fen** - Adult watchmaker who repairs the bureau's miracle clocks.
- **Leven Marr** - Adult luxury-quarter planner receiving unauthorized wonder allotments.
- **Judge Orel Pike** - Adult civic magistrate who hears the bureau accountability petition.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Denied Grace
1. **Form 12-B** - Mira denies a bakery's tiny oven miracle and notices the code was pre-blocked.
2. **The Humming Cabinet** - Jon hears unused miracles humming in a sealed allocation cabinet.
3. **Decorative Rain** - A luxury quarter receives scheduled moonlit rain while market petitions fail.
4. **Noma's Oven** - Noma explains the denied miracle cost her payroll and bread.
5. **Director's Speech** - Director Vale praises visible miracles as civic confidence.
6. **Street Petitions** - Pavel brings a bundle of low-priority requests marked impossible.
7. **Miracle Clocks** - Ari shows the bureau clocks losing seconds in poor districts.
8. **Audit Smile** - Rhea's audit dashboard hides denials behind efficiency scores.
9. **Priority Blue** - Mira finds a forged priority-blue code on a garden fountain request.
10. **First Swap** - A denied debt-extension miracle matches a luxury banquet wonder.

### Part II: Allotment
11. **The Ledger of Mercy** - Mira opens the yearly allotment ledger after hours.
12. **Jon's Key Ring** - Jon reveals keys to cabinets no one admits exist.
13. **Petition Dust** - Old petitions vanish from the denied archive.
14. **Council Liaison** - Iris Cal says spectacle keeps citizens patient.
15. **Quarter Planner** - Leven Marr treats miracles as urban design accents.
16. **Rhea's Doubt** - Rhea admits the dashboard was built to please council.
17. **Lost Hour** - A debtor denied one hour loses his shop.
18. **The Small Rule** - Mira recalls the founding rule: miracles must answer need before beauty.
19. **Locked Stair** - Jon finds a stair to the dormant allocation room.
20. **Sleeping Sparks** - Unassigned small miracles sit bottled below the bureau.

### Part III: Reassignment
21. **Illegal Mercy** - Mira considers releasing one bottled miracle to Noma.
22. **Bread at Dawn** - The oven miracle works and exposes the diversion.
23. **Audit Trail** - Rhea finds priority changes from Director Vale's office.
24. **Council Night** - Iris demands the bureau protect the gala miracle schedule.
25. **Pavel's Map** - Pavel maps denials by district and finds a ring of absence.
26. **The Hearing Notice** - Mira files an internal petition for allotment review.
27. **Suspended Clerk** - Director Vale suspends Mira pending discipline.
28. **Jon's Testimony** - Jon records the hum of bottled miracles.
29. **Rhea Turns** - Rhea submits raw audit tables to the magistrate.
30. **The City Notices** - Small failures across the city become public questions.

### Part IV: Public Wonder
31. **Banquet Light** - A council banquet receives a stolen dawn-glow miracle.
32. **Broken Fountain** - Mira traces the glow to a denied clinic request.
33. **Civic Petition** - Pavel gathers signatures for an emergency hearing.
34. **The Bureau Doors** - Citizens line up outside with denied forms.
35. **Magistrate Pike** - Judge Pike orders the allocation ledgers opened.
36. **Director's Defense** - Vale argues spectacle preserves belief in the system.
37. **The Small Witnesses** - Noma, the watchmaker, and shopkeepers testify.
38. **Miracle Accounting** - Rhea explains need-weighted allotments and forged codes.
39. **Iris's Bargain** - Iris offers Mira reinstatement for silence.
40. **Cabinet Opened** - Jon opens the sealed cabinet in court.

### Part V: Ordinary Light
41. **Released Sparks** - Bottled miracles appear smaller than the public expected.
42. **Founding Rule** - Mira reads the bureau's original charter aloud.
43. **Leven's List** - Leven's planner notes prove donor districts received need codes.
44. **Director Removed** - Vale is removed pending civil sanction.
45. **Reallocation Day** - The city begins reassigning miracles by need.
46. **One Extra Hour** - A shopkeeper receives the hour that should have been granted.
47. **Rain for the Garden** - A community garden receives a modest rain.
48. **No Spectacle** - The first honest public miracle is almost invisible.
49. **Bureau Windows** - Mira redesigns the office so citizens can see the ledgers.
50. **Small Miracles** - Mira returns to Form 12-B with a different kind of faith.
