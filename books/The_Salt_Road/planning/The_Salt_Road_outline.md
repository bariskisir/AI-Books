# The Salt Road — 50-Chapter Outline

## Premise

Central Anatolia, 2018. Mükerrem Karadağ, a seventy-two-year-old salt merchant, prepares to make his final journey along the ancient salt road that his family has traveled for four generations. The village of Yassıhöyük — his home and the home of his ancestors — will be flooded within the year by a new dam on the Kızılırmak River. His granddaughter Derya, twenty-two and freshly graduated from university in Istanbul, arrives for one last summer before the valley disappears. She expects to help him pack. Instead, he asks her to walk the salt road with him one final time. Over ten days, they traverse a landscape that has known human feet for ten thousand years — passing through villages that are already emptying, encountering the last people who still remember the old ways, and confronting the silence between generations that neither of them knows how to bridge.

## Core Cast

- **Mükerrem Karadağ** — 72, salt merchant. His family has worked the salt road since his great-grandfather's time. He is the last. Widowed, stubborn, a man who measures his life in miles walked and salt delivered. His hands are stained white at the creases. He does not know what he will do when the road is gone.
- **Derya Karadağ** — 22, Mükerrem's granddaughter. Born in Ankara, raised in Istanbul. She has a degree in environmental engineering and no idea what to do with it. She came to Yassıhöyük to say goodbye to the valley of her childhood summers. She did not expect to walk across it.
- **Hatice** — 68, Mükerrem's sister-in-law. She runs the village tea house. She has been in love with Mükerrem for forty years. He has never noticed.
- **Yusuf** — 55, a beekeeper in the village of Kayalıkdere. His family has kept bees in the same valley for two hundred years. The dam will flood his hives. He is learning to smoke them out and relocate them, one by one.
- **Zeynep** — 30, an archaeologist at the rescue dig. She is racing to document the valley's history before the water comes. She knows more about the Karadağ family than they know about themselves.
- **Adil** — 45, the village teacher in Yassıhöyük. He is the only one still teaching in a school that has three students. He compiles a dictionary of the local dialect, which no one will speak in twenty years.

## Chapter Plan

### Part 1: The Last Summer (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Bus** — Derya steps off the bus in Yassıhöyük after six years away. The village is smaller than she remembered. The air smells of dust and wild thyme. Her grandfather is not there to meet her.
2. **The House** — Mükerrem's house is unchanged: the same wooden divan, the same copper coffee pot, the same photograph of his wife Emine on the wall. She has been dead for twelve years, and he has not rearranged the furniture.
3. **The Salt Shed** — Derya finds the salt shed behind the house. Empty. For the first time in her memory, there is no salt waiting to be loaded. Mükerrem explains that the salt mine at Tuzköy closed in the spring.
4. **The Offer** — Derya meets Hatice at the tea house. Hatice tells her that Mükerrem has not made the journey in two years. He has been waiting for something. The salt road, she says, is the only thing that still makes him a man.
5. **The Map** — Mükerrem unfolds the hand-drawn map that his father gave him in 1962. The route runs south from Yassıhöyük through seven villages, crosses the Kızılırmak at the old stone bridge, and ends at the salt market in Kayseri. Nearly a hundred miles.
6. **The Invitation** — Mükerrem asks Derya to walk with him. One last time. He says it flatly, as if asking her to pass the bread. She says she needs to think about it.
7. **The Excavation** — Derya walks to the archaeological dig on the edge of the valley. Zeynep shows her a Hittite seal she found that morning — four thousand years old, a bird and a sun. Derya touches it and feels something she cannot name.
8. **The Decision** — Derya tells her grandfather she will go. He nods once and hands her a pair of walking shoes.
9. **The Preparations** — They pack: dried bread, dried fruit, a bag of salt, a copper teapot, blankets, the map. Mükerrem ties the bundle the same way his father tied it. Derya watches and learns.
10. **The Leaving** — Dawn. The village is still asleep. Derya and Mükerrem walk south out of Yassıhöyük. The valley stretches before them, hazy and golden. Somewhere in the distance, a dog barks.

### Part 2: The Valley (Chapters 11–20)

11. **The First Miles** — The road is not a road. It is a path worn into the earth by feet and hooves, barely visible in places. Mükerrem walks slowly, his stick finding the ground ahead of him. Derya matches his pace.
12. **The Mine** — They pass the abandoned salt mine at Tuzköy. The entrance is boarded up. Mükerrem stands before it for a long time. He does not speak. When he turns away, his eyes are dry.
13. **The Beekeeper** — Yusuf is smoking his hives when they arrive in Kayalıkdere. He offers them tea and tells them about the relocation. The bees do not understand, he says. They keep returning to the valley. Derya helps him seal a hive for transport.
14. **The Village** — Kayalıkdere is half-empty. The school is closed. The mosque is locked. The tea house is a room in someone's house. Mükerrem delivers salt — the last he will ever deliver — to an old woman named Gülseren. She pays him with a jar of honey.
15. **The Story** — Gülseren tells Derya about Mükerrem's wife Emine. She died of a fever that the doctor in the nearest town could not diagnose. Mükerrem carried her on his back for twenty miles to reach the hospital. She died in his arms in the waiting room. Derya had never heard this story.
16. **The Crossing** — Day three. They reach the Kızılırmak. The old stone bridge is still standing. Mükerrem stops in the middle and looks down at the water. The river is low — the dam upstream has already begun to slow it.
17. **The Photograph** — Derya takes a photograph of her grandfather on the bridge. He does not smile. But he does not tell her to stop.
18. **The Cave** — They shelter in a cave that Mükerrem has used for forty years. There is a fire ring, a stack of dry wood, a tin of matches wrapped in oilcloth. The cave walls are covered in marks — names, dates, a bird scratched into the stone. Derya asks who made them. Mükerrem says: *Everyone who came before.*
19. **The Storm** — A summer thunderstorm catches them on the open plain. They take cover under an overhang. The rain turns the earth to mud. Mükerrem tells Derya about the year the rain did not come — 1973 — when the salt road was the only thing that kept the valley alive.
20. **The Silence** — Walking side by side, not speaking. Derya realizes she has never been silent with anyone before. The silence is not empty. It is full of things they cannot say.

### Part 3: The Road (Chapters 21–30)

21. **The Market** — They reach a small town where the weekly market is still held. Mükerrem sells salt to a butcher, a baker, an old woman who remembers his father. Derya watches him work. He does not bargain. He takes what they give him.
22. **The Mosque** — The minaret of a village mosque appears on the horizon. But the village is gone — demolished, the land cleared for the reservoir. Only the minaret remains. They stand and look at it in silence.
23. **The Archaeologist** — Zeynep catches up with them at the next village. She has found something: a tablet from the Old Assyrian trading period, mentioning a salt merchant from the region. She shows it to Mükerrem. He holds it in his salt-stained hands.
24. **The Teacher** — Adil, the village teacher from Yassıhöyük, has walked ahead of them. He is compiling his dictionary. He asks Derya which words she would save if she could only keep a hundred. She cannot answer.
25. **The Gift** — Mükerrem gives Derya the map. He says: *You will need it more than I will.* He does not explain.
26. **The Funeral** — They come upon a funeral in a village that is already half-submerged. The graveyard is being relocated. A family is digging up their grandmother's bones to move them to higher ground. Mükerrem stops and helps them dig. Derya does not know whether to help or to watch.
27. **The Night** — They sleep under the stars. Derya asks her grandfather if he is afraid of dying. He takes a long time to answer. *I am afraid of being forgotten,* he says. *Not me. The road.*
28. **The Letter** — Derya writes a letter to her mother, whom she has not spoken to in two years. She writes about the valley, the road, the salt. She does not mail it. She folds it and keeps it in her pocket.
29. **The Spring** — They find a spring that Mükerrem remembers from his childhood. It is dry. He kneels beside it and touches the cracked earth. Derya kneels beside him.
30. **The Halfway Point** — Day six. They have walked fifty miles. Mükerrem's feet are blistered. Derya's legs ache. They sit on a rock and share a piece of bread and a handful of dried apricots. The road stretches ahead.

### Part 4: The Crossing (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The River People** — They meet a family of nomadic herders who still move their goats between summer and winter pastures. The old woman of the family speaks a dialect that Mükerrem can barely follow. She gives them goat cheese wrapped in cloth.
32. **The Trade** — Mükerrem trades salt for a new pair of sandals, woven from goat leather. The sandal maker is blind, but his hands know the work. He says: *After the water comes, I will make sandals for fish.*
33. **The Well** — They stop at a village well. The water is brackish, undrinkable. The well has been poisoned by the dropping water table. A boy tells them the nearest clean water is three miles east.
34. **The Argument** — Derya and Mükerrem argue. She asks him why he never tried to leave, why he never moved to the city, why he let the salt road take everything from him. He does not answer. He walks ahead. She follows.
35. **The Apology** — That night, Mükerrem speaks first. He says: *I did not know how to be anything else.* Derya takes his hand. It is the first time they have touched in years.
36. **The Salt Pan** — They reach a dry salt pan, a white expanse shimmering in the heat. Mükerrem walks onto it and stands in the center. Derya watches him from the edge. He looks small against the white.
37. **The Memory** — Mükerrem tells Derya about the first time he walked the road with his own grandfather. He was seven years old. He was afraid of the dark. His grandfather gave him a piece of salt to hold. *So you will always find your way back.*
38. **The Dam** — Day eight. They see the dam in the distance — a white wall across the valley, still under construction. Cranes. Trucks. Men in hard hats. It looks like a wound.
39. **The Village Below** — They walk to the edge of the reservoir. The water has already risen halfway up the minaret of a submerged mosque. Derya counts the drowned houses beneath the surface. Mükerrem turns away.
40. **The Offer** — An official from the dam project offers Mükerrem compensation — a sum of money for the house, the land, the salt shed. Mükerrem refuses. The official says he will have to leave anyway. Mükerrem says: *I will leave when the water reaches my door.*

### Part 5: The End of the Road (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Last Village** — They reach the last village before Kayseri. The people here have already left. The doors are open. A dog waits on a porch for a family that will not return.
42. **The Feast** — An old friend of Mükerrem's in the last village insists on hosting them for dinner. The table is full: lamb, rice, yogurt, fresh bread. Derya realizes this is the first real meal she has eaten in nine days.
43. **The Telling** — Over dinner, Mükerrem tells stories of the road: the year the river froze, the year the wolves came down from the mountain, the time he met an English archaeologist in 1968 who told him the road was older than the Romans.
44. **The Waking** — Derya wakes before dawn. She walks to the edge of the village and watches the sun rise over the valley. She realizes she does not want to go back to Istanbul. She does not know what she wants.
45. **The Arrival** — Day ten. They reach Kayseri. The salt market is gone — replaced by a shopping center. Mükerrem stands in the parking lot, holding his empty sack.
46. **The End** — Mükerrem tells Derya that this was the last journey. There is no more salt. No more road. He folds the map and puts it in Derya's hands.
47. **The Return** — They take a bus back to Yassıhöyük. The valley looks different from the road. Smaller. More fragile. Derya watches her grandfather sleep with his head against the window.
48. **The Question** — Derya asks Hatice if she loves her grandfather. Hatice does not answer. She says: *Ask him. He is the only one who does not know.*
49. **The Evening** — Mükerrem sits on his porch and watches the sun set over the valley that will soon be water. Derya sits beside him. She does not speak. He does not speak. The light fades.
50. **The Salt** — Derya leaves the next morning. At the bus stop, Mükerrem presses something into her hand: a piece of salt, wrapped in cloth. The same piece his grandfather gave him, seventy years ago. She holds it. She does not cry. The bus comes. She boards it. The village shrinks in the window. The road is behind her. But the salt is in her hand.
