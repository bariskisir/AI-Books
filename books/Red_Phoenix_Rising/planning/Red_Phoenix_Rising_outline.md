# Red Phoenix Rising — Outline

## Premise

Fifteen years after hanging up his mask, former superhero Phoenix — now firefighter Dominic Cross — lives a quiet life haunted by the night he failed to save a young boy from a burning building. When a brilliant and theatrical arsonist called The Inferno begins systematically burning Iron City's landmarks, taunting the police with cryptic riddles, Dominic is pulled back into action. With only Detective Elena Park as an unwilling ally and his own aging body as an adversary, Phoenix must uncover the identity of the Inferno before the city burns to ash — and confront the terrible possibility that the villain he fights was forged in the fire of his own greatest failure.

## Core Cast

- **Dominic Cross / Phoenix** — Retired superhero, now a firefighter in his late forties. Once Iron City's greatest protector, he walked away after a tragedy he could not prevent. Carries guilt like a second skin. His power: generates and controls fire, but the flame within him has grown dim from disuse.
- **Elena Park** — Iron City PD detective, sharp and skeptical. Inherits the Inferno case. Does not believe in superheroes, only evidence. Becomes an unlikely partner to Dominic.
- **The Inferno** — A charismatic and methodical arsonist-villain. Intelligent, theatrical, and obsessed with fire and transformation. Leaves riddles at each crime scene. His identity is connected to Dominic's past.
- **Marcus Webb** — Dominic's former sidekick, now a mid-level city official. Carries resentment over Dominic's abandonment.
- **Captain Lorraine Voss** — Dominic's captain at the fire station. Knows his past. Protective and pragmatic.
- **Councilman Thomas Park** — Elena's estranged father, a powerful political figure with secrets tied to the Inferno.
- **Danny DeSantos** — A young firefighter who idolizes Phoenix without knowing his true identity.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Embers

1. Dominic Cross pulls a charred teddy bear from a warehouse fire, triggering a memory he has tried to bury for fifteen years.
2. Detective Elena Park arrives at the first Inferno crime scene — a landmark clock tower burned to its frame, a riddle carved into the stone.
3. Dominic studies the riddle in the newspaper and recognizes the handwriting as his own, copied from a letter he wrote years ago.
4. The Inferno releases a video message to the city, mocking the authorities and promising "seven pillars of flame."
5. Elena interviews the mayor and learns the city has a history with masked vigilantes from the Phoenix era.
6. Dominic visits his old rooftop vantage point and finds a shrine of burnt offerings left for him.
7. Elena tracks down Marcus Webb, Dominic's former sidekick, who gives her nothing useful.
8. The second pillar ignites — the Iron City Library's east wing, trapping a night custodian inside.
9. Dominic arrives at the fire in civilian clothes and enters the burning building to save the custodian.
10. Elena spots Dominic carrying the man out and notices something familiar in his movements.

### Part II: Sparks

11. Dominic's hands shake after the rescue — his body is not what it was, and the fire called to something dormant in him.
12. Elena digs into Dominic Cross's file and finds a gap of several years in his early twenties that no records explain.
13. The Inferno's third attack targets the old Iron City Gazette building, destroying the city archive.
14. Dominic realizes the Inferno is burning the city's history, not just its buildings — erasing Iron City's past.
15. Elena confronts Dominic at the fire station; he admits he was Phoenix but refuses to help beyond advising.
16. Marcus Webb discovers Dominic has been seen near a rescue scene and warns him to stay out of it.
17. The fourth pillar — the Iron City Bridge, a structural hit that shuts down the city's main artery.
18. Dominic dons a modified firefighter's mask and approaches the Inferno's perimeter, testing his old instincts.
19. Elena's investigation uncovers that the Inferno's riddles quote from a therapy journal she cannot find the source of.
20. Dominic visits the burned foundation of his old orphanage — the Inferno has hit close to home.

### Part III: Combustion

21. Elena's father, Councilman Park, pressures her to make an arrest before the city panics further.
22. Dominic attempts to use his powers for the first time in fifteen years — a single flame flickers and dies on his palm.
23. The Inferno's fifth target is the Iron City police precinct's evidence locker, destroying cold-case files.
24. Elena realizes the real target is the box of evidence from the old Phoenix-era "Needle" case — a serial arsonist Dominic put away.
25. Dominic and Elena form an uneasy partnership, agreeing to share information.
26. Dominic trains in secret, trying to rekindle his powers through meditation and exposure to controlled flame.
27. The Inferno sends a personal message to Dominic — a recording of a boy's voice that makes Dominic go pale.
28. Elena discovers that the therapy journal quoted in the riddles belonged to a patient at St. Catherine's Youth Home.
29. Dominic remembers the boy he could not save — Leo Marchetti, a mute child pulled from a fire who later disappeared from foster care.
30. The sixth pillar ignites — a chemical plant on the edge of the city, sending a plume of toxic smoke over Iron City.

### Part IV: Inferno

31. Dominic returns to St. Catherine's and finds records of Leo's treatment — he was diagnosed with pyromania and extreme trauma.
32. Elena finds Leo Marchetti's death certificate, but something is wrong — the body was never positively identified.
33. The Inferno calls Dominic on a burner phone and speaks in a voice that sounds familiar but distorted.
34. Dominic's powers return in a surge during a minor fire at the station, nearly revealing himself to Danny.
35. Elena confronts her father about the Needle case cover-up and learns Councilman Park suppressed evidence that would have freed an innocent man.
36. The Inferno's seventh target is revealed by riddle — the Iron City power grid.
37. Dominic suits up properly for the first time, wearing a modified fire-resistant suit with a phoenix crest painted on the chest.
38. A city-wide blackout plunges Iron City into darkness, and arson teams strike simultaneously across five neighborhoods.
39. Dominic corners one of the arson teams and extracts the location of the Inferno's lair.

### Part V: Ashes

40. The Inferno's lair is the abandoned St. Catherine's Youth Home — the place where it all began.
41. Dominic enters the building alone, walking through halls filled with gasoline-soaked debris and crucifixes of charred wood.
42. The Inferno reveals himself — Leo Marchetti, alive, his face scarred by the fire Dominic could not pull him from.
43. Leo explains his plan: to burn Iron City to nothing and rebuild from ash, because only Phoenix understood that destruction creates new life.
44. Dominic tries to reason with Leo, but the Inferno triggers a chain of explosions throughout the building.
45. Elena arrives with police backup and coordinates a rescue effort as the building collapses around them.
46. Dominic and the Inferno fight through the burning ruins — fire against fire, guilt against rage.
47. The Inferno is trapped beneath a collapsed beam and Dominic has to decide whether to save his enemy or let him burn.
48. Dominic pulls Leo from the wreckage, carrying him out of the building as Phoenix once failed to carry him.
49. Leo is taken into custody; Elena clears Dominic of any wrongdoing, and the city begins to rebuild.
50. Dominic stands on a rooftop at sunrise, the phoenix emblem visible through his turnout coat as Danny watches from below, realizing who his captain truly is.
