# Red Weather — Outline

## Premise

A mysterious red algae bloom begins consuming oxygen from the North Atlantic at an exponential rate. Dr. Simone Marchetti, a disgraced oceanographer, must convince a skeptical scientific community and a ruthless corporation that the bloom is not a natural phenomenon — it is a weaponized organism, and the countdown to planetary asphyxiation has already begun.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Simone Marchetti** — Oceanographer, Marine Biologist. Once a rising star at WHOI, now ostracized after sounding false alarms. Cynical, brilliant, relentless.
- **Captain Elias Rhodes** — Captain of the research vessel *Atmospheric Dawn*. A former Navy salvage operator who knows the Atlantic better than anyone. Pragmatic, quiet, capable.
- **The Corporation (Aetheria Global)** — A multinational energy conglomerate. Their deep-sea mining operations have breached a subglacial methane pocket. The red algae is their solution — and their secret.
- **Dr. Helena Voss** — Aetheria's head of biological research. She engineered the algae. She knows what it can do. She is not in control anymore.
- **Admiral Chen** — U.S. Navy, tasked with monitoring the Atlantic dead zone. Allied with Simone by necessity.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Stain (Chapters 1–15)

1. **The Sample** — Dr. Simone Marchetti analyzes a seawater sample from the Sargasso Sea. The oxygen levels are wrong. There is something in the water.
2. **The Bloom** — Satellite imagery reveals a crimson patch the size of Ireland spreading east.
3. **The Dismissal** — Simone presents her findings to the Oceanographic Board. They call it an algal bloom. Natural. Harmless. She is asked to leave.
4. **The Call** — Captain Rhodes receives a priority signal from an old Navy contact. Something is killing fish by the thousands off the Azores.
5. **The Contract** — Aetheria Global offers Simone a research position. She refuses. She trusts nothing with that name.
6. **The Vessel** — Rhodes docks at Norfolk. The *Atmospheric Dawn* is a rusting research ship, but she still runs.
7. **The Crew** — Simone meets the skeleton crew. Too few. Too young. But they know the sea.
8. **Departure** — The *Dawn* leaves Norfolk at dusk. Simone watches the coast shrink and feels she is leaving the world behind.
9. **The First Death** — A pod of whales beaches itself off the Virginia coast. Autopsy shows their lungs are full of red slime.
10. **Open Water** — Day three. The ship reaches the edge of the bloom. The water is the color of rust.
11. **Sampling** — Simone takes the first deep-core sample. The algae is denser than anything she has seen. It should not exist at this concentration.
12. **The Log** — Rhodes shows Simone his old Navy logs. There is a pattern. The bloom centers on coordinates that match Aetheria's leased seafloor plots.
13. **The Blackout** — Aetheria cuts the ship's satellite link. They are alone. No communication. No rescue.
14. **Acceleration** — Overnight, the bloom doubles in size. The oxygen readings drop three percent in twelve hours.
15. **The Decision** — Simone convinces Rhodes to push deeper into the bloom, toward the source.

### Act II — The Crimson Sea (Chapters 16–30)

16. **The Wall** — The ship hits a solid mat of algae. The engines strain. The water is thick as paint.
17. **Hull Stress** — The algae is corrosive. It eats through the anti-fouling paint. Rhodes calculates they have three days before hull integrity is compromised.
18. **Submersible** — Simone launches in the one-man submersible. Below the surface, the algae is layered like a second ocean.
19. **The Chimney** — On the seafloor, Simone finds a hydrothermal vent — but it is capped. Aetheria's drilling platform sits directly above it.
20. **The Seal** — The cap is cracked. Red slime leaks from it in pulsating ribbons. The algae is breeding inside the vent.
21. **Ascent** — Simone surfaces. The ship is surrounded by a ring of dead fish. The gulls that fed on them are dying too.
22. **The Message** — A garbled transmission comes through. It is Helena Voss. She says she wants to defect. She says the algae is intelligent.
23. **Mutiny** — Half the crew wants to turn back. Rhodes gives them a choice: stay or take the lifeboat. Three leave.
24. **The Fixer** — Aetheria sends a corporate security vessel. It hails the *Dawn* and orders them to stand down.
25. **Standoff** — Rhodes outmaneuvers the security vessel in a fog bank. Simone realizes Aetheria will kill them to keep the secret.
26. **The Scientist** — Simone decodes Voss's message. The algae was designed to consume methane. It was supposed to die after the methane was gone. It did not die.
27. **Adaptation** — The algae has evolved. It no longer needs methane. It feeds on dissolved oxygen. Everything in the water is fuel.
28. **The Hypothesis** — Simone believes the algae is a colony intelligence — a distributed mind. If they can introduce a genetic failsafe, the whole bloom could die at once.
29. **The Virus** — Simone works on a bacteriophage engineered to attack the algae's chloroplasts. She has twelve hours before the ship's fuel runs out.
30. **The Fever** — Simone collapses from exhaustion. Rhodes carries her to her bunk. He takes over the research logs.

### Act III — Asphyxiation (Chapters 31–45)

31. **Global Panic** — Oxygen levels drop worldwide. Cities report breathing difficulty. The UN convenes an emergency session.
32. **The Leak** — Admiral Chen leaks Simone's research to the press. The world learns the truth about Aetheria.
33. **Retribution** — Aetheria's stock collapses. But the damage is done. The algae cannot be stopped by lawsuits.
34. **The Delivery** — A Navy helicopter drops supplies to the *Dawn*. Among the crates is a bio-containment lab and Voss's research notes.
35. **The Code** — Voss's notes contain the genetic blueprint of the algae. Simone finds the kill switch — a protein that triggers apoptosis. But it must be delivered to every cell simultaneously.
36. **The Quantity** — They need fifty tons of the bacteriophage. The *Dawn* can carry five. The only facility capable of producing fifty tons is owned by Aetheria.
37. **The Plan** — Simone proposes they sail to Aetheria's floating platform and commandeer its bioreactors.
38. **The Approach** — The *Dawn* approaches the platform under cover of fog. Rhodes rigs the ship's crane as a boarding ramp.
39. **Boarding** — Rhodes and Simone board the platform. It is nearly abandoned. The crew fled when the algae breached the containment tanks.
40. **The Lab** — They find Voss in the bio-containment lab. She is sick. The algae infected her through a cut in her glove. She gives them the activation sequence.
41. **The Bioreactor** — Simone starts the bioreactor. It will take six hours to produce enough phage.
42. **The Fever Breaks** — Helena Voss dies. Her last words: "It remembers. It will not forgive."
43. **The Swarm** — The algae attacks the platform. It crawls up the legs. It finds every seam. The platform begins to sink.
44. **Containment** — The bioreactor finishes. Simone loads the phage into torpedo-shaped dispersal canisters. They have one chance.
45. **The Launch** — Rhodes fires the canisters into the bloom. The phage disperses. The water begins to clear.

### Act IV — The Aftermath (Chapters 46–50)

46. **Oxygen** — Within hours, oxygen levels stabilize. The algae is dying. The world breathes again.
47. **The Cost** — Simone and Rhodes are hailed as heroes. But the damage to the ocean is measured in decades. The dead zones will not recover in their lifetimes.
48. **Hearing** — Simone testifies before a global tribunal. Aetheria is dissolved. Its executives face charges of crimes against humanity.
49. **The Return** — Rhodes and Simone sail the *Dawn* back to Norfolk. The Atlantic is blue again. But Simone knows there are other leases, other vents, other secrets on the seafloor.
50. **Red Sky at Morning** — Simone stands on the deck at dawn. The sky is clear. The water is clean. She opens her laptop and begins to write the report that will change how humanity treats the deep ocean. Or she hopes it will.
