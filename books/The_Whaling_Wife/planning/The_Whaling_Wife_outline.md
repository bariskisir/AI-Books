# The Whaling Wife — Outline

## Premise

In 1845, a woman named Sarah Beckett disguises herself as a man to join a whaling voyage on the *Mary Rose*, the same ship her husband Thomas disappeared from a year earlier. She must survive the brutal world of Nantucket whaling, evade discovery, and uncover the truth about her husband's fate — while the harpooner who knew him on the previous voyage watches her with suspicion and the captain harbors a deadly secret.

## Core Cast

- **Sarah Beckett** — A Nantucket woman of twenty-eight. Determined, resourceful, desperate to find her missing husband. Disguises herself as Samuel Beckett.
- **Captain Nye** — Master of the whaling ship *Mary Rose*. A hard man with a hidden guilt. Knows what happened to Thomas.
- **Eben Cole** — The ship's harpooner. A seasoned whaleman with a quiet conscience. He was on the *Mary Rose*'s previous voyage and knew Thomas.
- **Thomas Beckett** — Sarah's husband. A navigator with a gift for numbers. Disappeared at sea under suspicious circumstances.
- **First Mate Higgins** — Nye's loyal second-in-command. Suspicious of everyone.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### PART I — THE RESOLUTION (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Empty Bed** — Sarah wakes in her Nantucket house, alone. Thomas has been gone a year. She traces his outline on the sheet beside her.

2. **The Letter** — A letter arrives from Thomas, posted nine months ago from Fayal. He writes of trouble with the captain and hints at proof of fraud.

3. **The Mary Rose** — Sarah goes to the wharf. The *Mary Rose* has returned for refitting. She watches the crew and makes a decision.

4. **The Disguise** — Sarah binds her chest, cuts her hair, and practices walking as a man in front of a mirror. Her hands shake.

5. **Samuel Beckett** — She invents a story: a displaced farmer from the mainland, fleeing debt, seeking a berth.

6. **Signing On** — Captain Nye interviews "Samuel" on the deck of the *Mary Rose*. Something flickers in the captain's eyes, but he signs the boy on as a green hand.

7. **The Fo'c'sle** — Sarah's first night in the cramped crew quarters. The smell of unwashed men. She barely sleeps.

8. **The First Watch** — Standing watch in darkness. The sea stretches black in all directions. She feels the weight of her lie.

9. **Eben Cole** — The harpooner watches her from across the deck. His gaze is too sharp. He says nothing.

10. **Landfall Lost** — Nantucket fades on the horizon. Sarah is committed. There is no going back.

### PART II — THE VOYAGE OUT (Chapters 11–20)

11. **The Green Hand** — Sarah learns the ropes badly. Rope burns. Mockery from the crew. Cole silently corrects her technique.

12. **The Captain's Cabin** — Sarah sees light under the captain's door at night. She hears voices — Nye arguing with someone who isn't there.

13. **The Logbook** — During a moment alone in the cabin, Sarah opens the captain's log. She finds Thomas's name, crossed out, with the word "deserted" written beside it.

14. **The Gale** — A storm hits. Green water over the bow. Sarah proves herself during the chaos, hauling rope alongside the men.

15. **The Azores** — The ship makes landfall at Fayal. Sarah could run. She stays aboard.

16. **The Doldrums** — Calm seas. Heat. Tension simmers below deck. A fight breaks out. Sarah watches, learning the men's hierarchies.

17. **Cole's Past** — Cole speaks to her for the first time about the previous voyage. He knew Samuel — her husband's alias — the name she stole.

18. **The Story** — Cole tells her Thomas was a good navigator who found something he should not have. He does not say what.

19. **The First Blow** — A whale is sighted. The ship erupts into organized action. Sarah is assigned to Cole's boat.

20. **The Chase** — The whale boat skims across the water. Cole stands in the bow, harpoon raised. Sarah pulls an oar until her hands bleed.

### PART III — THE HUNT (Chapters 21–30)

21. **The Strike** — Cole's harpoon sinks into the whale. Blood spreads across the water. Sarah stares at the dying creature.

22. **The Cut** — The whale is brought alongside. The crew flenses the blubber in long strips. The deck runs red.

23. **The Tryworks** — The stench of boiling blubber fills the ship. Sarah works the fires. Grease and smoke cling to everything.

24. **The Sperm Whale** — A bull sperm whale attacks the ship. The crew fights for their lives. Sarah sees Cole fall.

25. **Overboard** — Sarah dives after Cole into the freezing water. She drags him to a floating piece of wreckage.

26. **The Raft** — Alone on the makeshift raft, Cole is unconscious. Sarah holds him through the night, terrified he will die.

27. **Awakening** — Cole wakes. He sees her wet shirt clinging to her body. The binding shows through the soaked cloth. He looks away.

28. **The Confession** — Cole tells her the truth: Thomas discovered Captain Nye was selling oil on the side, cheating the owners. Nye confronted him.

29. **The Adrift** — Nye put Thomas in a whale boat with water and biscuit, miles from land. He called it desertion. Cole could not stop it.

30. **The Rescue** — The *Mary Rose* finds them. Nye's face is stone. Cole and Sarah share a look. Their pact is sealed.

### PART IV — THE TRUTH (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The Healing** — Sarah tends Cole's wounds in secret. The crew whispers about their closeness.

32. **The Evidence** — Sarah finds Thomas's sea chest among Cole's belongings. Inside: a navigation chart with figures in the margins — proof of Nye's fraud.

33. **The Calculation** — Sarah verifies the numbers. Thomas had tracked every barrel of oil. The discrepancy is unmistakable.

34. **The Accusation** — Sarah confronts Nye in his cabin. She lays out the evidence. Nye tells her she will not live to share it.

35. **The Locked Door** — Nye confines Sarah to a storage locker. She has no light. No food. The ship creaks around her.

36. **The Knife** — Cole slips her a knife through the grate. He says the crew is divided. Nye has Higgins on his side.

37. **The Confrontation** — Cole gathers the men. He tells them who Samuel really is. Half the crew turns against him.

38. **The Mutiny** — Men fight on the deck. Cole and Sarah are overwhelmed. They are put in chains.

39. **The Trial** — Nye convenes a ship's trial. He accuses Samuel of theft and Cole of mutiny. The verdict is death.

40. **The Storm** — Before the sentence can be carried out, a hurricane strikes. The *Mary Rose* begins to break apart.

### PART V — THE RETURN (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Wreck** — The chains break loose as the ship lurches. Sarah and Cole are free. Nye is pinned beneath a fallen mast.

42. **The Choice** — Sarah could let Nye die. She pulls him free instead. The three of them take the last intact boat.

43. **The Long Boat** — Days at sea in an open boat. Nye is injured, feverish. He speaks in delerium.

44. **The Coast** — They sight land — the coast of Brazil. They drag the boat ashore.

45. **The Shore** — On the beach, Nye's fever worsens. He knows he is dying.

46. **The Deathbed** — Nye confesses everything: he pushed Thomas overboard in open water. The logbook was a lie.

47. **The Passing** — Nye dies. Sarah and Cole bury him on the beach. She says nothing over his grave.

48. **The Passage Home** — They book passage on a merchant vessel. Sarah is no longer Samuel. She is herself again.

49. **Nantucket** — The harbor comes into view. Sarah returns not as a widow but as a woman who knows the truth.

50. **The Whaling Wife** — Sarah delivers the evidence to the shipping company. She establishes a fund for whalers' widows. She stands on the beach alone, the sea flat and gray before her. She is not the same woman who left.
