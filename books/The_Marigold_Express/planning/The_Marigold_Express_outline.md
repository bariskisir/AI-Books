# The Marigold Express — Outline

## Premise

The Marigold Express is the most glamorous train on the continent — four days of crystal, velvet, and impeccable dinners from the coastal city of Pell to the capital. This season it also carries the Saffron Star, a legendary yellow diamond, escorted by its magnificently broke owner, the Contessa della Vigna. Word has traveled, and so has half the criminal talent of three countries. Over four nights and a thousand miles the stone is stolen, re-stolen, swapped, lost, mistaken for a paperweight, and stolen again, while alliances form and dissolve over dinner, a corpse turns out to be merely inconvenienced, and everyone slowly realizes the cleverest crook on the train is not the one they were watching.

A fast, funny, twisting ensemble caper — a deliberate tonal departure from the somber literary and crime books in this series. Comedy of manners with a heist engine. Adult characters; clean (theft, farce, no gore, no abuse); witty dialogue; real plot mechanics and a satisfying twist.

## Tone & Rules

- Light, propulsive, dialogue-forward. Wit over gloom.
- Every chapter advances the scheme or the laughs (ideally both). No padding, no lyrical refrains.
- Fair-play caper: the twist is seeded; an attentive reader can catch it.
- Thieves with standards; marks with secrets; a straight-man cop who is dogged, not stupid.

## Core Cast

- **Cornelius Vane** — gentleman thief; elegant, ironic, allergic to fuss; narrates much of the rogue's view. Wants the Star clean and quiet.
- **Mrs. Adelaide Frost** — grand dame grifter, all furs and fabricated pedigree; brilliant, ruthless about everything but her "niece."
- **June Frost** — Adelaide's apprentice (not actually her niece); quick hands, quicker mouth; the heart of the book; tired of the long con.
- **Inspector Hugo Pellam** — railway police; has never lost a case; armed with a notebook, a packed lunch, and no imagination — which turns out to be a kind of superpower.
- **Anton Reyes** — the conductor; honest to a fault, in debt to a loan shark named Mr. Sweet; needs the Star to live past Thursday.
- **Salvatore Quill** — legendary retired safecracker; the only man alive who can open the Contessa's antique strongbox; promised his granddaughter he was done.
- **Contessa della Vigna** — owner of the Star; flamboyant, broke, and not at all what she seems.
- **Mr. Sweet** — the loan shark's smiling agent, aboard to collect.
- **Bartholomew "Barty" Lune** — fence and forger in a porter's uniform; supplies the fake.
- **Henrietta Voss** — insurance investigator posing as a travel writer; Pellam's rival and foil.

## The Shape of the Twist (for the author)

The Saffron Star in the Contessa's strongbox is already a fake: the Contessa, ruined, has arranged to have her own (insured) diamond "stolen" and sold quietly, intending to claim the insurance on a paste copy. The *real* Star was swapped out before boarding and is hidden in plain sight aboard the train. The thieves keep stealing the wrong stone from each other. June is the one who works it out. The ending: the genuinely guilty party (the Contessa, and Mr. Sweet's syndicate behind her) is undone by the very crooks they planned to use as patsies; Quill keeps his promise by not cracking a safe (he talks his way in instead); Pellam closes his case by accident and integrity; the Star ends up returned to the museum it was quietly looted from a generation ago; Vane and June walk off as (wary) partners. Everyone with standards wins; everyone without them is left holding paste.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part One — All Aboard (1–12)
1. **The Platform at Pell** — Vane boards the Marigold Express with one small bag and large intentions; the train and the era established.
2. **The Manifest** — We meet the players boarding, each introduced by what they're pretending to be.
3. **The Contessa and Her Star** — The Contessa sweeps aboard with the Saffron Star and a strongbox; a public display that is too public.
4. **Vane Takes Inventory** — Vane clocks three other professionals within an hour and is annoyed; the diamond is going to be crowded.
5. **Mrs. Frost Holds Court** — Adelaide and June run a warm-up con at dinner; June's growing weariness with the life.
6. **The Conductor's Arithmetic** — Anton counts his debt to Mr. Sweet against his honest wages and reaches a desperate total.
7. **A Porter Named Barty** — Vane recognizes the forger Barty Lune under a porter's cap; a fake Star is already in play.
8. **Inspector Pellam Packs a Lunch** — Pellam boards last, unglamorous and underestimated; he has a list and a sandwich.
9. **Quill in Compartment Nine** — The retired safecracker, traveling to a granddaughter's wedding, recognized by Vane; the promise he made.
10. **Henrietta Voss, Travel Writer** — The insurance investigator introduces herself to everyone and believes none of them.
11. **The Lay of the Carriages** — A tour of the train as a heist map: the strongbox car, the galley, the observation lounge, the Contessa's suite.
12. **Three Plans for One Diamond** — Cross-cut: Vane, the Frosts, and Anton each finalize incompatible plans for the first night.

### Part Two — The First Night (13–25)
13. **Soup and Surveillance** — A formal dinner where every thief watches every other thief watch the Contessa.
14. **The Lights Go Out** — A staged blackout (whose?) throws the dining car into farce.
15. **Two Hands on the Same Box** — In the dark, Vane and June both reach the strongbox and collide.
16. **The Star Is Gone** — Lights up: the strongbox is open and empty; pandemonium; Pellam produces his notebook.
17. **Everyone Is a Suspect** — Pellam confines the carriage and begins interviews; comic alibis.
18. **June's Pocket** — June discovers the Star is in her pocket and she did not put it there.
19. **The Body in the Corridor** — Mr. Sweet is found apparently dead; the stakes lurch.
20. **Not Quite Dead** — Sweet is merely drugged; he wakes furious and demanding Anton's payment.
21. **Vane and June, Briefly Allied** — Cornered, the rivals make a wary one-night pact to survive the inspector.
22. **The Contessa Weeps Convincingly** — The Contessa's grief is a performance; Vane notices the seams.
23. **Quill Is Asked Nicely** — Anton begs Quill to open something; Quill refuses, then wavers; the granddaughter's photo.
24. **Pellam Finds a Thread** — The inspector, plodding, notices the one detail everyone clever overlooked.
25. **The First Station: Tarn** — Dawn stop at Tarn; someone tries to get the Star off the train and fails comically.

### Part Three — The Long Middle (26–38)
26. **The Star Changes Hands (Again)** — The diamond passes from June to Adelaide to a soup tureen within an hour.
27. **Henrietta's Real Badge** — Voss reveals to Vane she's insurance, not press, and proposes a deal.
28. **The Math of the Fake** — Vane and Voss compare notes and suspect the stolen stone is paste.
29. **Mr. Sweet Collects** — Sweet leans on Anton; Anton makes a reckless promise involving the Star.
30. **Adelaide's Long Game** — We learn Adelaide's true target was never the Star but the Contessa's secret.
31. **June Tests the Stone** — June, who knows gems, scratches the "Star" and learns the truth: it's glass.
32. **Where Is the Real One?** — The crew's question becomes everyone's: if this is fake, where is the genuine Saffron Star?
33. **The Contessa's Suite** — A break-in to the suite reveals insurance papers and a second, hidden strongbox.
34. **Quill's Compromise** — Quill keeps his promise by talking a lock open with a steward instead of cracking it.
35. **Pellam Eats His Sandwich and Solves Half of It** — The inspector reconstructs the blackout; he's one step behind and gaining.
36. **The Second Station: Vesper Halt** — A midnight stop; Mr. Sweet's reinforcements try to board; chaos repelled.
37. **Alliance of the Honest Crooks** — Vane, June, Quill, and Anton pool what they know against the Contessa and Sweet.
38. **The Frost Between Them** — Adelaide and June fall out over whether to walk away rich or do the right thing.

### Part Four — The Last Station (39–50)
39. **The Plan to Out-Steal the Owner** — The honest crooks design a sting to expose the Contessa's insurance fraud.
40. **Baiting the Contessa** — They let the Contessa believe her paste Star has been recovered, to make her overplay.
41. **Sweet's Syndicate Revealed** — Mr. Sweet is no freelancer; he fronts the syndicate that's been bankrolling the Contessa's fraud.
42. **The Real Star in Plain Sight** — Revelation: the genuine diamond has been the Contessa's "paperweight" / brooch all along.
43. **Pellam Closes In** — The inspector assembles the carriage for a Poirot-style accounting and gets most of it gloriously right.
44. **The Switch** — During Pellam's reveal, a flurry of sleight-of-hand swaps the real and fake stones under everyone's nose.
45. **The Contessa Overplays** — Baited, the Contessa claims the theft for the insurance and incriminates herself and Sweet.
46. **Sweet's Last Move** — Sweet tries to take the Star by force at the final approach; foiled by Anton and Quill.
47. **The Capital Approaches** — The skyline of the capital; the law waiting on the platform; who walks off in cuffs.
48. **Pellam's Verdict** — The inspector hands the Contessa and Sweet to the police, credits no one cleverly, and is satisfied.
49. **The Right Owner** — The Saffron Star is quietly routed back to the small museum it was looted from; Voss signs off; debts squared, Anton freed of Sweet.
50. **The Platform at the Capital** — Goodbyes; Adelaide and June reconcile on new terms; Vane offers June a partnership; the train's whistle and one last grift.
