# The Hollow Men — Outline

## Premise

In the remote Appalachian town of Crag's Hollow, people have been vanishing. Not dying -- vanishing. They walk into the woods and do not come back. Then they come back, but something is wrong. Their eyes are the wrong color. Their skin has no pores. Their voices carry a faint echo, as if two people are speaking at once. Sheriff Eli Collier, a man who has spent twenty years keeping the peace in a town that barely needs it, is the first to notice that the missing are not dead -- they have been replaced by something that wears their faces. The entity calls itself the Hollow Man, and it has been waking beneath the mountain for a century, feeding on the identities of the living. Collier must find a way to stop it before the entire town is replaced and the Hollow Man walks out of the hollows into the wider world.

## Core Cast

- **Sheriff Eli Collier** -- Crag's Hollow's lawman for twenty years. Widower. Sees patterns others miss. Stubborn, methodical, exhausted.
- **Deputy Lena Hayes** -- Collier's deputy. Young, sharp, born in the hollows. Trusts her instincts and her shotgun.
- **The Hollow Man** -- An entity born from the collective anguish of miners who died in a cave-in a hundred years ago. It has no true face; it borrows them.
- **Rebecca Tolliver** -- Preacher's daughter. Quiet observer. Knows more about the old stories than she has ever told.
- **Sheriff Vance Corrigan** -- State police detective called in after the crisis. Skeptic. Buries what he cannot explain.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. **The First Missing** -- A hiker vanishes on Lost Knob; Sheriff Collier finds his camp abandoned, a half-eaten meal on the table.
2. **The Search Party** -- Collier and Hayes push into the deep woods; they find footprints that stop mid-stride on bare rock.
3. **What Comes Down** -- The hiker returns three days later, walking out of the fog, eyes the wrong shade of brown.
4. **The Fix** -- The returned man behaves perfectly, but Collier notices he blinks on the wrong rhythm.
5. **Deputy's Doubt** -- Hayes tells Collier he is reading too much into a man who survived the wilderness.
6. **The Second One** -- A woman vanishes from her porch; her husband hears nothing, not even the screen door.
7. **The Wrong Smile** -- The woman returns, face identical, but her smile does not reach her eyes.
8. **Patterns** -- Collier starts a log: speech oddities, memory gaps, the absence of breath fog on cold mornings.
9. **The Watcher at the Tree Line** -- Something stands at the edge of town each night, motionless, watching.
10. **Hayes Sees It** -- Hayes witnesses the returned woman's skin ripple; she opens fire.
11. **The Phone Lines Go Dead** -- The town loses contact with the outside; every line is cut clean.
12. **No One Leaves** -- Collier tries to drive out; the road loops back to town no matter which way he turns.
13. **Town Meeting** -- The returned ones attend the meeting; the real ones sit in paralyzed silence.
14. **The Hollow Man Appears** -- A figure of shifting human skin steps into Main Street; it speaks with many voices.
15. **What It Wants** -- The Hollow Man announces it intends to wear every face in Crag's Hollow.
16. **The Preacher's Test** -- Reverend Tolliver tries holy water on a returned; the thing laughs.
17. **The Divide** -- The town fractures between those who believe Collier and those who refuse.
18. **Escape Attempt** -- Three teenagers try to hike out; they return at dusk, hollowed and smiling.
19. **Collier's Theory** -- Collier realizes the displaced souls are not destroyed but stored.
20. **The Hidden Room** -- The mine shaft beneath Bald Knob holds the trapped souls, suspended and silent.
21. **What the Mine Holds** -- Hundreds of translucent figures float in the dark, their mouths open in a silent cry.
22. **The Bargain** -- The Hollow Man offers to free the town in exchange for Collier's willing body.
23. **Hayes Takes a Stand** -- Deputy Hayes refuses the deal and locks Collier in the office to stop him.
24. **The First Real Death** -- A returned man dies when his original body rejects the hollow thing inside.
25. **The Descent** -- Collier escapes and descends into the mine to find the soul-chamber.
26. **The Hollow Man's Origin** -- Collier learns the entity was born from the agony of thirty-seven miners trapped by a collapse.
27. **The Counterfeit Town** -- The Hollow Man conjures a perfect replica of Crag's Hollow to trap the freed souls.
28. **Who Is Real?** -- Collier returns to a town where he cannot tell who is human and who is hollow.
29. **The Trust Test** -- Hayes proposes testing everyone with a question only the real person would know.
30. **The Test Fails** -- The hollowed ones possess the memories too; the test proves nothing.
31. **The Screaming** -- The trapped souls begin to scream in unison, a sound that shakes windows from their frames.
32. **The Burning** -- Collier sets fire to the mine entrance to sever the entity's connection to the town.
33. **The Hollow Man Rages** -- The entity takes control of a dozen bodies at once and attacks.
34. **The Siege** -- The hollowed townspeople surround the sheriff's office, calling Collier out.
35. **What They Say** -- The possessed speak as one voice: Let us wear you or we will break you.
36. **The Dynamite** -- Collier recalls a powder store in the mine; he can collapse the mountain permanently.
37. **Hayes's Last Stand** -- Hayes holds the hollowed crowd at the door while Collier runs for the mine.
38. **The Fuse** -- Collier lights the fuse; the Hollow Man attacks his mind with every loss he has ever suffered.
39. **The Mountain Falls** -- The ridge collapses, burying the mine and the entity's anchor in the world.
40. **The Silence** -- Every hollowed body drops; the real townspeople wake in the street, bruised but alive.
41. **The Aftermath** -- The survivors count the cost: the dead, the missing, the hollowed who will not wake.
42. **The Mine Sealed** -- The town votes to seal the mine permanently with concrete and prayer.
43. **The Gathered Stories** -- Collier writes his full account, knowing no one outside will believe it.
44. **State Police Arrive** -- Detective Vance Corrigan arrives with a team to investigate the mass casualty event.
45. **The Skeptic** -- Corrigan suspects Collier orchestrated the disaster; he interrogates him like a suspect.
46. **Corrigan's Doubt Cracks** -- Corrigan finds a fragment of skin from the Hollow Man: it has no DNA.
47. **The Fragment** -- The skin sample is sent to a state lab; it dissolves before it arrives.
48. **The Departure** -- Corrigan leaves without filing charges, his report full of holes and silences.
49. **The New Normal** -- Spring comes to Crag's Hollow; the town begins to heal, but everyone watches the tree line.
50. **The Watch** -- Collier sits on his porch at dusk, a shotgun across his knees, knowing the mountain is quiet but not empty.
