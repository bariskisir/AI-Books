# The Rust Maiden — Outline

## Premise

In an alternate 1930s where the Dust Bowl spawned colossal war machines built by rival oil dynasties, Scout March is a grease-stained mechanic who talks to her walker, Rusty, as if it can hear her. Baron Hargrave controls every drop of crude west of the Mississippi through his armored fleet of crawlers, harvesters, and the monstrous Iron Judge battle-walker. When Hargrave's men massacre a homesteading community for refusing to sell their mineral rights, Scout straps Rusty with jury-rigged weapons and launches a one-woman war against the oil baron's empire, discovering along the way that Rusty carries a ghost in its core — a previous pilot whose consciousness was downloaded into the machine.

## Core Cast

- **Scout March** — a woman in her late twenties, a field mechanic for a small independent drilling co-op. Calloused hands, wary eyes, a stubborn streak wide enough to drive a truck through. She treats her walker like a partner, not a tool.
- **Rusty** (formally RX-7 "Rust Maiden") — a repurposed light utility walker, originally built by Hargrave Industries but salvaged by Scout. It is scored with rust, patched with scavenged plates, and carries the downloaded consciousness of its first pilot: a woman named Lena Voss.
- **Baron Hargrave** — the oil baron of the Western Interior Basin. Old money, new machines, no mercy. He believes the land belongs to those with the strength to take it and keep it.
- **Lena Voss** (ghost within Rusty) — the original pilot of the RX-7, who died in a test flight but whose neural map was preserved in the machine's core. She speaks to Scout through the walker's interface, a voice of static and memory.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part One — The Grease and the Gears (1-12)

1. **The Walkers at Dawn** — Scout March wakes before sunrise and walks the field where the co-op's walkers stand silent in the mist.
2. **Rusty's Circuits** — Scout runs diagnostics on Rusty's worn actuator arm and hears a voice in the static that should not be there.
3. **The Last Independent Well** — The co-op's foreman, Del, explains that Hargrave's buyout offer expires at noon.
4. **Hargrave's Man** — A sleek corporate crawler rolls into camp carrying a lawyer and a deadline; Scout tells him where to put the contract.
5. **The Co-op Vote** — The drillers vote to refuse the buyout, knowing what it means.
6. **The Warning** — A neighbor's homestead burns on the horizon; Hargrave's harvesters are moving.
7. **Iron on the Horizon** — Scout sees the Iron Judge for the first time, a walking fortress the size of a grain elevator.
8. **Rusty Speaks** — Lena Voss introduces herself through Rusty's speakers in a crackle of corrupted code.
9. **What Lena Remembers** — Lena tells Scout she was Hargrave's first test pilot and he killed her to protect the project.
10. **The Massacre at Red Mesa** — Hargrave's men attack the neighboring settlement; Scout arrives too late to save them.
11. **The Burning Fields** — The co-op's oil well is torched; Scout escapes with Rusty and a bag of tools.
12. **The Oath** — Scout swears she will make Hargrave pay; Lena's voice crackles agreement.

### Part Two — The Walker's War (13-25)

13. **Into the Badlands** — Scout and Rusty flee into the alkali flats, scavenging for parts and fuel.
14. **The Ghost Town** — They find shelter in an abandoned refinery town where Hargrave's ex-employees live in hiding.
15. **The Armorer** — A reclusive walker mechanic named Vega agrees to help Scout arm Rusty.
16. **Rusty's Refit** — Vega discovers the Lena-core in Rusty and warns Scout that the technology is dangerous.
17. **The First Raid** — Scout ambushes a Hargrave supply convoy and takes fuel and ammunition.
18. **The Broadcast** — Hargrave addresses the territories on the radio, threatening collective punishment for the raid.
19. **Vega's Story** — Vega was Hargrave's chief weapons designer before he fled; he knows where the bodies are buried.
20. **The Price of Parts** — Scout trades her grandfather's watch for a salvaged cannon mount.
21. **Lena's Deterioration** — Lena's code is fragmenting; she has weeks before her consciousness dissolves.
22. **The Recruitment** — Scout finds other survivors of Hargrave's brutality who want to fight.
23. **The Plan** — Vega reveals a weakness in the Iron Judge's coolant system; a single shot could cripple it.
24. **The Deserter** — A Hargrave pilot defects and brings intelligence about the Judge's patrol schedule.
25. **The March Begins** — Scout leads her small force toward the Hargrave refinery at Bitter Springs.

### Part Three — The Iron Judge (26-38)

26. **The Refinery at Dawn** — Scout scouts the Bitter Springs refinery and sees the Iron Judge docked at its charging station.
27. **The Diversion** — Vega triggers explosions at the fuel depot; Hargrave's forces scramble.
28. **Rusty vs. the Sentries** — Scout engages the perimeter walkers in a running firefight across the tank farm.
29. **Lena Takes Control** — Lena's ghost seizes Rusty's systems and moves with speed Scout cannot match.
30. **The Core Chamber** — Scout penetrates the refinery's central control building to disable the Judge's charging tether.
31. **Hargrave's Response** — Baron Hargrave personally boards the Iron Judge and powers it up.
32. **The Awakening** — The Iron Judge rises, steam venting from its joints, and steps through the refinery wall.
33. **Running the Gauntlet** — Scout pilots Rusty through the Judge's firing path, buying time for the others to escape.
34. **Lena's Sacrifice** — Lena overloads Rusty's core to create an EMP that staggers the Judge.
35. **The Fall** — The Iron Judge topples, crushing a section of the refinery, but Hargrave ejects before impact.
36. **The Aftermath** — Scout searches the wreckage for Hargrave and finds only his empty command chair.
37. **Rusty, Silent** — Lena's voice is gone; Rusty's systems are dark and cold.
38. **The Desolation** — Scout sits in the silent cockpit, alone for the first time in weeks.

### Part Four — The Reckoning (39-50)

39. **The Spark** — Scout refuses to abandon Rusty; she hand-cranks the reactor and Lena flickers back, barely.
40. **Hargrave's Last Card** — Hargrave retreats to his fortified mansion at Promontory Peak with his remaining loyalists.
41. **The March to Promontory** — Scout's band marches on the Hargrave estate, gathering defectors along the way.
42. **The Siege** — The battle for Promontory Peak begins; Hargrave's defenses are formidable.
43. **Lena's Final Words** — Lena tells Scout that she was Hargrave's daughter, hidden and denied.
44. **The Betrayal from Within** — Hargrave's second-in-command turns on him, opening the eastern gate.
45. **Scout vs. Hargrave** — Scout confronts Hargrave in his study, Rusty's cannon aimed at his chest.
46. **The Choice** — Hargrave offers Scout control of the company; she refuses and calls for the authorities.
47. **The Arrest** — Federal marshals arriving by gyrocopter take Hargrave into custody for war crimes.
48. **Lena's Goodbye** — Lena's consciousness dissolves into the static, but not before she thanks Scout for freeing her.
49. **The Future** — Scout takes control of the Hargrave refineries and begins redistributing oil rights to the territories.
50. **Rusty at Rest** — Scout parks Rusty on a ridge overlooking the plains, its work done, and walks home alone.
