# The Ash Garden — Outline

## Premise

In December 1944, Rosa Tanaka returns to her family's California farm after two years in an internment camp. She finds the house occupied by Henry Calloway, a white war veteran who was hired by the county to maintain the property under the Alien Land Laws. With her parents dead and her brother fighting in Europe, Rosa must navigate a legal battle for her own home — represented reluctantly by Judge Morrison, a local jurist who signed the eviction notices during the war — while confronting the stranger who has made a life in her absence.

## Core Cast

- **Rosa Tanaka** — 28, a Japanese-American woman, former schoolteacher, returned from the Gila River camp
- **Henry Calloway** — 35, a disabled Army veteran, wounded at Anzio, now farming the Tanaka land under a county lease
- **Judge Morrison** — 62, a superior court judge who administered property seizures in 1942, now struggling with conscience

## Chapter Plan

1. Rosa steps off the Greyhound bus in Bakersfield and sees the valley for the first time in two years.
2. She walks the two miles to the farm road and finds the gate chained shut.
3. A white man comes out of her house with a shotgun and tells her to leave.
4. Rosa goes to the county courthouse to file a claim and meets Judge Morrison.
5. Morrison recognizes her name and assigns himself to her case.
6. Rosa rents a room at Mrs. Pendelton's boarding house and lies awake.
7. Henry Calloway reads Rosa's letter of ownership and feels dread.
8. Morrison reviews the wartime property records and finds irregularities.
9. Rosa walks the perimeter of her farm through the fence line.
10. Henry brings Rosa a basket of tomatoes from her own garden.
11. Rosa refuses the gift and tells Henry she will take him to court.
12. Morrison interviews the county clerk who processed the Tanaka file.
13. Rosa visits her parents' graves at the edge of town.
14. Henry writes a letter to his sister in Nebraska about the situation.
15. The court date is set for late January 1945.
16. Rosa finds old photographs hidden in the boarding house wall.
17. Henry's nightmares about Anzio keep him from sleeping.
18. Morrison discovers his signature authorized the Tanaka seizure.
19. Rosa's brother Kenji writes from France that he will come home.
20. Henry repairs the front porch steps Rosa's father built.
21. The town newspaper runs a story about the Tanaka property dispute.
22. Rosa receives anonymous threats slipped under her door.
23. Morrison visits Rosa at the boarding house to warn her.
24. Henry tells his farmhand Miguel the whole story.
25. Rosa walks through the Bakersfield market and feels eyes on her.
26. Morrison finds the original deed in a misfiled cabinet.
27. Henry learns that Rosa's father fought for the US in the Great War.
28. Rosa dreams of the garden the way it used to be.
29. The first hearing is held in a crowded courtroom.
30. Henry testifies that he did not know the house was claimed.
31. Rosa testifies about the day the FBI came for her father.
32. Morrison rules in favor of Rosa's ownership claim.
33. Henry is given thirty days to vacate the property.
34. Rosa walks into the empty kitchen and smells Henry's cooking.
35. Henry sleeps in the barn and refuses to leave the land.
36. Morrison receives angry letters from townspeople.
37. Rosa finds Henry's medals in the drawer of her mother's dresser.
38. Henry admits he has nowhere else to go.
39. Rosa's brother Kenji arrives in Bakersfield with his Army discharge.
40. Kenji confronts Henry in the yard with a shovel.
41. Rosa stops the fight and tells Kenji the house is not worth blood.
42. Kenji explains what happened to their cousin in the camp.
43. Henry offers to work the orchard in exchange for a lease.
44. Rosa considers the offer while sitting in the ash-covered garden.
45. Morrison announces he will retire from the bench.
46. Rosa tells Henry he can stay until the harvest.
47. Henry and Rosa clear the ash from the garden beds together.
48. A letter arrives from the War Department about Kenji's deployment record.
49. Rosa decides to return to teaching at the local school.
50. Spring comes to the valley. Rosa plants the garden again.
