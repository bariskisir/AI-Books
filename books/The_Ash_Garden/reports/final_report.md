# The Ash Garden — Final Report

## Summary

The Ash Garden is a literary fiction novel set in California's Central Valley in 1944-1945. It tells the story of Rosa Tanaka, a Japanese-American woman who returns to her family farm after two years in the Gila River internment camp, only to find it occupied by Henry Calloway, a disabled white war veteran granted a county lease under the Alien Land Laws.

The novel follows Rosa's legal battle to reclaim her home, her uneasy relationship with the man living in it, and the moral reckoning of Judge Morrison, who signed the original seizure orders. The story explores themes of home, justice, displacement, and the quiet work of rebuilding.
