# The Last Silk Dress — Outline

## Premise

New York City, 1895. Evelyn Chase, a gifted seamstress stitching gowns for Caroline Astor's inner circle, leads a double life. By day she measures hems and pins silk for the Four Hundred; by night she runs a secret network helping women escape abusive marriages. When Annie, a young bride fleeing a violent industrialist husband, arrives at Evelyn's workroom door, the two women must navigate the treacherous lines between New York's glittering ballrooms and its shadowy backstreets. As Evelyn's underground railroad grows, Mrs. Astor's patronage becomes both a shield and a trap — and a single torn silk dress may unravel everything.

## Core Cast

- **Evelyn Chase** — A seamstress in her early thirties who inherited her mother's sewing skills and a hidden network of safe houses. Sharp-eyed, quiet, fiercely independent.
- **Mrs. Caroline Astor** — The self-appointed queen of New York society, who controls guest lists and reputations. Her patronage of Evelyn is genuine but conditional.
- **Annie Fletcher** — A twenty-three-year-old woman who escapes her abusive husband, Edward Fletcher, a textile magnate. She becomes Evelyn's assistant and accomplice.
- **Edward Fletcher** — Annie's husband. A wealthy industrialist who beats his wife and will stop at nothing to get her back.
- **Maeve O'Donnell** — An Irish laundress who runs a boarding house that serves as the first stop on Evelyn's network.
- **Mr. Alistair Vance** — Mrs. Astor's personal secretary, who suspects Evelyn's secret activities.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Workroom (Chapters 1-10)

1. Evelyn Chase pins a hem for Mrs. Vanderbilt while a bruise blooms beneath her sleeve — a reminder of her own past.
2. Annie Fletcher appears at the back door of the workroom, clutching a carpetbag and a split lip.
3. Evelyn hides Annie in the storage room and lies to a delivery boy asking questions.
4. Annie tells Evelyn her full story: Edward Fletcher, the beatings, the locked bedroom door.
5. Evelyn takes Annie to Maeve O'Donnell's boarding house under cover of darkness.
6. Mrs. Astor arrives for a fitting and discusses the upcoming Patriarchs' Ball.
7. Evelyn learns that Edward Fletcher has hired a private detective to find Annie.
8. Annie begins work as Evelyn's assistant, learning the trade.
9. A close call when a man claiming to be a fabric salesman comes asking about new girls.
10. Evelyn teaches Annie the secret signal — a white ribbon on the workroom door.

### Part II: The Ballrooms (Chapters 11-20)

11. Mrs. Astor commissions Evelyn to make her gown for the Patriarchs' Ball.
12. Evelyn uses the ball as cover to meet a contact who can forge travel documents.
13. Annie's confidence grows as she learns to stitch, but she misses her younger sister still in Edward's house.
14. Mr. Vance makes an unexpected visit to inspect the ball gown's progress, and his eyes linger too long.
15. Evelyn receives word that a woman in Boston needs extraction — a judge's wife.
16. Annie insists on helping with the Boston extraction; Evelyn reluctantly agrees.
17. The Boston trip succeeds, but a porter recognizes Annie from a wanted poster.
18. Evelyn burns the wanted poster and realizes the network is growing too visible.
19. Mrs. Astor throws a dinner where Evelyn must serve as both seamstress and silent messenger.
20. Edward Fletcher confronts Evelyn in her workroom, demanding to know Annie's whereabouts.

### Part III: The Silk (Chapters 21-30)

21. Evelyn holds her ground against Fletcher, but her hands shake after he leaves.
22. Mrs. Astor's ball gown is nearly finished — deep burgundy silk with pearl embroidery.
23. Annie discovers that Edward is planning to remarry, which would void any claims she has.
24. Evelyn arranges for a lawyer to meet Annie at Maeve's boarding house.
25. The lawyer explains the cruel truth: a married woman has no legal rights to her own body or property.
26. Annie decides she wants to fight, not just hide. She wants a divorce — nearly impossible in 1895.
27. Evelyn reaches into her past and contacts a sympathetic judge who owes her mother a debt.
28. The judge agrees to hear Annie's petition in chambers, but it must remain absolutely secret.
29. Mr. Vance finds a note with Maeve's address in Evelyn's workroom.
30. Evelyn must choose between moving Annie immediately or trusting Vance's silence.

### Part IV: The Reveal (Chapters 31-40)

31. Evelyn chooses to move Annie to a new safe house in Brooklyn.
32. Mrs. Astor notices Evelyn's distraction during the final fitting.
33. The Patriarchs' Ball arrives. Evelyn attends as Mrs. Astor's guest to adjust the gown.
34. At the ball, Evelyn spots Edward Fletcher speaking with Mr. Vance at the bar.
35. A maid passes Evelyn a note: Annie has been discovered. The Brooklyn house is compromised.
36. Evelyn delivers a message through a kitchen girl, activating the network's emergency protocol.
37. Mrs. Astor's gown tears during the waltz — a deliberate act by Evelyn to create a diversion.
38. In the chaos, Annie escapes through the back of a laundry wagon.
39. Mr. Vance confronts Evelyn the next morning: he knows everything but, surprisingly, will not expose her.
40. Vance reveals that his own sister disappeared years ago. He understands.

### Part V: The Reckoning (Chapters 41-50)

41. Evelyn moves Annie to a farm upstate, the last stop on the network before Canada.
42. Mrs. Astor summons Evelyn. The torn dress has created whispers. She demands the truth.
43. Evelyn tells Mrs. Astor everything. The silence stretches like thread.
44. Mrs. Astor reveals she knew about the network for months. She chose not to interfere.
45. Edward Fletcher is arrested for assault after a housemaid testifies against him.
46. Annie's divorce is granted in secret chambers. She is free.
47. Evelyn finishes a new dress for Mrs. Astor — a peace offering in pale gold.
48. Annie writes from Canada: she has opened her own dress shop in Toronto.
49. Evelyn receives a new visitor at the workroom door, a woman with a black eye.
50. Evelyn ties a white ribbon on the door and gets back to work.
