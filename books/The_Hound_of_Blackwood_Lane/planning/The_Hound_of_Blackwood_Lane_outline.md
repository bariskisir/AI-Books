# The Hound of Blackwood Lane — Outline

## Premise

Mina Hart, a retired librarian who now runs a small bookshop in the village of Blackwood Lane, finds herself drawn into a mystery when an exquisite antique map of the English countryside — the centerpiece of the annual village fete's exhibition — vanishes during the afternoon's festivities. With the local constabulary stumped and the map's owner, reclusive collector Edmund Thorne, growing increasingly agitated, Mina begins her own quiet investigation, uncovering a tangle of rival collectors, old village grudges, and a connection to a decades-old house fire that some villagers would rather forget.

## Core Cast

- **Mina Hart** — Retired librarian, owner of "The Turning Page" bookshop. Sharp-eyed, soft-spoken, relentless when curious.
- **Inspector Arthur Graves** — Local police inspector. Thorough, skeptical of civilians, but gradually comes to respect Mina's instincts.
- **Edmund Thorne** — Retired antiquities dealer and map collector. The victim. Prickly, precise, hiding something.
- **Constable Daisy Finch** — Young police constable, eager, friendly. Mina's quiet ally in the station.
- **Leo Croft** — Owner of the village pub, The Crooked Spire. Knows everyone's secrets.
- **Sylvia Pender** — Fete organizer, busybody, sharp tongue. Runs the village historical society.
- **Frank Boswell** — Thorne's former business partner. Bitter, broke, nursing a decades-old grudge.
- **Rose Winter** — Village seamstress. Quiet, watches everything. Knew the house that burned.
- **Peter Ashby** — Young antiques dealer from the next town. Ambitions, charm hides a ruthless streak.
- **Margaret Hound** — Owner of Hound Manor, the grand estate. Elderly, forgetful, says more than she means to.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part One: The Fete

1. Setting up for the fete on the village green — Mina arranges her bookstall and watches Edmund Thorne install the map in the exhibition tent.
2. The fete opens. Mina observes the villagers — collectors circle the map display with hungry eyes.
3. The map vanishes during the cake-judging commotion. Panic spreads through the tent.
4. Inspector Graves arrives. He questions everyone, gets nowhere. Mina's attention catches on a detail.
5. Mina examines the glass case. The lock was picked — professionally. She notes what Graves missed: a faint scent of bergamot.
6. Edmund Thorne gives a tense interview. He reveals the map was worth more than he let on. Someone knew its true value.
7. Mina visits Constable Finch at the station. She learns the fingerprint powder found nothing — gloves were worn.
8. Sylvia Pender insists on continuing the fete. Mina watches the crowd, noting who left early.
9. Mina finds a scrap of paper near the tent — a partial address. She keeps it to herself.
10. The fete ends. Mina walks home through Blackwood Lane, replaying the afternoon in her mind.

### Part Two: The Collector

11. Mina visits Thorne's cottage. He is evasive about the map's provenance. She leaves unsettled.
12. At her bookshop, Mina researches antique maps. She discovers the map might be a Portolan chart worth a fortune.
13. Leo Croft at the pub tells Mina that Thorne acquired the map five years ago from an estate sale. The estate: Hound Manor.
14. Mina calls on Margaret Hound. The elderly woman is vague but mentions a "fuss" about a map years ago.
15. Margaret mentions her late husband had a falling out with someone over "a piece of paper." She cannot recall who.
16. Mina learns from Daisy Finch that Frank Boswell was seen near the fete grounds before the theft.
17. Mina interviews Frank Boswell. He is angry, defensive. He admits he wanted the map — but claims he was not the thief.
18. Frank reveals that Thorne cheated him out of a partnership years ago. The map was part of the settlement.
19. Mina returns to Thorne. He admits the map was from Hound Manor but says he bought it legally.
20. Mina notes that Thorne's story about acquiring the map has small inconsistencies. She begins a list.

### Part Three: The Map

21. Mina visits the county archives to research Hound Manor. She discovers a house fire in 1987 that destroyed the east wing.
22. A painting survived the fire — it shows the map hanging on a wall in the manor's study.
23. Rose Winter is in the archives, looking at old photographs. She leaves hurriedly when she sees Mina.
24. Mina finds Rose at her shop. Rose is uneasy but mentions she worked at Hound Manor as a maid in the 1980s.
25. Rose reveals that the map was given to the manor's groundsman, a man named Albert Cole, who disappeared after the fire.
26. Mina searches for Albert Cole. He died in 2005 — but there is no record of a map in his estate.
27. Peter Ashby appears at Mina's shop, asking questions about the map. His interest is a little too eager.
28. Mina checks Peter's background. He has a reputation for aggressive collecting, and two lawsuits over disputed acquisitions.
29. A break-in at Thorne's cottage — someone ransacked the study. Nothing taken. A message: stop looking.
30. Graves warns Mina to stay out of the investigation. She agrees — and continues anyway.

### Part Four: The Hound

31. Daisy Finch shares that the partial address Mina found matches a derelict cottage on the Hound Manor estate.
32. Mina explores the cottage. It has been lived in recently — cigarette butts, a sleeping bag, bergamot-scented hand soap.
33. Mina realizes the scent from the theft scene matches the soap. The thief was staying here.
34. She finds a corner of an old map tucked behind a loose floorboard. The paper matches the description of the missing Portolan.
35. Mina pieces it together: someone with access to the estate took the map — or took a copy — and has been hiding out here.
36. She confronts Margaret Hound. Margaret breaks down and admits her son Simon did not die in the fire — he ran away.
37. Margaret believed Simon returned recently. She has been leaving food at the cottage.
38. Mina finds Simon Hound in the village, watching the bookshop. He is thin, nervous, smells of bergamot.
39. Simon tells his story: he fled after a quarrel with his father over the map. His father threatened to burn it. Simon took it instead.
40. Simon claims he did not steal the map at the fete. He came back to Blackwood to see his mother, not to steal.

### Part Five: The Truth

41. Mina believes Simon. She re-examines the crime: who else knew the map would be there? Who had the skill to pick the lock?
42. Mina realizes Peter Ashby arrived at the fete early — before it officially opened. Sylvia Pender let him in.
43. Mina questions Sylvia. The fete organizer admits Peter paid her to let him in early for a "private viewing."
44. Graves brings Peter in for questioning. Peter's alibi crumbles. His fingerprints are found on the glass case frame.
45. Graves searches Peter's shop and finds the map, already partially cut — Peter planned to sell it in sections.
46. Peter confesses. He learned the map's worth, knew Thorne would display it at the fete, and planned the theft for months.
47. The map is recovered. Thorne is relieved but embarrassed by the investigation's revelations about his past.
48. Frank Boswell is cleared. Simon Hound is reconciled with his mother. Rose Winter's secrets stay buried.
49. Mina writes the story for the village newsletter, omitting the sensitive details. Justice done quietly.
50. Autumn settles on Blackwood Lane. Mina locks the bookshop and walks home, content that the village is at peace again.
