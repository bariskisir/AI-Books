# The Last Taxi — Outline

## Premise

Kemal drives a worn-out yellow taxi through the streets of Istanbul, night after night. His passengers are strangers — until one evening he picks up a young girl who speaks of memories she could not possibly have. Then a soldier. Then an old man. Each shares the same face, the same tired eyes, the same odd details. They are all the same person at different stages of life, and the city itself is the thread that binds them. Through a long night of fares across the Bosphorus, Kemal begins to understand that time in Istanbul does not run in a straight line — it loops, bends, and doubles back on itself. Each passenger carries a fragment of a life that has not yet finished, and Kemal must help them complete the circuit before the last taxi stops running.

## Core Cast

- **Kemal** — A taxi driver in his late forties. Divorced. Drives the night shift through Istanbul. Quiet, observant, carries a grief he does not discuss. His father was also a taxi driver on these same streets.
- **The Girl** — The first passenger. Twelve years old, or perhaps younger. Lost. She speaks of a future she should not know and carries a toy boat her father gave her — before she was born.
- **The Soldier** — The second passenger. Young man in his late twenties, in uniform. Returning from or going to something unspeakable. He carries a letter he cannot bring himself to read.
- **The Old Man** — The third passenger. Nearing eighty. He knows exactly who Kemal is and why he is here. He holds the key to the loop.
- **The Woman** — The fourth passenger. Middle-aged, elegant, sad. She is the version of the person who stayed in Istanbul while everyone else left.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Kemal starts his night shift at the taxi stand in Taksim Square, engine idling, city lights flickering.
2. A girl in a school uniform climbs into the back seat, gives an address in Üsküdar she cannot afford.
3. The Girl tells Kemal about the ferry she took across the Bosphorus that morning — but the ferry line ended years ago.
4. Kemal notices her toy boat, a hand-carved wooden thing with the word "Levent" painted on its hull.
5. The Girl asks Kemal if he remembers the earthquake. He does not. She describes it in precise detail.
6. Kemal drops The Girl at a street that does not exist on his map — an old cobblestone lane named Çınar Sokak.
7. A young soldier flags him down near the Galata Tower, uniform crumpled, eyes hollow.
8. The Soldier asks to be taken to the Selimiye Barracks in Kadıköy — a building that was decommissioned decades ago.
9. The Soldier shows Kemal a photograph of a woman; it is The Girl, twenty years older.
10. They drive across the Bosphorus Bridge. The Soldier says he can see the city burning. Kemal sees nothing.
11. The Soldier confesses he is deserting — or returning from a war that has not been declared yet.
12. Kemal realizes the Soldier has the same scar behind his left ear that The Girl had.
13. The Soldier asks Kemal to read the letter he carries. Kemal refuses. The Soldier reads it aloud: it is from his future daughter.
14. The Soldier gets out at the barracks gate, which flickers between ruin and repair as he passes through.
15. Kemal parks at a seaside çay bahçesi, shaken, and drinks tea. The night is only half over.
16. An old man in a wool coat, despite summer heat, opens the taxi door and sits in the front passenger seat.
17. The Old Man addresses Kemal by name, tells him his father drove the same taxi with the same license plate in 1978.
18. The Old Man says he has been waiting for this ride for sixty years.
19. He directs Kemal through back streets of Fatih that Kemal has never seen — but the Old Man knows every turn.
20. The Old Man tells the story of a boy who carved a toy boat for his daughter and lost it in the current.
21. Kemal recognizes the toy boat from The Girl's hands — "Levent" was the boy's name.
22. The Old Man reveals he is Levent, and the girl in the car was his daughter — from a future he has already lived.
23. They stop at a mosque courtyard. The Old Man points to a date carved in the stone: 2027. It is 2026.
24. The Old Man speaks of a loop: a night when all the selves meet, and one of them must choose to break the circle.
25. Kemal asks what happens if the circle is not broken. The Old Man says nothing — he simply vanishes from the passenger seat.
26. The taxi clock reads 3:17 AM. It has read 3:17 AM for hours.
27. A woman in a dark coat stands under a streetlamp on İstiklal Avenue, hailing him with a hand that trembles.
28. The Woman gets in and gives an address in Beşiktaş — Kemal's own apartment building.
29. She knows his daughter's name, his ex-wife's name, the spot where his father collapsed at the wheel.
30. The Woman is Levent's daughter grown up. She remembers meeting Kemal when she was twelve.
31. She tells him the story from the other side: she saw her father across a crowd once, young and in uniform, and he did not recognize her.
32. Kemal drives in silence. The Woman cries quietly in the back seat.
33. She tells Kemal that her father — the Old Man — died alone in a hospital in 2043. She was not there.
34. Kemal asks why she is in his taxi tonight. She says: because you will remember us when no one else does.
35. They arrive at Kemal's building. He does not get out. She leaves a small wooden boat on the passenger seat.
36. Kemal drives back toward Taksim. The streets are empty. The city is holding its breath.
37. He picks up a fare: a boy, no older than eight, sitting alone on a curb in Karaköy.
38. The Boy is Levent as a child. He has run away from home. He is the version before the soldier, before the old man.
39. The Boy asks Kemal if he knows his father. Kemal says yes. The Boy does not believe him.
40. Kemal buys the boy a simit from an all-night bakery. They share it in silence.
41. The Boy shows Kemal a scar on his hand — the same scar the Soldier had, the same scar The Girl had.
42. Kemal tells the Boy about the boat he will one day carve. The Boy does not understand.
43. The Boy asks to be taken home. He gives an address in Üsküdar — the same address The Girl gave hours ago.
44. They drive through the dawn light. The city is waking. The Bosphorus is blue and gold.
45. At the address, a young woman answers the door — The Girl, but grown up, with no memory of the taxi ride.
46. She does not recognize Kemal. The Boy runs to her. She is his mother.
47. Kemal understands: the loop was not about Levent. It was about the woman — mother and daughter and wife across all times.
48. He returns to the taxi. The toy boat is still on the passenger seat. He places it on his dashboard.
49. Kemal drives to the ferry terminal and takes the first crossing of the day. The water is calm.
50. He picks up the evening paper. The date is the same as yesterday. The night will begin again.
