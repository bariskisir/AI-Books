# The Long Count — Outline

## Premise

Ray "The Hammer" Mason was a contender once, a decade ago, before the losses piled up and the booze blurred the years. Now pushing forty in 1970s Detroit, he works an assembly line and sleeps in a walk-up apartment that smells like the previous tenant. When his old trainer Sal surfaces with an opportunity — a ranked opponent needs a replacement, and the winner gets a shot at the undefeated champion — Ray steps back into the gym. But the body forgets faster than the mind, and the fight outside the ring — with family, with pride, with the simple question of what a man is worth — demands more than any opponent ever did.

## Core Cast

- **Ray "The Hammer" Mason** — A former middleweight contender, now 38, working at a Detroit auto plant. Still has the reach, the chin, and the right hand. Lost the feet, the reflexes, and most of his dignity.
- **Sal** — Ray's lifelong trainer, older now, carrying his own regrets. Runs a crumbling gym in the shadow of the Lodge Freeway. Believes in Ray more than Ray believes in himself.
- **The Champ (Tony "The Tiger" DeMarco)** — Undefeated middleweight champion, 27, at the peak of his powers. Class in public, killer in the ring. Respects Ray enough to give him the shot.
- **Doreen Mason** — Ray's ex-wife. She still carries the anger and the love in equal measure. Works as a nurse at Detroit Receiving.
- **Lena Mason** — Ray's seventeen-year-old daughter, refuses to see him fight. Has her father's pride and her mother's good sense.
- **Mickey DeLuca** — The promoter, a shark in a sharkskin suit.
- **Junior Reeves** — The young sparring partner Sal brings in. Fast, hungry, everything Ray used to be.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Ray wakes before dawn, hands swollen, the mirror showing a man he does not recognize.
2. The assembly line at the plant — grease, noise, the slow death of repetition.
3. Sal finds Ray at the bar and tells him about the opportunity.
4. Ray walks into Sal's gym for the first time in years — the smell of old sweat and liniment.
5. Flashback: Ray's first professional fight, 1962, a knockout in the second round.
6. Ray agrees to train but his body rebels against every demand.
7. The contract arrives — one tune-up fight, a win earns a shot at the champion.
8. Ray runs the Detroit streets at dawn, the city rising around him.
9. Sal puts Ray through mitt work; the timing is gone, the frustration builds.
10. Junior Reeves, the sparring partner, shows Ray what speed looks like.
11. Doreen appears at the gym — she heard the news, she wants Ray to stay away from their daughter.
12. Flashback: The fight that ended Ray's first run, a split decision loss he still argues about.
13. The tune-up fight — Ray wins ugly, surviving a cut over his eye.
14. The fight doctor clears Ray but warns him about his vision.
15. Media day — the Champ is respectful, Ray feels like a museum piece.
16. Lena refuses to take Ray's call; Doreen tells him to leave her alone.
17. Sal confesses he needs this as much as Ray does — a last chance for both of them.
18. The weigh-in — Ray makes weight, stands across from the Champ for the first time.
19. The night before the fight, Ray walks the empty arena and feels the ghosts.
20. Ray sits in his hotel room and thinks about what he is fighting for.
21. Fight night — the walk to the ring, the roar of the crowd.
22. Round one: The Champ tests Ray's chin with a right hand.
23. Between rounds, Sal works on Ray's cut, tells him to use the jab.
24. Round two: Ray finds his range, lands a clean combination.
25. Round three: The Champ adjusts and pounds Ray's body.
26. Round four: Ray hurts the Champ with a right uppercut.
27. Round five: Both men tire, the clinch becomes a weapon.
28. Round six: The Champ's corner makes adjustments, he starts circling away from Ray's right hand.
29. Round seven: Ray's left eye swells shut, he fights blind on one side.
30. Round eight: The Champ swarms, Ray covers up, survives.
31. Round nine: Ray digs into the deep well and lands a three-punch combination.
32. Round ten: The championship rounds — the Champs legs are gone too.
33. Round eleven: Both men exhausted, fighting on memory and breath.
34. Round twelve: The final three minutes, they empty everything.
35. The decision — the judges score it for the Champ, but Ray went the distance.
36. The locker room — Sal tells Ray he is proud, Ray cries for the first time in years.
37. Doreen pushes through the crowd and holds Ray's face in her hands.
38. The hospital — stitches above the eye, cracked ribs, a CAT scan that shows nothing new.
39. Mickey DeLuca offers Ray a rematch and a payday.
40. Ray turns it down — the first clear-headed decision of his adult life.
41. Sal and Ray share a bottle at the gym, talk about what comes next.
42. Ray goes to Lena's school, waits until she agrees to talk.
43. Ray quits the plant, puts everything into an old storefront he plans to turn into a gym.
44. Flashback: The first time Ray put on boxing gloves, age twelve, in a rec center basement.
45. Ray meets a kid from the neighborhood, starts teaching him the basics.
46. The Champ loses his title, calls Ray to say he respected the fight.
47. Ray's gym opens — the community shows up, Doreen and Lena too.
48. Ray watches his first protégé fight from the corner, shifts on the ropes just like he used to.
49. Ray realizes the long count was never in the ring — it was the years he spent not living.
50. Ray locks up the gym at night, checks himself in the mirror, and smiles.
