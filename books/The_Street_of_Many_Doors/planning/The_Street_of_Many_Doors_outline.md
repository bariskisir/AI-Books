# The Street of Many Doors — Outline

## Premise

Elena walks the same cobblestone street in Prague every day for a year. Each day she pauses at a different door — a blue door, a green door, a door with a brass lion's head, a door half-hidden by ivy — and when she opens it she steps into a different version of her life. She is a pianist in one, a widow in another, a mother of three in a third, a woman who never left her hometown. Some lives last minutes. Some last decades. All feel as real as the one she left behind. Through this labyrinth of possibility, Elena must discover if there is a true self beneath the choices, or if every self is just a door we walked through.

## Core Cast

- **Elena** — A woman in her early thirties, adrift after a loss she cannot name. She is a translator by trade, a wanderer by nature. Each door shows her a version of herself she might have been.
- **The Map Seller** — An old man who tends a tiny shop at the end of the street. He sells maps of places that do not exist. He seems to know what Elena is doing, and why.
- **The Man at the Cafe** — A quiet presence who sits at the same outdoor table every afternoon, reading a newspaper he never turns. Elena notices him more each time she passes.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: First Doors (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Street** — Elena arrives in Prague and finds the street. She meets the Map Seller for the first time.
2. **The Blue Door** — She opens the first door and finds herself in a life where she stayed with her ex-fiancé.
3. **The Green Door** — A life where she became a concert pianist. The applause sounds like rain.
4. **The Red Door** — She is a mother of twin girls. She feels their small hands in hers.
5. **The Black Door** — A widow's life. She visits a grave and cannot read the name.
6. **The Yellow Door** — She owns a bookstore in a small town. The shelves smell like vanilla.
7. **The Iron Door** — A life of wealth and isolation. A penthouse with no one to talk to.
8. **The Glass Door** — She is a scientist studying migratory birds. She watches them leave.
9. **The Worn Door** — Poverty and struggle, but also warmth and community.
10. **The Map Seller's Offer** — The Map Seller offers her a map of the street. She accepts.

### Part II: Love and Its Shadows (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Cafe Door** — She enters a cafe and meets the Man at the Cafe for the first time in a life.
12. **The Wedding Door** — She marries a man she barely knows. The ceremony is in Czech.
13. **The Nursery Door** — A child with her eyes. She watches him take his first step.
14. **The Empty Door** — A life where she chose solitude. She is content but alone.
15. **The Garden Door** — She tends a garden with an older woman who might be her mother.
16. **The Broken Door** — A love affair that ended badly. She feels the bruise of it.
17. **The Lantern Door** — She runs a small restaurant. The kitchen is warm and loud.
18. **The Silent Door** — Her partner does not speak. They communicate through touch alone.
19. **The Forgiveness Door** — She asks for forgiveness from someone she hurt long ago.
20. **The Door of Years** — She lives an entire marriage in an afternoon. Every fight, every kiss.

### Part III: Ambition and Art (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Studio Door** — She is a painter in a garret. Her hands are stained with ochre.
22. **The Stage Door** — She performs for an empty theatre. One man claps.
23. **The Laboratory Door** — A scientist who discovered something she cannot share.
24. **The Embassy Door** — A diplomat's life. Formal dinners and careful words.
25. **The Library Door** — She is a professor of forgotten languages.
26. **The Architect's Door** — She designs buildings that no one builds.
27. **The Writer's Door** — Her own novels line the shelves. She does not recognize them.
28. **The Healer's Door** — A midwife delivering babies in a village without electricity.
29. **The Teacher's Door** — She teaches children who do not speak her language.
30. **The Empty Stage** — She gives up art entirely. The silence is its own kind of music.

### Part IV: The Price of Choice (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Locked Door** — She cannot open it. The Map Seller watches.
32. **The Flood Door** — A life undone by water. She learns to swim too late.
33. **The War Door** — Prague under occupation. She hides a stranger in her attic.
34. **The Hospital Door** — She is a patient, not a visitor. The ceiling tiles are white.
35. **The Door of Bones** — A life built on a lie. She confronts her own deceit.
36. **The Falling Door** — She loses everything. The street looks different from below.
37. **The Thief's Door** — She steals something precious and is caught.
38. **The Refugee Door** — She crosses a border with nothing but a photograph.
39. **The Witness Door** — She sees something she should not have seen.
40. **The Return Door** — She finds her way back to the original street, changed.

### Part V: The Street Itself (Chapters 41-50)

41. **Morning Light** — She begins to recognize patterns. The doors rearrange themselves at night.
42. **The Map Seller's Story** — He tells her about the street's origin. He built it.
43. **The In-Between** — She walks the street without opening a door for the first time.
44. **The Man at the Cafe Speaks** — He has been waiting for her. He knows her from every life.
45. **The Door She Built** — She finds a door that was not there before. It bears her name.
46. **Through Her Own Door** — She enters the life she has been building, not choosing.
47. **The Garden Behind Everything** — A green space behind all the doors. The Man waits there.
48. **Winter** — The street changes with the season. Some doors freeze shut.
49. **Spring** — New doors appear. Old ones vanish.
50. **The Street** — Elena makes her final choice. She does not choose a life — she chooses to live.
