# The Clockmaker's War — Outline

## Premise

In the iron-grey city of Gearwright, every soldier is wound like a clockwork automaton. The Winder — a shadowy arm of the Imperium — implants brass cylinders into the chests of conscripts at age sixteen. These heart-keys wind weekly, and with each turn, a soldier's will erodes, replaced by the orders engraved on the cylinder. Defiance means slow unwinding: a death of stillness while the heart yet beats.

Master Ticker is the finest clockmaker in Gearwright, a man who has spent forty years repairing the very mechanisms that enslave his people. He has never questioned his work — until the day he is tasked with winding Sergeant Brass, a soldier who was once his apprentice. Brass's eyes still hold flickers of the boy who loved music and mathematics. But the cylinder on his heart reads: KILL WITHOUT MERCY. OBEY.

Ticker discovers a flaw in the winding mechanism — a seventh gear that can be threaded in reverse. He learns to overwrite the engraved orders. But the Imperium has an eye for rebellion. The Winder knows when a soldier misses a wind. And The Winder knows Ticker's name.

## Core Cast

- **Master Ticker** — A clockmaker in his late fifties, stooped from decades over the workbench. Widowed. Childless. His only legacy is the thousands of heart-keys he has built and repaired. He believes in precision, in order, in the sanctity of a well-made gear. The war has taken everything from him except his craft. Now his craft must take back the rest.

- **Sergeant Brass** — Born Elias Cobb, conscripted at sixteen, now twenty-nine. He was Ticker's apprentice before the drafting and showed a natural gift for fine mechanism. The cylinder has overwritten most of his personality, but fragments remain: a half-whistled tune, a sudden gentleness with animals, the muscle memory of disassembling a pocket watch. He kills because he is wound to kill. He is beginning to understand this.

- **The Winder** — No name. No face behind the brass visor. The Winder is the Imperium's most senior clockwork technician, responsible for the winding and maintenance of every soldier in the city garrison. He reports directly to the Governor. He was once, rumor says, Master Ticker's teacher. He believes in order absolutely — because without the winders, the soldiers would be men again, and men are too messy to rule.

- **Calla Torbin** — A captain in the Gearwright garrison, one of the rare high-ranked soldiers whose wind is set to loyalty rather than aggression. She suspects something is wrong with the system — too many deserters found dead, too many conscripts going mad. She becomes Ticker's uneasy ally.

- **Governor Vex** — The ruler of Gearwright, a man whose own heart-key has not been wound in forty years. He is kept alive by a custom-built mechanism that pulses his blood for him. He is the system's ultimate symbol: a man who replaced his own soul with a machine.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Winding (Chapters 1–15)

1. **The Shop on Cog Alley** — Master Ticker repairs a soldier's heart-key. The soldier's eyes are dead. Ticker remembers when he taught Elias to read a gear-train.

2. **The Winder's Visit** — The Winder arrives at Ticker's shop for an annual inspection. He is polite, precise, terrifying. He compliments Ticker's work. He mentions that Sergeant Brass will be in soon for a re-wind.

3. **The Boy Who Loved Music** — Flashback to Elias Cobb at sixteen, dismantling a music box in Ticker's workshop, whistling a tune he invented. Ticker's wife Alina is alive; she makes them tea.

4. **Sergeant Brass Reports** — Sergeant Brass arrives. He stands at attention, speaks in clipped sentences, does not remember the music box. Ticker finds the seventh gear.

5. **The Winding Chamber** — Ticker performs the re-wind on Brass. The procedure is intimate and brutal: he opens the chest cavity, turns the key, hears the clicks. He counts seven gears. Theory says six.

6. **The Seventh Gear** — Late that night, Ticker sketches the mechanism on parchment. A seventh gear engages only at maximum tension. Its function is undocumented. He dreams of brass and blood.

7. **The Deserter's Key** — Calla Torbin brings Ticker the heart-key of a dead deserter. The cylinder is cracked. Inside, the engraving reads OBEY but the seventh gear has snapped. Ticker realizes the seventh gear is a kill-switch for disobedience.

8. **Reverse Thread** — Ticker experiments with a broken heart-key. He discovers that turning the seventh gear counter-clockwise rewrites the engraving. The cylinder can be re-coded.

9. **The Risk** — Ticker knows he must re-wind Brass with the reverse thread. If he is caught, the penalty is public unwinding. He prepares his tools.

10. **Alina's Chair** — Ticker visits the cemetery. His wife's grave is overgrown. He tells her he is going to do something dangerous. He wishes she were here to stop him.

11. **The Second Winding** — Ticker re-winds Brass under pretense of a maintenance check. He threads the seventh gear backward. Brass's eyes change. He asks: "What did you do?"

12. **The First Memory** — The tune comes back to Brass. He can hear it in his head but cannot place it. He obeys an order to kill a prisoner — but hesitates. A full second of hesitation. This has never happened before.

13. **The Report** — The Winder reviews incident reports. He notices Brass's hesitation. He flags the file. He visits Ticker's shop again — this time unannounced.

14. **Tea with the Enemy** — The Winder sits in Ticker's kitchen. He talks about the old days, about their shared teacher, about the necessity of order. He leaves a pocket watch on the table. It is stopped at the exact time of Brass's hesitation.

15. **The Warning** — Calla warns Ticker: The Winder knows something. Three soldiers were seen at Ticker's shop. Ticker should run. Ticker decides to stay and fight.

### Act II: The Rewinding (Chapters 16–35)

16. **The Network** — Ticker begins re-winding every soldier he can access. He works through the garrison, one by one. Calla provides cover. Brass stands guard.

17. **The Whistler** — Brass fully remembers the tune. He whistles it in the barracks. Other soldiers react — something in the music stirs them. They have all forgotten music.

18. **The Governor's Ball** — The annual gala at the Governor's mansion. Ticker is invited as a distinguished craftsman. He sees Governor Vex's chest mechanism: a masterwork of brass and cruelty.

19. **The Dance** — Calla dances with Brass at the ball. He holds her like a man, not a machine. The Winder watches from the balcony. He sees the hesitation in Brass's grip.

20. **Crackdown** — The Winder orders a random audit of all heart-keys in the garrison. Ticker has three days to either undo his work or escalate.

21. **The Escape Plan** — Ticker, Brass, and Calla plan to flee Gearwright. But Brass refuses — there are two thousand soldiers in the garrison. They cannot leave them.

22. **The Underground** — Calla introduces Ticker to a network of dissidents — forgers, printers, failed conscripts who escaped the wind. They have been waiting for someone who understands the mechanism.

23. **The Workshop** — The dissidents set up a hidden workshop in the sewers. Ticker teaches them the reverse-thread technique. They begin a mass re-winding operation.

24. **The Winder's Student** — The Winder visits a young clockmaker named Pella, Ticker's former assistant. He questions her about Ticker's habits. She betrays nothing, but she is afraid.

25. **Pella's Choice** — Pella comes to Ticker in the sewer. She tells him about the Winder's visit. She begs him to stop. He shows her the reverse thread. She joins the cause.

26. **The Audit Begins** — Soldiers are lined up in the garrison courtyard. Their chests are opened one by one. The Winder himself checks each heart-key. He finds no defects yet — Ticker's work is too clean.

27. **Brass Under Inspection** — Brass stands in line. The Winder pauses at his chest, examines the seventh gear. It is perfect. But the Winder's hand lingers. He knows.

28. **The Signal** — Ticker realizes they need to act before the audit reaches the soldiers he has already rewound. He decides to trigger a mass awakening. The signal will be Brass's tune, played on the garrison bell-tower.

29. **The Bell Tower** — Brass climbs the bell tower at midnight. He plays the tune on the bells. Across the city, soldiers stir. They remember. They stop.

30. **The Prisoners** — Soldiers in the punishment block break their chains. They do not kill the guards — they hug them. The first act of human affection in Gearwright in decades.

31. **The Governor's Response** — Governor Vex activates emergency protocol: all non-rewound soldiers are to be wound to maximum aggression. The cull begins.

32. **The Battle of Cog Alley** — Ticker's workshop becomes a fortress. Rewound soldiers defend it against the aggressive ones. Brass fights Brass's former squadmates. He recognizes them. They do not recognize him.

33. **Calla's Wound** — Calla is shot in the shoulder. Ticker operates on her in the sewer. He sees her heart-key: set to LOYALTY. He considers changing it. She says no. She chose loyalty freely.

34. **The Winder's Offer** — The Winder contacts Ticker via messenger. He offers a deal: stop the rebellion, and Ticker will be made Chief Clockmaker of the Imperium. The alternative is public unwinding.

35. **The Refusal** — Ticker sends back a single gear — the seventh gear, threaded in reverse. The message is clear.

### Act III: The Unwinding (Chapters 36–50)

36. **The Siege of the Sewers** — The aggressive soldiers find the underground workshop. A battle in the dark. Brass holds the tunnel entrance alone.

37. **Pella's Invention** — Pella discovers that the heart-key can be made to unwind itself — a delayed-release mechanism that would free every soldier at once if triggered by a frequency.

38. **The Frequency** — Ticker calculates the resonant frequency of a standard heart-key. It is a specific note. The bell-tower tune. He needs to get to the bell tower one more time.

39. **The Diversion** — Calla leads a diversionary attack on the Governor's mansion. She draws the aggressive soldiers away from the bell tower. She knows she will not survive.

40. **Calla's Stand** — In the Governor's courtyard, Calla fights with a broken arm. She wounds three soldiers before she falls. She is captured, not killed — Governor Vex wants her alive for the unwinding.

41. **The Ascent** — Brass and Ticker climb the bell tower. The stairs are crowded with Aggressives. Brass fights upward while Ticker carries Pella's frequency device.

42. **The Price** — Brass takes a blade through the chest. His heart-key is pierced. He has minutes. He tells Ticker to keep climbing. He remembers the music box. He smiles.

43. **The Bell** — Ticker reaches the top of the tower alone. He sets the frequency device against the bell. But the device is too weak. He needs to ring the bell with his own hands.

44. **The Old Man's Strength** — Ticker, sixty years old, pulls the bell rope. The first chime rings. Across Gearwright, soldiers stop. The second chime. They drop their weapons. The third chime. They remember who they were.

45. **The Winder's Last Wind** — The Winder arrives at the bell tower with Governor Vex's elite guard. He finds Ticker at the bell. They face each other for the last time.

46. **The Student and the Master** — The Winder removes his visor. He is an old man, older than Ticker, scarred by decades of winding. He asks: "Was it worth it?" Ticker rings the bell again.

47. **The Governor's Fall** — The frequency reaches Governor Vex's custom mechanism. His heart-key — never wound, never changed — fails. He collapses at his desk. The mechanism that kept him alive for forty years grinds to a halt.

48. **The Winder's Choice** — The Winder could kill Ticker. He has a blade. He has the skill. But he looks at the soldiers below — men who are embracing, crying, laughing. He has not seen this since he was a boy. He drops the blade.

49. **Freedom** — The fighting stops. The city is quiet. Ticker descends the bell tower and walks through the streets. Soldiers sit on curbstones, learning to feel again. Brass is found alive — the blade missed his heart by a finger's width. The heart-key hangs broken outside his chest like an abandoned cage.

50. **The Unwound City** — One year later. Gearwright has no Winder, no Governor, no heart-keys. Ticker's shop is now a school. He teaches clockwork — not for soldiers, but for music boxes, for timepieces, for the simple joy of making something that tells the truth. Brass plays his tune on a rebuilt bell tower. Calla watches from the crowd, her arm healed. The heart-keys lie at the bottom of the river, rusting in the dark. And somewhere, a child learns to read a gear-train.
