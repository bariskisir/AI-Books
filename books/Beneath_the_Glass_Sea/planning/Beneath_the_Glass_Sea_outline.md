# Beneath the Glass Sea

## Premise

In 2089, the last habitable human settlement is Atlantica, a self-sustaining city sealed beneath a kilometer-wide glass dome on the seabed. When microfractures appear in the dome's engineered crystal, marine engineer Kai Takeda discovers the cracks are spreading faster than any model predicted. With 50,000 lives depending on her, Kai must navigate bureaucratic obstruction from Councilor Voss, uncover suppressed research from the exiled Dr. Nereus, and attempt an impossible deep-sea repair before the ocean crushes the city.

## Core Cast

- **Kai Takeda** — Chief Structural Engineer of the Atlantica Dome Authority. Practical, stubborn, haunted by the failure that killed her dive partner two years ago.
- **Dr. Elara Nereus** — Former lead materials scientist, exiled to the outer hab-ring for her "alarmist" crack models. Bitter but brilliant.
- **Councilor Hal Voss** — Head of the Atlantica Council. Pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness. Believes panic kills faster than pressure.

## Chapter Plan

1. Kai Takeda wakes to an alarm — not her clock, but a stress-warning from the dome's western quadrant.
2. She walks the inspection gantry, running her gloved hand along the glass. A hairline fracture catches her light.
3. Councilor Voss dismisses her report at the morning briefing. The city cannot afford a panic.
4. Kai visits the archive and pulls the original dome specifications. Something was changed in year four.
5. She finds Dr. Nereus's old research files — deleted from the main system but cached in a maintenance terminal.
6. Voss's security detail escorts Kai out of the archive. He warns her about "unauthorized research."
7. Kai requisitions a solo submersible to inspect the external surface. Voss denies the request.
8. She goes around the system — calls in a favor with a dockmaster named Sol to borrow a private rig.
9. Outside the dome, Kai sees the truth: the cracks form a branching pattern, like a tree in winter.
10. A deep tremor shakes the seabed. The lights flicker inside the dome.
11. Kai meets Dr. Nereus in the outer hab-ring, a damp corridor lined with hydroponic moss.
12. Nereus explains: the dome was built on an old geothermal vent. Heat cycling weakens the crystal lattice.
13. They need a sealant compound — Dense-Gel — but the formula was classified when Nereus was exiled.
14. Kai breaks into the materials lab with Nereus's old access code. The gel formula is stored on a dead drive.
15. She reconstructs the formula from fragments. It requires a catalytic agent no longer produced.
16. Voss learns of the break-in. He summons Kai to his office for a private conversation.
17. Voss admits he knew about the vent. The colony founders buried the data to secure funding.
18. Kai demands he authorize a full evacuation plan. Voss refuses — fifty thousand people cannot simply move.
19. The catalytic agent was manufactured in Jakarta, now submerged. Nereus believes a stockpile may survive.
20. Kai volunteers for a surface mission. Voss reluctantly approves a single-diver window.
21. She suits up in the pressure lock. The ascent elevator creaks in ways it should not.
22. On the surface, Kai sees the world: grey sky, toxic foam, the ruins of a city poking through the water.
23. She navigates the drowned Jakarta streets using old satellite maps on her helmet display.
24. A collapsed overpass traps her submersible. She has to cut free on foot, rebreather running low.
25. She finds the chemical plant — half-collapsed, but the storage vault is intact and pressurized.
26. Kai extracts three canisters of the catalyst. A seal fails on her suit — she patches it with emergency putty.
27. She returns to Atlantica with minutes of air left. Nereus begins synthesizing the gel in a makeshift lab.
28. Voss announces a city-wide "stability briefing" — his way of spinning the cracks as routine maintenance.
29. Kai confronts Voss publicly at the briefing. The crowd splits between fear and trust.
30. The gel synthesis fails on the first batch. Nereus recalibrates — the temperature gradient is wrong.
31. A class-three fracture opens in the eastern sector. Water jets spray into a residential corridor.
32. Kai leads an emergency crew to seal the breach with temporary plates. Three people are injured.
33. Voss finally orders a partial evacuation of the eastern sector. Families pack their lives into single bags.
34. Kai and Nereus work through the night on a second batch. The gel stabilizes but cures too slowly.
35. A seismic event — larger than before — knocks out main power. The emergency generators kick in.
36. In the dark, Kai walks the dome interior with a handheld scanner. The fracture map is a web now.
37. Nereus realizes the gel needs a higher pressure environment to cure properly — they must apply it externally.
38. Kai designs a delivery system: a frame that clamps to the outer surface and injects gel under pressure.
39. Sol fabricates the frame in his dock workshop. It is crude but functional.
40. Kai and Sol mount the frame on the exterior in a four-hour dive window. The current is brutal.
41. The injection works. Gel flows into the cracks. But a new fracture opens behind them as pressure shifts.
42. Kai makes a decision: she will stay outside and direct the application from the submersible while Sol returns.
43. The gel supply runs low. Kai has to choose which cracks to seal — and which to let fail.
44. Voss contacts Kai on the comm. A cargo ship has arrived from the surface offering evacuation. It is a scam.
45. Kai realizes Voss was right about one thing: outside help is a lie. They can only save themselves.
46. The final batch of gel is loaded. Kai positions the frame at the largest intersection of cracks.
47. The dome groans. A section of glass spiders outward. Kai injects everything they have.
48. The gel cures. The cracks stop propagating. The structure holds.
49. Kai returns inside. The city is battered, dark in places, but intact. Fifty thousand people remain.
50. In the quiet afterward, Kai watches the dawn filter through the glass sea. Repairs will take years. They have years.
