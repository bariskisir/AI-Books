## Plot

Dr. Elara Voss, a brilliant but haunted psychodrama therapist, is invited to the remote Kaltwasser Clinic in the Austrian Alps to implement her controversial treatment for phantom limb pain. Her four patients — a former rescue pilot, a concert pianist, a war photographer, and a dancer — all suffer from excruciating phantom pain that conventional medicine cannot touch. As Elara guides them through therapeutic role-play, a disturbing pattern emerges: all four describe the same mysterious figure, a Man in Grey, present at the moment of their accidents. When Elara discovers that her own estranged father, Karl Voss, was a patient at Kaltwasser thirty years ago and disappeared during an experimental psychodrama session, she realizes the Man in Grey is not a collective hallucination but a psychic splinter — a fragment of her father's psyche that has been witnessing trauma for three decades.
