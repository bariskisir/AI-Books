# The Dyer's Hand

**by Barış Kısır**

A literary historical novel set in 1954 Fez, following the last master indigo dyer as he decides whether to sell his family's ancient formulas to a Paris fashion house — and what a lifetime of craft is worth when no one is left to inherit it.

## About

Fez, 1954. Idris ben Omar is sixty-eight years old and his hands have been blue for fifty-three of those years — stained by the indigo vats that have been in his family for three centuries. He is the last master dyer in the Fez medina still using the old formulas. His son chose law over the vats. The Jewish dyers who once shared the quarter are all gone. And a Paris fashion house has sent a representative with an offer: buy the formulas outright, or watch them die.

When his old friend Yacob Cohen — a Jewish dyer now dying of cancer in Marseille — returns to Fez for one last visit, Idris must confront what the craft means, what friendship across faiths can survive, and how to pass on what cannot be written down.

A quiet, meditative novel about tradition, color, and the hands that hold what endures.
