# The Lantern Index

## Premise
When Nora Vale, an exhausted municipal archivist, finds a hand-copied index of ruined lighthouse records in her late father's effects, she discovers that the city's official history has been edited with care and cost. The names in the ledger point to a chain of demolished lantern houses, a fatal breakwater collapse, and a missing public report that was never allowed to surface.

Nora follows the index through damp storage rooms, sealed map cabinets, and the testimony of people who have spent years learning not to ask questions. As she works with a harbor electrician and a local journalist, she learns that the index was compiled by her mother to expose a civic cover-up. The closer Nora comes to the truth, the more she has to decide whether a city can be made honest without first breaking the people who built their lives on its lies.

## Core Cast
- Nora Vale - Municipal archivist and the book's central investigator; precise, tired, and harder to discourage than she looks.
- Tomas Vale - Nora's late father, a former harbor clerk whose private papers conceal the first layer of the mystery.
- Mara Vale - Nora's mother, a surveyor whose notes and recordings become the hidden architecture of the plot.
- Eli Mercer - A harbor electrician restoring old beacons and lamps; practical, calm, and increasingly loyal to Nora.
- Asha Ilyas - A local journalist and historian who understands both the archive and the city's appetite for silence.
- Jonas Vale - Nora's uncle, a civic lawyer who prefers compromise to scandal and knows more than he admits.
- Commissioner Holt - The city official who inherited the breakwater disaster and has spent years containing its consequences.
- Ruth Calder - A retired dock foreman whose memory of the collapse turns out to be exact and dangerous.

## 50 Chapter Plan
### Part I: The Ledger
1. Nora finds a damp index card in her father's desk and recognizes a set of lighthouse numbers that should not exist.
2. At Tomas's funeral, Jonas asks Nora to clear the apartment quickly and leaves no room for questions.
3. Nora opens the harbor records room and finds pages missing from the years around the breakwater collapse.
4. Eli arrives to repair the old eastern beacon and notices that Nora is looking at the wrong inventory.
5. Nora traces the first index numbers to a demolished lantern house at South Quay.
6. Asha Ilyas shows Nora a newspaper archive that stops talking about the collapse halfway through the summer.
7. Nora discovers that every name in the index is tied to a removed report, a lost photograph, or a sealed complaint.
8. She finds salt packed into the spine of her father's ledger, as if he wanted it to survive damp and time.
9. Nora and Eli break into the old map room and discover a second set of entries written in her mother's hand.
10. The first entries point not to the collapse itself, but to the years before it.

### Part II: The Missing Report
11. Nora walks the breakwater in winter rain and learns how the city still uses the old stones as a prideful memorial.
12. Asha uncovers council minutes that show the emergency budget was diverted before the wall failed.
13. Eli identifies repeated corrosion in the beacon hardware, suggesting deliberate neglect rather than simple decay.
14. Nora finds a taped voice memo from Mara, recorded days before she vanished from the city's records.
15. Jonas warns Nora that the family will not survive a public scandal and asks her to let the past stay buried.
16. Ruth Calder names three men who signed off on unsafe repairs and one who refused to sign at all.
17. Nora realizes her own name appears in the ledger as a later addition, written after her mother's disappearance.
18. She returns to the family house and finds a hidden drawer containing maps, receipts, and a list called Lantern Protocol.
19. The protocol describes when to copy, hide, and distribute evidence if the archive is raided.
20. Nora understands that her parents planned for someone to continue the work if they failed.

### Part III: Pressure and Exposure
21. The city posts notice that North Spit light will be demolished, taking away the last intact beacon.
22. Nora, Eli, and Asha inspect the tower and find a sealed chamber behind the lower stair.
23. Men from the harbor authority follow Nora home, making the threat visible without saying a word.
24. Asha leaks a small piece of the story, forcing the council to deny everything in public.
25. Nora reads a letter from Mara explaining that the collapse was preceded by an evacuation lie.
26. Jonas confesses that he altered one document to protect a client, then spent years pretending that one lie could remain isolated.
27. Nora confronts Commissioner Holt in the harbor office and is offered a quiet settlement.
28. A power cut locks Nora in the archive overnight, where she finds binders that were never entered into the public catalog.
29. The binders reveal payments, favors, and names of the people who were bought to stay silent.
30. Nora learns that Tomas hid the index to protect her from the first retaliation, not to protect the city.

### Part IV: The Vault Under the Customs House
31. Nora is invited to a meeting of the old keepers' society, where retired workers still speak in code.
32. The index numbering system turns out to map a route through the harbor's forgotten structures.
33. Holt tries to seize the papers under the pretense of preserving municipal records.
34. Eli produces maintenance logs that prove the beacons were sabotaged in stages, not damaged all at once.
35. Nora finds a postcard from Mara naming the last person who saw the evidence vault sealed.
36. Asha decodes the numbering system and identifies the customs house as the final destination.
37. In the vault beneath the customs house, Nora finds the full report, the ledgers, and a recorded confession.
38. The confession names the decision that doomed the breakwater and the officials who chose delay over evacuation.
39. Nora discovers that her father spent years protecting the final witness because he hoped one person might eventually tell the truth.
40. The witness is still alive, frail but alert, and ready to speak if Nora can keep the city from burying him again.

### Part V: A City Forced to Listen
41. Nora prepares the evidence for public release while Holt files injunctions and circulates doubt.
42. The witness gives sworn testimony, but the council attempts to stall by questioning every date and signature.
43. Nora presents the Lantern Index publicly, showing how each erased name connects to a deliberate act of concealment.
44. Eli lights North Spit during a storm, making the old beacon visible from the quay and the archive windows alike.
45. Floodwater enters the lower records room, and the staff who once ignored Nora now help move boxes to safety.
46. Jonas admits his role in the cover-up in front of the people he once advised to trust procedure.
47. Nora reads Tomas's final letter and understands that love, in her family, often arrived as restraint.
48. Asha publishes the complete story, and the city cannot pretend the record never existed.
49. The council preserves the lantern houses as a public archive and memorial instead of another polite ruin.
50. Nora adds one blank page to the index, leaving room for future names that the city has not yet learned to honor.
