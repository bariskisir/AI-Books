# The Still Point — Outline

## Premise

James has kept the lighthouse on Eilean Mor for twelve years, trading the mainland for solitude after a car accident that killed his wife and daughter. His days are measured in foghorns and fuel checks, his only human contact the weekly supply boat and Moira, a young radio operator on the coast. When a freak autumn storm drives a stranger onto the island's rocks, James must pull the man from the sea and shelter him. Over three days of hurricane-force wind, the visitor — a journalist researching a book on lighthouse keepers who walked into the sea — forces James to reckon with the grief he has buried beneath routine. The storm becomes an external mirror of an internal breaking, and by the time the skies clear, nothing on Eilean Mor is still anymore.

## Core Cast

- **James** — Age 49. Lighthouse keeper. Widower. A man who has perfected the art of not feeling. His grief calcified into protocol. Speaks in short sentences, moves with economy, watches the horizon because it is easier than watching himself.
- **The Visitor (Caleb Ross)** — Age 34. Journalist. Recovering alcoholic. He came to the islands to write about disappearances and found something he was not looking for. Talkative in the way of men who fear silence. Carries a water-damaged notebook he will not let dry.
- **Moira** — Age 22. Radio operator at Oban Coast Station. Perceptive, patient, lonely in her own way. She talks to James three times daily on the scheduled check-in. She is the only person who has heard his voice change over the years, and she knows something is wrong before the storm hits.

## Chapter Plan

1. James checks the generator at dawn. The sea is flat and wrong.
2. He eats breakfast alone. Two eggs, one cup of tea. He has done this 4,380 mornings.
3. Scheduled radio check with Moira at 0800. She mentions a depression forming in the Atlantic.
4. James walks the cliff path. He stops at the place where the old keeper's boot was found.
5. He remembers the day he arrived on Eilean Mor — hollowed out, grateful for the isolation.
6. Moira's voice tightens on the noon check. The depression has deepened to a named storm.
7. James lashes down loose equipment. His hands move fast, his face betrays nothing.
8. He finds a piece of driftwood carved with initials: A.M. + C.R. from decades ago.
9. In his quarters he opens the drawer he never opens. Photograph of a woman and a girl.
10. The wind arrives at dusk. He secures the lantern room. Glass rattles in its frame.
11. Caleb Ross wakes on the rocks, half-drowned, his leg gashed open.
12. James brings the man inside. Does not ask questions. Applies pressure to the wound.
13. He radios Moira: survivor pulled from the water. She says rescue cannot come until the storm passes.
14. Caleb sits wrapped in a blanket, shivering, watching James move around the room.
15. James boils water. He asks the man his name. Caleb says it twice, as if James did not hear.
16. Caleb notices the photograph face-down on the shelf. Says nothing.
17. Moira's midnight check. Her voice cracks with relief when James answers.
18. The storm intensifies. The lighthouse trembles. Caleb asks if the light will hold.
19. James tells him the light has held since 1892. He says it like a promise or a threat.
20. Caleb asks why James took the post. James says because it was open.
21. Caleb laughs at that. It is the first human sound in the room that is not weather.
22. James asks why Caleb was out in the storm. Caleb says he was looking for something.
23. The power flickers. James goes to check the backup generator. Caleb limps after him.
24. In the generator shed, Caleb sees the names carved into the wall — keepers who served here.
25. He reads the last one aloud. James. No second initial. No dates.
26. Caleb says his book is about keepers who disappeared. Men who walked into the sea and were not found.
27. James turns away. He says some men walk because they have nothing to walk toward.
28. Caleb asks if James ever thinks about walking into the sea. James does not answer.
29. Moira's voice on the radio: the storm has stalled. Another forty-eight hours.
30. James opens a bottle of whiskey he has kept for nine years. Pours two glasses.
31. Caleb tells him about a lighthouse off the coast of Maine. A keeper vanished in 1923. No note.
32. James finishes his whiskey. Pours another. He has not drunk in nine years.
33. Caleb asks about the photograph. James says his daughter would be twenty-three now.
34. Caleb reads the silence. He writes something in his damaged notebook.
35. The storm shakes the building. A window cracks in the kitchen. James boards it.
36. Caleb helps despite his leg. They work without speaking. The wind screams through the gap.
37. Moira calls in. She is scared. James tells her the light is steady. She believes him.
38. Caleb asks if James blames himself. James says the road was wet. That is all.
39. Caleb says the old keeper on Eilean Mor — the one whose boot was found — had a daughter too.
40. James goes still. He did not know that. The island keeps secrets.
41. Caleb hands him the notebook. Open to a page. A woman's name he does not recognize.
42. James reads a letter the keeper wrote but never sent. It says: I cannot stay where everything remembers her.
43. In the morning the storm begins to break. Light comes through the kitchen window gray and thin.
44. Caleb stands at the window. He says he thinks he found what he was looking for.
45. James asks what that was. Caleb says proof that men like him exist.
46. James puts the photograph back in the drawer. He leaves it open this time.
47. The rescue boat arrives at noon. Caleb shakes James's hand on the jetty.
48. James watches the boat become a dot on the horizon. He does not go back inside.
49. He radios Moira. He says he will be leaving when the relief keeper arrives.
50. He walks to the cliff. Looks at the sea. The still point is no longer still.
