# The Parachute Regiment — Outline

## Premise

On the night of June 5, 1944, a ragtag company of British paratroopers from the 6th Airborne Division drops behind enemy lines in Normandy. Their mission: seize and hold a bridge vital to the D-Day landings. But the intelligence was catastrophically wrong. The bridge is heavily fortified, a crack Panzer division is billeted in the surrounding villages, and the company is scattered across the wrong drop zone. Sergeant Miller, a hard-bitten veteran; Private Flynn, a green replacement; and Lieutenant Ashford, a newly commissioned officer untested in combat, must hold together a fractured unit, navigate hostile countryside, and complete their mission against impossible odds — or die trying.

## Core Cast

- **Sergeant Tom Miller** — Veteran NCO, late thirties. Fought in North Africa and Sicily. Practical, gruff, carries the weight of dead men he could not save. The backbone of the company.
- **Private Leo Flynn** — Young replacement from Bethnal Green, nineteen years old. Eager, naive, desperate to prove he belongs. His mother thinks he is a clerk in Aldershot.
- **Lieutenant James Ashford** — Newly commissioned officer, twenty-three. Public school and OCS. Book-smart, earnest, carrying a field manual and a deep insecurity about his own authority. He learns command the hard way.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Drop (Chapters 1-10)

1. **Stand To** — Miller sharpens his knife in a Nissen hut as the order comes in. Tonight is the night.
2. **The Briefing** — Ashford studies the aerial photos. The bridge looks quiet. Too quiet.
3. **White Horse** — The men board the Albemarle. Flynn sees a white horse cut into a hillside and takes it as an omen.
4. **Green Light** — Over the Channel, the dispatcher gives the signal. Flynn hesitates at the door.
5. **Silk and Static** — The drop goes wrong. Flak tears through the aircraft. Men are scattered into the dark.
6. **Wrong Field** — Miller lands alone in a bog. No sign of his stick. The locator beacon is dead.
7. **The First Dead** — Ashford finds a body hung in a tree. He recognizes the face. His hands shake.
8. **The Whistle** — Flynn hides in a ditch as a German patrol passes. He hears the recognition signal and answers.
9. **Strangers in the Dark** — Miller and Flynn converge at a crossroads. Two men from four different sticks.
10. **The Farmhouse** — Ashford gathers a handful of men in a ruined barn. They count their losses.

### Part II: The Rally (Chapters 11-20)

11. **A Boy Named Fritz** — They find a young German soldier hiding in a hayloft. He is not much older than Flynn.
12. **Miller's Count** — Miller tallies the company: eighteen men, one radio, no mortars. Not enough.
13. **The Roadblock** — A German checkpoint blocks the only route east. Ashford proposes a risky plan.
14. **Flynn's Watch** — Flynn pulls sentry duty and hears the Panzer engines grinding through the night.
15. **What Ashford Knows** — Ashford confesses to Miller that the maps are wrong. The bridge is reinforced.
16. **The Forgotten Order** — A dispatch rider found dead on the road carries orders for an SS division. They are already here.
17. **The Canal** — The company wades through a freezing canal. One man goes under and does not come up.
18. **Dead Ground** — They cross open fields under mortar fire. Miller runs the numbers on their survival.
19. **A Change of Plan** — Ashford decides to bypass the bridge and hit a secondary crossing. Miller disagrees.
20. **The Signal** — They hear the distant roar of the naval bombardment. The invasion has begun.

### Part III: The March (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Forge** — The company rests in a blacksmith's forge. Flynn tends to a blistered foot.
22. **A German Dinner** — They stumble on a field kitchen and take hot food from the enemy's pot.
23. **The Prisoner** — A German NCO is captured. He gives information freely. Too freely.
24. **Interrogation** — Miller smells a trap. The prisoner's story does not add up.
25. **The Tenth Man** — Ashford orders a forced march. Two men are too injured to continue. They must be left behind.
26. **Night March** — The company moves through a moonlit orchard. Every shadow is a threat.
27. **The Wood** — German engineers are felling trees to block the roads. The company gets pinned by a sniper.
28. **A Sound in the Fog** — Fog rolls in. Strange noises in the mist. Flynn's imagination runs wild.
29. **The Spandau** — A machine gun nest blocks the valley. Miller flanks it with a grenade.
30. **Breaking Contact** — The company exfiltrates under fire. Ashford learns to trust his gut.

### Part IV: The Siege (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Ridge** — They reach the high ground overlooking the bridge. The true scale of the defenses is revealed.
32. **What They Saw** — Three pillboxes, a flak position, infantry trenches, and a Panther tank in defilade.
33. **Ashford's Decision** — Ashford decides to attack at dawn. Miller argues for withdrawal. Ashford holds firm.
34. **The Plan** — Ashford draws up an assault plan. Flynn will be on the left flank. His hands do not shake anymore.
35. **Waiting** — The hours before the attack. Men write letters. Miller cleans his rifle. Ashford memorizes the terrain.
36. **The Bombardment** — Naval shells begin to fall. The company moves into position behind the barrage.
37. **Crossing** — The first section dashes across the open ground. A mine detonates. The assault stalls.
38. **The First Pillbox** — Miller reaches the first pillbox and fires through the firing slit. The crew burns.
39. **Flynn's Charge** — Flynn's section is pinned. He crawls forward alone, field dressings and courage.
40. **Miller's Gambit** — Miller draws fire so Ashford can reach the command bunker. A bullet finds his leg.

### Part V: The Reckoning (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Flank** — The Panther tank turns its turret. Flynn sees it and does not run.
42. **Close Quarter** — Ashford takes the command bunker with a pistol and a grenade. He does not think. He acts.
43. **The Last Pillbox** — One position remains. A PIAT round hits it square. The gun goes silent.
44. **The Bridge** — The company seizes the bridge. Ashford calls in the code word: HAMMER.
45. **Counterattack** — German infantry rallies and pushes back. The company fights for every yard.
46. **Ashford Stands** — Ashford holds the centre with a Bren gun, firing from the hip. The line holds.
47. **The Panzer** — The Panther advances onto the bridge. Flynn finds a Gammon bomb and crawls toward it.
48. **Dawn** — The tank brews up. Flynn is alive. Miller is dragged to cover. Ashford takes stock of the living.
49. **Relief** — Commandos reach the bridge. The company is relieved. Ashford salutes the major. Miller looks away.
50. **The Ledger** — Ashford writes the casualty report. Miller sits on the bridge parapet, staring at nothing. Flynn walks to the centre of the span and looks east, where the sun is rising.
