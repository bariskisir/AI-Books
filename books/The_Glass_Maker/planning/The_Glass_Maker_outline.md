# The Glass Maker — Outline

## Premise

In 1658 Venice, Matteo Dandolo is the most celebrated glass blower on Murano, master of a furnace that has belonged to his family for three generations. He has discovered a technique for producing a ruby-red glass so pure and luminous that it seems lit from within — a color the Venetian Republic has pursued for decades. When the Inquisition learns that Matteo's formula involves a forbidden alchemical process bordering on heresy, they send Father Ambrogio to investigate. Matteo must choose between surrendering his family's secret — the culmination of a lifetime's work — and risking everything to protect it, even as Elena, his model and muse, urges him to flee.

## Core Cast

- **Matteo Dandolo** — Master glass blower, late 30s. Proud, guarded, deeply tied to tradition yet possessing a secret innovation. A widower whose wife died in childbirth a decade ago.
- **Elena Morosini** — Matteo's assistant and muse, mid-20s. The daughter of a glass merchant, she grew up on Murano and knows the furnaces better than most men. Sharp, practical, deeply loyal.
- **Father Ambrogio Contarini** — Inquisitor of the Venetian Holy Office, early 50s. Educated, patient, genuinely pious. Not a monster, but a man who believes the Church's authority is absolute. His faith will be tested.
- **Doge Marco Pesaro** — The Doge of Venice, who protects Murano's glass secrets as state treasures but fears the Inquisition's reach.
- **Maestro Lorenzo Fuseri** — An older glass blower, Matteo's former master. Bitter that Matteo surpassed him, nursing old grudges.
- **Brother Michele** — Ambrogio's young assistant monk, earnest and conflicted.

## Chapter Plan

### Part I — The Furnace (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Morning Fire** — Matteo lights the furnace before dawn on Murano, alone with his work.
2. **The Ruby Secret** — Matteo pulls a finished goblet from the annealing oven — his ruby glass is perfect.
3. **Elena's Hands** — Elena arrives for work, sees the goblet. Their morning routine.
4. **The Order** — A messenger from the Doge's palace arrives: the Council of Ten requests Matteo's presence.
5. **San Marco** — Matteo crosses the lagoon to Venice, reflects on his father and the family trade.
6. **The Doge's Warning** — Doge Pesaro informs Matteo that the Inquisition has been asking questions about alchemical glass.
7. **The Denial** — Matteo claims his ruby glass is pure silica and minerals. The Doge is not entirely convinced.
8. **Elena's Concern** — Elena presses Matteo about what the Doge wanted. He deflects.
9. **Lorenzo's Visit** — Maestro Fuseri arrives at the workshop, feigning interest in Matteo's recent work.
10. **The Spilled Batch** — Matteo ruins a batch of glass, his hands trembling for the first time in years.

### Part II — The Questioner (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Black Gondola** — A black gondola arrives on Murano. Father Ambrogio steps ashore.
12. **First Interview** — Ambrogio visits the workshop, polite and observant. He asks about the furnace.
13. **The Formula** — Matteo reviews his recipe book, considers destroying it.
14. **Elena's Vigil** — Elena stays late helping Matteo, watches him grow more distant and haunted.
15. **The Denunciation** — Ambrogio receives a sealed letter from Lorenzo Fuseri, detailing what he suspects.
16. **The Summons** — Matteo is summoned to the Holy Office at San Zanipolo for questioning.
17. **The Examination** — Ambrogio questions Matteo about alchemical texts, the nature of his ruby glass.
18. **The Lie** — Matteo swears his technique is pure craft, not alchemy.
19. **Brother Michele's Doubt** — Michele witnesses the interrogation and begins to question his vocation.
20. **The Confiscation** — Ambrogio orders Matteo's workshop searched. They take his recipe book.

### Part III — The Siege (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Empty Furnace** — Matteo stands before his cold furnace, unable to work.
22. **Elena's Plan** — Elena proposes Matteo teach her the technique so the knowledge survives.
23. **The Anchoress** — Ambrogio studies Matteo's recipe book, consults with a scholar of alchemy.
24. **The Trap** — Ambrogio offers Matteo a deal: reveal the secret and be absolved, or face the tribunal.
25. **The Confession** — Matteo confesses a partial truth — he used antimony and gold, nothing more.
26. **The Test** — Ambrogio demands Matteo demonstrate the technique in the presence of Church witnesses.
27. **The Failed Demonstration** — Matteo deliberately sabotages the demonstration, producing only common glass.
28. **Elena's Dare** — Elena accuses Matteo of cowardice. She will not watch him destroy himself.
29. **The Arrest** — Ambrogio has Matteo taken to the Piombi prison beneath the Doge's Palace.
30. **The Cell** — Matteo in a narrow cell, heat unbearable, remembering his wife.

### Part IV — The Trial (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Tribunal** — Matteo stands before three inquisitors. Ambrogio presents the charges.
32. **Elena's Petition** — Elena begs the Doge to intervene, but his hands are tied by Church authority.
33. **The Witness** — Lorenzo Fuseri testifies against Matteo, embellishing his suspicions.
34. **The Scholar's Testimony** — The alchemical scholar confirms the recipe contains banned techniques.
35. **Matteo's Defense** — Matteo argues that glassmaking has always walked the line between art and magic.
36. **The Verdict** — Matteo is found guilty of heresy. The sentence: public abjuration and life imprisonment, or burning if he refuses.
37. **The Offer** — Ambrogio visits Matteo in prison. The offer still stands: the formula for his freedom.
38. **The Refusal** — Matteo refuses. The secret belongs to his family, not to the Church.
39. **Elena's Gambit** — Elena goes to Ambrogio with a proposal of her own: she knows the formula too.
40. **The Confession of the Muse** — Elena demonstrates the ruby glass before the Inquisition, claiming she alone discovered it.

### Part V — The Reckoning (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Bargain** — Ambrogio agrees: Elena's confession frees Matteo if she takes his place in penance — a year's confinement in a convent.
42. **The Release** — Matteo is freed, shattered and humiliated.
43. **The Farewell** — Elena is taken to San Zaccaria. Matteo watches from a gondola.
44. **The Accusation** — Lorenzo Fuseri is revealed as the informant who denounced Matteo. The glassmaking community shuns him.
45. **The Secret Passed** — Before her confinement, Elena wrote down the formula and gave it to Matteo.
46. **The Letter** — Matteo receives a letter from Elena within the convent. She does not regret her choice.
47. **The Ruby Gift** — Matteo creates a single perfect ruby goblet, his finest work, and sends it to the Doge as a gift for the Republic.
48. **The Recognition** — The Doge publicly honors Matteo, and by extension Elena, for the ruby glass. The Inquisition's grip loosens.
49. **The Wait** — Winter passes. Matteo works, but the furnace feels empty without Elena.
50. **The Return** — Spring, 1659. Elena's confinement ends. She returns to Murano. Matteo is at the furnace, working a new piece. He looks up. She is there.
