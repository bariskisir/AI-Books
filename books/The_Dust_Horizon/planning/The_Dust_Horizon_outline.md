# The Dust Horizon — Outline

## Premise

Elias Cole was the fastest gun in the territory until he put the Colt away and became a shopkeeper in a town called Mercy, a drying creek-bed of a place the railroad has already drawn a line through. Leland Cross, a railroad baron with a private car and a private army, wants Mercy gone — not bypassed, but burned to the ground and salted. Sheriff Marta Prynne refuses to yield an inch of ground she swore to protect, and Jin-soku, a Chinese laborer who escaped Cross's track gangs, carries a silent account of the dead. When the deadline comes and the fires start, Elias must decide whether the man he used to be is the only man who can save the town he has left.

## Core Cast

- **Elias Cole** — former gunslinger, now a shopkeeper in Mercy. Twelve years since he last drew. Trying to outlive his name.
- **Sheriff Marta Prynne** — the law in Mercy. Stubborn, principled, unimpressed by reputation. Will not be moved.
- **Leland Cross** — railroad baron. Velvet manners, iron will. He does not just want the land; he wants the town to suffer.
- **Jin-soku** — Chinese railroad laborer, escaped from Cross's camp. Quiet, watchful, nursing a precise and patient hatred.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part One — The Dying Town (1-13)

1. **The Man Behind the Counter** — Elias Cole sweeps his empty store and measures his wasted years in unpaid ledgers.
2. **Mercy, Population 82** — Sheriff Marta Prynne makes her morning rounds; the town is quiet, shrinking, and afraid.
3. **A Telegram from the East** — Leland Cross's wire arrives at the depot; the railroad will arrive within the week.
4. **The Surveyors** — Cross's men ride in with chains and survey stakes; Marta meets them on Main Street.
5. **Marta Draws a Line** — Sheriff Prynne tells the surveyors Mercy will not move and will not sell.
6. **The Man Who Would Not Draw** — A drunk forces a confrontation in the saloon; Elias takes the beating and walks away.
7. **Jin-soku** — A Chinese laborer slips into Mercy at night, wounded and hunted by railroad track gangs.
8. **What Jin-soku Knows** — Jin-soku tells Elias that Cross burned a Chinese labor camp two years ago.
9. **The Town Council** — Mercy's leaders debate the railroad's offer; the mayor urges them to accept and scatter.
10. **The Offer** — Cross's agent delivers a buyout so low the town would owe money to leave.
11. **The Locked Drawer** — Elias opens a drawer beneath his counter and finds a Colt revolver he swore he buried.
12. **The Ultimatum** — Cross gives Mercy one week to evacuate; Marta nails the notice to the church door.
13. **Dust at Dusk** — Elias walks the empty main street; the town holds its breath and waits for dawn.

### Part Two — The Reckoning Approaches (14-25)

14. **The Private Car** — Leland Cross arrives on his personal rail car, all velvet upholstery and quiet menace.
15. **The Security Badge** — Cross's hired guns badge themselves as railroad security and patrol the streets openly.
16. **The Livery Burns** — First fire in the night; the livery stable collapses; no one sees the arsonist.
17. **Jin-soku's Testimony** — Jin-soku tells Marta what Cross did to his camp; her resolve hardens into steel.
18. **The Man Who Was** — A traveling fossil hunter recognizes Elias from the territory's most wanted list.
19. **The Weight of a Gun** — Elias holds the Colt in his palm; the weight feels familiar and deeply wrong.
20. **Marta's Recruit** — Sheriff Prynne asks Jin-soku to stand with them; he agrees on one private condition.
21. **The Dinner Car** — Cross hosts Mercy's remaining merchants in his rail car, plying them with brandy and threats.
22. **The Porch Burns** — The church porch catches fire in the night; faith in a peaceful resolution dies with it.
23. **The Argument** — Marta calls Elias a coward; he tells her she does not know what she is asking him to become.
24. **The Exodus** — Families load wagons and flee east; Mercy's population drops below forty souls.
25. **Cross's Reason** — Cross remembers a dirt-poor boy humiliated by a town like this one, and smiles.

### Part Three — The Fire (26-38)

26. **The Night Riders** — Cross's men ride through Mercy firing into windows, terrorizing the remaining holdouts.
27. **Marta Stands the Line** — Sheriff Prynne faces five armed men on Main Street alone; they are the ones who blink.
28. **Jin-soku's War** — Jin-soku sabotages the rail line east of town, buying Mercy three more days to prepare.
29. **The Gunsmith's Passing** — Mercy's old gunsmith dies; his last words to Elias are a warning about the gun.
30. **The Letter** — Elias rides to a crumbling homestead and reads a decade-old letter from a woman he failed to save.
31. **The Colt Strapped On** — Elias buckles the gun belt; the man he was twelve years ago walks back into his skin.
32. **The Stranger in Town** — Marta sees the change in Elias's walk and wonders if she has made a terrible mistake.
33. **Cross's Order** — Cross gives the order to burn Mercy to the ground and salt the earth where it stood.
34. **The Defense** — Marta, Elias, and Jin-soku plan a defense with six able-bodied men and two days to build it.
35. **The Mayor's Betrayal** — The mayor slips out to Cross's camp and sells the town's defense plan for his own life.
36. **Cross Strikes First** — Cross's men torch the mercantile a night early; Elias watches his store become nothing but ash.
37. **Elias Fires** — Elias draws and shoots a man for the first time in twelve years; the bullet does not miss.
38. **Ash and Ember** — Dawn breaks over the smoldering town; Elias cleans his revolver by firelight, silent and changed.

### Part Four — The Reckoning (39-50)

39. **The Mayor Caught** — Jin-soku intercepts the mayor on the road; Marta gives him one chance to earn his life back.
40. **What the Mayor Gives** — The mayor reveals Cross forged the land grant; the railroad has no legal right-of-way through Mercy.
41. **The Papers** — Elias and Marta break into Cross's rail car under cover of darkness and find the proof.
42. **Cross Knows** — Cross discovers the papers missing and seals the town; no one leaves Mercy alive.
43. **The Hotel Barricade** — Mercy's remaining citizens fortify the hotel; the final stand takes shape in the lobby.
44. **Cross's Army** — Forty armed men surround Mercy at first light; the siege of the hotel begins.
45. **Jin-soku's Last Move** — Jin-soku derails a supply train east of town; Cross's reinforcements will not be arriving.
46. **House to House** — The fighting moves through burning streets; Elias moves through smoke like the killer he once was.
47. **The Walk** — Elias walks into Cross's camp alone, the forged deed folded in his breast pocket.
48. **The Confrontation** — Cross draws on Elias; Elias shoots the pistol from his hand and does not kill him.
49. **The Marshal Arrives** — The U.S. Marshal, summoned by Marta's wire, arrests Cross for fraud, arson, and attempted murder.
50. **The Horizon** — Elias leaves the Colt in the ashes and walks east into the dust horizon, unarmed and free.
