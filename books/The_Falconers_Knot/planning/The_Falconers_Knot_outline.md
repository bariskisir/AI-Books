# The Falconer's Knot — Outline

## Premise

In the autumn of 1348, Matteo Corvaro — the disgraced son of a Florentine falconer, fleeing an accusation of murder — takes refuge as a novice at the remote Benedictine monastery of San Colombano in the Tuscan hills. When a monk is found dead in the scriptorium clutching a torn page of illuminated manuscript, the abbot asks Matteo to investigate. As plague closes every road and more monks die — each left with a falcon's feather — Matteo must use the observational skills his father taught him to unmask the killer before suspicion, or the pestilence, destroys them all.

## Core Cast

- **Matteo Corvaro**: Twenty-two-year-old novice. Trained as a falconer by his father, he has a hunter's patience and a sharp eye for detail. He fled Florence after being accused of killing a rival in a street fight he did not start.
- **Father Silvestro**: The abbot of San Colombano. Sixty, lean, politically shrewd. He knows more about the monastery's secrets than he reveals and uses Matteo as a tool he can disown.
- **Brother Anselmo**: The elderly librarian. Seventy-six, half-blind, brilliant. He guards the scriptorium and its collection of rare texts with fierce devotion. He becomes Matteo's reluctant mentor.
- **Sister Beatrice**: A Benedictine nun from the attached women's house. She runs the infirmary with practical skill and quiet authority, and she is the only person who openly trusts Matteo.
- **Giacomo the Cellarer**: Forty, powerfully built. He controls the monastery's stores and wine. Jovial on the surface, covetous underneath. He has been skimming provisions.
- **Brother Luca**: A young illuminator, twenty-five, gifted and ambitious. He was close to the first victim and is terrified he will be next.
- **Elia of Fiesole**: A wandering friar who arrives at San Colombano seeking shelter from the plague. Charismatic and learned, he brings dangerous ideas — and possibly a dangerous past.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Arrival (Chapters 1–10)

- **Chapter 1: The Road to San Colombano**: Matteo climbs the cliff path to the monastery in cold rain, carrying nothing but a leather bag and his father's falconry glove.
- **Chapter 2: The Gate**: The porter, Brother Filippo, admits Matteo grudgingly, and the abbot interviews him in a bare stone cell.
- **Chapter 3: The Rule**: Matteo is assigned to Brother Anselmo in the scriptorium and learns the rhythm of monastic hours — Matins, Lauds, Prime, the bells that carve the day.
- **Chapter 4: The Scriptorium**: Brother Anselmo shows Matteo the library's treasures, including a locked cabinet he is forbidden to open.
- **Chapter 5: Vespers**: Matteo attends his first evening service and notices the monks watching him with guarded curiosity; Brother Luca alone offers friendship.
- **Chapter 6: The Falconer's Eye**: Matteo observes the monastery's routines with a hunter's attention — who moves where, who speaks to whom, who avoids the cloister after dark.
- **Chapter 7: The Body**: Brother Tommaso is found dead in the scriptorium at dawn, slumped over a desk with a torn manuscript page in his fist.
- **Chapter 8: The Page**: Matteo examines the torn page — a passage from a controversial treatise on poverty that the Church has condemned.
- **Chapter 9: The Abbot's Commission**: Father Silvestro privately asks Matteo to investigate, since the roads are closed and no magistrate will come.
- **Chapter 10: The First Feather**: Matteo finds a small falcon's feather tucked beneath Brother Tommaso's body and recognizes it as a peregrine's covert.

### Part II: The Hunt Begins (Chapters 11–20)

- **Chapter 11: Questions in the Cloister**: Matteo begins interviewing the monks and discovers that Brother Tommaso had been arguing with Giacomo the Cellarer about missing grain stores.
- **Chapter 12: The Cellarer's Ledger**: Matteo persuades Giacomo to show him the accounts and finds discrepancies — supplies vanishing into a column marked only with a cross.
- **Chapter 13: The Women's House**: Matteo crosses the courtyard to the infirmary and meets Sister Beatrice, who tells him Brother Tommaso came to her with bruises a week before his death.
- **Chapter 14: Night Walk**: Matteo breaks curfew to explore the monastery grounds and hears voices in the chapter house — a whispered argument he cannot identify.
- **Chapter 15: The Wanderer**: Elia of Fiesole arrives at the gate, ragged and feverish, claiming to have walked from Siena ahead of the plague.
- **Chapter 16: Brother Anselmo's Warning**: The old librarian tells Matteo that the torn manuscript page belongs to a text that was supposed to have been burned twenty years ago.
- **Chapter 17: The Locked Cabinet**: Matteo finds a way to examine the locked cabinet and discovers it contains copies of banned Franciscan writings on apostolic poverty.
- **Chapter 18: The Second Death**: Brother Marco, the herbalist, is found drowned in the rainwater cistern with a falcon's feather floating beside him.
- **Chapter 19: The Herbalist's Garden**: Matteo searches Brother Marco's workshop and finds a leather pouch of coins hidden inside a jar of dried chamomile.
- **Chapter 20: Suspicion Falls**: Several monks begin to whisper that Matteo, the newcomer with the falconer's past, is the killer; Giacomo openly accuses him at supper.

### Part III: The Deepening Dark (Chapters 21–30)

- **Chapter 21: Confined**: Father Silvestro confines Matteo to the scriptorium for his own safety but quietly tells him to keep investigating through Brother Anselmo.
- **Chapter 22: Anselmo's Story**: The old librarian reveals that San Colombano once sheltered a group of Spiritual Franciscans fleeing papal prosecution, and the banned texts are their legacy.
- **Chapter 23: The Tunnel**: Matteo discovers a passage beneath the chapter house leading to a vaulted chamber carved from the cliff — a hidden archive.
- **Chapter 24: The Archive**: The hidden chamber contains original manuscripts, correspondence with the Spiritual Franciscans, and a ledger recording payments from an unknown patron in Florence.
- **Chapter 25: Elia's Confession**: Elia tells Matteo he is not merely a wandering friar — he was sent by a cardinal in Avignon to locate and destroy the banned texts before they can be used against the papacy.
- **Chapter 26: The Third Death**: Brother Filippo, the porter, is found strangled in the gatehouse with a feather in his mouth; he was the only one who controlled access to the monastery.
- **Chapter 27: The Quarantine**: With the porter dead and plague rumored in the valley below, Father Silvestro seals the monastery; no one may enter or leave.
- **Chapter 28: Sister Beatrice's Discovery**: Beatrice examines the bodies and tells Matteo that the feathers are not random — they are from three different raptor species, each one rarer than the last.
- **Chapter 29: The Falconer's Code**: Matteo realizes the feather sequence follows an old falconer's ranking system his father taught him, where each bird represents a social class.
- **Chapter 30: Accusations**: Elia publicly accuses Matteo of being the killer, claiming the feather code proves it; the monks vote to lock Matteo in the cellar.

### Part IV: The Cage (Chapters 31–40)

- **Chapter 31: The Cellar**: Matteo sits in darkness among wine barrels, reviewing everything he knows, and realizes the killer is using the feathers to send a message — not about rank, but about guilt.
- **Chapter 32: A Friend in the Dark**: Brother Luca smuggles food and a candle to Matteo and passes along information he has gathered by listening at doors.
- **Chapter 33: The Ledger's Secret**: Through Luca, Matteo sends word to Anselmo asking about the Florentine patron in the hidden ledger; Anselmo identifies the family as the Corvari — Matteo's own ancestors.
- **Chapter 34: Blood Debt**: Matteo learns that his grandfather funded the Spiritual Franciscans' escape and hid their texts at San Colombano — a secret that someone is killing to protect or expose.
- **Chapter 35: Beatrice's Gambit**: Sister Beatrice argues before Father Silvestro that Matteo should be released, pointing out that the murders continued while he was confined — proving his innocence.
- **Chapter 36: Released**: Matteo is freed and finds the monastery changed — fear has fractured the community, monks sleep in groups, and Elia holds nightly sermons about divine punishment.
- **Chapter 37: The Fourth Death**: Giacomo the Cellarer is found dead in the wine cellar, poisoned, with a merlin's feather on his chest — the bird of the merchant class.
- **Chapter 38: The Poison Trail**: Matteo and Beatrice identify the poison as monkshood, grown in Brother Marco's old herb garden, and realize the killer has been planning for months.
- **Chapter 39: Elia Unmasked**: Matteo confronts Elia with evidence that the friar arrived at San Colombano not from Siena but from Florence, where he met with agents of the Corvaro family's enemies.
- **Chapter 40: The True Mission**: Elia admits he was sent not by a cardinal but by a Florentine banking family that wants the hidden documents to use as leverage against the papacy — he is a thief, not a killer.

### Part V: The Falconer's Return (Chapters 41–50)

- **Chapter 41: Process of Elimination**: Matteo maps every monk's movements on the nights of the murders and identifies three who had opportunity for all four deaths.
- **Chapter 42: The Illuminator's Secret**: Brother Luca breaks down and confesses that he has been copying the banned texts for Elia to smuggle out — but swears he killed no one.
- **Chapter 43: Anselmo's Silence**: Matteo realizes that Brother Anselmo has been evasive about the night of the first murder and presses the old man for the truth.
- **Chapter 44: The Librarian's Burden**: Anselmo reveals that he saw the killer leaving the scriptorium that first night but kept silent because he believed the murders were God's judgment on the monastery's corruption.
- **Chapter 45: The Name**: Anselmo identifies the killer as Father Silvestro himself — the abbot who commissioned Matteo's investigation to deflect suspicion from himself.
- **Chapter 46: The Motive**: Matteo pieces together the truth: Silvestro has been selling the monastery's legitimate treasures for years and killing anyone who discovered the theft, using the banned-text controversy as cover.
- **Chapter 47: The Trap**: Matteo, Beatrice, and Luca devise a plan to catch Silvestro in the act by letting him believe Matteo has found the last piece of evidence in the hidden archive.
- **Chapter 48: The Confrontation**: Silvestro comes to the archive at midnight with a knife; Matteo confronts him while Beatrice and Luca wait as witnesses behind the chamber door.
- **Chapter 49: The Reckoning**: Silvestro confesses to the murders, justifying them as necessary to protect the monastery from the chaos the texts would unleash; the monks, gathered in the chapter house, hear his words through the stone walls that carry sound like a whispering gallery.
- **Chapter 50: The Open Gate**: Spring comes. The plague passes. Matteo opens the monastery gate for the first time in months, walks into the morning light, and feels the wind on his face — the same wind that lifts a falcon.
