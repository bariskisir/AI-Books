# The Bone Garden — Outline

## Premise

A genetically modified _Candida auris_ strain — engineered for industrial enzyme production — mutates in an Atlanta high-security BSL-4 lab, becoming a weaponized pathogen that dissolves human bone from within. CDC epidemiologist Dr. Lena Cross must race against a military cover-up, a panicked government, and the pathogen's grueling 72-hour incubation window to find the source and prevent a global pandemic. The trail leads from a frozen research corpse in sublevel three to a corporate boardroom where profit margins were measured against human lives.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Lena Cross** — CDC senior epidemiologist, bone infection specialist, divorced, relentless
- **Colonel Marcus Harper** — U.S. Army Medical Research Institute for Infectious Diseases, tasked with containment and silence
- **Patient Zero — Daniel Okonkwo** — Nigerian-American lab technician at AeternaGen, first infected
- **Dr. Elaine Vesper** — AeternaGen CEO, former microbiologist, knows more than she admits
- **Special Agent Tessa Roy** — FBI Bio-Weapons unit, wary of Harper's agenda
- **Dr. Simon Park** — Lena's mentor, retired CDC pathologist, dying of pancreatic cancer

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. A frozen body arrives at the CDC morgue with chalk-white bones that crumble on touch.
2. Dr. Lena Cross is pulled from her morning lecture to consult on an unidentified decedent.
3. Initial tests show a complete absence of marrow — the bone cavity is hollow and etched.
4. Colonel Harper arrives from USAMRIID and invokes national security protocols.
5. Harper takes the samples. Lena keeps a fragment hidden in her coat lining.
6. At AeternaGen headquarters, Elaine Vesper monitors a news alert and makes a phone call.
7. Daniel Okonkwo wakes with a dull ache in his left femur that will not subside.
8. Lena visits her mentor Simon Park, who recognizes the pathology from a 1989 Soviet incident.
9. Daniel's fever spikes at 104. His wife Marie rushes him to Grady Memorial ER.
10. The ER doctor calls the CDC. Lena arrives as Daniel's X-ray shows moth-eaten femurs.
11. Harper intercepts Lena at the hospital and orders her off the case.
12. Lena defies the order and secretly cultures Daniel's blood in Simon's home lab.
13. The organism grows: a black filamentous fungus that liquefies calcium phosphate.
14. Vesper orders the AeternaGen sublevel-3 logs purged and the night shift reassigned.
15. Daniel's roommate in the isolation ward develops the same ache in his legs.
16. Harper declares a Level 4 bio-containment breach and seals the hospital wing.
17. Lena sneaks into the sealed ward disguised in a hazmat suit.
18. She interviews Daniel through glass. He remembers a needle stick six days prior.
19. AeternaGen's legal team files a restraining order against the CDC investigation.
20. The second patient's marrow liquefies overnight. He dies screaming.
21. Lena isolates the pathogen's genome and finds synthetic gene sequences.
22. Simon identifies the insert as a patent-protected AeternaGen enzyme vector.
23. FBI Agent Tessa Roy joins Lena, bypassing Harper's information blockade.
24. They break into Lena's lab at midnight — someone has deleted her genome data.
25. Lena's backup drive reveals the vector is designed for industrial paper pulping.
26. Harper threatens Lena with arrest. She threatens him with a press conference.
27. Vesper appears on CNN promising transparency while her lawyers destroy documents.
28. Daniel's condition stabilizes on an experimental antifungal — a temporary measure.
29. Lena traces the pathogen source to AeternaGen's BSL-4 waste disposal protocol.
30. A whistleblower from sublevel 3 agrees to meet Lena at a diner outside Atlanta.
31. The whistleblower is hit by a car before speaking. Tessa IDs the driver as private security.
32. Lena and Tessa fly to AeternaGen's Savannah pilot plant without authorization.
33. The plant floor manager admits they stopped using the enzyme after workers got sick.
34. Vesper orders the Savannah plant locked down. Lena and Tessa barely escape.
35. Harper shows up at Lena's apartment with a joint task force offer — his terms.
36. Lena refuses. She releases a redacted report to the Atlanta Journal-Constitution.
37. Vesper holds a board meeting. The board votes to quarantine the Atlanta campus.
38. Daniel relapses. The antifungal is losing effectiveness as the pathogen mutates.
39. Lena synthesizes a custom phage therapy targeting the synthetic gene insert.
40. Simon administers the phage to Daniel in the isolation ward. The fever breaks.
41. Harper finds Lena at Simon's house and admits the military knew about the enzyme.
42. They raided AeternaGen sublevel 3 together — the containment lab is a biohazard ruin.
43. In the sublevel 3 air handler, Lena finds a hidden spore reservoir still active.
44. Vesper is arrested at her home. She invokes her right to remain silent.
45. Lena coordinates a citywide prophylactic aerosol treatment for exposed residents.
46. The Senate calls hearings. Vesper testifies that the board knew and said nothing.
47. Lena is promoted to director of the CDC's Emerging Pathogens Unit — she declines.
48. Daniel is discharged from the hospital, walking with a cane, his femur rebuilt with titanium.
49. Harper's task force is disbanded. He leaves the military for private sector consulting.
50. Lena sits in Simon's garden, watching the sunset, the backup drive buried in her yard as insurance.
