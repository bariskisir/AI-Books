# The Candidate's Wife — Outline

## Premise

Grace Holloway, the polished wife of presidential candidate Senator Mark Holloway, has spent years playing the perfect political spouse. She smiles on cue, repeats approved talking points, and suppresses every honest thought. But when her phone autocorrects a draft tweet into a public post during a live debate broadcast, the unfiltered truth about her husband's campaign spills onto the internet. The tweet goes viral in minutes, exposing the cynicism, backroom deals, and outright lies behind the Holloway campaign. As the media descends and the campaign scrambles to contain the damage, Grace discovers that telling the truth is addictive — and that she might not want to stop.

## Core Cast

- **Grace Holloway** — The candidate's wife. Smart, observant, and deeply tired of performing. She has kept her head down for twenty years of political marriage. The accidental tweet cracks something open.
- **Senator Mark Holloway** — The presidential candidate. A man of carefully manufactured authenticity. He believes in nothing except winning. His public compassion masks a cold calculation.
- **Campaign Manager Pike** — Ruthless, competent, and perpetually stressed. Pike's job is to keep the message on track. Grace's thumb is a nightmare he cannot control.

## 50 Chapter Plan

**Part I: The Accident**

1. Grace deletes a truthful tweet instead of posting it, as she has done a thousand times before.
2. Mark delivers a speech about family values while his phone buzzes with his mistress's texts.
3. Pike runs debate prep, drilling hollow talking points into Mark's head.
4. Grace watches Mark lie on live television with a pastor's smile.
5. During a commercial break, Grace drafts a sarcastic tweet about the debate.
6. Her phone autocorrects the draft into a public post — and she hits "send" instead of "delete."
7. The tweet posts. Grace panics and tries to delete it, but a reporter has already screenshot it.
8. Within twenty minutes, the tweet trends nationally.
9. Pike wakes Mark in his hotel suite and shows him the phone.
10. Grace walks into the war room and faces three furious men.

**Part II: The Firestorm**

11. Cable news erupts with dueling panels debating Grace's tweet.
12. Mark demands Grace issue a retraction and call it a hack.
13. Grace refuses to lie for the first time in her marriage.
14. Pike stages a damage-control interview with a friendly anchor.
15. Grace accidentally reveals another truth during the interview.
16. The campaign's internal polling shows independents love her honesty.
17. Mark accuses Grace of sabotaging his lifelong ambition.
18. Grace realizes her marriage is a transaction, not a partnership.
19. Pike proposes a risky strategy: let Grace be "authentic" on a leash.
20. Grace agrees — but she has her own agenda now.

**Part III: The Leash**

21. Grace starts a controlled Twitter account managed by campaign staff.
22. She follows the script for exactly three days before breaking it.
23. She tweets support for a policy Mark openly opposes.
24. The policy position polls surprisingly well with swing voters.
25. Pike realizes Grace has become a wild card with political power.
26. Mark's mistress threatens to go public unless he pays her.
27. Grace discovers the affair through a leaked staff memo.
28. She does not confront Mark. She saves the information.
29. A debate moderator asks Grace a direct question on live television.
30. Grace tells the truth about the campaign's environmental record.

**Part IV: The Fracture**

31. Mark's donor base threatens to pull funding after Grace's honesty.
32. Pike brokers a secret meeting with the opposing campaign's manager.
33. Grace finds the meeting notes in Mark's briefcase.
34. She realizes Mark is willing to lose — as long as he can blame her.
35. Grace arranges her own media appearance without campaign approval.
36. She reveals the affair on national television.
37. The campaign implodes. Staffers quit in waves.
38. Mark begs Grace to stand by him at a press conference.
39. Grace stands at the podium and reads a prepared statement.
40. She steps away from the microphone without saying the words Mark wrote.

**Part V: The Aftermath**

41. Mark loses the primary by a landslide.
42. The media declares Grace the most interesting person in politics.
43. A publisher offers Grace a book deal to tell her story.
44. Mark moves out of the house. Grace does not cry.
45. Pike writes a tell-all memoir blaming Grace for everything.
46. Grace is invited to speak at a journalism summit.
47. She gives a speech about truth and complicity that goes viral.
48. A political action committee asks Grace to consider running for office.
49. Grace looks at the offer for a long time before putting it in a drawer.
50. Grace sits alone in a coffee shop, phone face-down, drinking her coffee in silence.
