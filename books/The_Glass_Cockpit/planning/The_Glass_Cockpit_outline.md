# The Glass Cockpit — Outline

## Premise

Captain Dana Reyes brings a crippled regional jet down onto a short runway in a thunderstorm and saves one hundred and twelve lives. She is hailed as a hero. But when the flight-data recorder is read out, the numbers contradict everything she remembers doing in the cockpit — they say she pulled the wrong control inputs, that the engines were healthy, that the airplane should never have been in danger at all. As NTSB investigator Marcus Bell digs into the discrepancy, Dana and her young first officer, Theo Okafor, follow the data into the avionics: a flight-control software fault, hidden by a manufacturer racing a certification deadline, and buried by people who decided that grounding a fleet was more expensive than a few quiet deaths. The closer they get, the more someone is willing to do to keep the cockpit a black box.

## Core Cast

- **Captain Dana Reyes** — Veteran airline captain, twenty-two years flying, calm and exact. Her memory of the flight and the recorded data do not match, and the gap is eating her.
- **First Officer Theo Okafor** — Young, sharp, a former software engineer who left coding to fly. He notices what the data nerds miss because he speaks both languages.
- **Marcus Bell** — NTSB investigator-in-charge, methodical, allergic to easy answers. He wants the truth more than he wants a clean report.
- **Sondra Mehta** — VP of engineering at the avionics manufacturer, Halcyon Aviation Systems. She knows where the fault lives. She has to decide what she is.
- **Glen Voss** — Halcyon's chief counsel and fixer. Smiles a lot. Solves problems that don't want to stay solved.
- **Priya Anand** — Software engineer at Halcyon who flagged the fault eighteen months before the crash, then was reassigned.
- **Carl Reyes** — Dana's husband, a high-school teacher, who holds the line at home while her name is dragged through the news.
- **Ed Lindqvist** — Retired test pilot, Dana's old mentor, who knows the airframe and the politics.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Landing (1–12)
1. Flight 1149 hits the storm; the controls go wrong and Dana fights the airplane down.
2. On the ground, fire trucks and survivors; Dana counts heads and goes numb.
3. Theo replays the last four minutes in his head and something doesn't fit.
4. Marcus Bell gets the call and flies to the wreckage that wasn't quite a wreck.
5. The press names Dana a hero; she can't sleep, can't stop replaying it.
6. The flight-data recorder is pulled and shipped to the lab.
7. First data readout: the FDR says the engines never failed.
8. Dana insists she lost both engines; the room goes quiet.
9. Theo, off the record, asks Dana exactly what she felt in the yoke.
10. Marcus interviews Dana formally; the gap between memory and data widens.
11. Halcyon's reps appear at the investigation, helpful and watchful.
12. Sondra reads the preliminary data and recognizes a ghost.

### Act II — The Discrepancy (13–24)
13. Theo pulls the maintenance history and finds a software load swapped weeks before.
14. Marcus learns the FDR was an older model that doesn't log the flight-control computer's commands.
15. Dana visits Ed Lindqvist; he tells her about airplanes that lie.
16. Priya Anand watches the news and recognizes the failure signature.
17. Halcyon's internal data shows the fault; Sondra is told to keep it filed.
18. Theo reconstructs the missing channel from accelerometer math.
19. The reconstruction shows the autopilot fighting Dana, not the other way.
20. Glen Voss visits the airline and suggests a tidy narrative: pilot error.
21. Marcus refuses to sign a pilot-error finding without the control data.
22. Dana is grounded pending the inquiry; the union lawyers circle.
23. Priya contacts Theo through a burner account with two words: "It's real."
24. The first warning: Theo's apartment is searched, nothing taken.

### Act III — The Fault (25–37)
25. Priya meets Theo and Dana; she explains the FCC corruption bug.
26. The bug: under a specific sensor disagreement, the computer commands the opposite of the pilot.
27. Priya flagged it eighteen months ago; she was moved to a basement project.
28. Marcus subpoenas Halcyon's software change logs.
29. Halcyon delivers logs that have been scrubbed; Marcus knows it.
30. Sondra is ordered by Voss to certify the logs as complete; she stalls.
31. Dana flies a simulator session that recreates the fault; it nearly kills the sim.
32. Ed helps them get an independent FCC unit to test on a bench.
33. The bench test reproduces the inverted command live.
34. Voss learns of the bench test; the lab's funding is suddenly threatened.
35. Priya's car is run off the road; she survives, shaken.
36. Marcus realizes the cover-up reaches the certification authority.
37. Sondra finds the original defect report with her own initials on the distribution list.

### Act IV — The Cover-Up (38–50)
38. Sondra copies the defect report and the unscrubbed logs to a drive.
39. Voss confronts Sondra; she plays loyal and buys a day.
40. Theo and Dana decode the timeline: Halcyon chose the deadline over the fix.
41. A second incident — a near-miss on another jet — surfaces from the data.
42. Marcus calls a hearing; Halcyon sends lawyers, not engineers.
43. Sondra agrees to testify; Voss makes a final offer, then a threat.
44. Voss arranges an "accident" for the bench unit; the lab burns.
45. They moved the unit; the fire destroys nothing that mattered.
46. Sondra hands Marcus the drive in a parking garage; she's followed.
47. Confrontation: Voss corners Dana and Theo; the recording is already uploaded.
48. The hearing: the unscrubbed logs and bench test put the fault on record.
49. Aftermath — the fleet is grounded, Halcyon indicted, Voss arrested; Dana cleared.
50. Dana returns to the cockpit; she trusts the airplane and her own hands again.
