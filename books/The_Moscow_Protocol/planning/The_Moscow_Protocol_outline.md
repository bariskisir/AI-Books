# The Moscow Protocol — Outline

## Premise

Disgraced MI6 agent Alex Gray, exiled to a desk job after a failed operation in Prague, is sent to Moscow for a routine dead drop — pick up a package from a low-level source and return. But the intelligence inside the package does not point at Russian targets. It points at MI6 itself. Someone inside his own service is running a parallel operation, and the information Gray now carries makes him a target for both the FSB and the people he once served. With no one to trust and a city that swallows men whole, Gray must navigate Moscow's shadow world to survive long enough to learn the truth.

## Core Cast

- **Alex Gray** — Disgraced MI6 field officer, brilliant but impulsive, exiled after Prague
- **Irina Volkov** — FSB captain assigned to the Gray case; sharp, disillusioned, running her own play
- **Control** — MI6 Moscow Station chief; Gray's former mentor, now his handler on this operation
- **Viktor Orlov** — Russian oligarch with ties to both FSB and British intelligence; the dead drop source
- **Sarah Finch** — MI6 analyst in London; Gray's only remaining ally inside the service
- **Dimitri Krasny** — FSB colonel; Irina's superior, brutal and politically ambitious

## Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Fallen

1. Alex Gray sits in a windowless London office, reviewing surveillance transcripts, when a summons from Control arrives.
2. Control offers Gray a path back: a simple dead drop in Moscow. Take it or stay chained to a desk forever.
3. Gray lands at Sheremetyevo. The cold hits him like a wall. He feels eyes before he sees them.
4. At the safe flat in Tverskaya, Gray checks the dead drop instructions: a train station locker, midnight.
5. Gray makes the pickup at Kursky Station. The package is lighter than expected. He feels watched.
6. Back at the flat, Gray opens the package. The documents are not about Russian assets — they list British intelligence officers.
7. Gray burns the documents but photographs them first. His hands shake. This is a trap or a truth he cannot walk away from.
8. Irina Volkov is assigned to track the unknown British asset spotted at Kursky Station. She studies Gray's file.
9. Gray tries to signal London using an emergency protocol. The channel goes dead before he can transmit.
10. Control calls Gray's burner. The conversation is clipped, wrong. Gray realizes Control knew what was in the package.

### Act II: The City of Secrets

11. Gray ditches the safe flat and moves to a budget hotel near Taganka. He goes dark.
12. Irina traces Gray to the hotel district through a traffic camera. She decides to follow alone, without reporting.
13. Gray visits the British Embassy, hoping to find a clean channel. The security attaché turns him away.
14. Irina approaches Gray in a metro station. She tells him she knows who he is and offers a deal: information for safety.
15. Gray does not trust her. They part. He doubles back three times before settling in a new safe house.
16. Viktor Orlov, the original source, reaches out through a dead drop of his own. He wants to meet.
17. Gray meets Orlov in a Georgian restaurant. Orlov claims the document came from inside MI6 — a leak, not a plant.
18. Irina reports to Krasny, who orders her to bring Gray in — alive. She begins to suspect Krasny knows more than he says.
19. Gray deciphers part of the photographed document: a list of five MI6 officers with ties to a private military contractor.
20. Krasny meets a known British asset in a Moscow hotel. Irina follows him and photographs the meeting.

### Act III: The Trust Fall

21. Sarah Finch in London notices Gray's emergency signal failure. She begins discreet inquiries.
22. Gray contacts Irina. He agrees to the deal: he will share what he knows if she gets him a clean line to London.
23. Irina arranges a meeting with a GRU defector who has a secure satellite phone. The price is one name from Gray's document.
24. The exchange happens in a derelict factory. Gray gets the sat phone. He calls Sarah Finch.
25. Sarah tells Gray that Control has reported him as a defector. MI6 has a kill order out on him.
26. Gray realizes the document is a purge list — someone is eliminating officers who know about an off-book operation.
27. Irina is summoned to Krasny's office. He knows she met Gray. He gives her forty-eight hours to deliver him.
28. Gray and Irina go to ground together in a rented dacha outside the city. An uneasy alliance forms.
29. Krasny sends a team to the dacha. Gray and Irina escape through a frozen drainage ditch.
30. The satellite phone rings. Sarah has traced the off-book operation to a man named Pendleton — a former MI6 director.

### Act IV: The Unraveling

31. Irina is wounded in the escape. Gray gets her to a back-alley doctor in the suburbs.
32. Gray calls the number Sarah found for Pendleton. A woman answers. Pendleton has been dead for three years.
33. The woman on the phone is Pendleton's daughter. She tells Gray her father was killed in a car accident that was no accident.
34. Gray connects the dots: Pendleton ran a covert operation called Moscow Protocol. The document is the operation's kill switch.
35. Krasny brings Control to a secret location. The two men talk. They have been working together all along.
36. Irina recovers. She tells Gray that Krasny has been feeding misinformation to London for a decade. The partnership with Control is the real treason.
37. Gray and Irina decide to go after the evidence they need: the Moscow Protocol records in Krasny's safe at FSB headquarters.
38. Sarah sends Gray the schematics for the FSB building from a decade-old construction file.
39. Gray and Irina execute the infiltration. They reach the safe but the documents are gone.
40. Krasny's office door opens. He is waiting for them with a smile. It was a trap.

### Act V: The Reckoning

41. Krasny does not kill them. He wants to make a deal — Control has become a liability and Krasny needs a witness.
42. Gray refuses. Krasny leaves them in a holding cell. Irina picks the lock with a bobby pin.
43. They escape through the basement. Gray finds the original Moscow Protocol files in an archive room — Krasny never destroyed them.
44. Gray copies the files. He and Irina flee through the Moscow sewers. The city above is waking up.
45. Krasny realizes the archive room was compromised. He orders a citywide lockdown.
46. Gray sends the files to Sarah via the sat phone. She begins distributing them through secure channels.
47. Control receives word that London has opened an investigation. He makes his own escape plan — but Krasny blocks him.
48. Gray and Irina reach the Finnish embassy. Irina uses her FSB credentials to get them inside.
49. From Finland, Gray calls a journalist in London with the full story. Control is arrested at Heathrow.
50. Gray watches the Moscow skyline from a train crossing the Finnish border. Irina sits beside him. Neither knows what comes next, but for the first time, they are free.
