# The Indigo Pattern - Outline

## Premise

When a model collapses backstage during the revival show of the legendary Maison Arel, textile forensic analyst Mina Tor is hired to determine whether a vintage indigo gown carried a toxic dye residue or whether someone weaponized the house archive. The answer lies in cut marks, mordant crystals, forged fabric labels, and a missing pattern book that could prove the fashion house's most famous silhouette was stolen from a dismissed atelier worker. As the runway deadline becomes a succession fight, Mina must read cloth as evidence before the archive is cleaned into myth.

## Core Cast

- Mina Tor - Adult textile forensic analyst who reads fibers, dyes, wear, and seams as physical testimony.
- Oren Vale - Adult backstage production manager who knows every rack, label, and panic in the fashion house.
- Celia Arel - Adult creative director trying to revive her grandmother's couture empire.
- Brigitte Arel - Adult board chair and family guardian who controls the archive with ceremonial ruthlessness.
- Luc Renard - Adult investor whose rescue money depends on a triumphant revival show.
- Anouk Saye - Adult model who collapses after wearing the indigo gown and refuses to be turned into publicity.
- Sabine Holt - Adult retired pattern cutter who claims the house stole its signature cut from her mentor.
- Dr. Elias Morn - Adult toxicologist who helps Mina separate dye chemistry from deliberate contamination.
- Talia Voss - Adult archive assistant whose neat records hide erased loans and substituted labels.
- Jules Fane - Adult fashion journalist whose old interview tapes contain a missing admission.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Mina enters Maison Arel after Anouk collapses and notices blue dust on the dressing-room latch.
2. Oren reconstructs the backstage sequence and the handling of the indigo gown.
3. Celia asks Mina to prove the gown is safe before the revival show dies.
4. Elias tests the gown and finds toxic residue that does not belong to historical indigo.
5. Mina studies the seams and sees the bodice was opened after archival conservation.
6. Brigitte limits archive access and insists the house's provenance is beyond dispute.
7. Anouk remembers a bitter almond smell and a scratch at the neckline.
8. Sabine claims the gown's pattern descends from stolen atelier drafts.
9. Mina finds a forged storage label on the indigo gown box.
10. Talia admits the archive loan log was rewritten for investors.
11. Mina maps dye particles from latch, glove, gown, and runway carpet.
12. Oren discovers a missing steamer head from the dressing room.
13. Elias identifies cyanogenic compound carried in a modern finishing spray.
14. Celia reveals the revival collection depends on the indigo silhouette.
15. Jules shares interview tapes with a late seamstress describing theft.
16. Mina compares old muslin pattern pieces and finds matching cut notches.
17. Brigitte announces the show will continue with another model.
18. Anouk leaves hospital and demands the gown be treated as evidence.
19. Mina traces the finishing spray to a supplier owned by Luc's fund.
20. Talia shows Mina a hidden shelf of relabeled pattern books.
21. The team builds a board of cloth, money, archive movement, and show pressure.
22. Oren finds backstage camera gaps created by a wardrobe-system update.
23. Sabine identifies the missing pattern book by a coffee stain and blue thread.
24. Mina proves the gown was dusted after it left conservation.
25. Celia confronts Brigitte about the stolen silhouette and family mythology.
26. Elias tests a steamer residue and matches it to the finishing spray.
27. Luc threatens to pull investment unless the contamination is called accidental.
28. Jules finds an old tape naming Brigitte as the person who removed the drafts.
29. Talia admits she altered logs under Brigitte's order but denies poisoning anyone.
30. Mina discovers Anouk was targeted because she asked about the scratch before going onstage.
31. The missing pattern book appears in Luc's safe as collateral for the investment.
32. Brigitte claims she protected the house from a dead founder's theft.
33. Mina reconstructs the backstage contamination path with fabric swatches.
34. Oren catches a dresser destroying blue-stained gloves.
35. The dresser says Luc's assistant paid her to switch gown bags.
36. Celia cancels the public show and turns it into a closed evidence review.
37. Luc frames Talia as a rogue archivist who hoped to sell the pattern book.
38. Talia produces duplicate metadata proving Luc knew the book's value.
39. Mina finds Brigitte's old needle case with the same indigo dust.
40. Sabine testifies that the stolen silhouette belonged to her mentor.
41. Mina confronts Brigitte, who admits theft but not poisoning.
42. Elias proves the poison dose required the steamer switch, not archive dust.
43. Oren traces the steamer switch to Luc's assistant and a service corridor badge.
44. Luc admits using the pattern book as leverage but blames Brigitte for contamination.
45. Mina links Luc's fund to the finishing spray and the show insurance clause.
46. During the closed review, Luc tries to remove the pattern book from evidence.
47. Anouk identifies the assistant's glove from the scratch at her neckline.
48. Celia publicly credits the stolen pattern and withdraws the false house myth.
49. Mina preserves the indigo gown as evidence and archive object, not costume.
50. Maison Arel reopens with the pattern book under shared custody and the blue dust finally named.
