# The Palermo Exchange — Outline

## Premise

Luca Ferrara, a money launderer for the Vitale family in Palermo, loses a suitcase containing two million euros of mafia cash during a routine handoff. Don Vitale, his boss, gives him exactly seventy-two hours to recover the money or face execution. As Luca scrambles through Palermo's underworld following a trail of small-time crooks and middlemen, Inspector Rosi of the Postal Police picks up the same thread and begins closing in. With the clock running and both the law and the mafia at his heels, Luca discovers the theft was not random — someone inside the organization set him up.

## Core Cast

- **Luca Ferrara** — A money launderer for the Vitale family. Forty years old, competent, quiet. He has been trying to find a way out for years but never found the exit. Now the exit has found him.
- **Don Matteo Vitale** — The head of the Vitale family. Old-school Sicilian mafia. He trusted Luca with the cash, and that trust is now a blade.
- **Inspector Chiara Rosi** — Postal Police, financial crimes division. She has been tracking Vitale's money for three years. The theft of the cash is the thread she needs to pull the whole tapestry down.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Loss (Chapters 1–10)

1. Luca collects a case of wine from a cafe in the Kalsa district — inside, two million euros bundled in newspaper.
2. He drives to a warehouse in Brancaccio for the handoff. The contact does not appear.
3. While Luca waits, a scooter stops behind his car. He does not see the thief until it is too late.
4. The case is gone. Luca searches the streets. Nothing.
5. Don Vitale receives the news in his garden. His voice stays calm. The clock starts.
6. Inspector Rosi gets a ping — a large cash transaction flagged by a bank, the first thread.
7. Luca works his contacts. A street name surfaces: a man called Scarpuzza.
8. He finds Scarpuzza in a bar near Ballarò market. The man knows nothing but points elsewhere.
9. The trail leads to a shuttered garage in Zisa. Someone was there before him.
10. Rosi pulls traffic camera footage. She sees Luca's car. She has a face.

### Act II — The Trail (Chapters 11–20)

11. Luca finds a receipt in the garage — a ferry ticket to Naples, unused.
12. He goes to the port. A dockworker remembers a man with a heavy suitcase.
13. The description matches a known thief: Bruno Mancuso.
14. Bruno's girlfriend works at a laundromat in Sampolo. Luca waits for her shift to end.
15. She does not know where Bruno is. She knows what he did. She is afraid.
16. Rosi interviews the girlfriend before Luca can return. She learns about Bruno.
17. Luca traces Bruno through a fence who handled the first fifty thousand.
18. The fence gives up a detail: Bruno was hired. He did not plan the job.
19. The money was meant for a broker in Naples named Cataldo.
20. Luca drives to Naples. The city is larger, the time shorter.

### Act III — The Truth (Chapters 21–30)

21. Cataldo's office is locked. His neighbor says he fled two days ago.
22. Luca calls Vitale. Vitale extends no mercy. Forty-eight hours remain.
23. Rosi gets a warrant for Luca's phone. She tracks him to Naples.
24. Luca finds Cataldo's mistress in a cafe in the Vomero district.
25. She gives up Cataldo's hiding place: a farmhouse outside Pompeii.
26. Luca drives to Pompeii. He searches among the ruins, the tourists moving like ghosts.
27. He corners Cataldo in a crumbling villa. Cataldo names the man who paid him.
28. The name is Ferruccio — Don Vitale's own driver and bodyguard.
29. Rosi arrives in Pompeii. Luca sees her car and slips away through the ruins.
30. He calls Rosi. A risk. He tells her he will give her Vitale if she gives him time.

### Act IV — The Race (Chapters 31–40)

31. Rosi agrees to twenty-four hours. She wants Vitale's books.
32. Ferruccio learns that Luca is following the trail. He moves the money.
33. Luca returns to Palermo. The city feels different now — smaller, more dangerous.
34. He watches Vitale's villa from a hillside. Ferruccio comes and goes.
35. He breaks into Ferruccio's apartment. Finds a key, a safe deposit box number.
36. The box is in a bank in Mondello. Empty. Ferruccio was faster.
37. Rosi arrests a low-level Vitale soldier on an unrelated charge. The soldier talks.
38. She learns about the seventy-two-hour deadline. She knows Luca is running out of time.
39. Luca stakes out Ferruccio's girlfriend. Follows her to a storage unit in Carini.
40. Inside the unit: the suitcase, the money. All of it.

### Act V — The Exchange (Chapters 41–50)

41. Luca does not take the money. He closes the unit and steps back.
42. He calls Ferruccio. Tells him he has the money. Arranges a meet.
43. He calls Rosi. Tells her where the meet will be.
44. The meet is at an abandoned fish market at the old port.
45. Ferruccio arrives with two men. Luca arrives with nothing but his phone.
46. The deal: Ferruccio pays Luca to disappear. Luca refuses.
47. Rosi's team moves in. Ferruccio is arrested. The money is recovered.
48. Luca is taken into custody. Rosi questions him for six hours.
49. He agrees to testify. He gives her Vitale's operation, names, dates, accounts.
50. Luca enters witness protection. He watches Palermo fade from a plane window. He does not know if he will ever return.
