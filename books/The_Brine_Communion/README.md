# The Brine Communion

**Genre:** Coastal Gothic / Folk Horror  
**Setting:** Porthallow, Norfolk salt marshes, present day  
**Word Count:** ~27,000 words over 50 chapters

## Synopsis

When marine biologist Dr. Iris Vane returns to the isolated Norfolk salt-marsh village of Porthallow to settle her estranged mother's estate, she finds the community in the grip of a strange collective fervour. The locals participate in a ritual called the Brine Communion — drinking cups of marsh-brine during the full moon, which grants them waking visions of a drowned congregation beneath the peat.

Iris discovers her mother was not merely a reclusive marsh-lover but the latest in a long line of Salt-Keepers who maintain a covenant between the village and something living in the deep peat layers. As the boundary between geological memory and the living present begins to collapse, Iris must reckon with what her mother hid from her, what the village has sacrificed to keep the covenant, and what the marsh truly wants in return — before the spring tide isolates Porthallow completely and the peat takes its due regardless.

## Characters

- **Dr. Iris Vane** — Marine biologist returning to her childhood home
- **Elias Carrick** — The current Salt-Keeper, keeper of marsh traditions
- **Morgelyn 'Morg' Salt** — Fisherwoman and skeptic
- **Reverend Caleb Thorne** — Village priest with a syncretic theology
- **DI Kieran Ash** — Outside investigator from Norwich
