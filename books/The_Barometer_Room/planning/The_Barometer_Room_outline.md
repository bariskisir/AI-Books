# The Barometer Room - Outline

## Premise

After a late-season coastal storm floods a harbor district that was told not to evacuate, meteorological data auditor Tessa Vale is brought into the old barometer room to compare analog pressure charts against the city's digital forecast archive. The official record shows the storm weakened before landfall; the inked barograph says pressure fell faster than any public warning admitted. As Tessa works with a harbor pilot, a municipal emergency planner, and a retired weather observer, she uncovers altered sensor feeds, suppressed buoy data, and a port insurance scheme that made delay profitable.

## Core Cast

- Tessa Vale - Adult meteorological data auditor who specializes in reconciling analog weather instruments with digital archives.
- Cal Ivers - Adult harbor pilot who warned the port to close and was ignored.
- June Narek - Adult municipal emergency planner blamed for a late evacuation order.
- Dr. Petra Sloane - Adult retired weather observer who maintained the analog barograph for forty years.
- Milo Grant - Adult port authority director whose revenue depended on keeping berths open.
- Arlen Pike - Adult marine insurer whose storm-loss clauses reward a narrow official timeline.
- Nia Sol - Adult buoy technician whose offshore sensor feed vanished during the critical hour.
- Roan Bell - Adult ferry captain who moved passengers through worsening water.
- Elise Mar - Adult city data contractor who managed the forecast dashboard.
- Judge Corin Hale - Adult inquest judge overseeing the storm-response record.

9. Arlen Pike produces insurance language tied to the recorded time of storm intensification.
10. Elise Mar says the dashboard used approved state data and denies manual edits.

13. June finds an emergency alert draft that was never sent.

15. Nia recovers partial buoy telemetry from a maintenance tablet.
16. The recovered telemetry proves B-17 warned forty minutes before the dashboard changed.
17. Milo pressures June to accept procedural blame to protect city funding.
18. Tessa finds dashboard screenshots from a newsroom showing an earlier severe forecast.
19. Elise admits a contractor patch allowed forecast smoothing but says it was automated.
20. Arlen's insurance schedule shows payouts change if intensification is recorded after port closure time.

25. Tessa traces the smoothing patch to a port-funded dashboard upgrade.

28. June discovers her alert draft was held in a supervisor queue tied to Milo's office.

31. The port authority releases a statement blaming sensor noise and human caution.
32. Cal testifies that pilots use pressure drops because ships cannot wait for press releases.

34. The cut strip matches the missing physical evidence Milo claimed never existed.
35. Nia identifies the relay command that disabled buoy alerts as coming from the contractor VPN.
36. Elise says Milo requested "visual stability" on the dashboard during the storm.
37. Arlen denies directing changes but admits receiving live dashboard exports.
38. Tessa matches export timestamps to insurance calls about berth exposure.
39. June refuses a settlement that would mark the evacuation delay as her judgment error.
40. Judge Hale orders a live reconstruction of the storm data timeline.
41. Tessa reconstructs the pressure fall minute by minute from analog, buoy, tug, and ferry records.
42. The reconstruction shows the port should have closed before final cargo loading.
43. Milo admits delaying a closure recommendation but denies altering data.
44. Elise identifies Arlen's analyst as the person who requested the smoothing patch.
45. Arlen argues financial modeling is not operational interference.
46. Petra reads the intact barograph trace into the inquest record.
47. June's unsent alert is released, showing the warning was drafted in time.
48. Judge Hale finds the official storm timeline unreliable and refers data tampering for prosecution.
49. The harbor adopts analog cross-checks and automatic buoy alert preservation.
50. Tessa leaves the barometer room with the old chart archived beside the corrected digital storm record.
