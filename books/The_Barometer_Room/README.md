# The Barometer Room

## Overview

When a coastal storm floods the harbor district before the public warning arrives, meteorological data auditor Tessa Vale is asked to reconcile the official archive with field reports that refuse to fit. In an old municipal barometer room, a brass instrument's ink trace shows pressure falling faster and earlier than the city's polished dashboard admits. As Tessa follows buoy telemetry, tugboat logs, alert queues, insurance exports, and a torn strip of paper, the disaster resolves into something colder than forecast error.

**Genre:** Meteorological Data Fraud / Storm Forecasting Suspense

## Story Engines

A harbor flood investigation becomes a public-record case when analog pressure traces, buoy cache data, and ferry logs prove that a warning delay came from altered systems rather than uncertain weather.

## Key Themes

- Public warning systems fail when display polish replaces raw evidence.
- Analog instruments can preserve truth when digital archives are managed.
- Financial exposure becomes dangerous when it reaches into emergency data.
- Accountability can hold individual hesitation and institutional manipulation at the same time.
