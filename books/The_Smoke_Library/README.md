# The Smoke Library

## Overview

Mara Voss is hired to inspect the ruins of the Halcyon Athenaeum after a night fire turns a pending redevelopment deal into a massive insurance claim. The official cause is old wiring, but the smoke sits too low on the walls, the alarm went quiet before heat arrived, and a dead watchman's shelf list points to books that should not have moved. Working with insurance analyst Theo Pallis, conservator Celine Orra, and assistant fire marshal Imani Crowe, Mara follows the evidence through forged provenance, doctored sprinkler heads, hidden vaults, and a charitable foundation that turned stewardship into profit.

**Genre:** Rare Book Provenance Mystery / Cultural Property Suspense
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~12000
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

A burned private library is treated as an insurance loss until its surviving books reveal altered labels, false donor histories, and a watchman's final list of shelves. The investigation moves from char patterns to cultural property fraud, asking whether an institution can preserve memory after its stewards sell the truth out from under it.

## Key Themes

- Provenance is custody, not decoration.
- Fire can erase surfaces while exposing buried histories.
- Institutions fail when loyalty becomes permission to lie.
- Technical evidence matters most when public pressure wants a clean ending.

## Characters

**Mara Voss** - Adult fire investigator and former firefighter who trusts physical evidence over official pressure.

**Theo Pallis** - Adult insurance analyst who follows debt, appraisals, and shell accounts with quiet precision.

**Celine Orra** - Adult rare-book conservator who reads smoke damage, bindings, and erased bookplates as evidence.

**Imani Crowe** - Adult assistant fire marshal determined not to close the city file around a false accidental origin.

**Faye Loring** - Adult head librarian whose loyalty to the Athenaeum curdles into concealment.

**Gideon Vale** - Adult developer and foundation patron whose civic language protects private gain.

**Elise Marr** - Adult board treasurer and collector who treats the library's history as property to be curated and sold.

**Nadine Cross** - Adult archivist dismissed after challenging forged provenance records.
