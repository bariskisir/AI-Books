## Description

A donor codicil appears just as a concert hall foundation considers selling its rare choir library. The document names an oxblood-titled cantata as private property, and the manuscript seems to confirm the claim.

Music conservator Lena Orsay finds modern thread, synthetic red pigment, a copied soprano line, and an accession stamp someone tried to erase. With a choir director, a foundation lawyer, a pigment chemist, and an elderly copyist, she follows the music back to a public gift that was never meant to be sold.
