# The Seventh Precinct

## Premise

Detective Joe Marlowe, forty-eight years old and drinking himself through the final stretch of a ruined career, catches the case nobody else wants: a showgirl named Lola Vance walked out of the Silver Room with a bag that belongs to the city's most dangerous men. The Seventh Precinct wants him off the force. The missing money makes him a target. And Lola Vance is not what she appears to be. Somebody burned a body in a lot behind the precinct, and every clue leads back to the one place Joe swore he would never go.

## Core Cast

- **Joe Marlowe** — A washed-up detective, 48, cynical, sharp when he wants to be, drinking himself toward early retirement.
- **Lola Vance** — A showgirl who walked out of the Silver Room with fifty thousand dollars that was never hers. Young, smart, born into a bad hand.
- **Captain Reilly** — Marlowe's boss. Old-school. Wants Marlowe gone but needs the case closed.
- **Frank D'Amato** — Owner of the Silver Room. Connected. Polite on the surface, animal underneath.
- **Detective Ana Cortez** — The only cop at the Seventh who still nods at Marlowe. Younger, sharper, cleaner.
- **Victor Strake** — D'Amato's enforcer. Silent. Efficient. The kind of man who leaves no witnesses.
- **Tommy Vance** — Lola's younger brother. A kid with a gambling problem and a big mouth.
- **The Man in the Gray Hat** — A fixer who appears whenever things get complicated. No name. No past.

## Chapter Plan

1. Joe Marlowe sits alone in a bar at 3AM, staring at a glass he cannot afford to refill.
2. The body is found behind the Seventh Precinct. Marlowe is the only detective who answers.
3. Captain Reilly assigns Marlowe to the case as a punishment. Take it or quit.
4. Marlowe visits the crime scene. A burned corpse, a single clue: a matchbook from the Silver Room.
5. The Silver Room. Marlowe meets Frank D'Amato, who smiles too much.
6. Lola Vance is introduced as a dancer who vanished three nights ago.
7. D'Amato makes it clear Marlowe is not welcome. Victor Strake watches from the shadows.
8. Marlowe returns to his office. Finds a bullet casing on his desk. A message.
9. Detective Ana Cortez warns Marlowe that Internal Affairs has its eye on him.
10. Marlowe pieces together Lola's last known movements. She was with a man named Tommy.
11. Tommy Vance is found beaten in an alley. He says Lola took the money and ran.
12. Marlowe arrests Tommy for questioning. The kid is scared of something bigger.
13. Captain Reilly pressures Marlowe to wrap it up. Close the book or be closed.
14. Marlowe visits Lola's apartment. The place has been tossed. He finds a photograph.
15. The photograph shows Lola with a man in a gray hat. No one knows who he is.
16. Victor Strake follows Marlowe home. A quiet warning. Stay away from D'Amato.
17. Marlowe talks to a retired hooker who knew Lola. She gives him a name: The Filmmaker.
18. The Filmmaker runs a side business in blackmail films. Lola was a subject.
19. Marlowe watches one of the films. He recognizes a city councilman on the screen.
20. The councilman's office calls. An offer. Money to disappear. Marlowe refuses.
21. Strake breaks into Marlowe's apartment. They fight. Marlowe wins by luck, not skill.
22. Marlowe hides the photograph and the film reel in a locker at the bus station.
23. Ana Cortez brings Marlowe a file. The Man in the Gray Hat works for the city.
24. Marlowe confronts Captain Reilly. Reilly knows more than he is telling.
25. Reilly admits the money was a payoff from city hall to D'Amato. Lola stole it.
26. Marlowe realizes Lola is not a thief. She is a witness running for her life.
27. A tip leads Marlowe to a motel on the edge of town. Lola was there two days ago.
28. The motel clerk describes a man following her. Gray hat. Always the gray hat.
29. Marlowe finds a torn page from a ledger in the motel trash. Names. Dates. Payments.
30. D'Amato puts a price on Marlowe's head. The word goes out on the street.
31. Marlowe goes underground. He sleeps in his car. He keeps moving.
32. Ana Cortez is pulled off the case. Reilly claims she is compromised.
33. Marlowe deciphers the ledger. It connects the city councilman to a land deal.
34. The land deal involves the old waterfront. The Seventh Precinct sits on it.
35. Marlowe understands. The precinct itself is the prize. D'Amato wants the land.
36. Lola surfaces. She calls Marlowe from a payphone. She wants to meet.
37. They meet in a diner at 2AM. Lola is terrified. She has the money and the proof.
38. Lola tells Marlowe the whole story: D'Amato, the councilman, the murder.
39. Strake finds them. A shootout in the diner. Lola and Marlowe escape.
40. Marlowe hides Lola in a flophouse. He goes to face Reilly one last time.
41. Reilly draws a gun. He has been on D'Amato's payroll from the start.
42. Reilly admits he killed the man in the lot. It was a dirty cop who talked.
43. Marlowe records the confession on his phone. Reilly does not know.
44. Victor Strake arrives at the precinct. A three-way standoff in Reilly's office.
45. Strake turns on Reilly. D'Amato is cleaning house. Reilly is the mess.
46. Reilly shoots Strake. Marlowe disarms Reilly. The confession is uploaded.
47. The councilman is arrested. D'Amato is picked up at the Silver Room.
48. Lola is placed in witness protection. She asks Marlowe to come with her.
49. Marlowe says no. His city is broken but it is his. He stays.
50. Marlowe sits in the same bar. A new glass. A different morning. The door opens.
