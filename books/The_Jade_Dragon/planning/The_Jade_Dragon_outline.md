# The Jade Dragon -- Outline

## Premise

Master Liang, once the most celebrated martial artist in the Jade Kingdom, was disgraced and exiled after his
student died during a forbidden technique he refused to condemn. Now the Warlord Kang -- a man who sealed a pact
with a demon named the Emperor of Ashen Thorns -- marches across the land with an army of shadow-bent warriors,
breaking every school and temple in his path. To stop Kang, Liang must master the forbidden Fifth Style of the
Jade Dragon, a technique that has killed every practitioner who attempted it. His sole remaining disciple, Mei,
struggles to keep him anchored to his humanity as the style demands he surrender it.

## Core Cast

- **Master Liang** -- Disgraced martial arts master. Once the heir of the Jade Dragon school, now a bitter
  exile. Quiet, deliberate, burdened. He believes mastery is worth any cost.
- **Mei** -- Liang's last disciple. Young, stubborn, sharp-eyed. She sees the man behind the master and refuses
  to let him become a weapon without a heart.
- **The Demon Emperor (Emperor of Ashen Thorns)** -- An ancient demon of pride and consumption. Speaks in
  silken promises. Feeds on the will of those who bargain with him.
- **Warlord Kang** -- The Demon Emperor's vessel. Once a nobody soldier, now a warlord carved from fury and
  spite. His armor is fused to his flesh. His army marches under a banner of bone.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I -- The Exile (Chapters 1--10)

1. **The Empty School** -- Liang returns to his abandoned school at dusk. Dust and silence. The training
   courtyard is overgrown. He touches the cracked jade bell.
2. **What Was Lost** -- Flashback: the student who died, the council's judgment, the brand of disgrace. Liang
   accepts exile without protest.
3. **Mei Arrives** -- A girl appears at Liang's mountain hut, demanding to be taught. He refuses. She does not
   leave.
4. **The First Lesson** -- Liang tests Mei with an impossible task. She surprises him. He agrees to one lesson
   only.
5. **Kang's March** -- Far south, Warlord Kang burns the Serpent Temple. His shadow-bent soldiers move like
   smoke. The Demon Emperor whispers approval.
6. **News on the Wind** -- A refugee reaches the mountain. The Jade Dragon school is named on Kang's kill list.
   Liang's past has found him.
7. **Mei's Second Lesson** -- Liang teaches Mei the Breath of Still Water, the first stance of the Jade Dragon.
   She struggles. He sees himself in her.
8. **The Pact Unfolds** -- Kang offers the Demon Emperor a village in exchange for deeper power. The Emperor
   takes more than offered.
9. **Council of Ghosts** -- Liang meditates and sees the spirits of past Jade Dragon masters. They warn him of
   the Fifth Style. He asks how to learn it. They fall silent.
10. **The Road** -- Liang decides to return to the lowlands. Mei follows. He does not send her back.

### Part II -- The Forbidden Style (Chapters 11--20)

11. **The Black Frost Village** -- Liang and Mei reach a village Kang's army passed through. Nothing lives.
    The ground is cold. A single jade token lies in the ashes.
12. **The First Shadow** -- A shadow-bent scout attacks. Liang defeats him, but the shadow-rot spreads from the
    wound. He cuts it out with his own blade.
13. **Mei's Fury** -- Mei confronts Liang about his recklessness. He tells her the Fifth Style demands
    absolute detachment. She calls him a coward hiding behind philosophy.
14. **The Hermit of the Burning Cliff** -- Liang seeks an old master who once attempted the Fifth Style. The
    hermit lives in a cave, his mind broken. He babbles fragments of the technique.
15. **The Three Keys** -- The hermit speaks of three keys: Sever the Heart, Empty the Vessel, Become the
    Dragon. Liang writes them down. The hermit weeps.
16. **Kang's Throne** -- Kang receives an envoy from the capital. He kills the envoy and sends the head back.
    The Demon Emperor laughs. The war is close.
17. **Sever the Heart** -- Liang tries the first key. He forces himself to feel nothing. The world goes grey.
    He nearly strangles Mei in a training accident.
18. **Mei Stands** -- Mei does not run. She tells Liang she will not let him become a monster. She stays.
19. **The Empty Village** -- Kang's army moves north. They leave empty villages like a trail of breadcrumbs.
    Liang realizes Kang is drawing him out.
20. **The Jade Compass** -- Liang finds an old Jade Dragon compass in a ruined shrine. It points not north, but
    toward the heart of Kang's territory.

### Part III -- The War Approaches (Chapters 21--30)

21. **The Conscript Camp** -- Liang and Mei enter a camp of refugees being forced into Kang's army. Liang
    teaches them a simple stance to survive.
22. **The Overseer** -- A shadow-bent captain challenges Liang. Liang uses only the first three styles and
    wins. The refugees see hope.
23. **Mei Teaches** -- Mei takes over training the refugees while Liang meditates. She finds purpose in their
    faces.
24. **Kang's Demon** -- Kang struggles with the Demon Emperor's hunger. The Emperor wants Liang's soul above
    all. Kang grows jealous of the demon's interest.
25. **The Price of the Fifth** -- Liang attempts the second key: Empty the Vessel. His qi drains into the
    earth. He collapses. Mei drags him to shelter.
26. **The Healer's Lie** -- A traveling healer treats Liang. She recognizes the signs of the Fifth Style. She
    warns Mei that no one survives the full path.
27. **Mei's Choice** -- Mei steals Liang's notes on the Fifth Style and burns them. Liang wakes in fury. Their
    confrontation cracks the mountain path.
28. **The Bridge of Tears** -- Kang's army reaches the Jade Bridge. The defenders hold for three days. Liang
    watches from a ridge and knows he must act.
29. **The Whisper of Scales** -- Liang dreams of the Jade Dragon. It coils around him and speaks: "To become
    me, you must cease to be you." Liang agrees.
30. **The Third Key** -- Liang begins Become the Dragon. His skin takes a pale green sheen. His eyes change.
    Mei watches in terror.

### Part IV -- The Dragon Rises (Chapters 31--40)

31. **The Fall of the Bridge** -- Kang takes the Jade Bridge. Liang arrives too late. The river runs red. He
    feels nothing -- and that terrifies him.
32. **The Demon's Summons** -- The Demon Emperor reaches through Kang and speaks to Liang directly. He offers
    power without the Fifth Style. Liang refuses.
33. **Mei's Technique** -- Mei develops a counterbalance: a set of breathing exercises that anchor the heart.
    She begs Liang to try them first. He refuses.
34. **The Army of the Broken** -- The refugees Liang trained choose to fight. They call themselves the
    School of the Last Breath. Mei leads them.
35. **Kang's Advance** -- Kang's army marches on the capital. The Emperor grows impatient. Kang makes a
    desperate bargain: his remaining humanity for victory.
36. **The Half-Dragon** -- Liang completes the Third Key but cannot close the transformation. He is neither
    man nor dragon. His power is unstable. He burns through everything he touches.
37. **The Capital Falls** -- Kang takes the capital. The king is executed. Kang crowns himself Emperor of
    Ashes. The Demon Emperor feasts on the despair.
38. **Mei's Gambit** -- Mei challenges Liang to a duel. She knows she cannot win. But she can remind him what
    it means to be struck by someone who loves him.
39. **The Crack in the Armor** -- Liang's hand trembles when he faces Mei. The dragon inside him recoils from
    her. He is still human enough to hesitate.
40. **The Jade Temple** -- Liang and Mei infiltrate the occupied capital. The old Jade Dragon temple stands
    desecrated. Kang waits inside.

### Part V -- The Final Reckoning (Chapters 41--50)

41. **The Demon's True Form** -- The Demon Emperor manifests through Kang. He is beautiful and terrible. His
    voice is honey and ash. Liang sees what he could become.
42. **The Fifth Style Unleashed** -- Liang opens the Fifth Style fully. His body transcends. He moves faster
    than sight. The temple pillars shatter around him.
43. **Mei's Anchor** -- Mei uses her breathing technique on herself, creating a resonance that pulls Liang back
    whenever he drifts too far into the dragon.
44. **Kang's Fury** -- Kang fights with the Demon Emperor's power. The battle tears the temple apart. Liang
    and the dragon within him begin to merge completely.
45. **The Price** -- Liang realizes the Fifth Style is winning. He is becoming the dragon. Mei's voice reaches
    him through the roar of his own blood.
46. **The Emperor's Offer** -- The Demon Emperor offers Liang the throne of the world. Liang looks at Mei. He
    looks at what Kang became. He refuses.
47. **The Severing** -- Liang turns the Fifth Style inward and severs the Demon Emperor's hold on the mortal
    plane. Kang crumbles to ash. The temple collapses.
48. **The Cost of Mastery** -- Liang survives, but the Fifth Style has taken his legs. He cannot walk. Mei
    carries him from the rubble. The dragon has released him.
49. **The New School** -- Months later. Mei opens a new school. Not the Jade Dragon -- something new. Liang
    sits in the courtyard and watches students learn. He smiles.
50. **The Jade Bell** -- Mei places a new jade bell at the gate. Liang rings it once. The sound carries across
    the valley. The story continues beyond them.
