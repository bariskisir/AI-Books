## Plot Synopsis

Kemal Özden, a third-generation master glassblower in Beyoğlu, is accused when his Sultan's Eye lantern is found at the murder scene of Geoffrey Harker, a British consular secretary. Inspector Leyla Aksoy — one of the first women in the Turkish Republic's police force — traces the case through embassy intrigue, a vanished French smuggler (Claude Mercier), a Greek antiquities dealer (Madame Eleni), and an estranged uncle (Yusuf Özden) who has been creating glass forgeries on Murano. The investigation peels back layers of fraud, family secrets, and a conspiracy to pass off forged Ottoman artifacts as genuine. The glass lantern serves as the novel's central metaphor for perception, memory, and the truths that fragile things hold.
