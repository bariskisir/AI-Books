## About

Istanbul, 1925. The Ottoman Empire has fallen, the Turkish Republic is three years old, and master glassblower Kemal Özden's Beyoğlu workshop produces the city's finest glass lanterns. When a British consular secretary is found stabbed to death with one of Kemal's distinctive lanterns shattered at the scene, Inspector Leyla Aksoy — one of the first women in the Republic's police force — is assigned the case.

The investigation leads through embassy intrigue, a vanished French smuggler operating under a false name, a Greek antiquities dealer who looks the other way, and an estranged uncle on the island of Murano whose gift for forgery is at the center of the conspiracy. As Kemal navigates suspicion and family secrets, the glass lantern becomes a metaphor for perception, memory, and the truths that fragile things can hold.
