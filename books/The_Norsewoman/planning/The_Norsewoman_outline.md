# The Norsewoman — Outline

## Premise

Iceland, 897 CE. Sigrid Ulfsdóttir is the only female skald in the settlement of Reykjavik, known for composing verse that preserves the deeds of the dead. When the powerful chieftain Ragnar Blackhand lies dying from a sword wound inflicted during a boundary dispute, he summons Sigrid to compose his memorial saga — but the truth behind his forty-year reign is darker than legend allows. Ragnar's past includes land seizures, the silencing of witnesses, and a first wife whose death was never adequately explained. Sigrid must choose between the flattery that will keep her safe and the honesty that the skald's art demands, as rival heirs circle the dying man's hall, a second wife plays her own game of survival, and old enemies move against the settlement under cover of winter.

## Core Cast

- **Sigrid Ulfsdóttir**, 38, female skald — Iceland's only woman who composes memorial verse. Pragmatic, perceptive, carries the weight of words as obligation.
- **Ragnar Blackhand**, 71, dying chieftain — Powerful, vain, and frightened. His hand was blackened in a fire decades ago; his past is darker still.
- **Eirik Ragnarsson**, 40, eldest heir — Calculating, politically astute, willing to pay any price for the right story about his father.
- **Astrid Thorvaldsdóttir**, 45, Ragnar's second wife — Practical survivor, married Ragnar for protection, now must navigate her own future after his death.
- **Gunnar the Bald**, 52, loyal huscarl — Served Ragnar for thirty years; the only man who witnessed everything and will not speak easily.
- **Leif Skjaldmær**, 29, rival poet — Hired by Eirik to compose a flattering alternative saga; talented but morally flexible.
- **Halla Ironteeth**, 60, völva — A seeress who knew Ragnar's first wife Ragna and carries knowledge of crimes the chieftain committed in his younger years.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Summons (Chapters 1–10)

1. Sigrid composes a funeral verse for a drowned fisherman in Reykjavik as winter sets in.
2. Ragnar's huscarl Gunnar arrives at Sigrid's longhouse with a summons from the dying chieftain.
3. Sigrid travels through ice and dark to Ragnar's hall at Blackhand Farm, observing the settlement's fear.
4. Sigrid first meets Ragnar in his sickbed; he is reduced but still commanding, tells her what he wants.
5. Astrid receives Sigrid coldly, warning her that this commission is more dangerous than it appears.
6. Eirik corners Sigrid privately and offers gold to ensure the memorial saga flatters his inheritance.
7. Sigrid meets Leif Skjaldmær, the young rival poet already composing at Eirik's direction, and takes his measure.
8. Gunnar shows Sigrid around the farm and tells her sanitized stories of Ragnar's deeds.
9. Sigrid begins her first verses, discovering that Ragnar's history has gaps that don't fit.
10. Halla Ironteeth arrives uninvited at the farm, and the household tenses at her presence.

### Act II: Old Ground (Chapters 11–20)

11. Halla and Sigrid meet privately; Halla hints that she knows the truth about Ragna's death.
12. Sigrid questions Ragnar directly about his first wife; he evades, grows angry, nearly dismisses her.
13. Eirik pressures Sigrid harder — the inheritance hinges on how Ragnar is remembered at the thing.
14. Astrid reveals to Sigrid that she never believed the story of Ragna's drowning and has her own suspicions.
15. Gunnar gets drunk and lets slip a detail about a fire at a neighboring farm thirty years ago.
16. Sigrid investigates the old boundary records, finding inconsistencies in how Ragnar expanded his holdings.
17. Leif attempts to befriend Sigrid; she sees through him but finds him more honest than Eirik about his own corruption.
18. Ragnar summons Sigrid again and recites his own version of events — compelling, shaped, persuasive lies.
19. Halla performs a seiðr rite near the farm and is threatened by Eirik's men; Gunnar intervenes.
20. Sigrid pieces together the outline of Ragnar's original crime: he killed a man named Ósvald for his land.

### Act III: The Shape of the Lie (Chapters 21–30)

21. Sigrid confronts Gunnar with what she has found; he confirms it but says speaking of it will kill them both.
22. Ragnar's condition worsens; the physician Thorkel warns he has perhaps a week left.
23. Leif composes a verse for Eirik's approval — Sigrid hears it and knows it is hollow but technically skilled.
24. Astrid makes a private arrangement with a neighboring chieftain, securing her future regardless of who inherits.
25. Eirik discovers Halla has been speaking with Sigrid and has her temporarily confined to a storage room.
26. Sigrid frees Halla at night and hears the full account: Ragnar drowned Ragna himself to marry Astrid's predecessor.
27. Sigrid sits with Ragnar as he sleeps and tries to understand what kind of man can build a community on murder.
28. Ragnar wakes lucid and tells Sigrid she is the first honest person he has spoken with in twenty years.
29. Sigrid asks Ragnar directly if the stories about Ragna are true. He is silent for a long time, then says yes.
30. Sigrid decides she will compose the true saga regardless of consequence, but must plan how to survive it.

### Act IV: Verse and Consequence (Chapters 31–40)

31. Sigrid drafts the memorial in secret — a saga that acknowledges Ragnar's political skill and his crimes together.
32. Leif discovers what Sigrid is doing and comes to her with an unexpected offer: he will not inform Eirik if she teaches him what she knows of true composition.
33. Gunnar tells Sigrid that men from outside the settlement have been watching the road — Ósvald's surviving son has grown old but not forgiving.
34. Astrid reads a passage of Sigrid's draft and does not condemn it; she asks only that her name is kept clear.
35. Eirik finds a partial draft and confronts Sigrid with violence; Gunnar intervenes, injuring one of Eirik's men.
36. Ragnar hears about the confrontation from his sickbed and overrules Eirik, giving Sigrid his formal protection.
37. Halla warns Sigrid that Ragnar's protection will die with him and she must finish and speak the saga before he does.
38. Sigrid works through a night of cold and low fire, completing the memorial verse.
39. Leif hears the completed composition and says nothing for a long moment; then tells her it is the finest thing composed in Iceland.
40. Ragnar asks to hear the saga before it is performed at the thing. Sigrid reads it to him in his dying room.

### Act V: The Thing (Chapters 41–50)

41. Ragnar listens without interrupting. When Sigrid finishes, he says: "You saw me clearly."
42. Ragnar dies before dawn. The household wakes to grief, politics, and the weighing of what comes next.
43. Eirik attempts to seize the saga manuscript; Sigrid has already committed it to memory and burns the written copy.
44. Halla performs the funerary rites. Eirik cannot stop her without losing the settlement's respect.
45. The thing convenes three days after Ragnar's death; people from across the island arrive in the cold.
46. Leif is called to perform first; he composes a graceful, hollow eulogy that pleases Eirik and impresses no one else.
47. Sigrid stands and recites the memorial saga from memory — the crowd grows very still.
48. The assembled settlement debates what they have heard; Ósvald's son speaks, confirming what Sigrid has said.
49. Eirik inherits despite everything; Ragnar's crimes cannot be undone. But the story is now part of the record.
50. Sigrid walks back from the thing into winter, her obligation met, composing the first lines of her own verse about what honesty costs.
