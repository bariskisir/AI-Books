# The Norsewoman

**Genre:** Viking Historical Fiction
**Author:** Barış Kısır
**Model:** opus-4.8
**Chapters:** 50
**Setting:** Iceland, 897 CE

## Premise

Sigrid Ulfsdóttir is the only female skald in the settlement of Reykjavik, known for composing verse that preserves the deeds of the dead with honesty rather than flattery. When the powerful chieftain Ragnar Blackhand lies dying from a sword wound, he summons Sigrid to compose his memorial saga — but the truth behind his forty-year reign is darker than legend allows.

As Sigrid works through conversations with the dying man, his calculating heir Eirik, his pragmatic second wife Astrid, and the long-silent völva Halla Ironteeth, she uncovers the crimes at the foundation of Ragnar's empire: land seized through arson and intimidation, a first wife who knew too much and did not survive the knowing. Rival heirs circle the hall, a young poet hired to compose the flattering alternative fights his own conscience, and the displaced son of Ragnar's oldest victim travels from the eastern valleys to speak at the thing.

## Chapter Count

50 chapters, structured in 5 acts of 10 chapters each:

- Act I (1–10): The Summons — Sigrid travels to Blackhand Farm and meets the principals
- Act II (11–20): Old Ground — Sigrid uncovers the shape of Ragnar's crimes
- Act III (21–30): The Shape of the Lie — Ragnar's own confessions begin
- Act IV (31–40): Verse and Consequence — Sigrid composes the true memorial
- Act V (41–50): The Thing — The memorial is performed before the assembly
