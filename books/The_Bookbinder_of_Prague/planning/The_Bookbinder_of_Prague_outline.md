# The Bookbinder of Prague

## Premise

In 1941 Prague, Jewish bookbinder David Rosen preserves forbidden Jewish texts and rare manuscripts by secretly binding them inside the covers of confiscated Nazi propaganda books. When SS officer Frau Doktor Greta Müller discovers discrepancies in the seized book inventory, she suspects someone in the state binding workshop. David's sixteen-year-old daughter Anna befriends the son of a Wehrmacht major, walking a dangerous line between survival and betrayal. As the net tightens and deportations begin, David must decide how many books — and how many lives — he can save before the Nazis uncover his secret.

## Core Cast

- **David Rosen** — Jewish bookbinder, master craftsman, widower. Quiet, meticulous, stubborn. Lost his wife to illness in 1939. Runs the Rosen Bindery, now forcibly conscripted into Nazi service.
- **Anna Rosen** — David's daughter, 16. Practical, watchful, brave. Helps her father in the bindery. Befriends a German officer's son.
- **Frau Doktor Greta Müller** — SS cultural officer assigned to "process" confiscated Jewish libraries. Cold, educated, observant. A former university librarian who now serves the Reich.
- **Pavel Novak** — David's apprentice, 19. Impulsive, loyal, less cautious than David. Smuggles books on his own.
- **Rabbi Samuel Löw** — Elder of the Jewish community. Brings David the most precious manuscripts. Wise, doomed.
- **Major Klaus Weber** — Wehrmacht officer, billeted near the Rosen workshop. Not a zealot, but a soldier. His son Erik befriends Anna.
- **Erik Weber** — Major Weber's son, 17. Confused by the war, drawn to Anna.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Binding (Chapters 1-15)

1. **The Workshop** — David Rosen opens his workshop on a cold Prague morning. A Nazi truck pulls up outside with confiscated books.
2. **The Order** — Frau Doktor Müller arrives and explains the new mandate: David will bind Nazi propaganda for shipment to Berlin. Resistance is death.
3. **The Last Delivery** — Rabbi Löw comes to the workshop with a satchel of rare Jewish manuscripts. He begs David to hide them.
4. **The First Risk** — David disassembles a Nazi propaganda book and places a small prayer manuscript inside the hollow cover. His hands tremble.
5. **Anna** — Anna cleans the workshop. She notices her father is quieter than usual. They share a sparse meal.
6. **The Inventory** — Frau Doktor Müller inspects the workshop. She questions David about a missing crate of books. He lies smoothly.
7. **Pavel's Discovery** — Pavel finds what David is doing in the back room. He wants to help, but David tells him to stay safe.
8. **The German Next Door** — Major Weber and his son Erik move into the house adjacent to the workshop. Anna sees Erik watching from the window.
9. **The Manuscripts** — David works late, cutting boards, creating false hollows, sewing manuscripts into the spines of propaganda tomes. Each is a small act of defiance.
10. **Deportation Rumors** — Word spreads through the Jewish quarter. Transports to the east have begun. Rabbi Löw urges David to work faster.
11. **The Visit** — Frau Doktor Müller makes an unannounced visit. David hides his work. She examines a freshly bound book — a copy of Mein Kampf she holds without suspicion.
12. **First Contact** — Erik Weber introduces himself to Anna near the courtyard. He is awkward, kind. She is wary.
13. **The Rabbi's Warning** — Rabbi Löw is summoned for "resettlement." He gives David his final treasure: a 14th-century Haggadah. David promises to save it.
14. **The Empty Synagogue** — David and Anna attend services at the Altneuschul. Half the pews are empty. The cantor's voice cracks.
15. **The Raid** — Gestapo raiders enter the Jewish quarter. David hides his latest work under the floorboards. Pavel is caught in the street and beaten.

### Act II: The Hollow Covers (Chapters 16-35)

16. **Aftermath** — Pavel is released but bruised. David tends his wounds. Anna brings them soup. The workshop feels like a cage.
17. **Erik's Gift** — Erik brings Anna a loaf of bread. She accepts but feels shame. Her father does not approve.
18. **The Warehouse** — David is ordered to the municipal warehouse to collect more confiscated books. He finds shelves of Jewish texts waiting for destruction.
19. **Sorting Salvation** — David sorts through piles, selecting which manuscripts to save, which to leave for the fire. He works quickly, heart pounding.
20. **The Inventory Book** — Frau Doktor Müller keeps a ledger of every book processed. David realizes she will notice if too many are missing.
21. **Falsifying Records** — David begins altering the inventory, recording valuable manuscripts as "damaged — destroyed." A quiet forgery.
22. **Anna's Secret** — Anna meets Erik in the garden between their buildings. He asks about her family. She tells him they are Jews. He does not recoil.
23. **The Dinner** — Major Weber invites David to his quarters for a repair job. David binds a book for the major. The major pays him with ration coupons.
24. **The Informant** — Someone has reported suspicious activity at the bindery. Frau Doktor Müller returns with two SS men for a thorough search.
25. **The Close Call** — The SS men pull books from the shelves, rip covers. David stands frozen. They find nothing — the manuscripts are hidden in books already stamped for shipment.
26. **Pavel's Anger** — Pavel wants to fight back. David argues that survival is resistance. Pavel storms out. Anna watches her father's exhaustion.
27. **The Haggadah** — David begins work on the 14th-century Haggadah. It is the most precious thing he has ever held. He binds it into the cover of a volume of Nazi racial theory.
28. **Erik's Confession** — Erik tells Anna that his father is being transferred to the Eastern Front. He wants to stay. He wants her to come with him. She says nothing.
29. **The Bookshop Raid** — David watches from his window as the SA clears a neighboring Jewish bookshop. Books burn in the street. He smells the smoke for days.
30. **The Rising Fear** — Pavel is taken in a roundup. David goes to the collection point but cannot reach him. Pavel is transported east.
31. **Rabbi Löw's Farewell** — Rabbi Löw is taken. He passes David's workshop one last time, escorted by guards. Their eyes meet. The rabbi nods.
32. **The Empty Chair** — Anna sets a place for Pavel at dinner. David cannot eat. The workshop feels smaller.
33. **Frau Doktor's Suspicion** — Müller cross-references her inventory against shipment manifests. Three crates are unaccounted for. She begins her own investigation.
34. **The Major's Offer** — Major Weber offers to help David get documents for Anna — a way out. David distrusts the offer but cannot refuse hope.
35. **The Trap** — Frau Doktor Müller sets a trap: she leaves a marked book in the workshop, then checks if it ships. David recognizes the trap and ships it untouched.

### Act III: The Unbinding (Chapters 36-50)

36. **The Gambit** — David knows time is running out. He begins packing the hidden manuscripts into a false-bottomed crate, disguising it as a shipment of bindings.
37. **Anna's Choice** — Erik gives Anna the papers his father promised. She can leave Prague as a German clerk's niece. She must decide alone.
38. **The Confrontation** — Frau Doktor Müller confronts David with her findings. Not proof, but enough. She gives him one week to confess.
39. **The Betrayal** — David discovers that Erik's father, Major Weber, has been reassigned unexpectedly — transferred to a punishment battalion. The offer of papers may have been a trap.
40. **The Last Binding** — David works through the night, binding the final manuscripts. He uses every trick he knows — false spines, hidden compartments, sewn-in leaves.
41. **Anna Stays** — Anna refuses the papers. She will not leave her father. She burns the documents in the stove. David weeps silently.
42. **The Arrest** — Frau Doktor Müller arrives with an arrest warrant. David is taken. Anna watches from the window, her hand pressed to the glass.
43. **The Interrogation** — David is questioned. He admits nothing. Müller respects his skill but cannot spare him. She has her orders.
44. **The Safe Room** — Anna, alone in the workshop, continues her father's work. She binds the last remaining manuscripts. Erik brings her food.
45. **The Aftermath** — David is sentenced to transport. He is allowed one letter. He writes to Anna: "The books are our memory. Guard them."
46. **The Transport** — David boards the train at Bubenec station. He carries nothing but the clothes on his back. He has saved two hundred books.
47. **The Promise** — Anna hides the remaining manuscripts beneath the workshop floorboards. She seals them with wax and lime, as her father taught her.
48. **The Occupation Ends** — 1945. Prague is liberated. Anna returns to the workshop. The floorboards are undisturbed. The books are safe.
49. **The Unbinding** — Anna carefully opens the covers her father bound. Each one contains a treasure. She begins the work of restoration.
50. **The Memory** — Years later, Anna teaches bookbinding at the Jewish Museum in Prague. She shows a student how to cut a hollow cover. Some secrets, she says, are meant to be hidden.
