# The Gilded Menagerie — Outline

## Premise

In the spring of 1911, the Hollis & Crane Combined Shows — a small, fraying American traveling circus — opens its season under a patched canvas big top. Nell Sparrow, a trapeze artist who has lived under the tent since she came to it as a runaway young woman, discovers that the show's beloved owner, August Crane, has been quietly mortgaging the circus's future to the Apex Amusement Syndicate, a ruthless combine that buys up failing shows for their routes, wagons, and animals. As the season grinds across small Midwestern towns through mud, rain, and dwindling crowds, Nell and Silas Roe, the show's taciturn animal handler, fight to understand what Crane has signed away — and to save the only family either of them has known. Each must decide what they are willing to risk for the high wire and for one another.

## Core Cast

- **Nell Sparrow** — Trapeze artist, late twenties, raised in orphan poverty before the circus took her in. Fearless above the ring, wary on the ground.
- **Silas Roe** — Animal handler, thirties, a quiet man who speaks more easily to the show's old elephant, Juno, than to people. Carries a past he won't name.
- **August Crane** — The aging owner and ringmaster, generous and beloved, drowning in debt and shame.
- **Cornelius Pratt** — The Apex Syndicate's advance man, smooth, patient, ruthless.
- **Madame Lorca** — The fortune-teller and de facto den mother, sharp-eyed and loyal.
- **Tom Bellamy** — Nell's flying partner and catcher, ambitious, tempted by Apex's offer.
- **Juno** — The show's elderly Asian elephant, Silas's charge and the heart of the menagerie.
- **Dewey Hale** — The boss canvasman, gruff, the show's spine.
- **Annie Crane** — August's late wife, present in memory and in the ledgers she once kept.

## 50-Chapter Plan (present tense)

### Act One — The Season Opens (1–12)
1. Nell rigs the trapeze in a cold dawn field and feels the show's frayed ropes.
2. August Crane gives his opening spiel to a thin crowd; the take is short.
3. Silas tends Juno in the menagerie tent and notices the feed bills going unpaid.
4. A stranger in a good suit, Pratt, watches the matinee and makes notes.
5. Nell and Tom fly a clean routine; afterward Tom mentions a richer show is hiring.
6. Madame Lorca reads cards for Nell and warns of a buyer at the door.
7. August meets Pratt in his wagon; Nell glimpses papers exchanged.
8. The show tears down in rain and rolls to the next town.
9. Silas finds a syndicate brand burned on a crate of Crane's own supplies.
10. Nell confronts August, who deflects and calls it a loan.
11. A wagon axle breaks; the company patches it and limps on.
12. Nell overhears Pratt promise August that "the animals come first."

### Act Two — The Web Tightens (13–24)
13. Nell searches August's wagon and finds an Apex contract, half-signed.
14. Silas teaches Nell what the contract's animal clause really means.
15. Tom is courted openly by Pratt with promises of a city show.
16. A short payday means no wages; the roustabouts mutter mutiny.
17. Dewey Hale tells Nell the show has been sold piece by piece for two years.
18. Nell and Silas drive to a county seat to read the filed liens.
19. They learn Apex means to dissolve the show and sell Juno to a fair.
20. August, drunk, confesses he signed to keep the company eating through winter.
21. Nell proposes the company buy back its own paper; the math is brutal.
22. A rival Apex-owned show arrives in the same town to bleed their crowd.
23. Nell flies without a net to draw a crowd and pull a bigger gate.
24. The stunt works; Pratt smiles and raises the interest on the note.

### Act Three — The Brutal Road (25–36)
25. Storm floods the lot; wagons sink to the hubs in the mud.
26. Silas and the elephant haul the rig free; Juno strains and limps after.
27. A fire in the cookhouse costs them a week's stores.
28. Pratt offers Nell a contract of her own — leave, and the others will follow.
29. Nell refuses; Tom does not, and signs with Apex.
30. The show plays a town that remembers August fondly; the gate is good.
31. August collapses during the spiel; his heart is failing.
32. Nell takes over the ring and finds she can hold a crowd.
33. Silas reveals he once worked for Apex and knows how they break a show.
34. Together they plan to expose Apex's cruelty to the local press.
35. A reporter follows the show; Pratt's men intimidate him off.
36. Apex calls the note due in full; the company has thirty days.

### Act Four — What They Risk (37–47)
37. Nell pitches a benefit week: every act, no pay, all gate to the note.
38. The company votes to stay; even the doubters take the rope.
39. Silas nurses Juno through a sickness, refusing to let her be sold weak.
40. Madame Lorca's takings and old jewelry go into the pot.
41. Tom returns, ashamed of Apex, and offers what he was paid.
42. A record crowd comes; the gate climbs but falls short of the sum.
43. Pratt arrives to seize the menagerie; Silas stands in the gate.
44. Nell discovers Annie Crane's old ledger proves Apex overcharged interest.
45. They confront Pratt with the fraud before the county clerk.
46. The clerk voids the unlawful interest; the true debt is payable.
47. The company pays it; the show, and Juno, are free of Apex.

### Act Five — The High Wire (48–50)
48. August, recovering, hands Nell the ring and the ledger both.
49. The season ends; the company winters together, lean but whole.
50. In a cold spring field, Nell rigs the trapeze again and flies for the show that is now hers and theirs.
