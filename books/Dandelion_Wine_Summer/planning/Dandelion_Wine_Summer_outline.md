# Dandelion Wine Summer

## Premise

In the summer of 1965, eleven-year-old Benny discovers that a single season can hold a lifetime. Between helping his grandfather Tom brew the family's dandelion wine and navigating a new friendship with Clara, the girl next door, Benny learns about love, loss, and the strange way time speeds up when you want it to slow down. Set in the farming country of rural Missouri, this is the summer Benny stops being a boy who thinks summers last forever and becomes someone who knows they don't.

## Core Cast

- **Benny** — Eleven years old, observant, old enough to know better but young enough to hope
- **Grandpa Tom** — Benny's maternal grandfather, a patient man who makes dandelion wine and knows when to speak and when to stay quiet
- **Clara** — The new girl next door, twelve, brave in ways Benny isn't, carries secrets of her own
- **Grandma Helen** — Grandpa Tom's wife, keeps the kitchen running and sees more than she says
- **Dad (Frank)** — Works at the grain elevator, tired most evenings
- **Mom (Margaret)** — Grandpa Tom's daughter, worries quietly

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Benny watches the clock on the last day of school and counts the seconds until freedom.
2. He walks home through the dust and heat, making plans for a summer that feels infinite.
3. Grandma Helen's kitchen smells like cinnamon and Benny finds Grandpa Tom on the porch.
4. Grandpa shows Benny the dandelion field behind the barn, golden in the late light.
5. They pick dandelions together, Grandpa teaching which ones to choose.
6. Grandpa reveals the family wine recipe, scribbled in a notebook older than Benny's father.
7. A moving truck pulls into the old Henderson place, and Benny watches from the fence.
8. Clara walks over and introduces herself, direct in a way that makes Benny blush.
9. Clara dares Benny to climb the abandoned water tower at the edge of town.
10. From the top, Benny sees the whole valley and feels both scared and alive.
11. Grandpa checks the bubbling wine jugs and talks about patience being part of the recipe.
12. Benny takes Clara to the swimming hole behind the Johnson farm.
13. Frank Miller catches a catfish bigger than his arm; Clara refuses to touch it.
14. A storm traps Benny and Clara in the barn loft, the rain hammering the roof.
15. Benny finds a faded photograph of Grandpa Tom as a young soldier.
16. Grandma tells Benny the story of how she and Grandpa met at a barn dance in 1938.
17. Benny and Clara catch fireflies at dusk, the jars glowing like lanterns.
18. The county fair arrives, and the whole family goes on opening night.
19. Grandma enters her apple pie and faces off against Mrs. Patterson from church.
20. Benny rides the Tilt-a-Whirl with Clara and feels something shift in his chest.
21. They sneak out to the cornfield at midnight and lie looking at the stars.
22. Clara confesses her father is looking for work in St. Louis and they might move.
23. Grandpa Tom starts coughing and can't stop, his handkerchief spotted with red.
24. Dr. Miller visits and speaks to Mom and Dad in hushed voices behind the kitchen door.
25. Benny eavesdrops and learns the word "cancer" for the first time.
26. Grandpa shows Benny the hidden wine cellar under the barn.
27. Grandpa lets Benny taste a single drop of the oldest dandelion wine.
28. Clara confirms they are moving in August, three weeks away.
29. Benny promises Clara they will write every week, no matter what.
30. The Fourth of July celebration fills the sky with color and noise.
31. Clara writes her name with a sparkler and Benny watches it burn in the dark.
32. Grandpa Tom sits Benny down and tells him plainly that he is dying.
33. They spend a quiet day fishing at the creek, saying things without words.
34. Grandpa and Benny look through the photograph album, each picture a story.
35. Benny realizes the summer is draining away and he cannot stop it.
36. They weed the garden together, Grandpa moving slower than he used to.
37. Benny and Clara take one last swim, the water colder than it was in June.
38. Clara's moving day arrives and Benny gives her a jar of dandelion seeds.
39. The house next door goes dark and Benny stands at the fence alone.
40. Grandpa grows weaker and stays in bed, the wine forgotten in the cellar.
41. Grandpa dictates the final wine-making steps from his bed, Benny taking notes.
42. The days stretch into a waiting game, Benny sitting by Grandpa's door.
43. A storm like the one in June rips through the county, lightning splitting the sky.
44. The morning after the storm, Grandpa cannot lift his head from the pillow.
45. Grandpa speaks his last real words to Benny, quiet and simple.
46. Grandpa Tom dies in the night, and Benny wakes to his mother's crying.
47. The funeral is small, and Benny does not cry until they lower the coffin.
48. Weeks later, Benny opens the dandelion wine alone in the cellar.
49. He pours two glasses and sits with one in Grandpa's empty chair.
50. Benny walks to the dandelion field and understands something about summers and seeds.
