## Narrative Structure

The novel spans 50 chapters in five parts: The Recording, The Mouth Map, Edited Breath, The Open File, Ordinary Speech. When a coastal court builds a kidnapping case on one disputed recording, forensic linguist Dr. Leila Arman hears what the transcript has erased: dialect, fear, editing, and a missing vowel that points away from the accused man. To save him, she must teach a courtroom to listen before a polished official story hardens into a verdict.
