# The Night Market — Outline

## Premise

Every full moon, a mysterious market materializes in the heart of a Filipino-American neighborhood in Oakland, California. By day the lot sits empty, but when the moon rises, stalls appear selling things that do not exist by daylight: bottled dreams, stitched memories, second chances made tangible. Young Tomas, sent to live with his grandmother Lola Esme after his mother's death, discovers the market and finds himself apprenticed to its enigmatic Keeper. Through fifty nights of wonders and dangers, Tomas must decide whether to stay in the world behind the veil or return to the ordinary life calling him back.

## Core Cast

- **Tomas** — A twelve-year-old boy grieving his mother, sharp-eyed and full of questions, sent to Oakland to live with his grandmother.
- **Lola Esme** — Tomas's grandmother, a wiry old woman who knows the market's secrets, keeper of recipes that taste like memories.
- **The Market Keeper** — A figure neither young nor old, dressed in a coat threaded with stars, who maintains the balance between the market and the waking world.

## 50-Chapter Plan

1. Tomas steps off the Greyhound into the Sacramento heat and meets Lola Esme for the first time.
2. Lola Esme's house smells of vinegar and dried herbs, and Tomas finds his room unchanged since his mother left it.
3. Tomas walks the neighborhood and sees the empty lot where nothing grows, ringed by dying streetlamps.
4. A full moon rises and Tomas wakes to the sound of hammering and strange music from the lot.
5. Tomas slips out of the house and finds the market alive with stalls and lanterns that glow without flames.
6. The Market Keeper watches Tomas from a distance, then vanishes into the crowd.
7. Tomas approaches a stall selling jars of light labeled with names he does not recognize.
8. A vendor offers Tomas a piece of fried dough that tastes like his mother's cooking.
9. Lola Esme finds Tomas at dawn sitting on the curb and says nothing, only takes him home.
10. Days pass and the lot remains empty; Tomas cannot convince himself he dreamed it.
11. Lola Esme teaches Tomas to make lumpia, and the oil smells like the vendor's dough.
12. Tomas searches the house and finds a locked cabinet filled with old photographs of the market.
13. Lola Esme catches Tomas at the cabinet and admits she used to walk the market too.
14. The next full moon arrives and Tomas returns to find the market Keeper waiting for him.
15. The Keeper offers Tomas a job: help tend the stalls in exchange for answers.
16. Tomas meets the Memory Peddler, a woman who weaves lost moments into fabric.
17. A man trades his grief for a watch that runs backward, and Tomas watches him walk away smiling.
18. Tomas discovers the Stall of Regret, where people buy chances to undo one mistake.
19. The Keeper warns Tomas that every purchase has a cost, and costs compound.
20. Tomas encounters a boy his age selling shadows that whisper secrets of their owners.
21. The Shadow Boy tells Tomas the Keeper was once a customer who never left the market.
22. Tomas starts to notice the market's rules: no names given freely, no debts unpaid.
23. A woman buys a dream of her dead husband and weeps with joy as the dream takes her.
24. Tomas asks the Keeper if the market ever sells something for free.
25. The Keeper shows Tomas the Stall of Forgotten Things, where the price is a memory.
26. Tomas learns that his mother visited the market before she died, bought something, never said what.
27. Lola Esme reveals she was the Memory Peddler before she married and left the market.
28. Tomas confronts the Memory Peddler about what his mother purchased.
29. The Memory Peddler shows Tomas a bolt of cloth woven from his mother's last week of life.
30. Tomas watches the cloth unfold and sees his mother saying goodbye to him while he slept.
31. Tomas realizes his mother knew she was dying and chose not to tell him to spare his fear.
32. Rage fills Tomas; he accuses the Keeper of letting his mother make a terrible deal.
33. The Keeper explains that the market only reflects what people already carry inside them.
34. A storm breaks over the market, and stalls begin to flicker and fade.
35. The Keeper tells Tomas that the market is dying because fewer people believe in it.
36. Tomas decides he will not let the market disappear and asks what can be done.
37. The Keeper says the market needs a new anchor, someone willing to give something true.
38. Lola Esme offers to return as the Memory Peddler, to strengthen the market's roots.
39. Tomas protests, but Lola Esme says she has been waiting for this calling all her life.
40. The Shadow Boy warns Tomas that Lola Esme's offer might trap her in the market forever.
41. Tomas searches for another solution and finds the Stall of Unanswered Questions.
42. The vendor tells Tomas that the answer to saving the market lies in his own name.
43. Tomas realizes his mother named him after the market's first anchor, a boy who chose to stay.
44. The Keeper confirms that Tomas is named for the original keeper's son, who gave his voice to the market.
45. Tomas understands that his mother bought the cloth so he would know she loved him, nothing more.
46. Tomas makes his choice: he will give his voice to the market, not his life, and speak through stories instead.
47. The Keeper accepts the bargain, and Tomas finds he can still speak, but his words now carry the market's magic.
48. Lola Esme weeps and hugs Tomas, proud and terrified, as the stalls grow solid again.
49. The market survives the storm, fuller and brighter than before, and new vendors arrive.
50. Tomas returns to school in September, carrying the market inside his chest, and watches the next full moon rise.
