# The Saffron Invoice

## Overview

When a guest collapses during a private tasting at Marigold Table, food fraud auditor Leona Vey is hired to determine whether the celebrated kitchen cross-contaminated a dish or whether the saffron supply chain failed. The almond smell in the saffron rice, the glossy replacement tins, and the perfect import paperwork point away from the pastry station and toward a forged luxury spice lot. As Leona works with chef Rafi Doran, pastry chef Sera Malk, health inspector Mina Calder, and a staff that tried to warn before service, the case widens into investor pressure, altered prep logs, and a blending facility selling cheap dyed petals and allergen-bearing carrier oil as premium saffron.

**Genre:** Food Fraud Mystery / Restaurant Supply-Chain Suspense
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~10400
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

A restaurant allergy crisis becomes a supply-chain investigation when saffron invoices, retained tins, and receiving photos show that a premium spice lot was swapped for an adulterated product. The kitchen must prove responsibility without accepting a false internal blame narrative.

## Key Themes

- An invoice can become an ingredient when service depends on trust.
- Staff warnings are food safety intelligence, not inconvenience.
- Luxury packaging can hide cheap and dangerous substitutions.
- Responsibility means tracing the hazard without scapegoating the nearest worker.

## Characters

**Leona Vey** - Adult food fraud auditor who specializes in spice adulteration, supplier audits, and restaurant traceability.

**Rafi Doran** - Adult chef-owner of Marigold Table, brilliant and defensive, with a kitchen built on trust.

**Sera Malk** - Adult pastry chef whose allergy controls are strict enough to make her the first suspect and best witness.

**Ilya Noor** - Adult spice importer whose saffron lots are pristine on paper and impossible under market conditions.

**Mina Calder** - Adult health inspector balancing public safety with political pressure.

**Tomas Reed** - Adult guest who survives the reaction and refuses to let the restaurant scapegoat a server.

**Priya Sen** - Adult line cook who noticed the saffron oil smelled wrong before service.

**Celeste Wyn** - Adult food critic invited to the tasting, carrying more influence than appetite.
