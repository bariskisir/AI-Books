# The Saffron Invoice - Outline

## Premise

When a guest collapses during a private tasting at the celebrated restaurant Marigold Table, food fraud auditor Leona Vey is hired to determine whether the kitchen cross-contaminated a dish or whether a supplier's forged saffron invoices conceal a wider adulteration scheme. The allergic reaction points to undeclared nut oil, but the saffron tins, batch labels, and purchase orders tell a different story: someone has been blending cheap petals, dye, and allergen-bearing carrier oil into luxury spice while using the restaurant's reputation as cover. Leona must trace the invoice trail before the chef, the staff, and the next guest are made responsible for a crime cooked upstream.

## Core Cast

- Leona Vey - Adult food fraud auditor who specializes in spice adulteration, supplier audits, and restaurant traceability.
- Rafi Doran - Adult chef-owner of Marigold Table, brilliant and defensive, with a kitchen built on trust.
- Sera Malk - Adult pastry chef whose allergy controls are strict enough to make her the first suspect and best witness.
- Ilya Noor - Adult spice importer whose saffron lots are pristine on paper and impossible under market conditions.
- Mina Calder - Adult health inspector balancing public safety with political pressure.
- Tomas Reed - Adult guest who survives the reaction and refuses to let the restaurant scapegoat a server.
- Vale Arno - Adult restaurant investor whose expansion deal depends on a clean private tasting.
- Priya Sen - Adult line cook who noticed the saffron oil smelled wrong before service.
- Omar Jale - Adult warehouse manager whose receiving photos show tins that do not match invoices.
- Celeste Wyn - Adult food critic invited to the tasting, carrying more influence than appetite.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Leona enters Marigold Table after Tomas collapses and smells almond in a saffron dish that should contain none.
2. Rafi reconstructs the tasting menu and insists no nut oil entered the savory kitchen.
3. Sera shows Leona the pastry allergy controls and the locked nut cabinet.
4. Mina Calder opens the health investigation and warns the restaurant may be suspended.
5. Leona examines the saffron tins and finds batch labels printed over older codes.
6. Tomas describes the dish and the server's warning that the saffron oil had been changed.
7. Priya admits she questioned the saffron oil before service and was told to trust the invoice.
8. Ilya Noor produces perfect import paperwork that Leona finds too perfect.
9. Omar sends receiving photos showing different tin seams and lot colors.
10. Vale pushes Rafi to blame internal cross-contamination before investors walk.
11. Leona maps the ingredient path from importer to warehouse to prep station.
12. Lab tests show undeclared almond oil in the saffron carrier, not in pastry.
13. Sera proves her nut inventory never moved before service.
14. Leona spots dyed marigold petals mixed with the saffron threads.
15. Celeste Wyn reveals the tasting was meant to secure a public expansion announcement.
16. Mina finds three other restaurants with quiet reactions tied to Ilya's saffron oil.
17. Omar admits a replacement pallet arrived after the original tins were photographed.
18. Priya finds a discarded invoice with a lower-grade spice code under the premium lot.
19. Leona traces the lower-grade code to a blending facility owned by Vale's fund.
20. Rafi realizes his investor may profit from both the fraud and the scapegoat narrative.
21. The team builds a board of dishes, tins, invoices, allergy logs, and expansion money.
22. Ilya claims the almond oil was introduced in Marigold's kitchen after delivery.
23. Sera and Leona stage a kitchen trace test that clears the pastry station.
24. Mina orders temporary closure unless supplier fraud can be documented by Friday.
25. Omar produces warehouse camera stills of a late-night pallet switch.
26. The stills show Vale's logistics consultant entering with Ilya's driver.
27. Tomas releases a statement naming the server's warning and refusing a quiet settlement.
28. Celeste delays her review and gives Leona notes from the tasting table.
29. Leona identifies the almond smell in the oil as bitter almond fragrance masking stale carrier.
30. Rafi finds a prep log altered after the collapse to implicate Priya.
31. Priya is threatened with firing unless she signs a contamination statement.
32. Leona traces the altered prep log to Vale's operations account.
33. Mina subpoenas Ilya's import certificates and finds a duplicated customs stamp.
34. The blending facility records show saffron oil batches relabeled as premium threads.
35. Ilya argues he was only moving product supplied by Vale's facility.
36. Vale claims he invested in logistics, not ingredients.
37. Leona finds Vale's fund memo pricing the allergy incident as a restaurant acquisition opportunity.
38. Rafi publicly pauses the expansion and names the supplier investigation.
39. Sera discovers a retained sample from the original unopened saffron tin.
40. Lab comparison proves the retained tin is clean while the replacement pallet is adulterated.
41. Omar identifies the consultant who ordered the switch and hid the photos.
42. The consultant says Vale wanted Marigold weakened before the expansion buyout.
43. Ilya admits falsifying lot paperwork to keep Vale's contract.
44. Mina clears Marigold's internal controls but keeps pressure on supplier records.
45. Vale tries to destroy the lower-grade invoice during a board call.
46. Celeste publishes a factual column on food fraud rather than a restaurant review.
47. Tomas testifies that the warning came before service, proving staff concern.
48. Leona completes the traceability report and refers the supplier chain for prosecution.
49. Rafi reopens with a reduced menu and full supplier disclosure.
50. Leona leaves the clean saffron tin in evidence storage and writes the invoice number that began the case.
