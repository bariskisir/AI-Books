Chapter 1: White Vein

Dr. Mira Vale arrived in Graybarrow with a hand lens, a field hammer, and one clean shirt folded around a notebook. The station smelled of brake dust and rain. Above the roofs, the quartz ridge held a white line of light even under clouds.

Leona Senn met her beside a county truck. "The sample is at the arbitration hall."

"Not at a lab?"

"The company wanted theatre. The judge wanted custody."

The specimen sat in a glass case under two cameras. It was fist-sized, milky, and too clean. Mira bent close. Quartz did not lie, but people cut it, washed it, and taught it to stand where it had never grown.

"This is their witness?" she asked.

Leona nodded. "They say it proves the vein lies inside Aster's claim."

Mira saw a flat face where no flat face belonged. "Then it has already said something else."
