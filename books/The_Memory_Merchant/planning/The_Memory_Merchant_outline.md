# The Memory Merchant — Outline

## Premise

In the near-future city of Sable, memories can be extracted, distilled into a pale fluid, bottled, and sold. Mara Veil is a licensed memory broker who has spent six years numbing her own grief by quietly selling off her happiest recollections — the ones that hurt to keep. When a soft-spoken client brings her a purchased memory to authenticate, the recording contains evidence of a crime and a face Mara is certain she chose to forget. To learn what she did, she must buy back the pieces of herself she once let go, and decide which truths are worth carrying.

## Core Cast

- **Mara Veil** — Licensed memory broker, late thirties. Precise, tired, a connoisseur of other people's joy. Has sold most of her own happiness to survive losing her husband, Daniel.
- **Otto Renn** — Mara's client, mid-forties, a mild civil engineer who bought a stranger's memory and found something wrong inside it.
- **Daniel Veil** — Mara's late husband, an archivist, dead three years. Present only in the memories Mara sold.
- **Pell** — Mara's assistant, twenty-six, sharp and loyal, runs the shop's intake.
- **Inspector Sana Cole** — A patient detective from the Bureau of Recollection, investigating an unlicensed extraction ring.
- **Lou Vesper** — A back-alley distiller who buys and recuts illegal memories; charming, dangerous.
- **Halloran** — Owner of the brokerage chain Mara contracts under; smooth, corporate, complicit.
- **Edie** — Elderly regular who sells her dead sister's memories one drop at a time.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act One — The Shop of Small Comforts (1–12)

1. **The Decanting** — Mara extracts and bottles a widow's wedding day; the ordinary machinery of grief and trade.
2. **Inventory** — Mara reviews her own dwindling private shelf; she has three happy memories left to sell.
3. **The Client** — Otto Renn arrives with a bottle he bought legally, asking Mara to verify it is real.
4. **A Stranger's Afternoon** — Mara samples Otto's memory: a kitchen, an argument, a man falling.
5. **The Face at the Edge** — In the memory's background, a woman turns — and Mara knows her, though she cannot say from where.
6. **Pell's Doubts** — Pell warns Mara that authenticating crime-tainted product is illegal; Mara keeps the bottle.
7. **The Empty Anniversary** — Mara sells a recollection of Daniel laughing to pay rent, and feels the gap open.
8. **Edie's Drop** — Edie sells one more sip of her sister; Mara sees what selling does over years.
9. **The Ledger** — Mara finds her own name in an old intake log she does not remember signing.
10. **Inspector Cole** — Detective Sana Cole visits, asking about unlicensed extractions and a dead man named Tomas Ash.
11. **The Dead Man** — Mara recognizes the falling man in Otto's memory as Tomas Ash from Cole's photograph.
12. **What Mara Sold** — Mara realizes the woman in the memory might be herself, edited out of her own past.

### Act Two — Buying Back (13–28)

13. **The First Repurchase** — Mara tracks a buyer who owns one of her sold memories and asks to buy it back.
14. **A Kitchen She Knew** — Restored, the memory shows Mara was in that kitchen the night Tomas Ash died.
15. **Daniel's Box** — Mara visits the storage unit holding Daniel's effects, looking for the year she erased.
16. **Lou Vesper** — Mara meets the back-alley distiller who first cut and sold the crime memory.
17. **The Recut** — Vesper explains memories can be trimmed; someone paid to remove a witness — her.
18. **Otto's Reason** — Otto confesses he bought the memory hunting for his missing brother, Tomas Ash.
19. **Brothers** — Otto reveals Tomas was his brother; the two had been estranged for years.
20. **The Bureau** — Cole offers Mara immunity if she helps trace the extraction ring; Mara hesitates.
21. **Halloran's Office** — Mara's chain-owner boss warns her to drop the matter, too smoothly.
22. **The Second Repurchase** — Mara buys back a memory of Daniel teaching her to swim; it costs everything saved.
23. **The Swim** — Inside it, a detail: Daniel knew Tomas Ash. The threads tie tighter.
24. **Pell's Find** — Pell discovers the shop's records were altered the same week as Mara's erasure.
25. **The Distiller's Price** — Vesper offers Mara the original uncut memory — for the last of her private shelf.
26. **The Trade** — Mara sells her wedding day to buy the truth; the shop feels colder after.
27. **Uncut** — The full memory: Mara saw Tomas Ash pushed, and chose to have herself removed.
28. **Who Paid** — The recut was ordered through Halloran's brokerage; the chain laundered the edit.

### Act Three — The Weight of Keeping (29–42)

29. **The Confession Tape** — Mara finds an extraction she made of her own guilt, hidden in inventory.
30. **Daniel's Voice** — A surviving fragment: Daniel begging her to forget, to be safe.
31. **The Night Itself** — Reconstructed: Daniel and Tomas argued over stolen memories; Tomas fell.
32. **Not Murder** — Mara realizes it was an accident Daniel covered, then erased from her to protect her.
33. **Three Years** — Mara understands Daniel died soon after, carrying the secret she paid to lose.
34. **Cole Closes In** — Cole has enough to arrest Vesper; she still wants the broker who ordered the recut.
35. **Otto's Grief** — Mara tells Otto his brother's death was an accident, not the murder he feared.
36. **What Otto Wants** — Otto asks only to keep one true memory of Tomas alive; not vengeance.
37. **Halloran's Offer** — Halloran offers to restore all of Mara's sold memories if she stays silent.
38. **The Temptation** — Mara could have Daniel back, whole, in exchange for letting the edit stand.
39. **Edie's Counsel** — Edie tells Mara what it is to sell everything and keep nothing true.
40. **Pell's Stand** — Pell refuses to help bury the records; threatens to leave if Mara takes the deal.
41. **The Choice Forms** — Mara decides truth she carries is worth more than comfort she buys.
42. **Turning** — Mara agrees to wear a recorder and meet Halloran for the Bureau.

### Act Four — What We Keep (43–50)

43. **The Meeting** — Halloran admits, on record, ordering the recut to protect the brokerage's name.
44. **Vesper's Warning** — Vesper, cornered, tries to destroy the original memory; Mara stops him.
45. **The Arrest** — Cole takes Halloran and Vesper; the ring unravels along the city's quiet networks.
46. **The Returned Shelf** — Mara recovers her sold memories from evidence; she may keep them now.
47. **The Wedding Day** — She replays her wedding once, then decides whether to keep or release it.
48. **A Memory for Otto** — Mara gifts Otto a clean recollection of Tomas, distilled from her own.
49. **Closing the Shop** — Mara lets her license lapse; she will not sell grief anymore, hers or others'.
50. **What She Chooses to Keep** — Mara keeps Daniel, flaws and all, and walks into the ordinary morning.
