# The Kitchen Witch — Outline

## Premise

Hazel Marrow, a wandering truth-witch, settles in the misty village of Ashcombe and opens a pie shop. Her pies have a peculiar magic: whoever eats one cannot tell a lie for the rest of the day. In a village where the mayor is cooking the books, the baker's boy is hiding a stolen identity, and every villager nurses a secret, Hazel's pies begin cracking facades wide open. As truths spill like hot filling, Hazel must decide whether her gift is a blessing or a weapon — and whether she can stay in a place that may not want to be known.

## Core Cast

- **Hazel Marrow** — A wandering truth-witch in her early thirties. She fled her coven after they used her gift for political gain. Quiet, watchful, with steady hands and a gentle voice. She believes in second chances.
- **The Mayor (Alderman Prickett)** — Portly, florid, and charming. He has been skimming village funds for years. His smile never reaches his eyes. He sees Hazel as a threat the moment her pies touch his tongue.
- **The Baker's Boy (Finn)** — Thin, wary, with flour perpetually under his nails. He is not who he says he is. He fled the city after a crime he did not commit. He works for Hazel and falls for her, slowly, despite himself.

### Supporting Cast

- **Mrs. Hempstock** — The village gossip. She runs the dry-goods shop and knows everything about everyone. Her pies reveal the most dangerous truths.
- **Constable Briggs** — The village constable. Honest, slow-moving, and tired. He wants peace more than justice.
- **Larkin** — The mayor's clerk. A nervous young man who keeps the mayor's ledgers. He knows too much.
- **Old Sallow** — A reclusive herb woman who lives at the edge of the woods. She knows what Hazel is and warns her.
- **Nell** — A young mother whose husband disappeared. She comes to Hazel's shop for comfort and leaves with the truth.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I — The Arrival (Chapters 1–10)

1. Hazel arrives in Ashcombe on a foggy morning, her cart laden with pie tins and strange herbs.
2. She rents the old sweet shop on Market Lane and meets Finn, who offers to help clean.
3. Hazel bakes her first pie — a blackberry and sage — and senses the village's unease.
4. The Mayor stops by the shop, introduces himself, and eats a slice. He blurts out that he dislikes his wife's tea cakes.
5. Finn works beside her. She notices he flinches at loud noises and avoids questions about his past.
6. Mrs. Hempstock comes for a pie and accidentally reveals she knows about the mayor's missing funds.
7. Constable Briggs visits. His pie makes him admit he knows about the mayor but has no proof.
8. Hazel learns that the previous tenant of her shop vanished one night.
9. Old Sallow finds Hazel in the woods and warns her: "Truth is a blade. Do not hand it out with sugar."
10. Finn tells Hazel his real name is not Finn. She says she does not need to know more.

### Part II — Fault Lines (Chapters 11–20)

11. The Mayor tries to buy Hazel's property. She refuses. He smiles and says she will change her mind.
12. Hazel bakes a pear and ginger pie for the village fete. The entire town square tells awkward truths.
13. Finn's past catches up: a letter arrives with the mark of the city watch.
14. Hazel reads the letter before Finn can hide it. He tells her he was accused of arson.
15. Nell's husband, it turns out, did not leave — he is buried in the mayor's cellar.
16. Mrs. Hempstock spreads the news. The village is in uproar. The Mayor denies everything.
17. Hazel bakes a mince pie for the Mayor. He eats it and confesses to the missing man's death.
18. Constable Briggs arrests the Mayor, but the Mayor laughs — there is no body, no proof.
19. The Mayor posts bail and returns to his office. He watches Hazel's shop from his window.
20. Finn shows Hazel the trapdoor in the mayor's cellar. She now knows where Nell's husband lies.

### Part III — The Unraveling (Chapters 21–30)

21. Hazel and Finn dig in the cellar at midnight. They find bones.
22. Constable Briggs is summoned. He sees the bones and has no choice but to reopen the case.
23. The Mayor accuses Finn of planting evidence. The village is divided.
24. Larkin, the mayor's clerk, comes to Hazel's shop and eats a pie. He admits to falsifying ledgers.
25. The Mayor fires Larkin and threatens to burn Hazel's shop.
26. Hazel bakes a rosewater and honey pie. She gives a slice to the Mayor's wife, who reveals her husband's hidden accounts.
27. The Mayor's wife leaves him. The village begins to turn against him.
28. Finn's past accuser arrives in Ashcombe — a wealthy merchant who swears Finn burned his warehouse.
29. Hazel bakes a pie for the merchant. He admits he set the fire himself for insurance.
30. Finn is exonerated. He kisses Hazel in the middle of Market Lane.

### Part IV — Reckoning (Chapters 31–40)

31. The Mayor goes to ground. His house is dark. His carriage is gone.
32. Hazel finds a note under her door: "Leave before the next fog."
33. Finn wants to leave with her. She says she is tired of running.
34. The village elders hold a meeting. They decide the Mayor must face justice properly.
35. Constable Briggs finds the Mayor hiding in a hunting lodge outside town.
36. The Mayor escapes before Briggs can arrest him. The village goes into lockdown.
37. Hazel bakes her most powerful pie — nightshade and pennyroyal, a truth-brew that forces confession.
38. She feeds it to the Mayor in the village square. He confesses everything: the missing funds, the murder of Nell's husband, the bribes, the threats.
39. The Mayor is taken away in chains. The village is silent. Then Nell begins to clap.
40. Hazel collapses. The magic has taken everything from her. Finn carries her to her bed.

### Part V — Aftermath (Chapters 41–50)

41. Hazel wakes weak and feverish. Old Sallow tends to her with bitter tea.
42. The village holds a funeral for Nell's husband. Hazel watches from her window.
43. Finn takes over the shop while Hazel recovers. His bread sells well.
44. Hazel's magic returns slowly. She bakes a simple apple pie. It does not compel truth — only comfort.
45. She realizes her magic has changed. It no longer forces truth — it invites it.
46. Mrs. Hempstock suggests Hazel stay. The village has grown fond of her.
47. Finn asks Hazel to marry him. She says she will think about it.
48. The Mayor's trial is held in the next town. He is sentenced to twenty years.
49. Hazel bakes a wedding pie — not for herself, but for Nell and a new love Nell has found.
50. Hazel stays. She hangs a new sign: "Marrow's Pies — Where the Truth Tastes Sweet."
