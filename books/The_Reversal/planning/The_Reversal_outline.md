# The Reversal — Outline

## Premise
Dr. Cassia Morrigan, a respected psychiatrist at a quiet private clinic, takes on a new patient named Henry Voss — a soft-spoken, articulate man who insists he is also a psychiatrist. From the first session, Henry seems to know things about Cassia that no patient should know: her habits, her childhood, the name of the dog she had at age nine. As the weeks pass, his words begin to live inside her head. Her other patients start to sound like echoes of her own buried memories. The clinical boundary — the one that keeps the doctor on one side of the chair and the patient on the other — begins to dissolve. Slowly, Cassia stops being certain who is treating whom.

## Core Cast
- Dr. Cassia Morrigan — the protagonist; a 44-year-old psychiatrist with a careful, ordered life
- Henry Voss — Cassia's newest patient; claims to be a psychiatrist, knows too much
- Dr. Adam Wilkes — Cassia's supervising colleague and old friend; the voice of reason
- Mrs. Eloise Tennant — Cassia's longtime patient; an elderly woman convinced her dead husband is still in the house
- Jonah Park — a teenage patient of Cassia's; quiet, watchful, mirrors her own adolescence
- Mara Voss — Henry's estranged wife, who calls the clinic asking strange questions

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I: The New Patient (Chapters 1–10)
1. **The Intake** — Cassia meets Henry Voss for the first time. He is polite, calm, and asks her one question that lands wrong.
2. **The Question** — Henry asks Cassia why she became a psychiatrist. His answer is more precise than hers.
3. **The File** — Cassia reviews Henry's referral notes. The referring physician cannot be reached. The notes are unsigned.
4. **The Office** — A tour of Cassia's clinic: the waiting room, her consulting room, the locked drawer where she keeps personal notes.
5. **The Other Chair** — Henry comments on the chair across from Cassia's. He says he has sat in it before.
6. **The Childhood** — Henry mentions the name of a dog Cassia had at age nine. Cassia has never told anyone that name.
7. **The Sessions Resume** — Cassia sees her other patients: Mrs. Tennant, Jonah Park, a man convinced he is being replaced.
8. **The Ringing** — Cassia hears a phone ring in her empty office. The call is from a number she almost recognizes.
9. **The Reflection** — Cassia catches her own reflection in the clinic window. For a moment, the face does not match her movements.
10. **The Wrong Note** — Cassia finds a handwritten note in her own handwriting that she does not remember writing. It says: He is right.

### Act II: The Erosion (Chapters 11–20)
11. **The Tuesday Patient** — Cassia sees the man who believes he is being replaced. He says Cassia has also been replaced.
12. **The Locked Drawer** — Cassia opens the drawer. The personal notes are still there, but one is in a different ink.
13. **Mrs. Tennant** — Eloise describes her dead husband in detail. She describes Cassia's father, not her own.
14. **The Question Marked** — Henry asks Cassia whether she has ever had a patient who knew her secrets. He smiles before she answers.
15. **The Drift** — Cassia cannot remember her drive home. The car is parked in the driveway. The engine is warm.
16. **The Tape** — Cassia listens to a recording of her session with Henry. Her own voice on the tape says things she does not recall saying.
17. **Jonah** — Jonah Park tells Cassia he has been watching her through the window of the clinic. He describes her apartment, her kitchen, her dog.
18. **The Wrong Question** — In a session, Cassia asks Henry a question and is suddenly unsure which of them is supposed to answer.
19. **The Coffee** — Cassia makes coffee in her office kitchenette. She finds a second mug already in the sink, still warm.
20. **The Break** — Cassia cancels her afternoon appointments and goes home. She sits in her living room and does not move for two hours.

### Act III: The Mirror Patients (Chapters 21–30)
21. **The Catalog** — Cassia reviews her caseload. Each patient, she realizes, is describing a piece of her own life.
22. **Eloise's Husband** — Eloise tells Cassia her dead husband is in the next room. Cassia hears a chair creak in her own empty clinic.
23. **The Replaced Man** — The man who believes he is being replaced says his replacement has been here for years. He points at Cassia.
24. **Henry's Reading** — Henry quotes from a textbook Cassia keeps on her shelf. The book has never been opened to that page.
25. **The Mirror Patient** — A new patient arrives: a woman who claims her therapist has been treating her own reflection for years.
26. **Jonah's File** — Jonah's intake form is missing the page where he should have answered "Reason for visit." The space is blank. Cassia's handwriting fills it in.
27. **The Sleep** — Cassia cannot sleep. When she finally does, she dreams she is sitting in her own chair, across from herself.
28. **The Office Chair** — Cassia sits in her patient's chair, in her own clinic, after hours. From there, her desk looks like someone else's.
29. **The Voice in the Hall** — Cassia hears her own voice speaking in the empty hallway of the clinic. The words are ones she never said out loud.
30. **The Tuesday Return** — Henry arrives for his next session. He sits in his chair. He does not speak for ten minutes. He is waiting for her to begin.

### Act IV: The Reversal (Chapters 31–40)
31. **The Supervising Voice** — Adam Wilkes, Cassia's supervisor, calls. He has heard rumors about her caseload. He suggests she take a leave.
32. **The Refusal** — Cassia refuses. She insists she is fine. Adam hesitates. He says her voice on the phone sounds like someone else.
33. **Henry's Wife** — Mara Voss calls the clinic. She says Henry has never been a patient. He is, in fact, a psychiatrist. She wants to know who is treating him.
34. **The Confession** — In the next session, Henry tells Cassia he has been treating her for years. He lists the symptoms. He has notes.
35. **The Notebook** — Henry produces a notebook filled with Cassia's handwriting. She does not remember writing it. It is full of sessions she does not recall.
36. **The Reversal** — Henry asks Cassia to sit in the patient's chair. He sits in hers. He begins the session. She answers before he finishes the question.
37. **The Symptoms** — Henry recites her symptoms. Memory gaps. Auditory hallucinations. Misidentifying reflections. The clinical language is hers.
38. **The Acceptance** — Cassia begins to cry. It is the first honest thing she has done in weeks. Henry hands her a tissue. He is gentle.
39. **The Question of Identity** — Henry asks Cassia: if the doctor is the patient, then who is the doctor? She does not know. He says that is the right answer.
40. **The Bargain** — Henry offers to keep treating her, but only if she stops seeing other patients. Cassia agrees.

### Act V: The Quiet Cure (Chapters 41–50)
41. **The Empty Waiting Room** — Cassia cancels her entire caseload. She tells the clinic she is taking a leave. The waiting room is silent.
42. **The Hall** — Cassia walks the hallway of the clinic. She hears voices in the closed offices. They are her own patients, still talking, even after she has left.
43. **Adam's Visit** — Adam comes to Cassia's home. She does not answer the door. He leaves a note. The note says he is worried. The handwriting is hers.
44. **The Notebook, Continued** — Henry returns the notebook to Cassia. She reads it cover to cover. The first entry is dated eleven years ago. She was not yet a psychiatrist.
45. **The Wrong Name** — Cassia finds an old clinic directory. Her name is not on it. The name on the directory is a woman who looks like her, but is not.
46. **The Mirror Patient Returns** — The woman who came to Cassia about her reflection is at Cassia's door. She says she is here to treat Cassia, not the other way around.
47. **The Final Session** — Henry leads Cassia through a final session. He asks her one question: What did you lose? She answers: myself.
48. **The Admission** — Cassia is admitted, voluntarily, to a small psychiatric hospital. She signs her own intake form. She recognizes the woman at the desk.
49. **The Other Chair** — In her room, there is a chair across from her bed. Cassia sits in it. She waits for the patient who will never come.
50. **The Reversal** — Months later, Cassia is well enough to return to work. She does not. Instead, she sits in a chair across from a new therapist and begins, finally, to speak.
