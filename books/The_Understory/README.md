# The Understory

**Author:** Barış Kısır
**Genre:** Literary Fiction
**Subgenre:** Non-Human Point of View / Ecological Fiction
**Model:** sonnet-4.6
**Chapters:** 50

## Synopsis

Beneath an old-growth Pacific forest, a centuries-old mycelial network narrates its own slow life — tending a dying thousand-year fir, nursing her starved seedlings, warring with a root-rot rival, and enduring the loggers' road and the fire that follows — in a deep-time consciousness that measures the world in seasons, rot, and rain.

Beneath an old-growth Pacific Northwest forest lives a vast mycelial network — a single distributed fungal organism, centuries old, with no eyes, no center, and no hurry. It senses the world through chemistry, moisture, pressure, and the slow grammar of root touch, and it measures time in seasons, rot, and rain. This is its story, told in its own patient, distributed voice.
