# The Gravity Orchestra - Outline

## Premise

In a crowded near-future orbit, debris shepherd Kira Nalen pilots a small salvage tug that nudges dead satellites out of traffic lanes. When a collision cascade begins in a supposedly empty orbital shell, Kira notices that the fragments are not random: their timing resembles an engineered resonance pattern, a "gravity orchestra" once proposed for coordinated debris removal but abandoned as too risky. With ground controllers losing trust, insurers pushing blame, and a storm of metal threatening hospitals, navigation, and weather systems below, Kira must turn the hidden pattern into a rescue maneuver before orbit becomes unusable for a generation.

## Core Cast

- **Kira Nalen** - Adult debris shepherd and salvage tug pilot, calm in vacuum and blunt on radio.
- **Joss Ader** - Adult flight dynamics officer who hears orbital timing like rhythm.
- **Dr. Sian Rook** - Adult orbital mechanics researcher whose old resonance paper becomes central.
- **Malik Hesh** - Adult mission medic aboard the maintenance platform Anchor Nine.
- **Vera Sol** - Adult orbital traffic commander balancing public safety and political pressure.
- **Tane Orlo** - Adult executive for Helix Lift, owner of the dead satellite cluster.
- **Edda Lin** - Adult insurance investigator trying to protect coverage exposure.
- **Pavel Xu** - Adult robotics specialist maintaining Kira's capture arms.
- **Noor Vale** - Adult weather-satellite operator whose network sits in the debris path.
- **Captain Ren Ito** - Adult commander of the evacuation ferry docked at Anchor Nine.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Metal Weather
1. **Shepherd Burn** - Kira nudges a dead cubesat and sees unexpected fragments crossing her lane.
2. **Traffic Black** - Orbital traffic issues a temporary blackout in Shell C.
3. **Anchor Nine** - Malik reports the maintenance platform has lost one radiator loop.
4. **Joss's Rhythm** - Joss notices debris arrivals repeating at impossible intervals.
5. **Helix Denial** - Tane Orlo insists Helix's retired satellites were passivated.
6. **Old Paper** - Sian recognizes the timing from her abandoned gravity-orchestra proposal.
7. **Weather Net** - Noor warns weather satellites will enter the fragment sheet.
8. **Insurance Voice** - Edda asks whether the cascade can be called natural collision.
9. **Capture Arm** - Pavel repairs Kira's tug arm with limited spares.
10. **First Strike** - A fragment punches Anchor Nine's outer shield.

### Part II: Resonance
11. **Harmonic Shell** - Joss maps the fragments into a coordinated resonance.
12. **Dead Cluster** - Kira approaches the Helix satellite cluster that should be inert.
13. **Cold Transponder** - One dead satellite emits a guidance pulse.
14. **Sian's Guilt** - Sian explains how her theory could shepherd debris or amplify disaster.
15. **Evacuation Ferry** - Captain Ito prepares a risky departure from Anchor Nine.
16. **Blocked Corridor** - Traffic corridors close as debris counts rise.
17. **The Hidden Burn** - Kira finds evidence of unauthorized station-keeping fuel.
18. **Ground Argument** - Vera orders data sharing over Helix objections.
19. **Metal Rain Forecast** - Noor predicts loss of weather coverage if the sheet expands.
20. **The Quiet Satellite** - Kira captures one fragmenting satellite and finds modified software.

### Part III: Counter-Music
21. **Score Draft** - Joss and Sian design a counter-resonance using active tugs.
22. **Too Few Tugs** - Vera counts assets and finds the plan short by three vehicles.
23. **Borrowed Engines** - Kira proposes using dead satellites' remaining fuel.
24. **Helix Secret** - Tane admits Helix tested automated debris herding without approval.
25. **Anchor Choice** - Malik refuses evacuation until patients and crew are sequenced.
26. **Pavel's Patch** - Pavel rewrites Kira's tug arm software for remote burns.
27. **Edda's Coverage** - Edda releases Helix risk memos to keep blame from burying facts.
28. **First Counter-Burn** - Kira lights a captured satellite and shifts the fragment rhythm.
29. **Bad Note** - One old fuel tank tumbles out of phase and threatens Anchor Nine.
30. **Hand Capture** - Kira performs a manual capture on the tumbling tank.

### Part IV: Cascade Night
31. **No Window** - Traffic loses all clean departure windows for two hours.
32. **Patient Transfer** - Malik moves critical patients through a damaged pressure collar.
33. **Weather Blind** - Noor's network drops a hemisphere of storm prediction.
34. **Second Score** - Joss adjusts the resonance plan with incomplete telemetry.
35. **Command Split** - Vera resists political pressure to save commercial assets first.
36. **Helix Burn** - Tane provides command keys for the remaining cluster.
37. **Ferry Run** - Captain Ito departs Anchor Nine through a narrowing debris gap.
38. **Shield Loss** - Anchor Nine loses another shield section.
39. **Kira Outside** - Kira exits the tug to clear a jammed capture arm by hand.
40. **The Orchestra Starts** - Counter-burns begin across the shell.

### Part V: Usable Sky
41. **Wrong Silence** - The first wave succeeds, but a silent satellite fails to answer.
42. **Last Tug** - Kira commits her tug as the missing engine in the score.
43. **Falling Line** - The counter-resonance drives debris into planned decay paths.
44. **Anchor Dawn** - Anchor Nine survives long enough for repair crews.
45. **Public Orbit** - Vera broadcasts the raw cause and rescue record.
46. **Helix Reckoning** - Tane faces sanction while helping finish cleanup.
47. **Weather Returns** - Noor restores partial forecasting from patched satellites.
48. **Sian's Revision** - Sian rewrites the gravity-orchestra theory with hard limits.
49. **Shepherds' Memorial** - Kira marks the lost machines and near-missed people.
50. **The Gravity Orchestra** - Kira returns to orbit, hearing the sky as work rather than music.
