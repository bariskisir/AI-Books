# The Cold Water — Outline

## Premise

In the summer of 1992, twelve-year-old River Corso is left alone in a central Florida trailer park when his mother drives to Tampa for a waitressing shift she never comes back from. When Hurricane Celia — a Category 4 storm — barrels across the peninsula, River must board up their double-wide by himself, ride out the worst of it in a bathtub, and emerge into a world where everything he knew is underwater or gone. In the aftermath, an elderly Cuban-American neighbor who lost her own home teaches him that survival is not about being tough — it is about letting people in.

## Core Cast

- **River Corso** — A twelve-year-old boy, sharp-eyed and quiet, accustomed to fending for himself. His mother works double shifts; his father is a name on a birth certificate. He reads library books and watches the weather channel obsessively. He has never told anyone he is afraid of the dark.

- **Mrs. Delgado** — A seventy-three-year-old widow who has lived in the park for thirty years. She fled Cuba in 1961 with nothing but a sewing machine and her daughter's baptism dress. Her English is accented and precise. She does not own a television but can read a sky better than any meteorologist.

- **The Storm (Hurricane Celia)** — A presence as much as a weather system. The storm functions as antagonist, catalyst, and crucible — a force that strips away pretense and forces every character to decide what they are willing to lose.

- **Dina Corso** — River's mother. Waitress, smoker, dreamer. She loves her son but does not know how to stay. Her absence is the wound the book circles.

- **Luis Delgado** — Mrs. Delgado's son, a contractor in Miami who cannot reach his mother during the storm. His phone calls are the only connection to the outside world.

- **Randy Phelps** — The trailer park manager, a loud man with a bad back and a good heart. He drives a rusted F-150 and keeps a shotgun behind his desk.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Long Summer (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Heat** — River sits on the concrete steps of the Laundromat, watching heat shimmer off asphalt. His mother is late. Again.

2. **Marlboro Lights** — Dina comes home with a black eye and a story about a car door. River makes her macaroni and cheese.

3. **The Weather Channel** — River watches a tropical depression form off the coast of Africa. The map shows a dotted line.

4. **Mrs. Delgado's Porch** — River brings the mail to Mrs. Delgado. She offers him iced tea. He does not know how to talk to old people.

5. **The Mobile Home** — A catalog of the small, fragile world River inhabits: the cigarette burns in the carpet, the hum of the air conditioner, the photograph of his mother before she got tired.

6. **Dina's Shift** — Dina kisses River on the forehead and drives away in the Dart. She says she will be back by morning.

7. **The Upgrade** — The storm is now a named system. River watches the cone tighten. He counts the cans of soup in the pantry.

8. **Randy's Announcement** — Randy nails plywood schedules to the bulletin board. Mrs. Delgado asks River if he has a plan.

9. **The Lie** — River tells Randy his mother is at the grocery store. He buys the last two gallons of water.

10. **The Bathtub** — River fills the tub and lines it with pillows. The wind starts to hum.

### Part II: The Storm (Chapters 11-20)

11. **First Rain** — Sheets of water hit the trailer sideways. River watches from the kitchen window until he cannot see the road.

12. **The Power Goes** — The lights flicker and die. River finds the flashlight under the sink. The batteries are dead.

13. **The Roof Cries** — Water seeps through the ceiling in three places. River puts pots on the floor and listens to the drip-drip-drum.

14. **The Worst of It** — The trailer shakes on its blocks. River gets in the bathtub and pulls a mattress over himself. He prays to a God he is not sure he believes in.

15. **The Eye** — Silence. River crawls out of the tub and steps outside. The world is gray and still. He can see the sky.

16. **The Back Half** — The wind returns from the opposite direction. River barely makes it back inside. The front door tears off its hinges.

17. **Stuck** — A tree falls across the trailer, pinning the back bedroom. River cannot open the bathroom door. He uses a screwdriver to pop the hinges.

18. **The Basement** — The trailer has no basement, but the concrete pad underneath offers shelter. River crawls into the crawlspace with a can of SpaghettiOs and a lighter.

19. **The Long Hour** — River shares the crawlspace with a possum and the sound of the world ending. He tells himself a story about his father to keep from crying.

20. **Morning** — The storm passes. River emerges into a landscape he does not recognize. The trailer park is gone.

### Part III: The Aftermath (Chapters 21-30)

21. **What Remains** — River walks through the wreckage. The Laundromat is a skeleton. The office has no roof. He finds his mother's photograph, warped and wet.

22. **The Dart** — Dina's car is upside down in the drainage ditch, but she is not in it. River does not know whether to be relieved or terrified.

23. **Mrs. Delgado** — River finds Mrs. Delgado standing in the ruins of her home, holding a sewing machine — the only thing she carried from Cuba. She has a gash on her arm.

24. **First Aid** — River uses a first-aid kit from the flipped office to bandage Mrs. Delgado's arm. His hands are steady. He learned from watching Rescue 911.

25. **The Relief** — National Guard trucks appear on the highway. River and Mrs. Delgado are given water and MREs. Mrs. Delgado shares hers with a stray dog.

26. **Randy's Inventory** — Randy makes a list of who is accounted for. Seven people missing. River's mother is one of them.

27. **The Phone Bank** — A Red Cross trailer with a satellite phone. Mrs. Delgado calls Luis in Miami. River does not know who to call.

28. **The Shelter** — They are bused to a high school gymnasium. River sleeps on a cot next to Mrs. Delgado. She hums a song he does not recognize.

29. **The Search** — River tells a volunteer that his mother was driving home from Tampa. The volunteer writes it down with a serious face. River realizes she is not coming back.

30. **The News** — A list of the dead is posted on a bulletin board. River reads it three times. Dina Corso is not on it. He does not know what this means.

### Part IV: The Waiting (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Motel** — FEMA moves the displaced into a motel on the interstate. River shares a room with Mrs. Delgado and three strangers.

32. **Luis Arrives** — Luis drives down from Miami. He is a big man with a gray mustache and gentle hands. He hugs his mother for a long time.

33. **The Call** — The Red Cross calls the motel office. River's mother has been found alive in a hospital in Lakeland. She has no memory of the storm.

34. **The Visit** — Luis drives River to Lakeland. Dina is sitting up in bed, eating Jell-O. She smiles at River like he is a stranger.

35. **The Doctor** — A neurologist explains that Dina has a concussion and retrograde amnesia. She may not remember the last two weeks. River does not know if this is a gift or a cruelty.

36. **Mrs. Delgado's Offer** — Until Dina is released, River will stay with Mrs. Delgado in a FEMA trailer in her son's backyard. He is grateful and ashamed.

37. **Miami** — River sees the ocean for the first time. Mrs. Delgado makes him ropa vieja. Luis teaches him to play dominoes.

38. **The Yard** — The FEMA trailer is small and hot, but Mrs. Delgado plants a garden. She says the soil here remembers nothing. It will grow whatever you give it.

39. **The Phone Call** — Dina calls from the hospital. She remembers River's name but not the color of his eyes. River tells her they are brown. He is testing her. She does not correct him.

40. **The Visit Again** — River returns to Lakeland alone on a Greyhound. Dina is different — quieter, softer. She watches him eat and cries without explanation.

### Part V: The Emergence (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Return** — Dina is discharged. She cannot return to the trailer — there is nothing to return to. She stands in the rubble and does not speak for an hour.

42. **The Paperwork** — Dina signs forms at a FEMA office. She is eligible for housing assistance. She asks River to read the fine print because she forgot her reading glasses. She has never worn reading glasses.

43. **The New Place** — An apartment in Kissimmee. Two bedrooms. A window that faces a parking lot. Dina hangs the warped photograph on the wall.

44. **Mrs. Delgado's Kitchen** — River visits Mrs. Delgado one last time before she returns to the rebuilt trailer park. She gives him her sewing machine. He does not know how to sew. She tells him he will learn.

45. **The Apartment** — River finds a routine. School. Groceries. The Weather Channel. Dina gets a job at a diner. She comes home every night.

46. **The Scar** — River shows Mrs. Delgado the scar on his arm from the night he crawled out of the crawlspace. She traces it with her finger and says it will fade. It already has.

47. **The Gift** — Mrs. Delgado gives River a small wooden box. Inside is a photograph of her and her husband on their wedding day in Havana, 1968. She says he should keep it because he knows what it means to lose everything and start over.

48. **Dina's Truth** — Dina tells River the truth about the night of the storm. She did not just go to work. She went to leave him. She changed her mind on the road. The crash took the memory but gave her a second chance. River does not know if he can forgive her.

49. **The Cold Water** — River walks to the shore of a lake near the apartment. He puts his hand in the water. It is cold. He thinks about his mother, Mrs. Delgado, the storm, the trailer, the crawlspace, the photograph. He takes his hand out. He goes home.

50. **The Garden** — One year later. Mrs. Delgado's garden has grown. Dina is tending it. River watches from the porch of the rebuilt trailer. The sky is clear. The water is warm. He is not alone.
