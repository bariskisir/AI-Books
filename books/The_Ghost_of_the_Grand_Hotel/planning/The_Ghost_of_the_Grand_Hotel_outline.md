# The Ghost of the Grand Hotel -- Outline

## Premise

Claire Durand, a hotel historian specializing in the grand resorts of the Belle Epoque, arrives at the Hotel du Lac on Lake Geneva to document its fading glory before a corporate renovation erases its character. The hotel was once the playground of European royalty and artists; now it is a relic with peeling wallpaper and empty corridors. Assigned to Room 307 -- a room that should not exist according to the floor plans -- Claire discovers that the room's antique mirror connects to the hotel as it was in 1924. A woman waits for her on the other side: Marguerite, a guest who checked in a century ago and never checked out. As Claire investigates, she learns that Marguerite is trapped in a single night -- the night her lover was murdered -- and that Claire herself may be the key to releasing her.

## Core Cast

- **Claire Durand** -- A hotel historian in her late thirties. Methodical, skeptical, but haunted by her own inability to form lasting connections. She books into the Hotel du Lac for a three-week research residency.

- **The Night Porter (Jean-Luc)** -- An elderly man who has worked at the hotel for fifty years. He knows the building's secrets, maintains the silence of a generation of staff, and watches Claire with concern as she gets closer to the truth.

- **The Woman in 307 (Marguerite Delacroix)** -- A French socialite who stayed at the hotel in the summer of 1924. Elegant, sharp-tongued, and trapped in the moment her life was destroyed. She exists in a loop of that single night, aware of her imprisonment but unable to break free.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Arrival (Chapters 1-10)

1. **Arrival at Dusk** -- Claire takes the train along Lake Geneva. The hotel appears through the mist, grand and decayed. She registers at the deserted lobby.

2. **The Key to 307** -- The clerk hesitates before handing over the key to Room 307. Claire notices the old-fashioned brass key, different from the others.

3. **First Impressions** -- Claire explores the room: heavy velvet curtains, a tarnished mirror, a writing desk with old stationery. The room smells of dust and flowers.

4. **The Hallway Without Doors** -- Walking the third floor, Claire notices a discrepancy: the corridor outside 307 is longer than the building permits. A door appears in the shadows.

5. **The Night Porter's Warning** -- Jean-Luc, the night porter, finds Claire studying the hotel registry. He tells her some rooms are best left unopened.

6. **Archives and Photographs** -- Claire examines the hotel archives. A 1924 photograph shows a group of guests. One woman stares directly at the camera, her expression unnerving.

7. **The Woman in the Mirror** -- At midnight, Claire catches movement in the room's mirror. A woman in a silver dress stands behind her. Claire turns -- no one is there.

8. **Hotel Maps and Missing Rooms** -- Claire compares modern and 1924 floor plans. Room 307 is marked on neither. It exists in the gap between them.

9. **The Sound of Music** -- Faint jazz drifts up from the ballroom at 2 AM. The ballroom has been closed for years. Claire follows the sound.

10. **A Door Opens** -- Claire touches the mirror in 307. The surface ripples. She steps through into 1924 -- the hotel alive with light and laughter.

### Part II: The Lost Summer (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Hotel Alive** -- Claire stands in the Grand Ballroom of 1924. Crystal chandeliers, champagne, a jazz band. Guests in flapper dresses and tuxedos.

12. **Meeting Marguerite** -- A woman approaches Claire -- the woman from the photograph. Marguerite Delacroix assumes Claire is a fellow guest. She is warm and curious.

13. **The Rules of the Dream** -- Claire realizes she can interact with this world but cannot change the past. Marguerite treats the evening as if it is the first time -- and every time.

14. **Victor's Arrival** -- A man named Victor joins them. Marguerite's lover. He is distracted, looking over his shoulder. Something is wrong.

15. **A Walk on the Terrace** -- Marguerite shows Claire the hotel gardens as they were in 1924. She speaks of her love for Victor, of plans to run away together.

16. **The Argument** -- Claire witnesses a heated exchange between Victor and another man in the library. Victor owes money. The other man threatens him.

17. **Marguerite's Confession** -- In the early hours, Marguerite tells Claire she is afraid. Victor has secrets. She suspects he is involved in something dangerous.

18. **The Shot** -- A gunshot echoes from the lakeside terrace. Claire runs to find Victor dead. Marguerite kneels beside him, her dress soaked in blood.

19. **The Loop Begins** -- Time snaps backward. Claire is back in 307 in the present, the mirror solid. She understands: Marguerite relives this night forever.

20. **Research and Obsession** -- Claire dives into historical records. The 1924 summer season. Victor's identity. What really happened that night?

### Part III: The Investigation (Chapters 21-30)

21. **Jean-Luc's Knowledge** -- Claire confronts the night porter. He admits he has felt the room's presence for decades. Past guests have reported strange occurrences.

22. **The Thief's Trail** -- Claire discovers Victor was not a socialite but a thief. He stole a valuable diamond necklace from a Russian countess at the hotel.

23. **The Countess Returns** -- Records show the countess checked out the morning after the murder. The necklace was never recovered. Claire suspects she knows who fired the shot.

24. **Crossing Again** -- Claire steps back through the mirror, now better prepared. She arrives at the same moment, determined to watch closely this time.

25. **The Second Time** -- Claire observes the night from the edges. She tracks Victor's movements, his meetings, his fear. The pattern is the same, but she notices new details.

26. **The Third Man** -- A hotel employee appears in the background of the night's events. Claire realizes he was watching Victor too. An accomplice? A witness?

27. **Marguerite's Prison** -- Claire speaks with Marguerite about the loop. Marguerite is aware of her situation but cannot describe it. She only knows she must relive the worst moment of her life.

28. **The Missing Necklace** -- Claire searches the hotel archives for any mention of the stolen necklace. A clippings file reveals a scandal was covered up.

29. **Jean-Luc's Father** -- Jean-Luc admits his father worked at the hotel in 1924. He was the night porter. He witnessed something but never spoke of it.

30. **The Truth Begins** -- Claire pieces it together: the countess killed Victor in self-defense, or so it seemed. But the necklace was never found. Someone else took it.

### Part IV: Breaking the Loop (Chapters 31-40)

31. **A Different Approach** -- Claire decides not to observe but to act. She brings a modern object through the mirror -- a camera. It works in 1924.

32. **Photographing the Truth** -- Claire photographs the night as it unfolds. She catches images that contradict the official story. The countess had a companion that night.

33. **The Companion** -- A young man accompanied the countess. Claire finds no record of him in the guest ledgers. He was not registered.

34. **Jean-Luc's Secret** -- The night porter reveals his father was the young man. He was the countess's lover, and he helped cover up the truth.

35. **Victor's Crime** -- Claire learns the full story: Victor stole the necklace and planned to sell it. The countess caught him in the act. The companion (Jean-Luc's father) intervened.

36. **The Real Killer** -- It was not the countess who fired the shot. It was Jean-Luc's father, in a struggle. Victor died accidentally.

37. **Marguerite's Grief** -- Claire tells Marguerite the truth. Victor was not the man she thought. He was a thief who used her for access to the hotel's wealthy guests.

38. **The Choice** -- Marguerite must choose: accept the truth and move on, or remain in her loop forever, preserving the illusion of Victor's love.

39. **The Final Night** -- Claire steps through the mirror one last time. She brings proof -- the photographs, the records. Marguerite examines them.

40. **Letting Go** -- Marguerite accepts the truth. She asks Claire to take her to the lakeside terrace at dawn, the moment the loop resets. She is ready.

### Part V: Departure (Chapters 41-50)

41. **Dawn on the Lake** -- Claire and Marguerite watch the sunrise over Lake Geneva. The 1924 world begins to dissolve. Marguerite smiles.

42. **The Room Changes** -- Claire returns to 307 in the present. The room is different -- lighter, airier. The oppressive weight is gone.

43. **The Necklace Found** -- Claire discovers a hidden compartment in the writing desk. Inside is the diamond necklace, wrapped in a yellowed handkerchief.

44. **Returning What Was Lost** -- Claire contacts the current descendant of the countess's family to return the necklace. She keeps only a photograph of Marguerite.

45. **Jean-Luc's Relief** -- The night porter feels the change. He thanks Claire. His father's ghost has also found peace.

46. **The Final Archive Entry** -- Claire writes a new entry for the hotel's history. The true story of Room 307, the summer of 1924, and the woman who waited.

47. **The Last Night** -- Claire packs her belongings. She looks into the mirror one final time. Her own reflection looks back. No one else.

48. **Closing the Room** -- The hotel manager decides to seal Room 307 permanently. Claire agrees it is the right thing. Some rooms should remain private.

49. **Departure at Dawn** -- Claire leaves the hotel as she arrived, by train along the lake. The hotel recedes into the mist, no longer sinister, only sad and beautiful.

50. **The Photograph** -- Months later, Claire receives a letter from the hotel. The photograph of Marguerite has changed: the woman in the silver dress is smiling.
