# The House of Many Windows — Outline

## Premise

Iris, a young architect specializing in brutalist renovations, wins a commission to refurbish the Meridian Tower — a crumbling 1970s brutalist high-rise on the edge of the city. As she studies the original blueprints and walks the corridors, she discovers that the building's distinctive window grid, ventilation shafts, and structural core were not designed for aesthetics or efficiency. Every window, every angle, every reflective surface was calibrated to function as a monolithic surveillance instrument — a lens that sees into every home, every office, every private space in the surrounding city. The architect who designed it called it "The Panopticon of the People." The developer who funded it is still alive. And someone is watching Iris through the very windows she is trying to save.

## Core Cast

- **Iris** — A sharp, observant architect in her mid-30s. She specializes in brutalist renovation and has a deep respect for the honesty of concrete and glass. She is methodical, slightly withdrawn, and trusts what she can measure.
- **The Developer** — Elias Vancorp, 78. The original developer of Meridian Tower. A reclusive real-estate mogul with a passion for early cybernetics and surveillance theory. He never sold the building — he merely leased it. He holds the original design patent.
- **The Watchmaker** — A faceless figure who maintains the Meridian Tower's hidden systems. No one has ever seen his face. He lives in the sub-basement. He has been running the "observation protocols" for forty-seven years uninterrupted.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Lens (Chapters 1–15)

1. **The Commission** — Iris receives the call to bid on the Meridian Tower renovation.
2. **First Walkthrough** — She tours the tower with the building manager, noting the strange window geometry.
3. **The Blueprints** — Iris unrolls the original drawings. Something is wrong with the sightlines.
4. **The Grid** — She overlays the window positions on a map of the city. The pattern is too precise.
5. **The Resident** — A long-term tenant complains that the windows "watch back."
6. **Measurement** — Iris takes laser readings of every window angle. The data doesn't lie.
7. **The Core** — She discovers sealed access panels in the central service shaft.
8. **Hidden Space** — Behind the panels: a darkened room with operator stations from the 1970s.
9. **The Gears** — In the sub-basement, she finds a mechanical clockwork system still ticking.
10. **Elias Vancorp** — Iris researches the developer. His biography reads like a ghost story.
11. **The Archive** — She visits city records and finds construction documents that were never filed.
12. **The Patent** — Vancorp filed a patent for an "Optical Urban Observation System" in 1972.
13. **The Letter** — Iris receives an anonymous note: "The windows are not yours to open."
14. **Frequency** — She discovers the building's windows emit a low-frequency oscillation.
15. **The Missing Man** — The building manager who gave her keys has disappeared.

### Part II: The Watchmaker (Chapters 16–30)

16. **The Lock** — Iris changes the locks on the sub-basement. Next morning, they are original again.
17. **CCTV** — She reviews security footage. The cameras show only static during the lock change.
18. **The Tenant** — A tenant in 14C shows Iris his apartment: every window frames a specific building.
19. **The Map** — Iris pins aerial photos to her wall. The Meridian Tower is the hub of a wheel.
20. **The Shell Company** — Vancorp's holdings trace through seventeen shell corporations to one address.
21. **The Invitation** — Iris receives a formal dinner invitation from Elias Vancorp.
22. **The Dinner** — Vancorp is charming, ancient, and terrifying. He offers Iris a partnership.
23. **The Refusal** — Iris turns him down. That night, her apartment is broken into.
24. **The Archive II** — She returns to city records. Files on Meridian Tower have been removed.
25. **The Photograph** — A 1974 newspaper clipping shows the building's architect, who died in 1973.
26. **The Partner** — Iris tracks down the architect's surviving daughter, now in her 70s.
27. **The Confession** — The daughter reveals her father built the tower under duress. Vancorp threatened his family.
28. **The Mechanism** — Iris re-enters the sub-basement and fully maps the clockwork system.
29. **The Journal** — She finds the architect's personal journal, hidden in a wall cavity. It details everything.
30. **The Call** — The Watchmaker calls Iris directly. His voice is distorted. "Leave the building," he says.

### Part III: The Tower (Chapters 31–45)

31. **The City** — Iris realizes the tower's observation covers 90% of the downtown core.
32. **The Network** — Other buildings in the city have "companion lenses" — designed to receive the signals.
33. **The Witness** — A journalist who investigated Vancorp in the 1980s agrees to meet Iris.
34. **The Accident** — Before they can meet, the journalist dies in a "freak" elevator collapse.
35. **The Broadcast** — Iris discovers the tower transmits data via microwave relay. It is still active.
36. **The Archive III** — The city archive burns. Arson.
37. **The Ally** — The building's head of maintenance, Dez, agrees to help Iris expose the truth.
38. **The Plan** — Iris and Dez plan to disable the observation system from the sub-basement.
39. **The Intrusion** — The Watchmaker sabotages their plan. Dez is injured.
40. **The Blueprint Copy** — Iris retrieves a complete set of blueprints from the architect's daughter.
41. **The Weakness** — The system requires the central clockwork to maintain synchronization.
42. **The Decision** — Iris must decide: expose Vancorp or destroy the building's historical value utterly.
43. **The Media** — She contacts a reporter who agrees to run the story if she provides proof.
44. **The Trap** — Vancorp learns of her plan. He invites her to the penthouse for a "final negotiation."
45. **The Penthouse** — In Vancorp's apartment, Iris sees the ultimate control room. Every window is a screen.

### Part IV: Exposure (Chapters 46–50)

46. **The Ultimatum** — Vancorp offers Iris the building if she remains silent. She refuses.
47. **The Countdown** — The Watchmaker begins shutting down the tower's systems — which will destroy evidence.
48. **The Disable** — Iris reaches the sub-basement and stops the central clockwork mechanism.
49. **The Fall** — Vancorp is arrested. The Watchmaker disappears through a service tunnel.
50. **The New Building** — Iris opens the tower to the public. The windows no longer watch. They only let in light.
