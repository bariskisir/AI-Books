# The House of Many Windows

An Architectural Thriller

## Synopsis

Iris, a young architect specializing in brutalist renovation, wins a commission to refurbish the Meridian Tower — a crumbling 1970s brutalist high-rise on the edge of the city. As she studies the original blueprints and walks the corridors, she discovers that the building's distinctive window grid, ventilation shafts, and structural core were not designed for aesthetics or efficiency. Every window, every angle, every reflective surface was calibrated to function as a monolithic surveillance instrument — a lens that sees into every home, every office, every private space in the surrounding city.

The architect who designed it called it "The Panopticon of the People." The developer who funded it is still alive. And someone is watching Iris through the very windows she is trying to save.
