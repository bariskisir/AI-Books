# The Lost Colony — Outline

## Premise

In 1587, one hundred fifteen English settlers land on Roanoke Island to establish the first permanent English colony in the New World. Among them is Eleanor Sutton, a twenty-year-old tanner's daughter fleeing a contracted marriage in London. When Governor John White sails back to England for supplies and fails to return for three years, the colony unravels under the pressures of isolation, dwindling resources, and the growing rift between those who would fight the wilderness and those who would learn from it. Through Eleanor's eyes, the untold story of the Lost Colony unfolds not as a mystery but as a human reckoning with survival, identity, and the cost of empire.

## Core Cast

- **Eleanor Sutton** — A twenty-year-old English woman, practical and observant, who volunteered for the colony to escape an arranged marriage. She carries a small leather journal and records everything.
- **Manteo** — A Croatan man who traveled to England and returned as translator and intermediary. He moves between two worlds and trusts neither completely.
- **Governor John White** — An artist and gentleman, more suited to drawing maps than governing desperate people. His sense of duty wars with his need to return to England.
- **Ananias Dare** — Eleanor's cousin, a bricklayer, pragmatic and protective. He is Virginia Dare's father.
- **Eleanor Dare** — Ananias's wife, heavily pregnant at landing, who gives birth to Virginia Dare, the first English child born in the Americas.
- **Thomas Ellis** — A carpenter who becomes the colony's de facto leader after White sails. Suspicious of the natives and rigid in his thinking.
- **Margaret Lawrence** — A midwife and herbalist, the oldest woman in the colony. She becomes Eleanor's mentor in practical medicine.
- **George Howe** — A young soldier, impulsive and brave. His death early in the story marks a turning point.
- **Wanchese** — A Secotan man who also traveled to England but returned bitter and distrustful. He opposes Manteo's cooperation.

## Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Crossing — Chapters 1–10

1. **The Lion** — The fleet departs Plymouth in May 1587. Eleanor watches England shrink to a gray line and feels nothing but relief.
2. **Ship's Biscuit** — Life below decks: rats, weevils, and the slow erosion of hope. Eleanor meets Margaret Lawrence, who teaches her the names of medicinal plants.
3. **The Mapmaker** — Governor White sketches the coast from the quarterdeck. Eleanor asks him about the earlier colony. He tells her what the investors did not.
4. **A Cry in the Night** — Eleanor Dare goes into labor during a storm. Margaret delivers Virginia Dare, the first English child born in the New World.
5. **Landfall** — The settlers step ashore at Roanoke. The fort is ruined. The previous garrison left no message, only bones.
6. **The Carving** — Manteo watches the English from the tree line. Eleanor notices him first, a figure who does not move like a deer or a man she knows.
7. **Repair** — Thomas Ellis puts everyone to work rebuilding the fort. Eleanor carries timber until her hands bleed.
8. **The Welcome** — Manteo steps into the settlement with Croatan men carrying fish. White greets him in broken Algonquian.
9. **Translation** — Manteo sits at the governor's table and speaks of the mainland tribes. Wanchese says nothing, but his eyes say everything.
10. **The Map** — White draws a map of the sound and the sea. Eleanor traces the thin line between survival and oblivion with her finger.

### Part II: The Waiting — Chapters 11–20

11. **Summer Fever** — Sickness sweeps the colony. Margaret's herbs are all that stand between the settlers and the grave.
12. **The Garden** — Eleanor plants turnip seeds in sandy soil. Manteo shows her how to plant beans with the corn.
13. **Council** — The settlers debate whether White should return to England for supplies. Ellis argues against it. White decides to go.
14. **The Last Boat** — White sails on August 27, 1587. Eleanor stands on the shore until the sail vanishes. He promised to return by spring.
15. **First Frost** — Winter comes early. The corn harvest is thin. Rations are cut.
16. **The Hunt** — Manteo takes a hunting party into the woods. George Howe kills a deer but wounds a Croatan hunting dog.
17. **Bad Blood** — Wanchese leaves the settlement in anger. Howe's careless shot becomes a wedge between the peoples.
18. **The Journal** — Eleanor writes by firelight. Her entries grow shorter as the food runs out.
19. **The Signal** — A ship is sighted on the horizon. The settlers light a beacon fire. The ship passes in the night.
20. **Spring** — April arrives with no news from England. Eleanor finds Manteo waiting at the garden gate with a basket of clams.

### Part III: The Breaking — Chapters 21–30

21. **The Killing** — George Howe is found dead on the beach, his body punctured by arrows. The colony descends into fear and blame.
22. **Retaliation** — Ellis leads a dawn raid on a Secotan village. They burn three houses. Eleanor refuses to go.
23. **The Reckoning** — Manteo confronts Ellis. He tells him the Secotan were not responsible for Howe's death — that it was a war party from the mainland.
24. **Burial** — The colony buries Howe in unmarked ground. Margaret speaks a prayer. Eleanor cannot find words.
25. **Desertion** — Two men steal a skiff and try to reach the mainland. Their bodies wash up three days later.
26. **The Teacher** — Manteo begins to teach Eleanor the Croatan language in earnest. She learns the names of trees, fish, and the directions of wind.
27. **Drought** — The well runs low. The creek turns brackish. Arguments break out over water.
28. **The Split** — Ellis declares martial law. A group of settlers, led by Ananias Dare, argues for moving inland to live among the friendly tribes.
29. **The Vote** — The colony votes. Half will stay. Half will go. Eleanor's name is called and she must choose.
30. **Departure** — Ananias leads twenty-three settlers into the forest. Eleanor stays, but she watches them go with a feeling she cannot name.

### Part IV: The Dispersal — Chapters 31–40

31. **The Empty Fort** — The remaining settlers spread thin. Every face is gaunt. Every voice is sharp.
32. **The Gift** — Manteo brings Eleanor to his village. She sees Croatan life: the longhouses, the women weaving, the children who stare at her pale skin.
33. **The Dance** — A harvest ceremony. Eleanor is invited. She stands at the edge of the firelight, unsure whether she is a guest or a prisoner.
34. **The Fever Returns** — Margaret falls ill. Eleanor takes over the herb garden and the sickbed. She makes mistakes. People die.
35. **The Letter** — Eleanor writes to Governor White in her journal, knowing he will never read it. She tells him they are ghosts now.
36. **The Offering** — Manteo gives Eleanor a deerskin cloak. She wears it. The other settlers look at her differently.
37. **The Englishman's Grave** — Thomas Ellis dies of fever. Eleanor buries him herself. There is no one left to give orders.
38. **The Last Winter** — Snow buries the fort. Only fifteen settlers remain. They burn the palisade for warmth.
39. **The Decision** — The survivors gather. They will leave Roanoke. They will go to the Croatan, or to the mainland, or they will die trying.
40. **CROATOAN** — Eleanor carves the word into the palisade post. It is the only message she can leave.

### Part V: The After — Chapters 41–50

41. **The March** — Fifteen settlers walk into the forest. Eleanor carries Margaret on a litter. The fort burns behind them.
42. **The Village** — The Croatan take them in. Eleanor sleeps in a longhouse for the first time. She dreams in Algonquian.
43. **The Healer** — Eleanor apprentices herself to a Croatan medicine woman. She learns the root of the fever and the leaf that stops bleeding.
44. **The Stranger** — An English soldier, shipwrecked and half dead, is brought to the village. He says war with Spain keeps all English ships in port.
45. **The Message** — Eleanor writes a letter and gives it to the soldier. If he ever reaches England, he must find Governor White.
46. **The Second Winter** — The village survives. Eleanor does not think of England anymore. England is a story she once heard.
47. **The English Return** — Three years after he left, John White returns to Roanoke. He finds the word CROATOAN carved on the post and nothing else.
48. **The Search** — White tries to reach Croatoan but storms drive his ship back. He fires a cannon into the empty forest. No answer comes.
49. **The Report** — White writes his report. The colony is lost. He never sees his daughter or granddaughter again.
50. **The Name** — Eleanor hears the cannon shot from miles inland. She does not go to the shore. She has a new name now, and a new people, and the old world is as distant as the moon.
