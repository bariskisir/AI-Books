# The Lost Colony — Final Report

## Summary

The Lost Colony is a historical fiction novel of approximately 12,000 words across 50 chapters. It tells the untold story of the Roanoke Colony through the eyes of Eleanor Sutton, a young English woman who escapes an arranged marriage by joining the 1587 expedition to the New World.

## Story Arc

- **Part I (Ch 1-10):** The crossing from England, arrival at Roanoke, meeting Manteo
- **Part II (Ch 11-20):** Governor White's departure, the first winter, the colony begins to struggle
- **Part III (Ch 21-30):** George Howe's death, retaliation against the Secotan, the colony splits
- **Part IV (Ch 31-40):** Life in the Croatan village, Margaret's death, the carving of CROATOAN
- **Part V (Ch 41-50):** The march inland, building a new village, White's return, Eleanor's transformation

## Characters

- Eleanor Sutton (protagonist)
- Manteo (Croatan guide and translator)
- Governor John White
- Margaret Lawrence (midwife and herbalist)
- Thomas Ellis (carpenter, de facto leader)
- Kitchi (Croatan medicine woman)
- Ananias Dare, George Howe, Wanchese (supporting)
