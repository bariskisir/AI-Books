# The God Game — Outline

## Premise

Beneath the Vatican, in a sealed chamber untouched since the reign of Constantine, a librarian discovers a gospel written in the hand of the apostle Judas Iscariot — a text that claims Jesus of Nazareth did not die on the cross but escaped to Rome, married Mary Magdalene, and fathered a bloodline that rules the Church to this day. Father Matteo, a Jesuit scholar haunted by his own crisis of faith, is summoned to authenticate the scroll. But a shadowy cabal within the Curia, the Custodians of the Sacred Veil, has guarded this secret for centuries. They will burn the gospel, bury the truth, and kill anyone who reads it. With only an aging archaeologist as an ally, Matteo must race through the catacombs, libraries, and clandestine archives of Rome to decode the gospel before the Church destroys it — and before the gospel destroys everything he believes.

## Core Cast

- **Father Matteo Lombardi** — Jesuit priest, paleographer, former professor of early Christian history at the Pontifical Gregorian University. Sharp, weary, a man whose faith is a muscle he exercises out of habit rather than conviction.
- **Professor Evelyn Hale** — Archaeologist, epigrapher, expert in first-century Semitic languages. British, pragmatic, secular. She trusts carbon dating, not divine providence.
- **The Custodian** — Leader of the Custodians of the Sacred Veil, a secret society within the Vatican hierarchy answerable only to the Camerlengo. He wears the face of a mild-mannered monsignor.

## 50-Chapter Plan

1. A junior librarian finds a sealed amphora in a forgotten vault beneath the Vatican library.
2. Father Matteo is summoned from his classroom to the Prefect's office under false pretenses.
3. Matteo examines the amphora and recognizes the seal of the First Council of Nicaea.
4. Professor Hale arrives at the Vatican, skeptical of the discovery but bound by professional curiosity.
5. The scroll is unrolled in a sterile lab; the script is Aramaic, the ink is first-century.
6. Matteo translates the first lines and feels his pulse quicken — it claims to be written by Judas.
7. The Custodian watches from a security monitor, his face unreadable.
8. Hale confirms the carbon dating: 60-80 AD. The scroll is authentic.
9. Matteo confronts the Prefect, who denies knowing what the scroll contains.
10. The Custodian visits a cardinal in a private chapel; they speak in Latin about "the wound."
11. Matteo works through the night, translating the gospel's account of the crucifixion.
12. The gospel claims the Roman soldiers were bribed to let Jesus live.
13. Hale finds a reference to a journey — Jesus traveled to Rome with Mary Magdalene.
14. The Custodian orders a fire in the archive storage wing; alarms sound.
15. Matteo and Hale evacuate with the scroll as smoke fills the corridors.
16. They realize the fire was deliberate — someone wants the scroll destroyed.
17. Matteo takes the scroll to his private quarters; Hale insists on staying with it.
18. The Custodian learns the scroll survived; he summons his agent, a Swiss Guard captain.
19. Matteo reads a passage describing Jesus marrying Mary in a Roman garden.
20. Hale maps the geographical references in the text to ancient Roman catacombs.
21. The Swiss Guard captain searches Matteo's office, finds nothing.
22. Matteo and Hale slip out of the Vatican through a service entrance.
23. They visit the Catacombs of Priscilla, following clues in the gospel.
24. In a hidden chamber, they find a second scroll — a genealogy.
25. The Custodian realizes they have left Vatican grounds; he orders pursuit.
26. Matteo reads the genealogy: names of popes who are descendants of Jesus and Mary.
27. Hale photographs the second scroll as footsteps echo in the tunnel.
28. They escape through a narrow passage as the Swiss Guard arrives.
29. The Custodian reviews the security footage; he recognizes Hale's face.
30. Matteo and Hale take refuge in a safe house owned by a former colleague.
31. Matteo grapples with the implications: the papacy is built on a lie — or is it?
32. Hale analyzes the genealogy; it shows an unbroken line to the current pope.
33. The Custodian arrives at the safe house with armed men.
34. Matteo negotiates, offering a deal: a chance to read the full gospel before it is destroyed.
35. The Custodian agrees — he grants them 24 hours in exchange for the scrolls.
36. Matteo returns to the Vatican library and works through the night.
37. He finds a passage where Jesus says the Church must be "a veil over the truth."
38. Hale realizes the genealogy proves nothing — the names are symbolic, not literal.
39. Matteo experiences a crisis: the gospel may be a hoax, or its truth may be too dangerous.
40. The Custodian waits in the Prefect's office, his patience thinning.
41. Matteo discovers a final passage: Jesus commands his followers to protect the secret.
42. Hale finds a watermark on the papyrus that suggests a medieval forgery.
43. Matteo confronts the Custodian with evidence the scroll is a 12th-century fabrication.
44. The Custodian smiles: of course it is a forgery — but the Church has guarded the lie for eight hundred years.
45. The gospel is a test: those who find it are judged by what they do with it.
46. Matteo realizes the entire discovery was staged by the Custodians to test his loyalty.
47. Hale is furious; she has been used as an unwitting pawn in a theological game.
48. The Custodian offers Matteo a position among the Custodians — to become one of them.
49. Matteo refuses, choosing his conscience over the institution.
50. Matteo leaves the priesthood, and the gospel — a piece of historical ambiguity — is sealed again.
