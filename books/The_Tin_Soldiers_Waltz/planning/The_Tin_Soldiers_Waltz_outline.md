# The Tin Soldier's Waltz — Outline

## Premise

In the snowbound clockwork city of Vellmark, an aging toymaker named Anselm Roe has spent his life building wind-up wonders. His finest creation is a one-legged tin soldier who, unknown to anyone, has been quietly alive for years — watching, listening, loving the old man in silence. When a thief takes the music box that holds Anselm's fading memories, and the toymaker begins to forget his own life, the tin soldier steps down from his shelf and sets out across the workshop, the rooftops, and the frozen city to bring the music box home before the last tune runs out.

## Core Cast

- **Tib** — A one-legged tin soldier, painted blue and red, secretly alive. Brave, plainspoken, easily underestimated. Cannot dance; longs to.
- **Anselm Roe** — An aging toymaker whose memories live inside a wind-up music box. Kind, forgetful, going quietly dim.
- **Coppelia** — A clockwork ballerina, Anselm's other masterwork, vain and frightened, who learns courage.
- **Mr. Pim** — A wind-up monkey with cymbals, loyal and loud, Tib's companion.
- **The Ragged Man** — A down-and-out clockwork mender who stole the music box, not from greed but desperation.
- **Madame Sorrel** — A clockmaker who buys and sells stolen memory-works; cold, elegant, dangerous.
- **The Cat** — A real, living alley cat named Soot; the only real animal, a peril and an ally.
- **Old Borin** — A retired lamplighter automaton who guards the city's great clock.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Workshop (1–12)
1. Tib stands on his shelf at night and watches Anselm wind the music box that holds his memories.
2. Anselm forgets the name of a tool; Tib understands the forgetting has begun.
3. The music box plays a waltz; Anselm weeps without knowing why; Tib aches that he cannot help.
4. A storm; the workshop window blows open; a ragged stranger slips inside.
5. The stranger takes the music box and flees into the snow.
6. Morning: Anselm cannot remember his own mother; the box is gone.
7. Tib steps down from the shelf for the first time, falling, learning to balance on one leg.
8. Tib wakes Mr. Pim, the cymbal monkey, who can also move at night.
9. They find Coppelia frozen mid-pose; Tib begs her help; she refuses, afraid.
10. Tib finds the open window and the stranger's bootprints in the snow.
11. Coppelia, ashamed, relents and tells Tib where menders gather: the Tin Market.
12. Tib and Pim climb down the drainpipe into the snowbound street.

### Act II — The City (13–28)
13. The street is vast and cold; a sledge nearly crushes them; they shelter in a boot.
14. Soot the cat finds them; a terrifying chase across the cobbles.
15. They escape into a sewer grate; Pim's cymbals jam with ice.
16. Underground, they meet Old Borin, a retired lamplighter automaton.
17. Borin tells them of Madame Sorrel, who buys stolen memory-works.
18. Borin guides them up through the clockwork guts of the city.
19. They emerge in the Tin Market; vendors of springs, gears, and stolen toys.
20. Tib spies the Ragged Man trading the music box for a meal.
21. Tib confronts him; the Ragged Man flees; the box is already sold to Sorrel's runner.
22. The runner, a brass crow, carries the box over the rooftops.
23. Tib and Pim give chase across icy gutters and chimney pots.
24. Pim falls; Tib catches him; they lose the crow but learn Sorrel's address.
25. They rest in a bell tower; Tib confesses he has always longed to dance.
26. Soot the cat returns — but this time wounded and afraid, fleeing a bigger threat.
27. An unlikely truce: Soot carries them faster than they can walk.
28. They reach the frostbound mansion of Madame Sorrel.

### Act III — The Mansion (29–40)
29. Inside, shelves of stolen memory-works tick and murmur in the dark.
30. Tib hears Anselm's waltz playing somewhere deep in the house.
31. They slip past a clockwork hound that hunts by sound.
32. Coppelia arrives, having followed them, brave at last.
33. Sorrel catches Coppelia and adds her to her collection of dancers.
34. Tib bargains: his own living spark for the music box.
35. Sorrel, intrigued by a toy that lives, agrees — but means to keep them all.
36. The Ragged Man reappears; he has followed too, sick with guilt.
37. He reveals Sorrel took his own memories years ago; he wants them back.
38. Together they free Coppelia and the captured dancers.
39. The clockwork hound corners them; Pim's cymbals shatter its ear-drum mechanism.
40. They seize the music box; Sorrel winds her great mainspring to stop them.

### Act IV — The Long Way Home (41–50)
41. Escape across the frozen rooftops with Sorrel's brass crows in pursuit.
42. Soot fights off the crows; the cat is hurt but holds the line.
43. The Ragged Man stays behind to jam Sorrel's clock and buy them time.
44. Dawn breaks gray over Vellmark; the city's great clock begins to chime.
45. Tib, Pim, and Coppelia race the failing music box home through the snow.
46. The box's spring is nearly run down; the last tune is faltering.
47. They reach the workshop; Anselm sits dim and silent, remembering nothing.
48. Tib winds the box; the waltz plays; Anselm's memories return in a flood.
49. Anselm sees Tib move, and understands his soldier has always been alive.
50. Anselm mends Tib a second leg; at last, the tin soldier waltzes with Coppelia.
