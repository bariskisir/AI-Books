# The Mourning Doves — Outline

## Premise

After the sudden death of her husband Elias, young widow Eleanor Morwen travels to his crumbling Cornish estate, Penhallow Grange, to settle his affairs. There she encounters his brooding groundskeeper Mr. Graves, who harbors secrets about Elias's final days, and the estate's formidable matriarch Lady Morwen, who warns Eleanor to leave. As strange occurrences escalate — mourning doves appearing at windows, ghostly footsteps in empty halls, Elias's study locked from within — Eleanor begins to suspect that Elias's death was not natural and that his ghost remains tethered to the house, guarding a truth that could destroy the Morwen name.

## Core Cast

- **Eleanor Morwen** — A young widow, determined and perceptive, unwilling to be dismissed.
- **Mr. Graves** — The groundskeeper, haunted by his own past and complicit in Elias's secrets.
- **Lady Morwen** — Elias's formidable mother, keeper of the family's darkest truths.
- **Elias Morwen** — The deceased husband, seen in flashbacks and as a restless spirit.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. The Letter — Eleanor receives word of Elias's death while living in London.
2. The Journey West — She travels by train through the misty Cornish countryside.
3. Penhallow Grange — First sight of the crumbling estate on the cliffs.
4. Lady Morwen — Meeting her cold, watchful mother-in-law.
5. The Groundskeeper — Mr. Graves appears from the fog.
6. Elias's Study — A locked room she is forbidden to enter.
7. The Key — Eleanor finds a brass key among Elias's belongings.
8. First Night — Restless sleep; strange sounds in the walls.
9. The Mourning Doves — A gray dove perches at her window.
10. Opening the Study — Eleanor enters and finds scattered papers.
11. Mr. Graves's Warning — He tells her to leave Cornwall at once.
12. Lady Morwen's Tea — A tense conversation that reveals nothing.
13. The Portrait — Elias's painted eyes seem to follow her.
14. Footsteps — Eleanor hears pacing in the empty upstairs hall.
15. The Garden — Mr. Graves tends the overgrown rose garden.
16. Elias's Journal — A cryptic entry about "the cost of truth."
17. The Storm — A Cornish gale batters the house.
18. The Ghost — Eleanor sees Elias's silhouette in the library.
19. Lady Morwen's Secret — She admits Elias was frightened before he died.
20. The Cove — Mr. Graves shows Eleanor a hidden beach below the cliffs.
21. Shipwreck — The story of a wreck on the rocks years ago.
22. The Cargo — Something was salvaged from that wreck, then hidden.
23. Elias's Desperation — Flashback: Elias at the cove under moonlight.
24. The Cave — Eleanor discovers a concealed cave behind the waterfall.
25. Letters — A cache of correspondence with a London stranger.
26. Mr. Graves Confesses — He helped Elias conceal what they found.
27. The Stranger — A man from London arrives at the Grange.
28. Blackmail — The stranger demands papers from Elias's study.
29. Eleanor Refuses — She sends the stranger away.
30. The Broken Window — A rock thrown through the library glass.
31. Lady Morwen's Plea — She begs Eleanor to surrender the documents.
32. The Truth — The cargo was smuggled antiquities, one item cursed.
33. Elias's Crime — He sold a forged artifact; the buyer died.
34. The Curse — Lady Morwen believes the family is doomed.
35. Mr. Graves's Past — He was Elias's accomplice, wracked with guilt.
36. The Ghost Speaks — Eleanor dreams of Elias, his urgent warning.
37. The Artifact — A small carved dove, the source of the trouble.
38. The Return — The stranger comes back with a solicitor.
39. Legal Threat — They claim Elias defrauded a peer of the realm.
40. Eleanor's Choice — Surrender the artifact or face financial ruin.
41. The Bonfire — Eleanor burns the incriminating papers.
42. Mr. Graves Resists — He refuses to let her destroy the dove.
43. The Confrontation — Eleanor faces Lady Morwen in the great hall.
44. The Ghost Appears — Elias's spirit manifests before them all.
45. The Revelation — Elias died protecting Eleanor from the truth.
46. The Dove Destroyed — Eleanor smashes the carved artifact.
47. The Stranger Defeated — The legal case collapses without evidence.
48. Morning Light — The doves depart the estate grounds.
49. Mr. Graves's Farewell — He leaves Penhallow for a new life.
50. Eleanor's Decision — She chooses to stay and rebuild the Grange.
