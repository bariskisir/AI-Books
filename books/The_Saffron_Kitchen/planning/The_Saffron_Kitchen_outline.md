# The Saffron Kitchen — Outline

## Premise

At Saltmarsh, a celebrated restaurant on a windy stretch of the Cornish coast, head chef Daniel Reyes is found dead in the cold room the night before the most important review of his career. The body is discovered by Nora Ellery, his sous-chef — a sharp, observant woman who knows every recipe in the building and every grudge in the brigade. When the police find Nora's prints on the wine and her name in Daniel's last text, suspicion falls on her. To clear herself she must work the case the way she works a service: methodically, tasting for what is wrong. The answer is hidden in a missing batch of saffron and a signature recipe that has been quietly altered. Among the staff, the investors, and a rival restaurateur down the coast, someone changed the dish — and someone changed the man.

## Core Cast

- **Nora Ellery** — Sous-chef at Saltmarsh; precise, watchful, loyal to the kitchen if not always to the man who ran it. Our investigator.
- **Daniel Reyes** — Head chef and part-owner; brilliant, exacting, recently secretive. The victim.
- **Imogen Vale** — Front-of-house manager and Daniel's business partner; runs the books and the room with equal cool.
- **Theo Brandt** — Chef de partie on fish; ambitious, hot-tempered, passed over for promotion.
- **Pavel Novak** — Pastry chef; quiet, observant, keeps a notebook of everything.
- **Sadie Quinn** — Commis chef and Nora's protégée; young, eager, frightened.
- **Marcus Friel** — Lead investor; wants Saltmarsh sold, or saved on his terms.
- **Lena Coyle** — Rival restaurateur from Porthcove down the coast; once Daniel's lover and partner.
- **DS Geraldine Okafor** — Detective sergeant; tired, fair, willing to listen if Nora gives her reason.
- **Hassan Reyes** — Daniel's estranged brother and supplier of the saffron.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Act One — The Cold Room (1–12)
1. Nora preps the saffron broth before service and notices the spice tin is lighter than it should be.
2. Daniel arrives tense, announces the critic comes tomorrow, and locks himself in the office.
3. Service runs; Theo and Daniel clash over a sent-back plate of monkfish.
4. After close, Nora finds Daniel dead in the cold room, a spilled cup beside him.
5. Police arrive; DS Okafor takes the kitchen as a scene and the staff as suspects.
6. Nora learns her prints are on the wine bottle and her name was in Daniel's last text.
7. Okafor questions Nora; Nora insists the death is wrong, like a dish out of balance.
8. The brigade is sent home; Nora lingers and sees the saffron tin has been refilled with the wrong threads.
9. Imogen tells Nora the restaurant may not survive the scandal, or the review.
10. Nora visits Daniel's flat with Sadie and finds his recipe ledger missing a page.
11. Theo is taken in for questioning after the monkfish row surfaces; he begs Nora to vouch for him.
12. Nora tastes the leftover broth from the bin and knows: someone changed the recipe.

### Act Two — The Altered Recipe (13–25)
13. Nora reconstructs the broth and finds it laced with a bitter, cheaper substitute saffron.
14. Pavel shows Nora his notebook; he logged a late delivery the night before the death.
15. The delivery was signed for by someone who wasn't on shift.
16. Nora traces the saffron supplier to Hassan, Daniel's estranged brother.
17. Hassan reveals Daniel owed him money and had switched to a cheaper source in secret.
18. Marcus Friel pressures Imogen to sell; Nora overhears the figure and the buyer.
19. The buyer is Lena Coyle, the rival from Porthcove.
20. Nora drives to Porthcove; Lena is gracious, sharp, and unsurprised by Daniel's death.
21. Lena admits she and Daniel were once partners — and that he stole the saffron broth recipe from her.
22. Nora returns to find Sadie has been suspended; Imogen blames the commis for the delivery.
23. Nora defends Sadie and realizes Sadie was covering for someone she admired.
24. Okafor reveals the cause of death: a cardiac drug, not in Daniel's prescriptions.
25. The drug could have been carried in something bitter enough to hide it — like adulterated broth.

### Act Three — The Brigade (26–37)
26. Nora maps who had access to the cold room, the office, and the spice store.
27. Theo confesses he doctored a plate once out of spite, but swears not the broth.
28. Pavel's notebook shows Imogen entered the office late, after Daniel locked it.
29. Nora confronts Imogen, who admits she and Daniel fought over the sale.
30. Imogen has an alibi: she was on the phone with the bank, logged and timed.
31. Nora finds the missing ledger page hidden in Pavel's station, in his careful hand.
32. Pavel copied the page to protect the original recipe, not to steal it.
33. Hassan returns with a receipt: the cheap saffron was ordered under Marcus Friel's account.
34. Friel wanted the restaurant to fail a health or quality check to force the sale.
35. But poison is not the same as cutting corners; Nora separates the two crimes.
36. Sadie remembers seeing a guest slip into the back during the last service.
37. The guest matches a description of Lena Coyle's business manager.

### Act Four — The Missing Saffron (38–50)
38. Nora and Okafor reconstruct the night hour by hour using Pavel's log.
39. The real saffron — the good batch — was removed, not used; someone took it away.
40. Whoever took it wanted the bitter substitute in the pot when the critic came.
41. Nora realizes the killer needed Daniel to taste the broth himself before service.
42. Daniel always tasted the saffron broth alone, first, every night — a known ritual.
43. The drug was in the tasting spoon's cup, not the service pot.
44. Only someone who knew the ritual and had a grudge both old and fresh could set it.
45. Lena's manager delivered the substitute, but the drugged cup came from inside.
46. Nora confronts Imogen again; the alibi covers the phone call, not the cold room after.
47. Imogen breaks: Daniel was selling without her, cutting her out of her own recipe history.
48. But Imogen swears she only meant to ruin the review, not kill — and Nora believes the dose was meant to sicken, not stop a heart.
49. The truth lands: Daniel's heart was already failing; the dose he hid, the secret he kept, finished what the bitterness began — guilt shared, guilt divided.
50. Saltmarsh reopens for one quiet service; Nora plates the true saffron broth and lets the kitchen breathe.
