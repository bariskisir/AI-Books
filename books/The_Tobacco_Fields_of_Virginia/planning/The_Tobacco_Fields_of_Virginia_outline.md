# The Tobacco Fields of Virginia — Outline

## Premise

Dr. Simone Hayes, a thirty-eight-year-old professor of African American Studies at Howard University, returns to her rural Virginia hometown after her grandmother's death. The funeral stirs buried memories, but it is the appearance of her grandmother's ghost that forces Simone to confront what the family has spent generations ignoring: the unmarked grave of an enslaved ancestor named Ezra, buried somewhere in the tobacco fields that still surround the family home. With the help of her cousin Ray — who never left — Simone must uncover the truth about Ezra, reckon with the land's bloody history, and find a way to lay the dead to rest.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Simone Hayes** — 38, professor, Howard University. Distant from her roots, intellectual, guarded. Returns to Virginia feeling like a stranger in her own history.
- **Grandma's Ghost (Leona Hayes)** — Appears as she was in life: sharp, tired, knowing. She speaks in riddles and fragments. She cannot rest until Ezra is found.
- **Cousin Ray** — 45, Simone's cousin who stayed in Virginia. Runs a small auto repair shop. Laconic, deeply rooted, watches Simone struggle with what he already knows.

## Act I: The Funeral and the Haunting (Chapters 1-15)

1. **The Call** — Simone receives word of her grandmother's death while grading papers. The news hits harder than she expects. She books a flight to Richmond.
2. **The Drive South** — Rental car, familiar exits, the landscape flattening into farmland. Memories surface: summers on the porch, Grandma's warnings about the fields.
3. **The Hayes House** — Arrival at the weathered farmhouse. Cousin Ray is already there, sitting on the porch. The house smells of mothballs and old wood.
4. **The Viewing** — The funeral home. Grandma Leona in her casket, wearing the blue dress. Relatives Simone barely remembers offer condolences.
5. **The First Sight** — That night, Simone sees a figure at the edge of the tobacco field. She tells herself it was a trick of the light.
6. **The Funeral** — Burial at the small church cemetery. The preacher speaks. Simone's eulogy catches in her throat. She sees the figure again among the tombstones.
7. **The Wake** — Fried chicken, macaroni and cheese, too many pies. Cousin Ray drinks beer on the porch. Aunt Carol cries in the kitchen.
8. **Grandma's Room** — Simone sorts through her grandmother's belongings. She finds a worn Bible with a newspaper clipping tucked inside about a construction crew that unearthed human bones in 1987.
9. **The Second Sighting** — The figure approaches the house. Simone sees it clearly now: an old Black woman in a housedress. Grandma's ghost.
10. **Ghost's Words** — The ghost speaks only three words: "Find the bones." Then she vanishes. Simone sleeps poorly.
11. **The Question** — Simone asks Ray about unmarked graves on the property. He looks at her for a long time. "You saw her too, didn't you?"
12. **Family Silence** — Simone calls her mother in Atlanta. Her mother deflects. "Some things are best left in the ground." Simone presses. Her mother hangs up.
13. **Church Records** — Simone visits the church archives. The old pastor is reluctant. "That history is painful." She insists. He gives her a crumbling ledger from 1849.
14. **Ezra's Name** — In the ledger, a single entry: "Ezra, field hand, d. August 1856, no marker." The cause of death is blank. Simone copies the page.
15. **The Survey** — Simone walks the property edge with Ray, trying to match the 1856 boundaries. The tobacco has been planted and harvested for 170 years. The dead are everywhere.

## Act II: Excavation (Chapters 16-35)

16. **The Archive** — Simone drives to the county historical society. She finds land deeds, probate records, the names of the white family who owned her ancestors.
17. **Ezra's Story (I)** — A letter in the historical society, written by the plantation mistress in 1856. Ezra was sold away from his wife and child. He ran. They caught him.
18. **The Lashing Tree** — Ray shows Simone an old oak at the far end of the field. Scars on the branches where ropes were thrown. Simone touches the bark.
19. **Ezra's Story (II)** — The whip marks were not the end. Ezra was buried alive as punishment. Simone reads this in a plantation diary. She vomits behind the courthouse.
20. **Ghost at the Oak** — Grandma's ghost appears under the lashing tree. She points at the ground. Simone digs with her hands until Ray stops her.
21. **Ground Penetrating Radar** — Simone researches non-invasive archaeology. She calls a colleague at UVA. The equipment is expensive. Ray offers his savings.
22. **The Radar Team** — Two graduate students arrive with the GPR unit. They scan the field in careful grid lines. Ray watches from the porch, silent.
23. **Anomaly** — The radar shows something six feet down, near the oak tree. A disturbance in the soil, man-sized. Simone feels the ghost watching.
24. **Permission** — Simone needs the county to allow excavation. The current landowner (the farm is rented to a tobacco company) resists. This is a legal fight.
25. **Cousin Ray's Story** — Ray tells Simone about the summer he tried to dig up the field himself, age sixteen. His father beat him. "Some things stay buried," his father said.
26. **The Lawyer** — Simone finds a pro bono attorney who handles cultural heritage cases. She files a petition for excavation.
27. **Newspaper Interest** — A reporter from the Richmond Times-Dispatch picks up the story. Simone is quoted. The ghost is silent for three days.
28. **Ezra's Story (III)** — Simone pieces together Ezra's lineage. He had a daughter who survived. That daughter was Simone's great-great-grandmother. Ezra is her blood.
29. **The Hearing** — County court. The tobacco company lawyer argues that the bones could be anyone, that excavation disrupts business. Simone testifies. She shakes but does not break.
30. **Victory** — The judge grants limited excavation. Conditions: one week, archaeologists on site, remains to be handled with dignity. Ray cries on the courthouse steps.
31. **The Dig Begins** — A real archaeologist arrives. The graduate students return. The grid is laid. Simone takes the first shovel of dirt herself.
32. **Unearthed** — On the third day, a trowel hits bone. The crew switches to brushes. A human skeleton emerges, curled on its side. The ghost appears at Simone's elbow.
33. **Identification** — The remains are consistent with a Black male, mid-twenties, circa mid-1800s. Trauma to the spine consistent with a beating. Simone identifies the remains as Ezra.
34. **Ghost's Gratitude** — Grandma Leona appears in Simone's bedroom that night. She smiles. "Thank you." Then she is gone. Simone feels the empty room settle.
35. **Reburial Planning** — Simone and Ray plan a proper burial. The church offers a plot. Simone wants to bury Ezra in the family cemetery, next to Grandma.

## Act III: Rest and Return (Chapters 36-50)

36. **The Coffin** — Ray builds the coffin himself. Pine, plain, with Ezra's name burned into the lid. He works in his garage, late into the night.
37. **The Service** — A small crowd gathers. The new pastor speaks Ezra's name. Simone delivers a eulogy about the ancestors who survive in memory.
38. **The Hands** — Simone takes a handful of dirt from the tobacco field and places it in the grave. The soil that held Ezra for 168 years returns with him.
39. **Reading the Names** — Simone reads the names of every enslaved person she found in the records. Her voice carries across the cemetery. A wind rises.
40. **The Burial** — Ezra's coffin is lowered. Ray throws the first shovel of dirt. Simone follows. The grave is filled.
41. **The Ghost Departs** — That night, Grandma Leona appears one last time, whole and young. She kisses Simone's forehead. Simone wakes to an empty room and the smell of lavender.
42. **The House** — Simone decides to keep the house. She and Ray begin repairs. The roof, the porch, the kitchen. Work that connects them to the land.
43. **The Historical Marker** — Simone successfully petitions for a historical marker at the edge of the property. It names Ezra and the enslaved people who worked these fields.
44. **The Marker Unveiling** — A ceremony. The county supervisor speaks. Simone gives a short speech. Ray stands at her side. The tobacco company sends a representative.
45. **Ezra's Descendants** — Through Ancestry.com, Simone finds distant cousins she never knew existed. They come to visit. The house fills with family again.
46. **The Professor Returns** — Simone goes back to Howard for spring semester. She teaches a new course: "Archaeology and the Ancestors." She is changed.
47. **Phone Calls** — Weekly calls with Ray. The roof is done. The garden is planted. He sends her photos of the tobacco field, green and growing.
48. **The First Anniversary** — Simone returns a year later. She visits Grandma's grave and Ezra's grave side by side. She talks to them both.
49. **The Field at Sunset** — Simone walks to the lashing tree at sunset. She is not afraid. The ghosts are quiet. She hears only the wind through the tobacco leaves.
50. **The Seedlings** — In the final scene, Simone plants a small garden behind the house — okra, tomatoes, collard greens. Seeds her grandmother saved. The earth accepts them.

## Themes

Memory and forgetting, the persistence of the past, the violence of plantation agriculture, the redemptive power of truth-telling, the meaning of home for those who left.

## Tone and Style

Southern Gothic: atmospheric, grounded in place, the supernatural feels natural. Short sentences. Sensory detail (tobacco smell, red dirt, cicada sound). Restrained prose. Dialogue in dialect for some characters but not all. The ghost is not a horror figure but a presence.
