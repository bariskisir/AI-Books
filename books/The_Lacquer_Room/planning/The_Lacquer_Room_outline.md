# The Lacquer Room - Outline

## Premise

Art conservator Selin Arda is preparing a monumental lacquer screen for a museum gala when microscopic cleaning exposes an older inscription beneath the donor-facing label. The screen's layers point not to a private workshop acquisition, but to a temple inventory mark removed during wartime looting and later concealed by restoration lacquer. As the museum director, a powerful donor, and an insurer push for the opening, Selin follows pigment, dust, repair seams, shipping crates, and archive ledgers to prove the screen's rightful history before the museum turns a stolen object into civic pride.

## Core Cast

- **Selin Arda** - Adult art conservator specializing in lacquer, meticulous and wary of donor pageantry.
- **Mina Park** - Adult assistant curator, ambitious but increasingly troubled by the screen's gaps.
- **Dr. Kenji Sato** - Adult materials scientist who reads resin, pigment, and tool marks.
- **Elias Venn** - Adult collector and donor whose family foundation gifted the screen.
- **Tomas Reeve** - Adult museum director protecting the gala, expansion campaign, and board.
- **Farah Nouri** - Adult provenance attorney advising the museum on cultural property risk.
- **Lian Cho** - Adult historian whose temple archive contains matching inventory rubbings.
- **Greta Solm** - Adult insurance appraiser with access to old valuation photographs.
- **Ivo Marek** - Adult art handler who remembers the screen arriving in a false crate.
- **Judge Helena Voss** - Adult civil judge hearing the emergency ownership injunction.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Under Black Shine
1. **Cleaning Window** - Selin cleans a small test area and sees an older inscription under lacquer.
2. **Gala Label** - Mina shows the official donor label that omits wartime provenance.
3. **Resin Sample** - Kenji identifies two lacquer campaigns separated by decades.
4. **The Donor Walkthrough** - Elias Venn demands no delay before the foundation gala.
5. **Director's Calendar** - Tomas ties the screen opening to the museum expansion campaign.
6. **Hidden Syllable** - Selin photographs a character that matches temple inventory marks.
7. **Provenance Gap** - Farah warns that a 1938-1954 ownership gap is legally dangerous.
8. **Old Crate** - Ivo recalls a crate marked as furniture rather than art.
9. **Insurance Photo** - Greta finds a 1962 valuation image with a visible removed seal.
10. **First Hold** - Selin requests a conservation hold and is told to keep working.

### Part II: Removed Names
11. **Layer Map** - Kenji maps soot, shell powder, and modern fill across the panels.
12. **Temple Rubbing** - Lian sends a rubbing from a lost temple screen inventory.
13. **Board Preview** - Board members praise the screen while avoiding provenance questions.
14. **Tool Marks** - Selin finds scrape marks where an ownership seal was removed.
15. **False Workshop** - Mina traces the claimed workshop to a dealer catalog error.
16. **Foundation File** - Elias's file contains a private note about "sensitive origin."
17. **Packed as Furniture** - Ivo describes the false crate and customs category.
18. **Dust in the Hinge** - Kenji identifies temple ash trapped under later lacquer.
19. **Farah's Memo** - Farah writes a memo recommending postponement.
20. **The Memo Vanishes** - Tomas circulates a gala-ready provenance statement without it.

### Part III: Provenance Room
21. **After-Hours Light** - Selin uses raking light to reveal the full hidden inventory line.
22. **Donor Pressure** - Elias threatens to withdraw the expansion gift.
23. **Mina's Copy** - Mina gives Selin a saved copy of Farah's memo.
24. **Auction Ledger** - Greta finds a private auction ledger with a changed object number.
25. **Archive Match** - Lian matches the hidden line to a temple in the northern hills.
26. **Board Split** - The board divides between gala money and legal risk.
27. **Restoration Recipe** - Kenji proves the concealment lacquer used postwar commercial resin.
28. **Public Teaser** - The museum releases a teaser video calling the screen "found in peace."
29. **Historian Arrives** - Lian arrives with temple photographs and survivor testimony.
30. **Object Lesson** - Selin refuses to finish cosmetic treatment.

### Part IV: Injunction
31. **Suspension Letter** - Tomas suspends Selin from the project.
32. **Press Leak** - Provenance questions reach a cultural desk reporter.
33. **Foundation Counsel** - Elias claims the screen was bought in good faith.
34. **Farah Files** - Farah files for emergency court review.
35. **The Lacquer Room** - The judge orders the screen examined in the conservation room.
36. **Panel Three** - Raking light reveals the removed temple seal across panel three.
37. **Valuation Lie** - Greta testifies that insurance photos were cropped in later files.
38. **Customs Category** - Ivo confirms the screen entered as household furniture.
39. **Sensitive Origin** - Elias admits his father knew of unresolved origin questions.
40. **Chain of Custody** - Selin lays out the physical and paper chain.

### Part V: Returned Shine
41. **Cross-Examination** - Donor counsel attacks conservation interpretation and bias.
42. **Material Truth** - Kenji demonstrates the resin timeline in court.
43. **Mina's Testimony** - Mina admits the public label was written around known gaps.
44. **Temple Record** - Lian authenticates the inventory and identifies the lost screen.
45. **Director's Choice** - Tomas resigns rather than answer the board's withheld memo.
46. **Findings** - Judge Voss orders the screen held pending restitution negotiations.
47. **No Gala** - The gala opens as a public provenance forum instead.
48. **Conservation Plan** - Selin designs a treatment that preserves the hidden inscription.
49. **Return Terms** - The museum and temple trust agree on shared custody and eventual return.
50. **The Lacquer Room** - Selin closes the room with the screen no longer pretending to be silent.
