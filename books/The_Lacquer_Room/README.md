# The Lacquer Room

## Overview

Selin Arda is preparing a monumental lacquer screen for a museum gala when a tiny cleaning window exposes an older inscription under later black lacquer. The donor label says private collection, but resin layers, tool marks, insurance photographs, and temple rubbings point to a wartime removal that the market made beautiful and silent. As donor pressure rises, the lacquer room becomes the only place where the screen is allowed to tell the truth.

**Genre:** Art Conservation Mystery / Museum Repatriation Suspense
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~10200
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

A hidden temple mark under later lacquer forces a museum to choose between gala-ready donor history and the material record of a possibly stolen screen.

## Key Themes

- Conservation preserves evidence as well as beauty.
- Donor generosity cannot cure concealed provenance.
- Labels can become instruments of omission.
- Restitution begins when an institution stops forcing one story to cover another.
