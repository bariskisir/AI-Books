# The Lighthouse Keeper's Wife — Outline

## Premise

In 1870, Isobel MacKenzie, a twenty-eight-year-old woman from Edinburgh, marries Hamish Campbell, a lighthouse keeper on the remote Scottish rock of Sgeir Mhor. She arrives expecting a new beginning and finds instead a world of relentless sea, crushing isolation, and a husband whose years of solitude have turned him nearly mute. She must learn to survive the elements, understand the man she married, and discover her own strength — or be broken by the silence of the sea.

## Core Cast

- **Isobel Campbell (nee MacKenzie)** — Twenty-eight. Born in Edinburgh, educated, orphaned, accustomed to society. She marries Hamish for security and a fresh start after her father's death leaves her without prospects. She is practical, observant, and possesses a quiet resilience she does not yet know.
- **Hamish Campbell** — Forty-two. Lighthouse keeper on Sgeir Mhor for sixteen years. A tragedy in his past — the drowning of his first wife, Morag — has left him silent and guarded. He knows the sea intimately but has forgotten how to speak to people.
- **The Sea** — The third character. Unpredictable, beautiful, merciless. It shapes every day on Sgeir Mhor. It is the source of their livelihood and their isolation.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part One: The Crossing (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Letter** — Isobel receives Hamish's proposal in Edinburgh. Her father has died. She has no money, no suitors. She writes back yes.
2. **Departure** — Isobel leaves Edinburgh by train, then steamer. The city fades. She watches the coast disappear.
3. **Oban** — A night in a boarding house. She meets a fisherman's wife who warns her about the loneliness of keeper's life.
4. **The Boat** — A supply boat takes her to Sgeir Mhor. The sea is rough. She is sick and afraid.
5. **Sgeir Mhor** — First sight of the rock. The lighthouse rises from the mist. She feels she has arrived at the edge of the world.
6. **Hamish** — She meets her husband. He is tall, weathered, silent. He carries her trunk up the spiral stairs without a word.
7. **The Tower** — Isobel explores the lighthouse. The rooms are spare, clean, functional. The sea sound never stops.
8. **First Night** — Hamish shows her to her room — they will not share a bed. She lies awake listening to the waves.
9. **The Routine** — Hamish rises before dawn to trim the lamp. She watches him work in silence. The days take on a pattern.
10. **The Garden** — Isobel finds a patch of soil behind the tower. She plants seeds. Hamish watches her from the doorway.

### Part Two: The Silence (Chapters 11–20)

11. **The Bell** — A fog bell on the cliff. It clangs all night. Isobel cannot sleep. Hamish does not seem to hear it.
12. **The Diary** — She finds an old journal in a drawer. It belongs to a woman named Morag. She realizes it was Hamish's first wife.
13. **The Question** — She asks Hamish about Morag. He walks away. She does not ask again for weeks.
14. **The Storm** — The first autumn storm. The tower shakes. She is terrified. Hamish is calm, almost indifferent to the wind.
15. **The Light** — She watches him tend the lantern during the storm. His hands are steady. She sees his devotion to this work.
16. **The Ship** — A schooner passes close to the rock. Isobel waves from the cliff. No one waves back.
17. **The Garden Dies** — The salt spray kills her seedlings. She cries silently in the garden. Hamish finds her and says nothing, but brings her a cup of tea.
18. **The Story** — She pieces together Morag's story from the diary. A young woman who grew ill from the damp and could not be saved. The doctor came too late.
19. **The Photograph** — She finds a small photograph of Morag tucked in the diary. She understands now — Morag was pretty, young, loved.
20. **The Birds** — Seabirds nest on the cliffs. Isobel watches them every morning. The sea birds become her company.

### Part Three: The Winter (Chapters 21–30)

21. **First Snow** — Winter comes. The days shorten. The supply boat stops coming. They are alone until spring.
22. **The Lamp** — Hamish teaches Isobel to trim the wick and polish the lenses. She learns the language of the light.
23. **The Crack** — A crack appears in the lantern room glass. Hamish repairs it with putty and care. It holds.
24. **The Chair** — She moves a chair into the lantern room. She begins to sit with him during the night watches. Neither speaks.
25. **The Story He Tells** — One night, without warning, Hamish speaks. He tells her about the night Morag died — how he was in the tower, tending the light, and could not be with her.
26. **The Tears** — Isobel cries for Morag, for Hamish, for herself. He does not comfort her. He does not know how.
27. **The Frost** — Ice forms on the inside of the windows. They burn more coal. The lighthouse feels smaller.
28. **The Kitchen** — Isobel learns to bake bread in the small stove. Hamish eats more at dinner. A small victory.
29. **The Fever** — Isobel falls ill with a cough that will not leave. Hamish cares for her: broth, blankets, the same silence.
30. **The Recovery** — She recovers. She sees that he was afraid. He does not say it, but she sees it in how he checks on her each morning.

### Part Four: The Sea (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The Wreck** — A ship founders on the rocks during a spring gale. Hamish rows out alone into the breakers.
32. **The Rescue** — He brings back a sailor, half-drowned. Isobel strips the man, warms him, saves his life.
33. **The Stranger** — The sailor, a young man named Callum, stays with them for two weeks. He brings news from the mainland.
34. **The Envy** — Isobel realizes she envies Callum's freedom. She could leave. She is not trapped. But she does not want to.
35. **The Goodbye** — Callum leaves. The lighthouse is quiet again. But something has shifted between them.
36. **The Walk** — Hamish takes Isobel to the highest point of the rock. She sees the horizon. She understands now what he sees.
37. **The Touch** — Hamish touches her hand for the first time — a light brush of fingers as he passes her the salt.
38. **The Bed** — She moves her things into his room. They lie together in the dark, not touching, but together.
39. **The Word** — He says her name. For the first time, he says "Isobel." She cries.
40. **The Spring** — The supply boat returns. Letters from Edinburgh. She reads them but does not want to go back.

### Part Five: The Light (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Decision** — Isobel writes to her cousin in Edinburgh. She is staying. This is her home now.
42. **The Garden Again** — She plants again. This time she builds a stone wall to break the salt wind. The seeds survive.
43. **The Promise** — Hamish tells her about the light — how it is not just for ships, but for him. A purpose. She understands.
44. **The Song** — She sings while she works. He does not join, but he listens.
45. **The Boat Trip** — Hamish takes her out in the boat to the fishing grounds. She sees the lighthouse from the sea. It is beautiful.
46. **The Summer** — A season of peace. They talk in fragments — not many words, but enough. A conversation that spans months.
47. **The Visitor** — A minister comes to the rock. He asks if Isobel is happy. She says yes, and realizes it is true.
48. **The Anniversary** — One year since she arrived. She walks the cliff path he walked her on their first day. It no longer feels like the edge of the world.
49. **The Light in the Dark** — A winter night. She tends the lamp herself while Hamish sleeps. She is the keeper now, too.
50. **The Sea, Still** — Morning. Isobel and Hamish stand on the cliff together. The sea is calm. She takes his hand. She belongs here.
