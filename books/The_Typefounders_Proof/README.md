## Description

Type historian Ada Kern is hired to authenticate a proof sheet said to validate the sale of a nineteenth-century letterpress foundry. If genuine, the document lets a developer dissolve a workers' trust and turn the old pressworks into offices.

Ada finds the sheet too clean, the type too sharp, and one damaged lowercase e that could not have existed in the year claimed. With a typefounder, a union archivist, a paper chemist, and an ink analyst, she follows matrices, alloy recipes, punch marks, strike circulars, and a hidden galley proof.
