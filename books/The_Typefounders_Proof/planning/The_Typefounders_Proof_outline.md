# The Typefounders Proof - Outline

## Premise

Type historian Ada Kern is hired to authenticate a newly discovered proof sheet said to validate the sale of a nineteenth-century letterpress foundry. If genuine, the sheet lets a developer dissolve a workers' trust and turn the old pressworks into offices. Ada finds the document too clean, the type too sharp, and one damaged lowercase e that could not have existed in the year claimed. Matrices, alloy recipes, punch marks, union ledgers, ink chemistry, and a missing galley proof reveal a modern forgery built from historic tools.

## Core Cast

- **Ada Kern** - Type historian and proofreader, exacting, patient, and alert to tiny defects.
- **Theo Mott** - Last working typefounder at the old Bell & Vale pressworks.
- **Livia Crane** - Museum curator under pressure to certify the discovery.
- **Rafe Bell** - Independent printer descended from one of the foundry families.
- **Neris Holt** - Estate lawyer handling the trust dissolution.
- **Hugo Senn** - Developer funding the proof's sudden appearance.
- **Celia Ward** - Union archivist guarding wage books and strike circulars.
- **Owen Pike** - Paper chemist who tests fibers and watermarks.
- **Dr. Iman Aral** - Ink analyst with a quiet dislike for theatrical certainty.
- **Judge Maren Quill** - Probate judge overseeing the foundry trust.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. **The Proof Sheet** - Ada sees the disputed proof for the first time.
2. **Bell and Vale** - Theo shows her the decaying pressworks.
3. **Lowercase E** - A damaged letterform raises the first doubt.
4. **The Trust Clause** - Neris explains the stakes of authentication.
5. **Museum Light** - Livia reveals donor pressure behind the exhibit.
6. **Punch Marks** - Ada studies the metal punches and their flaws.
7. **The Alloy Book** - Theo finds a recipe missing from the foundry ledger.
8. **Watermark Noon** - Owen tests the paper against the claimed date.
9. **A Clean Margin** - Ada notices trimming that erased handling marks.
10. **Hugo's Model** - The developer presents the conversion plan.
11. **Union Ledger** - Celia produces wage entries that contradict the sale.
12. **Ink Under Glass** - Iman begins the chemical comparison.
13. **Rafe's Press** - Rafe proves old type can still print new lies.
14. **The Broken Matrix** - A matrix crack matches Ada's damaged e.
15. **Probate Notice** - Judge Quill schedules an evidentiary hearing.
16. **Galley Rumor** - Theo recalls a missing galley locked after a strike.
17. **Dust in the Cases** - Ada searches type cases for substituted sorts.
18. **Developer Visit** - Hugo offers a quiet consulting fee.
19. **The Wrong Ligature** - A ligature entered the font after the proof's date.
20. **Livia's Inventory** - The museum inventory exposes a recent accession gap.
21. **Strike Circular** - Celia's circular uses the genuine foundry face.
22. **Paper Bath** - Owen shows artificial aging in the sheet.
23. **Ink Split** - Iman identifies modern drying agents.
24. **Night Pull** - Rafe and Ada print a control proof.
25. **The Locked Cabinet** - Theo opens a cabinet of rejected matrices.
26. **Hugo's Receipt** - Neris finds a payment for private restoration.
27. **The Founder Mark** - Ada discovers a hidden founder's mark.
28. **Court Sample** - The judge orders comparative printing.
29. **Celia's Testimony** - Celia ties the trust to unpaid wages.
30. **Mott's Furnace** - Theo melts alloy to show period limitations.
31. **The False Wear** - Ada proves the proof was distressed after printing.
32. **Livia Breaks** - Livia admits she hid donor correspondence.
33. **The Substitute Sort** - A single replacement letter identifies the press.
34. **Estate Pressure** - Neris explains why the heirs wanted certainty.
35. **Iman's Chart** - Ink peaks expose a contemporary formulation.
36. **Rafe Accused** - Hugo tries to blame the independent printer.
37. **The Old Galley** - Ada finds the genuine locked galley.
38. **Matrix Number** - The forged sheet matches a recut matrix made last year.
39. **Hearing Room** - Ada explains type evidence in plain terms.
40. **Hugo's Workshop** - Search records reveal the hidden proofing press.
41. **Trust Survives** - The judge blocks dissolution.
42. **Restoration Plan** - The pressworks becomes a public print shop.
43. **Livia's Correction** - The museum posts a corrected label.
44. **The Wage Book** - Celia updates the public archive.
45. **Rafe's Edition** - Rafe prints the hearing transcript by hand.
46. **Theo's Apprentice** - Theo agrees to train new founders.
47. **Neris Settles** - The estate accepts a lawful compromise.
48. **The Clean Pull** - Ada prints from the genuine galley.
49. **Foundry Morning** - The pressworks reopens with honest noise.
50. **The Typefounder's Proof** - Ada files the final proof that preserves the trust.
