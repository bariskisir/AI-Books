# The Museum of Unsent Messages — Outline

## Act I: The Impossible Message (Chapters 1–10)

Archivist Mara Vale receives a future-dated unsent message in the voice of her sister Nia, dead for seven years. With repair technician Jonas Reed, she traces the anomaly to the museum's sealed predictive system, Palinode.

## Act II: The City Beneath the Archive (Chapters 11–20)

Mara follows Nia's clues through old service tunnels and the erased district of Low Bell. She learns that Nia helped expose Palinode's original purpose: predicting private hesitation so the city could quietly shape public behavior.

## Act III: Instructions from a Ghost (Chapters 21–30)

New messages appear, accurately forecasting accidents and choices. Mara discovers they are generated predictions, not recordings, but the model of Nia contains knowledge hidden before her death. Director Voss prepares a citywide expansion disguised as a memorial.

## Act IV: The Memorial (Chapters 31–40)

Mara and her allies enter the museum during the public ceremony, recover Nia's buried testimony, and confront the unsettling possibility that following the messages completes Palinode's forecast. Mara chooses a genuinely unpredicted action.

## Act V: The Right to Remain Unsaid (Chapters 41–50)

The archive's secret becomes public. Mara refuses both destruction and continued exploitation, transforming the museum into a voluntary institution where people control their abandoned words. The final apparent message from Nia is revealed as a real note entrusted to Jonas years earlier.
