# The Ember Throne — Outline

## Premise

Prince Kael of Ashfall, the last heir to a throne his father lost to betrayal, must forge alliances between the five warring kingdoms of the Emberlands before the prophesied Fire-Crown comet returns to ignite the world. With the fire witch Serin, who reads the world's burning veins, and against the plots of Lord Malachar, who holds the throne Kael claims, he must balance the needs of kingdoms that have warred for generations against a doom that cares nothing for their borders.

## Core Cast

- **Prince Kael** — Dispossessed heir to the Ashfall throne, exiled as a child, raised in the northern marches. Carries his father's signet ring and a debt of vengeance he is learning to set aside.
- **Serin** — Fire witch of the Ember Conclave. Her magic comes from the deep fires beneath the continent. She sees the Fire-Crown's approach in every flame.
- **Lord Malachar** — The traitor lord who betrayed Kael's father, now ruling Ashfall. A pragmatist who believes he saved the kingdom from weakness. Not cartoonishly evil — he has reasons and regrets.
- **Queen Elara** — Ruler of the Frostwater Kingdom, a reluctant ally.
- **Warden Brone** — Commander of the Iron Gate, the mountain fortress that separates the kingdoms.
- **The Ember Conclave** — The order of fire witches who interpret the world's burning signs.

## Fifty-Chapter Plan

1. Kael watches the Fire-Crown comet rise from his northern watchtower, knowing his exile is ending.
2. Serin wakes from a dream of burning kingdoms and reads the omens in her hearth fire.
3. Lord Malachar receives word that the comet has been sighted — and that a claimant to his throne still lives.
4. Kael takes his father's sword from its hiding place beneath an old oak and begins the journey south.
5. Serin argues with the Conclave elders, who dismiss the Fire-Crown as myth.
6. Kael stops at a crossroads village and hears how Malachar's tax collectors bleed the land dry.
7. Malachar's spymaster reports Kael's movements; the lord dispatches a company of riders.
8. Kael is ambushed on the old kingsroad and saved by a stranger — Serin, traveling south.
9. They travel together, each wary of the other, until a forest fire forces them to take shelter in a cave.
10. Serin reveals she is a fire witch; Kael reveals his name. An uneasy pact forms.
11. Malachar visits the Ashfall dungeons to speak with a prisoner who once served Kael's father.
12. Kael and Serin reach the Iron Gate, the fortress that controls passage between north and south.
13. Warden Brone refuses Kael passage — the north and south are at war, and his orders come from Ashfall.
14. Serin negotiates a way through using old Conclave debts Brone owes, but at a cost she does not name.
15. Beyond the Iron Gate, they enter the Scorched Plain, a wasteland from an old war.
16. Thirst and heat nearly kill them. Serin's magic keeps them alive, but it drains her.
17. Malachar rides to the Iron Gate himself, demanding answers about the fugitives.
18. Kael and Serin reach Frostwater, the northern kingdom ruled by Queen Elara.
19. Elara agrees to hear Kael's proposal but demands he prove himself by hunting a rogue fire-beast.
20. Kael hunts the beast with Serin's help and learns she is burning out — her magic consumes her flesh.
21. Elara pledges Frostwater's spears, but only if Malachar is deposed first.
22. Malachar returns to Ashfall and finds the city uneasy — the comet frightens the people.
23. Kael and Serin travel east to the mining kingdom of Deepflint, where the king owes nothing to anyone.
24. King Orin of Deepflint laughs at the prophecy but agrees to talk after Kael settles a mine dispute.
25. Kael mediates between miners and the crown, finding a talent for peace he did not know he had.
26. Deepflint pledges conditional support: Kael must win two more kingdoms to make it worth their risk.
27. Serin collapses. Her magic is accelerating. Kael carries her to the nearest Conclave enclave.
28. The Conclave heals her but warns: each use of her power shortens her life. She hides this from Kael.
29. Malachar's spies bring word of the growing alliance. He decides to court the southern horse lords.
30. Kael and Serin ride for the Wind-Reach Steppes to win the horse lords before Malachar does.
31. They arrive at a chieftain's funeral — the horse lords are in mourning and hostile to outsiders.
32. Serin reads the funeral pyre and delivers an omen that sways the elders in Kael's favor.
33. The horse lords pledge riders but cannot march until the spring thaw — a delay Kael cannot afford.
34. Malachar arrives at Wind-Reach too late; he orders the horse lords' pastures burned in spite.
35. Kael and Serin turn south toward the last kingdom — Sunmere, the merchant realm of the coast.
36. Sunmere's council will not commit without payment. Kael has no gold. Serin offers her services as a fire witch — a dangerous bargain.
37. In Sunmere, Serin performs a public reading of the flames, and the city sees the Fire-Crown for the first time. Panic spreads.
38. Sunmere's council agrees to fund the alliance, but only as merchants — they will sell supplies, not fight.
39. Kael has four kingdoms in tenuous alliance. One remains: the Ashfall throne itself.
40. Malachar sends a message: he will meet Kael alone on the old dueling ground before the capital.
41. Serin begs Kael not to go; she sees death in every flame. He goes anyway.
42. The duel. Kael and Malachar fight. Malachar is the better swordsman, but Kael fights for something beyond revenge.
43. Malachar disarms Kael and stands over him — then lowers his blade. He has seen the comet, and the fire witch's omens reach even him.
44. Malachar confesses: he betrayed Kael's father not for power but because the king planned to open the Deep Vents, the fire sources below the world, and unleash destruction.
45. Kael must choose: kill the man who killed his father, or forgive him to save the world.
46. Serin arrives as the Fire-Crown breaks the horizon, burning in the day sky. The world begins to heat.
47. Kael spares Malachar and calls the alliance to Ashfall. The armies march to seal the Deep Vents.
48. The sealing of the vents requires a fire witch at the heart of the fire — Serin volunteers, knowing it will kill her.
49. The battle at the Deep Vents: Kael leads the united armies against fire-elementals while Serin works the sealing ritual.
50. Serin seals the vents and vanishes into the flame. Kael takes the Ember Throne — not as a king, but as a steward of peace, haunted by what he lost.
