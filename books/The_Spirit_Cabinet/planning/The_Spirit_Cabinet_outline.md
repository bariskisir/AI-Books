# The Spirit Cabinet

## Premise

London, 1921. Thomas Gale, a cynical former stage magician turned investigator for the London Rationalist Society, is dispatched to expose Madame Seraphina, a rising medium whose séances have captivated wealthy grieving families still reeling from the Great War. Posing as a bereaved uncle, Thomas documents every trick: hidden wires, luminous gauze, ventriloquist's palming. But when a séance attendee dies in a locked room during a sitting, Thomas discovers that Seraphina herself is terrified — convinced that something genuine and predatory has latched onto her performances. As the body count rises and Scotland Yard circles closer, Thomas must confront the possibility that a fraud can summon a truth she never believed in, and that some spirits cannot be reasoned with or debunked.

## Core Cast

- **Thomas Gale (Protagonist):** 34, former stage magician and sapper in the Royal Engineers during the war. Investigator for the London Rationalist Society. Observant, skeptical, haunted by the men he could not save under fire. Carries a small notebook in which he diagrams every séance trick he witnesses.
- **Evelyn Croft / Madame Seraphina (Antagonist / Co-Protagonist):** 29, born in a Bermondsey tenement, taught herself stage magic from a borrowed book. Built the Seraphina persona to escape poverty. Charismatic on stage, terrified in private — she controls her séances with precision but fears what visits her when she loses focus.
- **Julian Hartwood (Patron):** 56, shipping magnate whose only son died at Passchendaele. Funds Seraphina's private séances and has become her most devoted believer. Grief has made him impervious to doubt, and his money shields the circle from outside scrutiny.
- **Dr. Margaret Ashe (Employer / Skeptic):** 48, psychologist and co-founder of the Rationalist Society. Lost her younger brother to a fraudulent medium's 'treatment.' Methodical, principled, wants proof that will stand in court — but her patience thins as the investigation stalls.
- **Inspector Frank Mercer (Detective):** 45, Scotland Yard's shadow liaison for cases involving occult circles. Widower who keeps his skepticism private. Has seen three séance-related deaths in two years and believes the common thread is money, not spirits — until the evidence begins to say otherwise.
- **Alice Phelps:** 22, Evelyn's quiet assistant. Acts as the séance parlor's maid-of-all-work. She knows every trick Evelyn uses and keeps the cabinet in order. Fiercely loyal but increasingly frightened.
- **Mr. Grimshaw:** Evelyn's former mentor — an aging stage magician who taught Evelyn the trade. Now a bitter alcoholic who knows where the bodies are buried.
- **Captain Leonard Hartwood:** Julian's deceased son. Died at Passchendaele in 1917. His photograph presides over the séance parlor.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Investigator (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Sapper's Return** — Thomas Gale, former sapper and stage magician, walks into the London Rationalist Society for a new assignment.
2. **The Gilded Parlor** — Thomas visits Julian Hartwood's Mayfair townhouse to observe the séance setting for the first time.
3. **Madame Seraphina** — The medium performs her introductory routine. Thomas begins cataloguing her techniques.
4. **The Floating Table** — During the first full séance, the table rises. Thomas notes the method but catches something he cannot explain.
5. **After the Circle** — Thomas debriefs with Dr. Margaret Ashe, reporting his initial findings while withholding a small doubt.
6. **The Rationalist's File** — Thomas reads the Society's existing dossier on spiritualist frauds, finding patterns that will prove useful.
7. **The Second Sitting** — Thomas attends a second séance to gather more evidence. Spiritual manifestations intensify.
8. **Alice's Warning** — After the sitting, Evelyn's assistant Alice warns Thomas in private that he should leave the circle.
9. **The Photographic Plate** — Thomas attempts spirit photography detection and captures an anomalous image on his plate.
10. **The Names of the Dead** — Julian Hartwood provides a list of names for the next sitting — names only Seraphina should know.

### Act II: The Unraveling (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Backstage Door** — Thomas follows Evelyn after a séance, tracking her to her Bloomsbury lodgings.
12. **Evelyn's Room** — He confronts her privately; she admits the fraud but insists something real has attached itself to her performances.
13. **The Machinery of Belief** — Thomas examines the séance apparatus more closely, finding modifications he did not expect.
14. **Julian's Grief** — Julian Hartwood reveals the depth of his loss to Thomas over brandy in his study.
15. **The Blackmailed Medium** — Evelyn confesses that someone is blackmailing her with knowledge of her past.
16. **The Fourth Séance** — A larger sitting with new attendees. The manifestations are especially violent.
17. **A Death in the Parlor** — An elderly widow, Mrs. Whitmore, is found dead in the locked séance parlor.
18. **The Interrogation** — Inspector Mercer questions the circle. Thomas must conceal his investigation role.
19. **Evelyn's Confession** — Privately, Evelyn tells Thomas the death was not her doing — and she believes the spirit she summoned did it.
20. **The Autopsy** — Dr. Ashe obtains the coroner's report. Cause of death: heart failure, but with unusual petechial hemorrhaging.

### Act III: The Haunting (Chapters 21-30)

21. **Grimshaw's Price** — Thomas tracks down Evelyn's former mentor, Mr. Grimshaw, in a Whitechapel pub.
22. **The Séance Without a Medium** — Thomas proposes a controlled séance without Evelyn to test whether phenomena occur independently.
23. **The Fifth Visitor** — During the controlled sitting, an attendee claims to see a figure Evelyn did not summon.
24. **Evelyn's Notebook** — Evelyn shows Thomas her personal records — drawings of a figure that appears when she loses concentration.
25. **The Photograph Revealed** — Thomas develops his anomalous photographic plate properly: a face no one present recognizes.
26. **Mr. Phelps's History** — Alice Phelps's father has a history with the Hartwood family that predates the séances.
27. **The Closed Circle** — The original circle members meet privately without Thomas. A message comes through that shakes Julian.
28. **Alice's Secret** — Alice reveals that she has been hiding evidence: a photograph from the last sitting that shows something impossible.
29. **The Cabinet** — Thomas examines the spirit cabinet itself, discovering old carvings inside that predate Evelyn's use.
30. **Grimshaw's Accident** — Grimshaw is found beaten in an alley. Before losing consciousness, he whispers a name to Thomas.

### Act IV: The Confrontation (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Suspect** — Scotland Yard arrests a man with occult connections. Thomas believes they have the wrong person.
32. **Evelyn's Choice** — Evelyn must decide whether to continue the sittings or flee. Thomas urges her to stay and fight.
33. **The Cabinet Séance** — Thomas agrees to enter the cabinet himself for a controlled experiment.
34. **What Came Through** — Something enters the cabinet that Thomas cannot explain by any trick he knows.
35. **The Rationalist's Crisis** — Dr. Ashe confronts Thomas about his changing conclusions. He can no longer give her the debunking she wanted.
36. **Julian's Son** — Julian reveals that he has been in contact with his son's spirit independently of Evelyn — and has proof.
37. **The Photograph Album** — Alice produces a family album showing Edward Phelps, her father, in military uniform with a man who looks like Captain Hartwood.
38. **Evelyn's Theory** — Evelyn posits that the entity is not a spirit at all, but something that feeds on belief.
39. **The Trap** — Thomas and Evelyn devise a plan to trap the entity during a final séance.
40. **The Empty Circle** — The ritual is prepared. The circle sits without a medium, waiting.

### Act V: The Reckoning (Chapters 41-50)

41. **Julian's Demand** — Julian insists on being present for the final séance. He believes his son will finally speak clearly.
42. **The Night Before** — Thomas and Evelyn spend the night before the final sitting together, sharing their histories.
43. **The Final Circle** — The séance begins. All members are present. The cabinet is prepared.
44. **The Cabinet-Maker** — Thomas discovers the original builder of the cabinet: Edward Phelps, Alice's father, a joiner who worked for the Hartwood family.
45. **Mr. Phelps's Role** — Edward Phelps, alive and hidden, is brought forward. He reveals the cabinet's true origin.
46. **The Bargain** — The entity offers a trade: knowledge of the dead in exchange for continued belief. Evelyn refuses.
47. **The Transfer** — The entity attempts to transfer itself through the table. Thomas intervenes with a magician's misdirection.
48. **The Morning After** — The séance is over. Julian has what he wanted. Mercer makes his arrests. Not everyone survives.
49. **Evelyn's Exit** — Evelyn disappears from London, leaving her apparatus behind. Thomas finds only a note.
50. **The Cold Draft** — In the empty parlor, Thomas sits alone with the cabinet. The draft returns. The ending.
