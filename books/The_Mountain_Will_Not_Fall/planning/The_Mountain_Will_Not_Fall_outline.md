# The Mountain Will Not Fall — Outline

## Premise

A climbing expedition on K2 is caught in a sudden storm that pins six climbers at 26,000 feet in the Bottleneck, a steep couloir of bulletproof ice. After the avalanche sweeps three climbers off the face, the survivors — Tenzing, a veteran Sherpa guide carrying a secret injury; Dr. Ashford, a British mountaineer driven by a private grief; and a young Korean climber named Min-seo — must descend through the death zone with a broken radio, failing oxygen, and a storm that will not break. At Base Camp, an inexperienced radio operator named Rana must navigate the ethics of rescue from 10,000 feet below, knowing that any rescue attempt will likely kill the rescuers too.

## Core Cast

- **Tenzing Dorje** — 38, head Sherpa guide, third-generation climber from Khumbu. Carries a shoulder injury from a crevasse fall last season that he has hidden from his clients.
- **Dr. Eleanor Ashford** — 42, British physician and veteran of three 8,000-meter peaks. On K2 to complete a memorial ascent for her brother, who died on the mountain a decade earlier.
- **Min-seo Park** — 29, Korean climber, youngest of the summit team, on her first 8,000-meter peak.
- **Rana Thapa** — 24, Base Camp radio operator and logistics manager, a Nepali university graduate in his first season as a full expedition staff member.
- **Pemba** — 35, Sherpa, Tenzing's cousin and climbing partner.
- **Russell** — 45, American client, experienced but arrogant.
- **Colonel Vinod Thapa** (voice only) — 52, Rana's father, former Indian Army mountaineer, reached by satellite phone.

## 50-Chapter Plan

1. Summit Push — The team leaves Camp 4 at 2 AM, headlamps bobbing in the darkness below the Shoulder.
2. The Bottleneck — They traverse the infamous ice couloir beneath a hanging serac the size of an apartment block.
3. First Light — Dawn breaks over the Karakoram, revealing the scale of what they must climb.
4. The Altitude — At 26,000 feet, every step costs three breaths. Ashford struggles with a faulty regulator.
5. Tenzing's Secret — Tenzing's left shoulder seizes as he places a line. He hides the pain from his clients.
6. The Serac — A crack splits the ice above them. The hanging glacier shifts.
7. Min-seo's Doubt — Min-seo stops climbing. Ashford talks her through the fear.
8. The Collapse — The serac calves. Ice and debris roar down the couloir.
9. Avalanche — Three climbers are swept from the face. Russell and Pemba vanish.
10. Aftermath — The survivors regroup on a ledge. Tenzing takes a head count. Two missing.
11. The Broken Radio — Ashford's hands shake as she tries to raise Base Camp. Static.
12. Rana Listens — At Base Camp Rana hears the roar and then silence. He calls every frequency.
13. The Inventory — They count oxygen bottles, food, water. They have two days, maybe less.
14. The Casualty — Min-seo finds Russell's ice axe wedged in a crack. No sign of the man.
15. The Descent Begins — Tenzing leads them down through ground fog that swallows the fixed lines.
16. Lost in White — Whiteout conditions. The three climbers rope up and move blind.
17. The Crevasse — The snow bridge collapses beneath Ashford. Rope holds.
18. The Rescue of Ashford — Tenzing and Min-seo haul her out, bleeding from a gash on her forehead.
19. Pemba's Signal — A distant flicker of a headlamp below them. Someone is alive.
20. The Injured Man — They find Pemba pinned against a serac, his leg crushed. Russell is dead.
21. The Splint — Ashford improvises a splint from a tent pole and a sleeping pad. Surgery at 25,000 feet.
22. The Triage — They cannot move Pemba and reach Camp 3 on remaining oxygen. A decision looms.
23. The Radio Lives — Ashford's spare radio crackles. Rana's voice breaks through.
24. The Situation Report — Ashford gives Rana the casualty count. Rana relays it to the world.
25. The Satellite Call — Rana calls his father, Colonel Thapa, for advice on high-altitude rescue.
26. The Colonel's Answer — Thapa tells his son: do not send climbers up. It will kill them too.
27. Rana's Choice — Rana decides to organize a rescue against his father's advice.
28. The Volunteers — Rana asks the remaining guides at Base Camp. Only two agree to go up.
29. The Descent to Help — Tenzing's team continues down, carrying Pemba between them.
30. The Oxygen Debate — Min-seo's tank reads zero. She removes her mask. Brainstorm.
31. The Hallucination — Min-seo sees a figure on the ridge. Ashford pulls her back from stepping off a drop.
32. Camp 3 — They reach the tents at 23,500 feet. Shelter. Tenzing's shoulder collapses.
33. The Diagnosis — Ashford examines Tenzing's shoulder. Torn rotator cuff. He has been climbing one-armed.
34. The Second Wave — Rana's rescue team ascends from Camp 2. One turns back.
35. The Night — Four survivors in a three-man tent. Breathing. Waiting.
36. The Wind — The storm intensifies. The tent fly rips free. They anchor it with their bodies.
37. Pemba's Fever — Infection in his leg wound. Ashford gives the last antibiotics.
38. The Rescue Arrives — Headlamps above them at dawn. Rana's men have climbed through the night.
39. The Handoff — Pemba is transferred to a rescue sled. Tenzing refuses to be carried.
40. The Descent Resumes — Tenzing walks with Ashford steadying him. Pain in every step.
41. The Last Fixed Line — The rope at the bottom of the ice wall is frayed. They must cut and retie.
42. The Fall — Ashford slips on black ice. Tenzing catches her with his good arm.
43. Camp 2 — They stagger into camp at 21,000 feet. Hot tea. The first real food in days.
44. The Debris Field — Rana radios that the helicopter cannot land above 18,000 feet. They must keep walking.
45. Pemba's Ride — The rescue sled crosses the icefall. One runner breaks. A field repair.
46. The Icefall at Night — Crossing the Khumbu Icefall in darkness. Ladders over crevasses.
47. The Collapse of Rana — Rana has not slept in 48 hours. His father calls. They argue.
48. The Doctor's Confession — Ashford tells Tenzing why she came: her brother died on this mountain, and she wanted to die here too.
49. The Valley Floor — The survivors reach Advanced Base Camp. Pemba is evacuated by helicopter.
50. The Mountain Stands — Ashford and Tenzing sit on a moraine and watch the summit. K2 is unchanged. They are not.
