# The Gaslight Grimoire

## Premise

In the fog-choked alleys of Victorian London, magic pulses through the city's gaslights—a hidden network of power unknown to all but a few. Finn, a homeless urchin who knows every shadow and shortcut in Whitechapel, steals a leather-bound book from a dying man and discovers it contains spells that let him see and shape the light-magic. When the gaslights begin guttering with a sickly amber glow and people vanish into the fog, Finn finds himself pursued by Inspector Graves of Scotland Yard and sought out by Lady Blackwood, an aristocratic occult scholar. The three must unite against a forgotten enemy who means to extinguish every flame in London and plunge the city into eternal dark.

## Core Cast

- **Finn** — Street urchin, orphan, thief. Quick-witted and mistrustful. Age 17. He carries the grimoire.
- **Lady Eleanor Blackwood** — Widowed aristocrat and secret practitioner of the old light-magic. Her family has guarded London's gaslight network for generations.
- **Inspector Edmund Graves** — Scotland Yard detective. Methodical, skeptical, haunted by a case he could not solve. He follows the evidence, which leads him to Finn.

## Chapter Plan

1. Finn lifts a purse in the Spitalfields market and feels eyes on him.
2. A dying man presses a worn book into Finn's hands and whispers a warning.
3. Inspector Graves arrives at the scene of a strange death—the man's eyes are burned white.
4. Finn opens the book and reads aloud; the gaslights around him flare bright.
5. Lady Blackwood senses a disturbance in the light-web from her townhouse study.
6. Finn discovers he can see threads of light connecting the gas lamps.
7. Graves interviews witnesses who describe a boy fleeing with a book.
8. The grimoire shows Finn a vision of a vast dark shape moving under the city.
9. Lady Blackwood sends her manservant to find the source of the magical disruption.
10. Finn tests a spell that lets him walk unseen through the crowded streets.
11. Graves finds the burned man's lodgings and uncovers symbols carved into the floorboards.
12. A pack of fog-shrouded hunters pursues Finn through the alleys of St. Giles.
13. Finn uses a binding spell to trap one of the hunters and sees it dissolve into smoke.
14. Lady Blackwood reads old family journals and finds mention of "the Drowned God."
15. Graves's investigation leads him to a gasworks foreman who speaks of tampered lamps.
16. Finn seeks shelter in a ruined church; the grimoire glows in response to the altar.
17. The hunters find the church; Finn escapes through a crypt tunnel.
18. Lady Blackwood arrives at the church ruins and finds traces of spent magic.
19. Graves and Lady Blackwood meet at Scotland Yard—their cases intertwine.
20. Finn steals food from a market stall and is cornered by a constable; he vanishes using the book.
21. The Drowned God's followers gather in a candlelit cellar beneath the Thames.
22. Lady Blackwood performs a divination ritual and sees Finn's face in the flame.
23. Graves traces the burned man to a secret society called the Candle-Eaters.
24. Finn reads a chapter on the history of light-magic and learns of an ancient pact.
25. The Candle-Eaters send a messenger to Lady Blackwood's home with a threat.
26. Finn is captured by the hunters and brought to their underground lair.
27. Graves interrogates a Candle-Eater informant and learns of the Drowned God's prophecy.
28. Lady Blackwood uses a tracking spell to locate Finn's position beneath the city.
29. Finn wakes in a cell lined with lead; the grimoire feels weak.
30. The leader of the Candle-Eaters explains their goal: to unmake the sun.
31. Graves and Lady Blackwood descend into the sewers to reach the Candle-Eater lair.
32. Finn picks the lock of his cell using a bent nail hidden in his shoe.
33. Graves shoots a Candle-Eater guard; Lady Blackwood's light-spell blinds the rest.
34. The three meet in the corridor—Finn does not trust the lady or the inspector.
35. The grimoire reveals a spell to seal the Drowned God's prison permanently.
36. The Candle-Eaters begin their ritual, each gaslight in London dimming one by one.
37. Finn agrees to work with Graves and Lady Blackwood to stop the ritual.
38. They navigate the sewers toward the ritual chamber beneath the Thames.
39. The hunters attack in force; Graves fights with a truncheon and a revolver.
40. Lady Blackwood erects a barrier of light to hold the hunters back.
41. Finn reads the sealing spell from the grimoire but falters on a crucial passage.
42. The Candle-Eater high priest summons a manifestation of the Drowned God.
43. Graves fires his last bullet into the manifestation; it reforms instantly.
44. Lady Blackwood channels the gaslight network into a lance of pure flame.
45. The Drowned God's hand reaches through the summoning circle and grabs Finn.
46. Finn speaks the sealing spell from memory, completing it correctly this time.
47. The ritual circle shatters; the Candle-Eaters are consumed by their own dark fire.
48. The gaslights across London flare back to life one by one.
49. Lady Blackwood offers Finn a home; Graves offers to clear his record.
50. Finn chooses the grimoire over safety, and walks into the fog toward a new road.
