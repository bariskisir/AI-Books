# The Rain Archive - Outline

## Premise

In a drought-struck near-future city, municipal archivist Mara Venn discovers that rainfall records have been altered to transfer public water rights to a private citrus consortium. With a mechanic, a reporter, a farmer, and a compromised database engineer, she must prove that the archive itself has been made to lie before emergency powers seal every record that can save the city.

## Core Cast

- **Mara Venn** - Adult municipal archivist who trusts paper records because paper shows damage.
- **Tomas Reza** - Adult pump mechanic and former canal diver who knows where the city hides its valves.
- **Imani Cho** - Adult investigative reporter who treats invoices as weather instruments.
- **Alma Ruiz** - Adult dryland farmer with cistern jars, receipts, and no patience for polished lies.
- **Soren Vale** - Adult database engineer whose migration work helped conceal the fraud.
- **Mayor Celia Marr** - Adult city executive protecting an emergency water regime.
- **Dr. Elian Park** - Adult retired hydrologist who explains the legal memory of rain.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Records
1. **The Empty Gauge** - Mara Venn reopens the municipal rain archive after a records clerk dies beside a dry gauge.
2. **Zero Millimeters** - She finds a month of official rainfall entered as zero while private cisterns show wet roofs.
3. **The Reservoir Floor** - At the exposed reservoir, pump mechanic Tomas Reza shows her survey stakes hidden in cracked mud.
4. **Witness Water** - Reporter Imani Cho brings a leaked canal invoice signed before the water existed.
5. **The Blue Ledger** - Mara finds the old handwritten rainfall ledger overwritten by a clean digital export.
6. **Council Heat** - A public hearing turns the archive into an accusation and the mayor demands silence.
7. **Dust on the Server** - Database engineer Soren Vale admits the archive was migrated through an offsite contractor.
8. **The Farmer with Receipts** - Alma Ruiz arrives with jars of roof water and dates written on masking tape.
9. **A Map Without Shade** - The city planning map erases three old neighborhoods from emergency allotment routes.
10. **Night Sampling** - Mara and Tomas take illegal samples from a sealed diversion pipe under the bypass.

### Part II: Diversions
11. **The Private Forecast** - A climate broker sells perfect rain predictions to the citrus consortium.
12. **Invoice for a Storm** - Imani traces a payment for cloud seeding on a day no aircraft flew.
13. **The Cistern Choir** - Residents open their tanks and prove the archive has been made to lie.
14. **Ash in the Filter** - Lab results show wildfire ash in water that the official record says never fell.
15. **The Board of Allocation** - Mara testifies and learns that truth without custody is treated as rumor.
16. **Soren’s Key** - Soren gives her a physical encryption key and warns that the checksum is wrong.
17. **The Man in the Canal** - A private security guard follows Tomas into the dry canal and offers him money.
18. **Rain Names** - Mara decodes field notes where old observers named storms by smell and direction.
19. **Twelve Missing Pages** - The ledger jumps from June to August; July has been cut out with a razor.
20. **The First Threat** - A brick breaks Mara’s kitchen window with a photocopy wrapped around it.

### Part III: Custody
21. **Salt Bloom** - At the lowest basin, the water table has turned white with salt and dead frogs.
22. **The Contractor’s Wife** - Mara interviews the dead clerk’s widow and hears about a locked orange drive.
23. **The Dry Chapel** - The orange drive is hidden in a closed chapel where evacuees once slept.
24. **Ghost Rain** - Deleted radar loops show a storm cell diverted across private groves.
25. **The Citrus Gate** - Tomas opens the consortium gate and sees municipal water feeding young trees.
26. **Emergency Powers** - The mayor declares a conservation emergency that would seal the archive for ninety days.
27. **Imani’s Tape** - Imani records the broker naming the council votes he purchased.
28. **The Old Hydrologist** - Retired hydrologist Dr. Elian Park explains the fraud as a theft of legal memory.
29. **Break in the Glass Room** - The archive server room is vandalized, but Soren’s checksum survives on paper.
30. **A Public Dryness** - Mara stages a public reading of the original observers’ logs on the courthouse steps.

### Part IV: Storm Evidence
31. **The Night Canal** - A rainless thunderstorm covers Mara and Tomas as they follow the diversion trench.
32. **The Hidden Valve** - They find the valve that can turn the city’s allocation into private irrigation.
33. **Alma’s Petition** - Alma gathers farmers, renters, and brewery workers into one signed demand.
34. **The Broker’s Forecast** - The broker predicts rain on television using stolen municipal instruments.
35. **The Flood That Never Was** - The record invents a flood to justify selling emergency water rights.
36. **Custody Chain** - Mara builds a chain of custody from paper, jars, lab work, and human witnesses.
37. **Tomas in Holding** - Security frames Tomas for sabotage and the police take him before dawn.
38. **The Court of Dust** - In an emergency court, Mara learns that hydrology can sound like grief.
39. **Soren Goes Missing** - Soren disappears after sending one line: the root certificate is under the fountain.
40. **The Fountain Box** - The fountain in city hall hides a metal box with the original export drive.

### Part V: One Millimeter
41. **Two Archives** - Mara compares the public archive and the hidden archive before a live camera.
42. **Rain at Noon** - A real storm breaks over the city while officials argue that it is not raining.
43. **The Mayor’s Measure** - The mayor tries to surrender the broker and keep the falsified water regime.
44. **The Long Valve** - Tomas is freed and opens the bypass valve under court order.
45. **Names on the Tank** - Residents paint the dead clerk’s name and the missing July dates on the water tower.
46. **The Allocation Vote** - The council must vote with every forged number projected behind them.
47. **Imani Publishes** - The story goes national, but Mara stays with the local records.
48. **After Rain** - The city learns how to measure again, slowly and with witnesses.
49. **The New Ledger** - Mara begins a handwritten duplicate ledger kept in a public room.
50. **One Millimeter** - The last chapter records one honest millimeter and the human noise around it.
