# The Meridian Door — Outline

## Premise

Miranda Hale runs a failing antique shop in a rain-soaked city. Behind an unassuming door in the back room lies the Meridian — a once-vibrant pocket world now dying from the spread of the Corrosion, a silent blight that drains color, memory, and life. Miranda is the last in a line of Guardians, and when a curious customer named Finn stumbles too close to the truth, she must choose: seal the door forever and stay safe, or open it wide and save a world that is not her own.

## Core Cast

- **Miranda Hale** — Quiet, solitary antique shop owner and reluctant last Guardian of the Meridian door.
- **Finn** — A warm-hearted regular customer with a restless spirit who becomes drawn into Miranda's hidden world.
- **The Custodian** — An ethereal being woven from memory and light, keeper of the Meridian's history and its original architect.
- **The Corrosion** — The silent, consuming blight eating away at the Meridian — not evil, but lonely; a void that yearns for connection.

---

## Part I: The Door (Chapters 1–13)

1. **The Rain Shop** — Miranda Hale opens her antique shop on a grey morning, the city weeping outside.
2. **The Brass Compass** — Finn enters seeking a gift; Miranda feels an inexplicable pull toward him.
3. **A Key That Fits No Lock** — Miranda finds an old brass key in a drawer she has never opened before.
4. **The Door at the Back** — A door appears in the back hallway that was never there before.
5. **The First Tug** — Miranda touches the door and feels warmth, smells ozone and wildflowers.
6. **Finn Returns** — Finn comes back and confesses he cannot stop thinking about the shop.
7. **Shared Tea** — Miranda and Finn talk over tea; she almost tells him about the door.
8. **The Knock** — The door thumps from the other side while Finn is still in the shop.
9. **A Sliver of Light** — Miranda cracks the door open and sees a meadow under a violet sky.
10. **The Custodian's Whisper** — A voice drifts through the crack: "You have waited long enough."
11. **What Finn Saw** — Finn glimpses the violet sky and presses Miranda for the truth.
12. **The Decision** — Miranda decides to step through the door; Finn refuses to let her go alone.
13. **Threshold** — They cross into the Meridian together, the shop door vanishing behind them.

## Part II: The Crossing (Chapters 14–25)

14. **The Violet Meadow** — They emerge into a beautiful but eerily silent meadow beneath a fading sun.
15. **The Village of Dust** — They find a village where everything touched by grey loses its color.
16. **The Well of Echoes** — A village elder tells them of the Well of Echoes, where answers may remain.
17. **The Custodian Appears** — The Custodian materializes: an old woman woven from light and memory.
18. **The Meridian's Story** — The Custodian explains the Meridian was once a bridge between worlds.
19. **The Corrosion's Nature** — The Corrosion is a void — the world consuming itself from loneliness.
20. **The Map of Fading Stars** — The Custodian gives them a star-map showing the last bright places.
21. **The Silk Road of Ghosts** — They travel an ancient trade road where echoes of past travelers linger.
22. **The Garden of Fixed Moments** — They find a garden where time stands still, preserved by a Guardian.
23. **Finn's Question** — Finn asks Miranda why she chose solitude; her answer reveals her fear of loss.
24. **The Singing Bell** — They ring a bell at the Corrosion's edge and hear an answering chime.
25. **The Hermit of the Amber Fields** — They meet an old Guardian who stayed when all others fled.

## Part III: The Corrosion (Chapters 26–37)

26. **What the Hermit Knows** — The old Guardian reveals the Corrosion can only be reversed by choice.
27. **The Heartwood** — They must reach the Heartwood, the Meridian's seed, before it is consumed.
28. **The Grey River** — They cross a grey river; Miranda touches the water and feels the world's sorrow.
29. **Campfire Stories** — Around a fire, Finn speaks of his loneliness; their bond deepens.
30. **The Corrosion Speaks** — Miranda hears the Corrosion as a whisper hungry for connection.
31. **The Mirror Pool** — They find a pool that shows them what they most fear and most desire.
32. **Miranda's Reflection** — She sees herself closing the door forever and living a small, safe life.
33. **Finn's Reflection** — He sees himself walking away, too afraid to stay and face the truth.
34. **The Pact** — Miranda and Finn promise to see this through together, whatever it means.
35. **The Approaching Grey** — The Corrosion accelerates, reaching toward the Heartwood faster than hoped.
36. **The Custodian's Farewell** — The Custodian fades, leaving them a final riddle before dissolving.
37. **The Heartwood Doors** — They arrive at the Heartwood to find it ringed by doors — each a choice.

## Part IV: The Meridian (Chapters 38–50)

38. **The Last Door** — Miranda understands each door leads to a world the Meridian once connected.
39. **What the Corrosion Wants** — The Corrosion is the loneliness of a world full of travelers, now empty.
40. **The Offering** — Miranda realizes the Corrosion does not want to be fought — only acknowledged.
41. **Finn's Role** — Finn sees his presence as an outsider is the key; he carries new stories.
42. **The Gathering** — The Meridian's people gather at the Heartwood, drawn by something unspoken.
43. **The Telling** — Miranda tells the story of the Meridian aloud; the Corrosion listens.
44. **The First Color** — A patch of grey flickers back to life as the Corrosion draws back.
45. **The Unlocking** — Finn opens a Heartwood door, reconnecting the Meridian to another world.
46. **The Tide Turns** — The Corrosion recedes as connection begins to heal the dying world.
47. **A New Guardian** — The people ask Miranda to stay; she must choose between two worlds.
48. **The Door Between** — Miranda and Finn decide the door should remain open — but watched.
49. **Coming Home** — They return to the shop. Only hours have passed. The rain has stopped.
50. **A Quiet Morning** — Miranda opens the shop with Finn beside her; the door at the back waits.
