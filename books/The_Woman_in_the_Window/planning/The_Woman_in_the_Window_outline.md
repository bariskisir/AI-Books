# The Woman in the Window - Outline

## Premise

Zoe Cross, an agoraphobic street photographer living alone in a crumbling apartment building in a declining city, witnesses a violent murder through her telephoto lens from her window. She calls the police immediately. Detective Marco Ruiz arrives, but there is no body, no sign of struggle, no forensic evidence. The apartment across the courtyard is pristine. Worse still, when Ruiz runs the woman's face through the system, the database returns a name: Elena Marchetti -- a woman who died in a car accident twenty years ago. Trapped in her apartment by her condition, isolated and gaslit by every official who investigates, Zoe must solve the mystery of the woman in the window before whoever staged the murder decides she knows too much.

## Core Cast

- **Zoe Cross** -- A reclusive street photographer in her early forties. Agoraphobic after a traumatic event in her twenties. Sharp, observant, deeply lonely. She sees the world through lenses because she cannot bear to be in it.
- **Detective Marco Ruiz** -- A weary homicide detective, mid-fifties, nearing retirement. Cynical but not cruel. He wants to believe Zoe but the evidence keeps pointing to delusion or deception.
- **The Woman (Elena Marchetti)** -- A strikingly pale woman in a red dress, glimpsed only through the zoom lens. Her photograph matches a woman who supposedly died two decades ago. Whether ghost, twin, or something else is the central question.
- **Frank Marchetti** -- Elena's elderly father, a retired restaurateur living in the same building. Quiet, watchful. He has secrets.
- **Dr. Lydia Thorne** -- Zoe's therapist, who monitors her agoraphobia from a careful professional distance.
- **James Adler** -- A true-crime podcaster who takes an interest in Zoe's story and becomes an unlikely ally.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### PART ONE: WHAT SHE SAW (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Window** -- Zoe Cross adjusts her telephoto lens from her third-floor apartment, scanning the courtyard for texture and shadow. She frames the building opposite and lands on a woman in a red dress standing at a window.
2. **The Flash** -- Through the zoom, Zoe sees a figure enter the apartment behind the woman. A struggle. The woman falls. A shadow stands over her. Zoe's camera fires automatically, capturing the moment.
3. **Nine-One-One** -- Zoe calls 911 with shaking hands. The dispatcher keeps her on the line. Zoe watches the apartment across the courtyard. No one leaves. The lights stay on.
4. **The Detective** -- Detective Marco Ruiz arrives. He is polite, thorough, skeptical. He takes Zoe's statement and her camera.
5. **The Clean Apartment** -- Ruiz enters the apartment Zoe identified. It is staged like a model home. No body. No blood. No signs of violence. The resident, an elderly man named Frank Marchetti, claims he was home all evening and heard nothing.
6. **The Contact Sheet** -- At the precinct, Ruiz reviews Zoe's photos. The images are high resolution, well composed. A woman in red, a shadow, a fall. But the face of the woman is surprisingly blurred in every frame, as if the camera refused to focus.
7. **The Cold Trail** -- Ruiz runs facial recognition on the woman in the red dress. The system returns a match: Elena Marchetti. Date of death: twenty years ago. Vehicular accident. Ruiz stares at the screen and says nothing.
8. **The Return** -- Ruiz comes back to Zoe's apartment the next morning. He returns her camera. He tells her, carefully, that there is no case. No body. No evidence. He asks if she is under any treatment for anxiety.
9. **The Zoom** -- That night, Zoe sets up her camera again. The apartment across the courtyard is dark. But at 11:04 PM, a light flicks on in the front room. A woman in a red dress crosses the frame.
10. **The Callback** -- Zoe calls Ruiz. He does not answer. She leaves a voicemail. Then she takes another photograph. When she downloads it, the woman is gone -- but the window is open. It was closed before.

### PART TWO: THE SKEPTIC AND THE BELIEVER (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Voicemail** -- Ruiz listens to Zoe's message the next morning. He deletes it. But something pulls him back to the file on Elena Marchetti.
12. **The Accident Report** -- Ruiz pulls the original case file on Elena Marchetti's death. A car went off the Pacific Coast Highway. The body was recovered, identified by dental records. Closed case.
13. **The Father** -- Ruiz interviews Frank Marchetti in his apartment. Frank is seventy-two, polite, composed. He confirms his daughter Elena died twenty years ago. He keeps her room exactly as it was. Ruiz notices a faint chemical smell.
14. **The Photograph** -- Zoe develops her film in the bathroom darkroom she built herself. The new image of the open window reveals something else: a figure standing in the alley below, looking up at her camera.
15. **The Therapist** -- Dr. Lydia Thorne makes a house call. Zoe is agitated, obsessed. Lydia prescribes a mild sedative and reminds Zoe that paranoia is a known pattern in her condition.
16. **The Name** -- Zoe searches Elena Marchetti online. She finds a death notice, a grainy newspaper photo, and a blog post from someone calling themselves "The Last Witness" asking questions about the accident.
17. **The Blogger** -- The "Last Witness" is James Adler, a true-crime podcaster. Zoe emails him. He responds within an hour. He has been investigating Elena's death for three years.
18. **The Story** -- James tells Zoe Elena was a photographer too. She vanished for three weeks before the "accident." Her body was so badly burned that dental records were the only identification. He believes it was a switch.
19. **The Archive** -- James sends Zoe a file of everything he has on Elena Marchetti. Among the documents is a contact sheet from the last roll of film Elena ever shot. Zoe studies it. The last frame is a mirror self-portrait.
20. **The Face** -- Zoe zooms in on Elena's self-portrait. The face is different from the woman in the window. Similar bone structure, but the eyes are wrong. The woman in the window has lighter irises, almost gray. Elena's eyes were brown.

### PART THREE: THE GHOST IN THE MACHINE (Chapters 21-30)

21. **The Discrepancy** -- Zoe calls Ruiz and tells him about the eye color difference. Ruiz sighs. He says he will look at the original case photos, but his voice carries no conviction.
22. **The Morgue** -- Ruiz visits the medical examiner's office. The original ME who handled the Marchetti case died five years ago. The file is thin. Photos are missing.
23. **The Graveyard** -- James Adler drives to the cemetery where Elena is buried. The grave is overgrown. He checks the cemetery records and finds the plot was never actually occupied -- the family cremated the remains.
24. **The Darkroom** -- Zoe processes a new roll. She has been shooting the apartment across the courtyard obsessively. In one frame, she catches the woman in red looking directly at the camera. The woman is holding a sign. The text is too small to read.
25. **The Enhancement** -- James uses photo software to enhance the image. The sign reads: "HELP ME."
26. **The Shift** -- Ruiz begins to take the case seriously. He calls Frank Marchetti for a follow-up interview. Frank is evasive. He cannot produce his daughter's death certificate.
27. **The Second Visit** -- Ruiz returns to Frank's apartment with a warrant. Frank meets him at the door, calm, unsurprised. He hands over a folder. Inside is Elena's death certificate, certified and sealed.
28. **The Bedroom** -- While Frank makes coffee, Ruiz glances down the hallway. He sees a door that was open before, now closed. He asks about it. Frank says it is Elena's room, preserved exactly as she left it.
29. **The Piano Bench** -- Ruiz asks to see Elena's room. Frank reluctantly opens the door. It is a young woman's bedroom, frozen in time. Ruiz notices the piano bench has recent dust patterns, as if someone sat there in the last day or two.
30. **The Photograph** -- Ruiz takes out the photo Zoe captured. He shows Frank the woman in red. Frank's face goes pale. He says, "That's not my daughter." He walks to a drawer and pulls out an old photograph. Elena had a mole on her left cheek. The woman in the window does not.

### PART FOUR: THE OTHER WOMAN (Chapters 31-40)

31. **The Sister** -- Ruiz investigates deeper. Elena Marchetti had a younger half-sister, Carla, who was put up for adoption as an infant. Carla Marchetti would be thirty-nine now. No current address on file.
32. **The Adoption File** -- Ruiz gets a court order for the sealed adoption records. The agency has no record of Carla after age eighteen. She vanished from the system in 2005.
33. **The Connection** -- James calls Zoe with a new discovery. Carla Marchetti studied photography at the same university Zoe attended, overlapping by one semester. James sends a class photo. Zoe scans the faces. She does not recognize Carla.
34. **The Memory** -- Zoe lies awake. She digs out her old university albums. She finds a photograph of herself at a gallery opening, twenty-two years old, smiling. In the background, a woman with gray eyes watches her. The woman in the window.
35. **The Fear** -- Zoe calls Dr. Thorne in a panic. The therapist tries to calm her. Zoe realizes the woman has been following her for twenty years, and she never knew. Her agoraphobia was not random -- it was triggered.
36. **The Trigger** -- Zoe remembers. Three weeks before her first panic attack, she was nearly hit by a car on Pacific Coast Highway. The car that almost killed her was a red sedan. The same stretch of road where Elena Marchetti died.
37. **The Podcaster** -- James publishes an episode about the case. Within hours, a woman named Carla Marchetti calls the tip line. She says she has been hiding for twenty years. She is the woman in the window.
38. **The Confession** -- Carla, on speakerphone with Ruiz and James present, tells her story. Elena did not die in the accident. Elena switched identities with a homeless woman who had no family, paid her to take her car that night, and disappeared. The body was not Elena.
39. **The Motive** -- Carla explains: Elena was not running from the law. She was running from Frank, their father. Frank was abusive, controlling, obsessed with keeping Elena dependent on him. Elena faked her death to escape. Carla was the only one who knew.
40. **The Trap** -- Carla tells Ruiz that Frank found her two months ago. He has been keeping her in the apartment across from Zoe's building. She tried to escape and he attacked her. Zoe saw the attack. Frank staged the clean apartment afterward. He thought the old woman who lived across the courtyard was blind.

### PART FIVE: THE EXPOSURE (Chapters 41-50)

41. **The Warrant** -- Ruiz obtains an arrest warrant for Frank Marchetti. But Frank has vanished. His apartment is empty. The piano bench is bare. The bedroom has been stripped.
42. **The Underground** -- James finds a clue in Frank's financial records: he owns a storage unit on the edge of town. Ruiz obtains a search warrant and finds a second apartment, fully furnished, hidden inside the storage facility. A prison.
43. **The Prisoner** -- Ruiz enters the hidden room. It is equipped with a bed, a bathroom, and a wall of photographs. Every image is of Carla, at different ages, going back twenty years. Frank has been watching her her entire life.
44. **The Darkroom** -- Zoe, unable to leave her apartment, realizes she may have captured more than she knows. She develops every roll she has shot in the past month. In the background of a street scene, she finds Frank's face, watching her building from a parked car.
45. **The Photograph Album** -- Zoe finds an old album in her storage. She and Carla were at the same gallery opening. They were photographed together in a group shot. They met once, briefly, and never knew they would be connected.
46. **The Hunt** -- Ruiz issues a city-wide BOLO for Frank. Traffic cameras catch his car leaving town. Ruiz coordinates with highway patrol. Frank is heading north toward the mountains.
47. **The Cabin** -- State troopers locate Frank's cabin in the Sierra foothills. Frank is inside with a gun. Ruiz negotiates by phone for three hours. Frank surrenders when Ruiz promises that Carla will not testify against him.
48. **The Trial** -- Frank is charged with kidnapping, false imprisonment, and assault. Carla testifies anyway, knowing Frank will never be free. Zoe watches the trial through a live stream in her apartment.
49. **The Rooftop** -- For the first time in twenty-two years, Zoe leaves her apartment. She goes to the rooftop of her building. She takes a photograph of the sky. It is the first time she has framed something beyond her window.
50. **The New Frame** -- Zoe begins a new project. Portraits of people who survive. Carla sits for her. Ruiz sits for her. James sits for her. Zoe's camera, once a tool of observation from a distance, becomes a way of finally being present. She keeps the window open.
