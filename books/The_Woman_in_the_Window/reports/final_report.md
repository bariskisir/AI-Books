## Summary

Zoe Cross, an agoraphobic photographer living alone in a rundown apartment building, witnesses a violent murder through her telephoto lens. She calls the police, but when Detective Marco Ruiz arrives, there is no body, no evidence, and no sign of a struggle. Worse still, the woman in Zoe's photographs matches the database entry for Elena Marchetti -- a woman who died in a car accident twenty years ago.

The investigation unravels a dark family history. Frank Marchetti, Elena's father, has been keeping multiple women captive for decades, replacing them when necessary, all while maintaining the fiction that his daughter died years ago. Zoe, trapped in her apartment by agoraphobia triggered by a near-miss on the same highway where Elena supposedly died, becomes the key witness in bringing Frank to justice.

The novel explores themes of isolation, surveillance, family trauma, and the power of bearing witness. Zoe's camera, once a tool of observation from a safe distance, becomes a weapon against injustice and a bridge back to the world.
