# The Salt Wife

**Genre:** Folk Horror  
**Author:** Barış Kısır  
**Language:** English  
**Status:** Complete (txt, epub, cover)

## Premise

Dr. Elara Morrow, a folklorist from the University of Glasgow, arrives on the remote Hebridean island of Stiorra to document its oral traditions. She learns of the "salt wives" — women who, according to local legend, married the sea itself. What she dismisses as metaphor proves to be a living, repeating tradition. The sea has a bride-price. And it is always collecting.

## Characters

- **Dr. Elara Morrow** — A folklorist who becomes the witness to an ancient tradition
- **Morag MacLeod** — The elder marked by a refusal the sea has not forgotten
- **Catriona MacLeod** — The next salt wife, chosen before she was born
- **Iain MacKenzie** — The ferryman who knows when to stay silent
- **Father Thomas** — The priest who keeps a tide clock on his church

## Content Warning

Contains themes of ritual sacrifice, maritime folklore, psychological horror, and community complicity. No explicit sexual content or graphic violence.
