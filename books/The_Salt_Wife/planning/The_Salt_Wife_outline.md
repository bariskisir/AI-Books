# The Salt Wife — Outline

## Premise

Dr. Elara Morrow, a folklorist from the University of Glasgow, arrives on the remote Hebridean island of Stiorra to document its oral traditions. She learns of the "salt wives" — women who, according to local legend, married the sea itself. What she dismisses as metaphor proves to be a living, repeating tradition. The sea has a bride-price. And it is always collecting.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Elara Morrow** — Folklorist, 34, rational, driven by academic ambition, increasingly unmoored
- **Morag MacLeod** — Elder of Stiorra, 78, keeper of the island's truths, speaks in riddles
- **The Sea Husband** — The entity beneath the waves. It courts. It claims.
- **Iain MacKenzie** — Ferryman, 52, weathered, knows when to stay silent
- **Catriona** — A girl of fourteen, the next salt wife
- **Father Thomas** — Island priest, 60, tries to reconcile faith with older truths

## Chapter Plan

### ACT ONE: THE ARRIVAL

1. **The Crossing** — Dr. Morrow takes the ferry to Stiorra. Sea mist. Iain speaks little. The island appears like a dark tooth.

2. **The Cottage** — She settles into her assigned croft. Salt film on every window. The fireplace burns kelp.

3. **First Night** — Sleep is broken by a sound: low singing beneath the wind. She tells herself it is the tide.

4. **Morag** — Introduction to the elder. Morag's hands are webbed with scars. She speaks of the sea as a sentient thing.

5. **The Story** — Morag tells the legend of the first salt wife: a girl named Mhairi who walked into the water in 1743.

6. **The Shore** — Morrow visits the strand. Black sand. White salt crust. She finds a dress, barnacle-encrusted, spread on a rock.

7. **Priest's Warning** — Father Thomas warns her away from "sea superstitions." His church has a tide clock instead of a crucifix.

8. **The Tapestry** — In the village hall, a woven tapestry shows women processing into the sea. Seven panels. One empty.

9. **Catriona** — A girl watches Morrow from a distance. Her mother pulls her inside. The girl's dress is the same fabric as the one on the shore.

10. **Archives** — Morrow examines parish records. Every fifty years, a young woman vanishes in June. The cause: "drowned."

11. **The Cairn** — A pile of stones above the beach. Each stone has a woman's name. Morag says it counts them.

12. **Village Silence** — The islanders stop speaking when she approaches. She feels their attention like a weight.

13. **Dreams** — She dreams of cold water, a hand around her ankle, a voice asking for a name.

14. **The Offering** — On the beach at dawn, she finds a small clay figure of a woman, half-dissolved in brine.

15. **Iain's Truth** — Iain the ferryman tells her: "You should not have come. The sea noticed."

### ACT TWO: THE COURTING

16. **Morag's Confession** — Morag admits she was a salt wife. The sea rejected her. That is why she still lives.

17. **The Marks** — Morag shows the scars on her hands. Salt crystals embedded under the skin. They glow in moonlight.

18. **The Pattern** — Morrow maps the vanishings. 1743, 1793, 1843, 1893, 1943, 1993. Sixty years between. The next is now.

19. **Catriona's Fear** — The girl confesses she has been chosen. She finds salt in her bed. Her skin tastes of the sea.

20. **The Bride Clothes** — The dress on the shore is Catriona's. The sea has already claimed her in ritual. The physical follows.

21. **The Gifts** — Shells appear on Catriona's windowsill. Perfect spirals. A seal carcass on the strand. A wedding ring made of braided kelp.

22. **Morrow's Research** — She finds accounts in Gaelic poetry. The Sea Husband chooses his bride by tasting her tears.

23. **The Choosing** — Morag explains the process: the sea sends signs. The girl accepts or refuses. Refusal means the island starves.

24. **Storm** — A storm isolates the island. No ferry for a week. Morrow is trapped.

25. **The Phone Lines** — They go dead. The radio crackles with only static. The island is cut off.

26. **Father Thomas's Ritual** — He performs an exorcism of the sea. The tide rises higher than it should. It does not recede for hours.

27. **The Beach at Night** — Morrow walks the strand. She sees lights beneath the surface. A shape. Something that paces below.

28. **Catriona's Poem** — The girl writes a poem about a bride who lives in a house of water. Her mother burns it.

29. **The Mark** — Morrow wakes with a line of salt on her wrist. Like a bracelet. It will not wash off.

30. **Morag's History** — Morag was chosen in 1963. She cut her own hands to make the sea reject her blood. It worked, but left a debt.

31. **The Debt** — The sea requires a bride. Morag's refusal meant the island paid in other ways. Three fishing boats lost. Eight men.

32. **Morrow's Guilt** — She realizes she is not an observer. Her presence has accelerated the timeline.

33. **The Proposal** — A wave deposits a ring of braided kelp at Morrow's feet. The sea has chosen a second bride.

34. **The Weeping** — Catriona weeps. Her tears are salt. They crystallize on her cheeks. The sea tastes her.

35. **The Mothers** — The island mothers gather. They speak of what must be done. Morrow is not welcome at the meeting.

### ACT THREE: THE SALT WIFE

36. **The Decision** — The elders decide the wedding will happen at the next full moon. Catriona will walk into the sea.

37. **Morrow's Plan** — She will document the ritual, then use the evidence to stop it. She believes reason can defeat superstition.

38. **The Dress** — Catriona is dressed in white linen. Her hair is braided with bladderwrack. She is calm. She has accepted.

39. **The Procession** — The village walks to the strand at midnight. Torches. Singing. The words are in Gaelic. Morrow follows.

40. **The Witness** — Morrow watches Catriona enter the water. The sea takes her gently. She does not struggle. She is gone.

41. **Aftermath** — The village returns. The torches die. Morrow stands on the beach alone. The sea is still.

42. **The Shift** — Morag tells her: "You saw. Now you are part of it." The island has a new witness. The next salt wife in sixty years.

43. **The Fever** — Morrow falls ill. Her skin salt-crusted. She dreams of Catriona laughing beneath the waves.

44. **Morag's Vigil** — Morag nurses her. Explains that witnessing means you inherit the knowledge. The sea keeps its records in living minds.

45. **The Return of Iain** — The ferry returns. Morrow can leave. She packs her notes. She stands at the dock.

46. **The Choice** — She cannot leave. Her research is incomplete. She tells herself this lie. She knows the truth: the sea has marked her.

47. **The Manuscript** — She writes her account. Sends it to Glasgow by the ferry. The pages describe everything. No one will believe them.

48. **The Last Chapter of the Book** — She realizes she is writing not a folklore collection but a witness statement. She is the chronicler of the salt wives. The sea's archivist.

49. **The New Vigil** — She stays on Stiorra. The island accepts her. She is not a salt wife. She is a salt witness. Her duty is to remember.

50. **The Tide Turns** — She walks the beach at dawn. The sea is calm. She touches the salt line on her wrist. It has faded. But it is still there. She will be here when the next bride is chosen. Sixty years. She will wait.
