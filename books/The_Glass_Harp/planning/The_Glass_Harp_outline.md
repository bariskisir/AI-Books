# The Glass Harp - Outline

## Premise

When the opening rehearsal at the new Aster Hall ends with a suspended glass acoustic canopy shattering over the stage, acoustic consultant Dr. Livia Senn is asked to prove the disaster was a freak resonance event before the hall's wealthy patrons lose their naming gala. Her measurements show the room did not merely fail; it was tuned to fail at one precise note. With a stage manager, a materials engineer, and a skeptical city inspector, Livia follows hidden frequencies through forged glass certificates, donor vanity, and a composer whose final score was altered to make the building confess.

## Core Cast

- Dr. Livia Senn - Adult acoustic consultant who maps rooms by listening to what others dismiss as echo.
- Marco Vey - Adult stage manager at Aster Hall, practical, exhausted, and protective of his crew.
- Dr. Anika Ro - Adult materials engineer who tests glass, resin, and stress fractures without theatrical language.
- Jasper Cale - Adult star architect whose concert hall is his public monument.
- Mirelle Ardent - Adult patron and foundation chair who wants the opening gala saved at any cost.
- Tomas Ivo - Adult composer whose new piece contains a dangerous sustained note.
- Pearl Ivo - Adult cellist and Tomas's estranged wife, present at the rehearsal and unwilling to lie about the score.
- Declan Shore - Adult city inspector whose earlier warnings were buried.
- Sima Verret - Adult glass fabricator whose signature appears on certificates she did not sign.
- Anton Reeve - Adult acoustics subcontractor who vanished after final tuning.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Livia hears a wrong echo in Aster Hall before anyone tells her where the glass fell.
2. Marco describes the rehearsal and the note that made the canopy tremble.
3. Mirelle pressures the team to call the accident unforeseeable before the gala.
4. Anika begins fracture testing and finds two kinds of glass in one shattered panel.
5. Livia maps the hall's response and discovers a narrow spike at the score's longest pitch.
6. Declan reveals his inspection memo on canopy stress was removed from the permit file.
7. Pearl gives Livia the original score, which differs from the parts used at rehearsal.
8. Jasper insists his design was compromised by subcontractors and donor changes.
9. Marco finds a tuning laptop hidden behind the stage shell.
10. Livia recovers files showing the hall was equalized after city acceptance.
11. Sima denies signing the final glass certificates and names a cheaper supplier.
12. Anika identifies resin that cannot survive the canopy's designed load.
13. Tomas admits he changed one passage after receiving anonymous acoustic notes.
14. Mirelle threatens to cancel Livia's contract unless the gala proceeds.
15. Declan gives Livia photographs of unauthorized night work.
16. Livia and Marco test the empty hall and make the glass railings hum.
17. Anton Reeve is found in a hotel under another name and refuses to discuss the tuning files.
18. Pearl remembers a rehearsal instruction that isolated the musicians under the canopy.
19. Anika links the cheaper glass to a foundation board member's investment.
20. Livia realizes the score was used as an ignition source for resonance, not as art.
21. The team builds a frequency map tying architecture, score, and materials together.
22. Jasper admits he allowed a last-minute canopy redesign to satisfy sightlines.
23. Mirelle announces a reduced gala, forcing the investigation into a public clock.
24. Marco discovers missing rigging reports in the stage manager's archive.
25. Sima produces an email chain proving her refusal was edited into approval.
26. Livia finds a hidden speaker array used to mask early rattles during demonstrations.
27. Anton claims he was paid only to hide a flaw, not to create danger.
28. Pearl and Tomas reconstruct the altered score from marked parts.
29. Anika proves the fracture began before rehearsal night.
30. Declan obtains a warrant for Jasper's model room.
31. The model room contains scaled glass pieces tuned to the same dangerous pitch.
32. Livia learns Mirelle demanded the hall open before a bond deadline.
33. Marco's crew discovers a false maintenance log for canopy bolts.
34. Jasper blames Anton, but Livia finds Jasper's handwritten frequency notes.
35. A gala sound check nearly triggers another failure in the balcony glass.
36. Livia stops the event publicly by playing the dangerous tone into the room.
37. Mirelle tries to frame Tomas as a reckless composer seeking notoriety.
38. Pearl reveals Tomas removed the note until someone restored it in the parts.
39. Anika traces the altered parts to the foundation print office.
40. Declan reopens the occupancy approval under emergency authority.
41. Livia confronts Mirelle with the bond deadline and cheap glass investment.
42. Marco testifies that staff reported rattles before opening week.
43. Anton gives up the account that paid him to retune measurements.
44. Jasper admits designing the spike but denies knowing the glass substitution.
45. The team proves the substitution made the architectural flourish lethal.
46. Mirelle attempts to destroy the model-room notes during a board meeting.
47. Livia uses the room itself as evidence in a controlled demonstration.
48. The gala is cancelled and the bond fraud referral begins.
49. Pearl performs the restored score in a safe rehearsal room for the injured crew.
50. Aster Hall is stripped of its glass canopy, and Livia hears the room's honest echo.
