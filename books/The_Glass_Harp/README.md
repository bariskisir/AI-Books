# The Glass Harp

## Overview

Dr. Livia Senn is called to Aster Hall after a glass acoustic canopy breaks during the opening rehearsal. The foundation wants a quick finding of unforeseeable resonance, but Livia hears a narrow frequency spike that matches one altered note in the new commission. With stage manager Marco Vey, materials engineer Anika Ro, and city inspector Declan Shore, Livia traces the failure through substituted glass, removed inspection memos, hidden speaker presets, and a gala deadline tied to public bonds.

**Genre:** Acoustic Architecture Mystery / Concert Hall Suspense

## Story Engines

A new concert hall promises civic beauty until its glass canopy fails under a note the room has been tuned to flatter. The investigation turns acoustics, materials engineering, stage records, and score revisions into one chain of evidence.

## Key Themes

- Beauty becomes dangerous when it is protected from testing.
- Technical warnings matter most before public failure.
- Music should not be made to carry institutional blame.
- A room's honesty depends on the people willing to hear its flaws.

## Characters
