# The Last Job — Outline

## Premise

Frank Decker was the best safecracker on the Eastern Seaboard until he walked away from the life thirty years ago. Now he lives in a furnished room above a bar in Queens, paying his tab with stories about the old days. When Tommy Moretti shows up with a photograph and a name, Frank learns that the man who stole his wife—Victor Strand—has built a fortress tower on the Manhattan waterfront. Inside that tower is a private vault holding the evidence that ruined Frank's life: a falsified deed, a doctored photograph, and the necklace Frank gave Clara on their wedding day. Strand is dying. The vault comes down when he does. Frank agrees to one last job: crack the vault, take back what was stolen, and bury the past before it buries him.

## Core Cast

- **Frank Decker** — Aging safecracker, sixty-eight years old, steady hands gone arthritic, three decades of regret packed into a cheap suit.
- **Tommy Moretti** — Frank's former partner, seventy-two, sharp as a tack, with one bad lung and a gift for reconnaissance. He never stopped working the edges of the underworld.
- **Victor Strand** — The Mark. A real-estate mogul who built an empire on stolen land and stolen women. He took Clara from Frank and never looked back. Now he is dying of cancer in a penthouse overlooking the city he bought.
- **Clara Decker Strand** — Frank's ex-wife, Victor's wife. She chose money over love and has spent thirty years regretting it from inside a gilded cage.
- **Rosa Mendez** — Victor's head of security, a former Cuban intelligence officer with her own reasons to want the vault open.
- **Leo "The Ghost" Farnetti** — Retired forger, the only living man who can reproduce the 1970s banking seals they need.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### PART I — THE CALL (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Offer** — Frank sits in his usual booth at O'Malley's when Tommy walks in after fourteen years of silence. Tommy slides a photograph across the table: Victor Strand's tower, rising above the Hudson.
2. **Old Bones** — Frank's hands shake as he lights a cigarette. Tommy explains the job: Strand is dying, the vault is scheduled for demolition, and everything Frank lost is inside.
3. **The Name on the Paper** — Tommy lays out the take: proof that Strand stole the deeds to Frank's family land, the forged evidence that sent an innocent man to prison, and Clara's necklace. Frank says no.
4. **The Night She Left** — Flashback to 1995. Clara stands in the doorway with a suitcase. Victor Strand waits in a black car at the curb. Frank's hands are bleeding from pounding on the safe he cannot open fast enough.
5. **The Pictures** — Tommy produces surveillance photos. The vault is a Sentry Mark IV — Frank's old specialty. He is the only man alive who can open one quietly.
6. **The Cost of No** — Tommy explains what happens if the vault is demolished: the evidence against a sitting congressman goes public, three innocent families are destroyed, and Clara's necklace ends up in a landfill.
7. **The Machine** — Frank digs his old tool case out of storage. The stethoscope is cracked. The dial-spinner is rusted. He spends the night restoring them.
8. **The Tower** — Frank and Tommy stake out the Strand Building. The target is a private vault on the forty-eighth floor. Security is layered: biometric, thermal, and old-fashioned steel.
9. **The Weak Spot** — Rosa Mendez, Strand's security chief, approaches them in a diner across the street. She has been watching them watch the building. She wants in.
10. **The Decision** — Frank agrees to the job. Rosa will disable the electronic security. Frank will crack the vault. Tommy will run the extraction. They have seventy-two hours to plan.

### PART II — THE ASSEMBLY (Chapters 11-20)

11. **The Forger** — They drive to New Haven to recruit Leo Farnetti, the Ghost. Leo needs a new set of banking credentials to get them past the building's document scanner. He agrees for old times' sake.
12. **The Floor Plan** — Rosa delivers the building schematics. The vault room is a box within a box — steel walls, motion sensors, a pressure floor. The only way in is through the door.
13. **The Practice Lock** — Frank visits a salvage yard where a retired Sentry Mark IV door sits rusting in the weeds. He spends the afternoon with his tools, relearning the music of the tumblers.
14. **The Crew Dinner** — Frank, Tommy, Leo, and Rosa meet in a back room at Carmine's. They talk about old jobs, old scars. Frank realizes he is not alone anymore.
15. **The Ghost's Price** — Leo demands twenty thousand dollars cash for the forged credentials. Frank does not have it. Tommy calls in a debt from a bookie in Brighton Beach.
16. **The Wife** — Clara calls Frank from a blocked number. She knows about the job. She begs him to stay away. Strand has private detectives everywhere.
17. **The Warning** — Frank meets Clara in a museum cafe. She looks older, thinner. She tells him Strand has a failsafe — a dead man's switch that will incinerate the vault's contents if the door is forced.
18. **The Failsafe** — Frank brings the news to Rosa. She already knew. She has been working on a bypass for six months. The countermeasure requires a biometric override — Strand's thumbprint.
19. **The Plan** — Rosa's plan: Tommy gets the thumbprint from Strand's doctor's office. Frank waits for the signal. The vault opens, Frank takes the contents, and they are gone before the failsafe arms.
20. **The Doctor** — Tommy locates Strand's oncologist. The doctor keeps a digital file with Strand's biometric data for the clinical trial. Tommy pays a nurse five hundred dollars for a copy.

### PART III — THE JOB (Chapters 21-35)

21. **The Night Before** — Frank cannot sleep. He sits in the dark with his tools laid out on the table, each one a memory. He thinks about Clara. He thinks about what comes after.
22. **The Approach** — The crew enters the Strand Building at midnight. Rosa's guard rotation puts them in the service elevator. The climb to forty-eight takes three minutes that feel like three hours.
23. **The Corridor** — The hallway to the vault is empty. Too empty. Rosa's people have pulled the night shift, but Frank feels eyes on him.
24. **The Door** — Frank faces the Sentry Mark IV. It is pristine, oiled, maintained. He fits his stethoscope to the dial and begins the long listen.
25. **The First Tumbler** — Frank finds the first contact point at seventeen. Sweat runs down his neck. His hands are steady for the first time in years.
26. **The Second Tumbler** — The lock resists. Frank adjusts his angle. The tumbler drops at thirty-one. Tommy radios that a guard is making rounds.
27. **The Third Tumbler** — Rosa reroutes the guard. Frank digs deeper into the lock. The third tumbler gives at forty-four. He is halfway home.
28. **The Fourth Tumbler** — A false gate. Frank almost misses it. His hands cramp. He drinks water and starts again.
29. **The Fifth Tumbler** — Frank feels the feedback change. The lock is warmer here. He pictures the mechanism inside — every gear, every pin. The tumbler drops at nine.
30. **The Final Tumbler** — Frank closes his eyes. He listens to the lock breathe. The last tumbler falls at fifty-three. The handle is live.
31. **The Open** — Frank pulls the handle. The vault door swings open on silent hinges. Inside: a small room with a single steel shelf holding three items.
32. **The Contents** — The deed. The photograph. The necklace. Frank takes all three. His hands are shaking again.
33. **The Alarm** — A siren screams. The failsafe was not where Rosa expected it. The vault room begins to seal itself. Steel shutters drop over the doorway.
34. **The Escape** — Rosa hits the shutters with an EMP grenade. They fall back through the smoke. Tommy is hit in the shoulder. Frank drags him into the elevator.
35. **The Aftermath** — The crew escapes into the night. Tommy is bleeding badly. Frank drives with one hand pressed to his friend's wound.

### PART IV — THE RECKONING (Chapters 36-45)

36. **The Safe House** — Rosa knows a medic in Jersey City. Tommy is patched up. The bullet went clean through. They hide in a basement apartment.
37. **The Evidence** — Frank spreads the contents of the vault on a card table. The deed is real. The photograph proves Strand framed an innocent man. The necklace — Clara's necklace — still glows amber in the dim light.
38. **The Call** — Strand calls Frank's cell phone. He knows everything. He offers Frank a trade: return the contents, and he will let the crew walk.
39. **The Refusal** — Frank refuses. Strand tells him Clara will pay the price. The line goes dead.
40. **The Trap** — Frank realizes Strand never intended to keep the vault secure. He wanted it opened. The contents are bait in a larger game.
41. **The Frame** — Leo deciphers the documents fully. The deed is a forgery. The photograph is a composite. The evidence chain implicates Frank, not Strand. It was a setup from the start.
42. **The Turn** — Rosa admits she knew. She was working for the FBI. They have been building a case against Strand for years, and they needed someone inside the vault to trigger the evidentiary chain.
43. **The Truth** — Frank confronts Rosa. She tells him Clara was the informant. Clara has been working with the FBI since she married Strand.
44. **The Meeting** — Clara meets Frank in the basement. She tells him everything: the years of pretending, the evidence she gathered, the price she paid.
45. **The Deal** — The FBI gives Frank immunity in exchange for the vault contents. Tommy is treated. Rosa stays in place. The case against Strand goes to trial.

### PART V — THE LAST THING (Chapters 46-50)

46. **The Trial** — Frank testifies. The courtroom is cold. Strand watches from the defendant's table with dead eyes.
47. **The Verdict** — Strand is convicted on twelve counts of fraud, racketeering, and conspiracy. He is sentenced to twenty-five years. Clara watches from the gallery.
48. **The Return** — Frank goes back to Queens. O'Malley's is still there. His usual booth is empty. Tommy is recovering. Life has settled into a new quiet.
49. **The Necklace** — Frank gives the necklace to Clara in a park overlooking the river. She does not take it back. She tells him some things cannot be unfractured.
50. **The Last Listen** — Frank sits alone in his room. He holds the necklace one last time, then wraps it in cloth and puts it in a drawer. He closes his tool case. Some doors stay shut.
