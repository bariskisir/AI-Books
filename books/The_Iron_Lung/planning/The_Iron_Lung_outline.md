# The Iron Lung — Outline

## Premise

Polio summer of 1952. Nurse Elinor Chase tends the iron lung ward at Saint Catherine's Hospital, a room of breathing machines and paralyzed bodies. When Dr. Henry Keller arrives with a secret experimental treatment and promises of a cure, he disrupts the careful rhythm of the ward. Young Tommy Beldon, twelve years old and trapped in a machine, becomes the test case. Elinor must decide whether to trust a man who sees patients as data points or cling to the slow, grim work of keeping them alive one breath at a time.

## Core Cast

- **Elinor Chase** — Ward nurse, thirty-two. She has been on the polio ward for four years. She knows the iron lungs by their individual rhythms. She does not believe in miracles.
- **Dr. Henry Keller** — Young physician from a Boston research hospital. He carries a satchel of unlabeled vials and a letter of introduction that raises questions. His previous trial was shut down.
- **Tommy Beldon** — Twelve years old. Polio took his legs and his diaphragm. He lives in an iron lung at the back of the ward. He communicates with blinks and a letter board.
- **Sister Margaret Burke** — Head nurse, fifty-eight. She ran a field hospital in France during the war. No doctor intimidates her.
- **Dr. William Chen** — Staff physician, forty-five. He oversees the ward day to day. He has seen six experimental treatments fail.
- **Ruth Beldon** — Tommy's mother. She visits every afternoon. She is running out of hope.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Ward (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Breathing Room** — Elinor starts her shift in the iron lung ward. She checks each patient, listens to the machines, knows them all by name.
2. **Rounds** — Dr. Chen makes morning rounds. A new patient arrives: a girl named Maria who cannot breathe on her own.
3. **Tommy** — Elinor works with Tommy on his letter board. He asks for a window. She moves his bed so he can see outside.
4. **The Man in the Suit** — A stranger appears on the ward. Dr. Keller arrives with a letter from the state health board.
5. **Permission** — Sister Margaret reviews Keller's credentials. Something is missing. She refuses him access to patients.
6. **The Old Ways** — Elinor aspirates Maria's airway. A moment of crisis passes. The ward settles back into its rhythm.
7. **Keller's Pitch** — Keller corners Dr. Chen in the cafeteria. He claims to have a serum that reverses paralysis. Chen is skeptical.
8. **Ruth** — Tommy's mother arrives. She watches her son breathe by machine. She has heard about Keller. She wants to meet him.
9. **The Call** — A phone call to the hospital where Keller claims to have worked. The records are sealed. Sister Margaret grows suspicious.
10. **First Crack** — Ruth Beldon demands a meeting with Keller. Elinor tries to intervene. The administration overrules her.

### Part II: The Experiment (Chapters 11–20)

11. **Permission Granted** — The hospital board approves a limited trial. Tommy is the candidate. Elinor objects.
12. **The First Injection** — Keller administers his serum. Tommy's vitals spike. The ward holds its breath.
13. **Waiting** — Twenty-four hours pass. Tommy shows no improvement. Keller insists the treatment needs time.
14. **Side Effects** — Tommy develops a fever. Elinor stays by his bedside through the night. Keller does not come.
15. **Sister Margaret's Investigation** — Sister Margaret calls the state medical board. They have no record of Keller's license in this state.
16. **The Second Dose** — Keller pushes for a second injection. Chen demands data. Keller produces a single case study from a defunct hospital.
17. **Crisis** — Tommy seizes in the night. Elinor stabilizes him. She tells Keller he cannot touch her patient again.
18. **The Confrontation** — Elinor and Keller face off in the staff room. He admits his Boston trial was shut down after a death.
19. **Ruth's Choice** — Tommy's mother must decide: continue the treatment or stop. She asks Tommy. He blinks once for yes.
20. **The Third Injection** — Tommy receives the last dose. His fever breaks. He moves one finger.

### Part III: The Waiting (Chapters 21–30)

21. **A Breath** — Tommy's diaphragm twitches. A small, independent breath. The first in eight weeks.
22. **Fragile Hope** — Elinor charts the progress. The improvement is real but slight. Keller claims vindication.
23. **The Second Patient** — The board authorizes treatment for two more patients. Maria is among them.
24. **Maria's Turn** — Elinor prepares Maria for the injection. She sees the fear in the girl's eyes.
25. **Compound Questions** — Dr. Chen analyzes Keller's serum. He cannot identify all the compounds. One looks familiar, and dangerous.
26. **Maria Responds** — Maria improves faster than Tommy. Keller cannot explain why.
27. **The Letter** — A letter arrives from the Boston hospital. It contains the full report on Keller's previous trial. Three patients died.
28. **Reading the Report** — Elinor reads the report at home. The patients died of cardiac arrest after the second dose. Just like Tommy almost did.
29. **The Board Meeting** — The hospital board convenes. Sister Margaret presents the evidence. Keller is asked to leave.
30. **Before He Goes** — Keller tells Elinor that the serum works but needs refinement. He offers her the formula. She refuses.

### Part IV: The Fallout (Chapters 31–40)

31. **Aftermath** — Keller is gone. Tommy can breathe for thirty seconds without the machine. Maria is weaning off the lung.
32. **The Formula** — Chen decodes the remaining compounds. One is a known toxin in high doses. The serum was poison.
33. **The Children** — Tommy asks about Keller. Elinor says he left. Tommy says he knew the injections hurt.
34. **Recovery** — Tommy progresses to five minutes off the lung. Maria is moved to a regular bed.
35. **The Investigation** — State authorities arrive. Elinor gives her statement. They ask why she did not stop it sooner.
36. **Blame** — Sister Margaret takes responsibility. The hospital board accepts her resignation. Elinor cannot stop it.
37. **New Leadership** — A new head nurse arrives. She changes the ward routines. The iron lungs sound different.
38. **Tommy's Courage** — Tommy tells Elinor he is not afraid of dying. He is afraid of never seeing outside again.
39. **Maria Leaves** — Maria is discharged. She walks out of the hospital on crutches. The ward celebrates.
40. **The Empty Bed** — Maria's iron lung sits empty. Elinor feels the weight of the machines.

### Part V: The Going On (Chapters 41–50)

41. **Winter** — The polio season ends. Fewer patients arrive. The ward grows quiet.
42. **Sister Margaret's Farewell** — Sister Margaret visits one last time. She gives Elinor her nursing pins. She is moving to a mission.
43. **The New Normal** — Elinor trains the new nurses. She shows them the machines. She teaches them to listen.
44. **Tommy's Measured Breath** — Tommy can breathe on his own for fifteen minutes now. The iron lung is his cage and his cradle.
45. **The Letter from Keller** — A postcard from California. Keller is at another hospital. Elinor burns it.
46. **The Next Summer** — A new polio season begins. The Salk vaccine trials are starting. There is hope.
47. **The Vaccine News** — The radio announces the vaccine is effective. The ward erupts in applause. Elinor cries.
48. **Tommy's Question** — Tommy asks if the vaccine means he will walk again. Elinor cannot answer.
49. **The Long Goodbye** — Tommy is transferred to a rehabilitation hospital. He can breathe on his own for an hour.
50. **The Iron Lung** — Elinor stands in the empty ward. The machines are silent. She hears them anyway.
