# The Upper Room — Outline

## Premise

Sarah and her twelve-year-old son Leo move into a pre-war apartment building in Boston's Back Bay, hoping for a fresh start after a difficult divorce. The rent is impossibly low for a three-bedroom in a doorman building, and the reason emerges slowly: no one stays long in 4B. The elderly upstairs neighbor, a woman named Mrs. Pendleton who died in 1952, still occupies the apartment above them. She walks the hallway at night, plays records on a Victrola no one owns, and she has been waiting a long time for someone to notice her. As Sarah and Leo settle in, the presence in the upper room begins to make itself known — not with malice, but with an aching, lonely persistence that forces them to confront what it means to be seen, remembered, and finally released.

## Core Cast

- **Sarah Vasquez** — mother, mid-thirties, graphic designer, pragmatic but sensitive, newly single
- **Leo Vasquez** — her son, twelve, observant and quiet, feels things before he understands them
- **Mrs. Pendleton** — ghost of a woman who died in apartment 5B in 1952, lonely, formal, desperate for connection

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. **The Key** — Sarah turns the key in the lock of 4B and feels the dead bolt give with a sound like a held breath.
2. **Back Bay, November** — First night in the new apartment; Leo counts the sirens outside his window.
3. **The Rent** — Sarah explains to her sister on the phone why the rent is suspiciously low.
4. **The Super's Warning** — The super, Mr. Kowalski, tells Sarah the previous tenants left suddenly.
5. **Footsteps at Midnight** — Leo hears footsteps overhead at 12:47 AM, steady and unhurried.
6. **Empty Upstairs** — Sarah checks the fifth-floor hallway; apartment 5B has been sealed for decades.
7. **The Photograph** — Leo finds a sepia photograph wedged behind a radiator grate.
8. **The Name on the Back** — "Eleanor Pendleton, 1947" is written on the photograph in careful script.
9. **Victrola Music** — Sarah wakes to the sound of a woman singing "I'll Be Seeing You" from above.
10. **The Management Office** — Sarah visits the management office; the file on 5B is missing.
11. **The Security Deposit** — What the previous tenant wrote in the margin of the move-out form.
12. **Mrs. Pendleton's Arrival** — Sarah sees a woman in a green dress standing at the top of the stairs.
13. **The Morning After** — Sarah convinces herself it was a trick of the light.
14. **Leo Knows** — Leo tells his mother he has seen the woman too, many times now.
15. **The Rules of the House** — Leo makes a list of patterns: when she appears, where she walks, what she wants.
16. **The Temperature Drop** — The apartment above them grows cold even when the boiler is running.
17. **The Scent of Lavender** — Mrs. Pendleton's perfume lingers in the hallway; Sarah recognizes it from her grandmother's generation.
18. **The Boston Public Library** — Sarah searches the library archives for Eleanor Pendleton.
19. **The Obituary** — Sarah finds a notice from 1952: "Eleanor Pendleton, 53, of Commonwealth Avenue, survived by no one."
20. **The Funeral Home** — The funeral home records show no one claimed Mrs. Pendleton's remains.
21. **The Unopened Door** — Sarah tries the knob of 5B; it is locked. The lock is original 1920s brass.
22. **A Locksmith's Job** — Sarah hires a locksmith who opens 5B and finds it perfectly preserved.
23. **The Apartment Frozen in Time** — A 1952 newspaper on the coffee table, dishes in the sink, a half-finished letter.
24. **The Letter** — The letter is addressed to a man named Thomas, never finished, never sent.
25. **The Records** — Leo plays the Victrola record he finds in 5B; it is the same song they hear at night.
26. **Thomas** — Sarah searches for Thomas and finds a death certificate: Thomas Hale, Korean War, 1951.
27. **The Waiting** — Mrs. Pendleton has been waiting fifty-four years for a letter to arrive.
28. **Sarah Speaks** — Sarah stands in the empty hallway and speaks to Mrs. Pendleton aloud.
29. **The Light Flickers** — Mrs. Pendleton answers by flickering the hallway light three times.
30. **The Questions Begin** — Sarah starts asking yes-or-no questions; one flicker for yes, two for no.
31. **The Story Emerges** — Mrs. Pendleton tells them about Thomas, the war, the letter she never mailed.
32. **The Guilt** — Mrs. Pendleton blames herself for Thomas's death; she believed her letter would have called him home.
33. **Leo's Dream** — Leo dreams of Mrs. Pendleton standing at a mailbox in the rain, unable to let go of the envelope.
34. **The Mailbox** — Sarah and Leo find the original mailbox in the lobby; slot 5B has not been opened since 1952.
35. **The Letter Found** — They retrieve two decades of flyers and one envelope addressed to Thomas, undelivered.
36. **The Reading** — Sarah reads the letter aloud in the hallway: it says nothing Mrs. Pendleton feared.
37. **What the Letter Says** — The letter says "Come home. I love you. I will wait." That is all.
38. **The Release** — The temperature in the hallway warms by ten degrees in one minute.
39. **The Final Night** — Mrs. Pendleton appears one last time, younger, lighter, dressed in white.
40. **The Gift** — Mrs. Pendleton leaves Leo a small button from her coat as a keepsake.
41. **The Goodbye** — Mrs. Pendleton walks up the stairs and does not turn back.
42. **The Morning Silence** — No footsteps. No Victrola. The apartment above is quiet for the first time in decades.
43. **The Open Door** — Sarah opens 5B and finds it empty and dusty, an ordinary abandoned apartment.
44. **The Management Learns** — The building manager is astonished: the cold spot in the hallway is gone.
45. **The New Tenant** — A young couple signs a lease for 5B; the cycle could begin again.
46. **Leo Watches** — Leo watches the new tenants carry boxes up the stairs and wonders if they will hear anything.
47. **The Button** — Leo keeps the button in his pocket; it is still warm.
48. **The Last Visit** — Sarah feels a faint brush of lavender in the elevator and smiles.
49. **The Lease Renewal** — Sarah signs the lease renewal; she and Leo will stay.
50. **The Upper Room** — Leo looks up at the ceiling of the upper room and knows it is empty now, but he will never forget.
