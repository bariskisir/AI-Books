# The Coral Clock

## Overview

When marine archaeologist Mara Venn finds a coral-encrusted marine chronometer inside a disputed dredge channel, the official survey insists the wreck lies safely elsewhere. The reef, the stopped clock, old captain's logs, and raw coordinate files tell a different story. With help from a local diver, a coral ecologist, an archivist, and a reluctant survey technician, Mara must prove that a living reef can also be a historic archive before a marina project cuts through it.

**Genre:** Marine Archaeology Mystery / Reef Restoration Suspense
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50
**Word Count:** ~11400
**Status:** Complete with EPUB and cover

## Story Engines

A stopped marine chronometer embedded in coral turns a dredging dispute into a fight over survey truth, living evidence, and an island's debt-backed future.

## Key Themes

- Living ecosystems can preserve historical evidence.
- Public maps become dangerous when commercial alignment replaces disclosure.
- Economic need is real, but it does not make false records harmless.
- Restoration is skilled work, not decorative apology.
