# The Coral Clock - Outline

## Premise

Marine archaeologist and reef restoration lead Mara Venn is preparing a damaged island reef for legal protection when her team finds a coral-encrusted marine chronometer inside a collapsed wreck pocket. The official seabed survey says the wreck lies outside the proposed dredge channel, but the clock, old captain's logs, coral growth rings, and anchor scars indicate the survey boundary was shifted to clear a resort marina project. As Mara follows the evidence through salvage permits, living coral, forged coordinates, and an island council under pressure, she must prove that a reef can be both archive and witness before the dredges arrive.

## Core Cast

- **Mara Venn** - Adult marine archaeologist and reef restoration lead, precise underwater and impatient with ceremonial delay.
- **Tavio Cale** - Adult local diver and boat mechanic whose family knows the reef by memory and hazard.
- **Dr. Lina Sorell** - Adult coral ecologist who reads growth bands and bleaching scars like a ledger.
- **Nico Arden** - Adult resort development executive pushing the marina and dredge schedule.
- **Ada Pike** - Adult island council chair balancing jobs, debt, and public trust.
- **Emmett Rhys** - Adult salvage historian whose archive contains the missing captain's log.
- **Sela Voss** - Adult survey technician uneasy about the official seabed map.
- **Captain Orrin Vale** - Adult dredge contractor who wants written orders before cutting reef.
- **Noemi Dar** - Adult journalist from the island paper, careful with facts and fearless with names.
- **Judge Ren Ilya** - Adult maritime magistrate who hears the emergency injunction.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: Reef Archive
1. **The Pocket** - Mara finds a marine chronometer wedged in coral beside the disputed channel.
2. **Survey Line** - The official survey places the wreck safely outside the marina dredge path.
3. **Tavio's Reef** - Tavio argues the old wreck has always been inside the living reef.
4. **Growth Rings** - Lina shows coral bands that place the object in the same pocket for decades.
5. **The Developer** - Nico Arden dismisses the find as salvage clutter.
6. **Council Tide** - Ada Pike warns that the island's debt makes delay politically dangerous.
7. **Stopped Hands** - The chronometer's stopped time suggests a wreck event not in the official file.
8. **Anchor Scars** - Mara maps scars showing recent survey disturbance.
9. **Missing Log** - Emmett mentions a captain's log removed from the archive.
10. **First Marker** - Sela quietly gives Mara a raw coordinate file.

### Part II: False Channel
11. **Two Maps** - The raw coordinates and public map do not align.
12. **Blue Stakes** - Dredge stakes in the water cross living coral.
13. **Council Hearing** - Nico promises jobs and calls the reef already degraded.
14. **Night Dive** - Mara and Tavio document fresh cuts near the wreck pocket.
15. **Clockmaker's Mark** - The chronometer maker ties the wreck to a known 1891 schooner.
16. **Bleach Scar** - Lina distinguishes storm damage from mechanical scraping.
17. **Permit Language** - The dredge permit depends on the wreck being outside the channel.
18. **Sela's Warning** - Sela says the survey datum changed after client review.
19. **Archive Drawer** - Emmett finds a copy of the missing log under an old insurance index.
20. **The Wrong Reef** - The log names a reef feature that matches Mara's site, not the public map.

### Part III: Living Evidence
21. **Coral Calendar** - Growth bands confirm the chronometer was sealed before recent surveys.
22. **Tide Table** - The stopped time matches a historic storm tide in the log.
23. **Soft Boundary** - Nico's team argues reef boundaries shift and cannot halt development.
24. **Hard Stone** - Tavio finds a ballast stone with carved initials.
25. **Survey Room** - Mara confronts Sela with the datum change and export logs.
26. **Pressure Lunch** - Ada admits investors threatened to withdraw bridge funding.
27. **Dredge Window** - Captain Vale announces the legal start date is three days away.
28. **Photogrammetry** - The team builds a 3D reef model from dive images.
29. **False Depths** - The public survey deepens the channel by averaging out coral heads.
30. **Reporter on Deck** - Noemi publishes the raw-map discrepancy.

### Part IV: Injunction Reef
31. **Cease Letter** - Nico sends a cease-and-desist accusing Mara of damaging investor confidence.
32. **Witness Coral** - Lina prepares expert testimony on coral growth as evidence.
33. **Diver's Memory** - Tavio testifies that named reef features were moved on the map.
34. **Client Review** - Sela proves the datum shift followed a development meeting.
35. **The Captain's Refusal** - Captain Vale refuses to dredge without a court ruling.
36. **Emergency Filing** - Mara files for injunction with the chronometer, log, and reef model.
37. **Maritime Court** - Judge Ilya asks whether living coral can establish location.
38. **Nico's Map** - Nico produces a consultant report that calls the wreck debris non-historic.
39. **Maker's Number** - Emmett traces the chronometer number to the lost schooner Maribel.
40. **Underwater Record** - The 3D model overlays wreck, coral, and proposed channel.

### Part V: The Clock Runs Again
41. **Cross Tide** - Cross-examination tests whether Mara's team contaminated the site.
42. **The Datum Email** - Sela releases the email requesting "commercially usable alignment."
43. **Ada's Vote** - Ada breaks with Nico and asks the court to pause the project.
44. **Reef Room** - The court views the underwater model in silence.
45. **Old Storm** - The captain's log and stopped chronometer align with the 1891 wreck.
46. **Findings of Place** - Judge Ilya finds the public survey unreliable.
47. **Protected Water** - The reef receives emergency cultural and ecological protection.
48. **Restoration Stakes** - Mara's team replaces the blue dredge stakes with restoration markers.
49. **Jobs in the Reef** - The island turns toward restoration work instead of dredging.
50. **The Coral Clock** - Mara archives the chronometer beside the corrected reef map.
