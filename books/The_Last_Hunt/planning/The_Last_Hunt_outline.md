# The Last Hunt — Outline

## Premise

An aging Comanche tracker, Santiago, is hired by Texas Ranger McAllister — the same man who once hunted his people during the Indian Wars — to find a kidnapped girl in the harsh Texas plains. The trail pushes both men across a landscape of dust and memory, forcing them to reckon with the violence that shaped them. The girl is not waiting to be saved.

## Core Cast

- **Santiago** — Comanche elder, tracker, nearly seventy. Quiet, watchful. His knees ache and his eyes are still sharp. He survived the Reservation, the killing of his family, and the slow erasure of his world. He tracks because it is the only language he still speaks fluently.

- **Ranger Elias McAllister** — Texas Ranger, mid-fifties. Former Indian fighter. A man of law who once wore the uniform of conquest. He hired Santiago years ago for a different hunt. He has never apologized.

- **Maria Gutierrez** — Fifteen years old. Kidnapped from her family's farm. Not a child to be rescued — a girl who watches, who learns, who waits for her moment. Her father is a tenant farmer. Her mother is dead.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Summons

1. **The Visitor** — McAllister's horse appears on the road to Santiago's shack at dawn. The old man knows why.
2. **Old Bones** — Santiago rises stiff from his bedroll. He sees the Ranger waiting and feels the weight of years.
3. **The Offer** — McAllister states his case: a girl taken, three days gone, no trail the Rangers can hold.
4. **The Debt** — McAllister reminds Santiago of the old arrangement. Santiago says nothing. He remembers a different debt.
5. **The Leaving** — Santiago packs his few possessions. A knife, a canteen, a pouch of tobacco. No gun.
6. **The Photograph** — McAllister shows a daguerreotype of the girl. Santiago studies her face. She has strong hands.
7. **The Farm** — They reach the Gutierrez homestead. The father stands in the yard, hollow-eyed.
8. **The Room** — Santiago examines the girl's bedroom. Signs of struggle. A window forced. Small boot prints outside.
9. **The Tracks** — Santiago finds the trail leading west toward the Llano Estacado. Three horses, one riderless.
10. **The First Camp** — They make camp at sundown. McAllister builds a fire. Santiago sits apart and watches the dark.

### Part II: The Trail

11. **Dust and Silence** — The second day of riding. The landscape opens into flat emptiness. Santiago reads the ground.
12. **The Confession** — McAllister admits he cannot read sign the way Santiago can. The words cost him.
13. **The Water Hole** — They find a watering stop. Signs the kidnappers stopped here. A dropped button. Boot prints in mud.
14. **The Story of the Horse** — Santiago tells how he lost his finest horse to a Ranger patrol in '74. McAllister was there.
15. **The Dead Man** — They find a body by the trail. Not the girl. One of the kidnappers, shot, left for buzzards.
16. **The Split** — The trail divides. Three riders went one way. Two went another. Santiago chooses.
17. **The Storm** — A dust storm hits. They lose half a day. McAllister's temper frays.
18. **The Scar** — Santiago removes his shirt to wash a wound from a fall. McAllister sees the scars on his back.
19. **The Hunters** — They spot riders on the horizon. Not the kidnappers. Comancheros, maybe. They hide.
20. **The Night Watch** — Santiago takes first watch. He sits in the dark and remembers his wife's face.

### Part III: The Territory

21. **The Canyon** — The trail leads into a red rock canyon. Good cover. Good ambush ground.
22. **The Girl's Voice** — A short chapter from Maria's perspective. She is tied to a saddle. She counts the days.
23. **The Argument** — McAllister wants to push faster. Santiago refuses. The horses need rest. Old anger surfaces.
24. **The Cave** — They find a cave with old Comanche markings. Santiago touches the rock. His father's hand, maybe.
25. **The Dream** — Santiago dreams of the burning of his village. He wakes with McAllister's hand on his shoulder.
26. **The Rope** — They find a length of rope with blood on it. McAllister's jaw tightens. They ride harder.
27. **The Trade Post** — They stop at a trading post. The owner has seen the men. A girl with them. Still alive.
28. **The Bottle** — McAllister drinks that night. He talks about the war. Santiago listens. Says nothing.
29. **The Shortcut** — Santiago proposes a route through badlands. Faster but dangerous. McAllister agrees.
30. **The Fall** — Santiago's horse steps in a hole. The old man is thrown. His ankle twists badly.

### Part IV: The Crossing

31. **The Splint** — Santiago splints his own ankle with sage and cloth. He will not stop.
32. **The Slow Mile** — They ride at half speed. Santiago's ankle swells. He chews willow bark for the pain.
33. **The Second Camp** — Maria's chapter. She has been moved to a stone hut. She begins to plan.
34. **The Signal** — Santiago finds a scrap of cloth tied to a bush. Maria's work. She is marking the trail.
35. **The Three** — They catch sight of the kidnappers' camp. Three men. The girl is inside a lean-to.
36. **The Plan** — McAllister wants to ride in shooting. Santiago counsels patience. Wait for dark.
37. **The Old Ways** — Santiago demonstrates how to move without sound. McAllister watches and remembers his own lessons.
38. **The Girl** — Maria sees them in the dusk. She recognizes the Ranger's star. She does not call out.
39. **The Shot** — McAllister's rifle cracks. One man falls. The camp erupts.
40. **The Knife** — Santiago moves through the dark. His knife finds the second man's throat. The old skills hold.

### Part V: The Reckoning

41. **The Third Man** — The last kidnapper grabs Maria. He puts a gun to her head. Santiago speaks to him in Spanish.
42. **The Trade** — Santiago offers himself in exchange. The man hesitates. Maria moves. She drives an elbow into his ribs.
43. **The End of It** — McAllister shoots the man as he staggers. Maria stands over the body, breathing hard.
44. **The Silence** — The camp is quiet. Three men dead. Maria is alive. Santiago sits down on the hard ground.
45. **The Ride Back** — They start the long return. Maria rides Santiago's horse. He walks beside her on his bad ankle.
46. **The Story** — Maria tells them what happened. The men talked of selling her across the border. She never wept.
47. **The Crossing Back** — They stop at the canyon with the Comanche markings. Santiago touches the wall again. A goodbye.
48. **The Homecoming** — The Gutierrez farm appears. Maria's father runs toward her. Santiago stays on the road.
49. **The Last Word** — McAllister tries to thank Santiago. The old man stops him with a look. Some debts cannot be spoken.
50. **The Horizon** — Santiago walks back toward his shack alone. The sun sets behind him. He does not look back.
