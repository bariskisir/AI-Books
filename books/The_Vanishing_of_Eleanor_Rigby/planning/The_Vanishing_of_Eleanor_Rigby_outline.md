# The Vanishing of Eleanor Rigby — Outline

## Premise

Tom Caldicott and his wife Eleanor check into a historic hotel for a weekend getaway. In the morning, Eleanor is gone. The door was locked from the inside. The chain was on. Hotel security shows nothing. There is no sign of struggle, no forced entry, no explanation. The only clue: the night before, Eleanor hummed a Beatles song — "Eleanor Rigby" — and when Tom asked why, she said nothing. As Detective Harris works the case, Tom discovers his wife had secrets he never imagined: a second phone, a hidden past, and a twin sister she thought was dead. The search for Eleanor Rigby becomes a search for the woman Tom never truly knew.

## Core Cast

- **Tom Caldicott** — Husband, early 40s, architect. Methodical, quiet, devoted. He thought he knew his wife.
- **Eleanor Caldicott (née Rigby)** — Missing woman, late 30s. Born Sarah Rigby. Adopted. She built a new life and ran from an old one.
- **Detective Margaret Harris** — Veteran detective, mid-50s, sharp-eyed and patient. She trusts evidence, not feelings.
- **Arthur Pence** — Night clerk of the Hotel Adair, mid-60s. Quiet, watchful. Knows more than he says.
- **Rebecca Rigby** — Eleanor's twin sister. Institutionalized. Released. Searching.
- **Claire Bishop** — Eleanor's best friend. She saw something but didn't understand it.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Vanishing (Chapters 1–10)

1. **Arrival** — Tom and Eleanor check into the Hotel Adair. Snow falling. Eleanor is quiet, distracted.
2. **Dinner** — They eat in the hotel restaurant. Eleanor picks at her food. She stares at the window.
3. **The Song** — In the elevator, Eleanor hums. Tom asks what it is. She says "Eleanor Rigby" and stops.
4. **Night** — Tom wakes at 3 AM. Eleanor is sitting in the dark, looking out the window.
5. **Morning** — Tom wakes alone. Her side of the bed is cold. The door is locked from inside.
6. **The Search** — Tom searches the hotel. No sign of Eleanor. Her things remain.
7. **Detective Harris** — Harris arrives. She questions Tom. She examines the room. Nothing.
8. **The Lock** — Forensics confirms: door was locked, chain engaged, no forced entry.
9. **The Clerk** — Arthur Pence says no one left through the lobby. He saw nothing.
10. **Alone** — Tom sits in the empty room. He replays the night before.

### Part II: The Investigation (Chapters 11–20)

11. **Phone Records** — Tom gets the phone bill. Eleanor called a number he doesn't recognize.
12. **The Second Phone** — He finds a prepaid phone in her coat pocket. One number in it.
13. **Dead End** — The number is disconnected. Harris tries to trace it. Nothing.
14. **The Friend** — Tom calls Claire. She hasn't heard from Eleanor in two weeks.
15. **The Job** — Eleanor quit her job two weeks ago. She told Tom she was on leave.
16. **The Desk** — Tom checks her home office. The desk is cleaned out.
17. **The Key** — Under her nightstand, taped to the wood, a small brass key.
18. **The Locker** — The key opens a Greyhound locker. Inside: a photograph and a letter.
19. **The Photograph** — Two girls, maybe ten years old, identical faces. Twins.
20. **The Letter** — Addressed to "Sarah." Handwriting is Eleanor's.

### Part III: The Identity (Chapters 21–30)

21. **Sarah** — Harris runs "Sarah Rigby" through records. An adoption file surfaces.
22. **The Mother** — Tom drives to Ohio to meet Eleanor's adoptive mother, Linda.
23. **The Adoption** — Linda confirms: Eleanor was adopted at birth. Her birth name was Sarah.
24. **The Twin** — Eleanor had a twin. Rebecca. She died at birth. The records say so.
25. **The Discrepancy** — Harris finds a second birth certificate. Rebecca Rigby lived.
26. **The Hospital** — Rebecca was institutionalized at age twelve. Schizophrenia diagnosis.
27. **The Release** — Rebecca was released six months ago. Last known address: this city.
28. **The Room** — Rebecca's apartment is empty. On the wall, in marker: "She took my name."
29. **The Record** — On the floor, a Beatles album. "Eleanor Rigby" scratched into the label.
30. **The Clerk** — Harris discovers Arthur Pence worked at the same hospital as Rebecca's orderly.

### Part IV: The Convergence (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The Connection** — Pence knew both sisters. He was the link Eleanor feared.
32. **The Confrontation** — Tom questions Pence in the hotel lobby. Pence admits nothing.
33. **The Tape** — Harris reviews more security footage. Pence was on Eleanor's floor at 2 AM.
34. **The Corridor** — Behind the wall panel in room 317: a service corridor. Hidden.
35. **The Escape** — Pence led Eleanor through the service corridor to the basement exit.
36. **The Interrogation** — Harris brings Pence in. He breaks down. He confesses.
37. **The Story** — Pence says Eleanor was terrified. She paid him to help her disappear.
38. **The Direction** — She took a bus west. Pence doesn't know where she got off.
39. **The Choice** — Tom can follow the bus route. Or he can go home.
40. **The Letter Found** — Harris finds a sealed envelope in Pence's possession. Addressed to Tom.

### Part V: The Resolution (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Letter** — Tom reads Eleanor's words for the first time. She explains everything.
42. **The Twin** — Rebecca was real. She was dangerous. Eleanor changed her name, moved, married.
43. **The Fear** — Rebecca found her through a private investigator. Eleanor had to run again.
44. **The Love** — She loved Tom. She knew he would search. She left the trail for him.
45. **The Song** — "Eleanor Rigby" was her mother's favorite song. Her mother who gave her up.
46. **The Understanding** — Tom finishes the letter. He sits in the hotel room and weeps.
47. **The Acceptance** — He goes home. He tells no one about the letter. Not even Harris.
48. **The Twin Found** — Harris locates Rebecca in a shelter. Rebecca is medicated, calm.
49. **The Year** — One year passes. Tom still lives in the house. He keeps Eleanor's things.
50. **The Postcard** — A postcard arrives. No return address. Just a single line: "All the lonely people."
