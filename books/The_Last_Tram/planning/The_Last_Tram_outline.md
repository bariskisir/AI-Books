# The Last Tram — Outline

## Premise

Jorge Mendes has driven Lisbon's oldest tram line for eighteen years. At midnight on the last Sunday of every month, his route passes through a shadow version of the city — Lisbon as it existed in 1955, complete with cobblestone streets, black-market cafes, and the electric hum of a world that history forgot. The passengers who board in that other Lisbon are real, warm, and unaware that half a century has passed. When Jorge falls for Maria, a woman from the lost city, and attracts the attention of the Night Conductor — the enigmatic gatekeeper of the time-slip — he must choose between the life he knows and a love that belongs to a ghost.

## Core Cast

- **Jorge Mendes** — A tram driver in his late forties. Divorced. Quietly observant. He has driven the same route for so long that the city's rhythms have become his own. He notices the anomaly before he can name it.

- **The Night Conductor** — A tall, gaunt man in an antique uniform. He appears only during the midnight passages. He is not hostile, but he is not kind. He enforces the rules of the crossing.

- **Maria dos Santos** — A young woman from 1955 Lisbon. She boards the tram at the Chiado stop every month, always wearing a green coat. She does not know she is dead. Or rather, she does not know that her version of the city is gone.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Route Invisible

**Chapter 1: The Number 28**
Jorge begins his shift at the Estrela depot. The tram is old, the night is damp. He checks his watch: 11:47 PM. Thirteen minutes until midnight.

**Chapter 2: Cobblestones**
He rattles through the familiar streets of Lisbon. Sao Bento. Camoes. The city is quiet. A single passenger boards at Martim Moniz — a man in a brown suit who says nothing.

**Chapter 3: The Light**
Just before midnight, the overhead wires begin to glow faintly. Jorge notices a shift in the air pressure. The tram shudders.

**Chapter 4: The Other Lisbon**
The tram emerges onto a street Jorge does not recognize. The buildings are older. The advertisements are for products that no longer exist. 1955.

**Chapter 5: Black Market**
A woman with a loaf of bread under her coat boards at Chiado. She pays with old escudo coins. Jorge accepts them without comment, playing along.

**Chapter 6: The Green Coat**
Maria dos Santos steps aboard at the next stop. She is young, maybe twenty-five. She wears a green coat that seems to glow in the dim light.

**Chapter 7: First Words**
"Does this tram still go to Belem?" she asks. Her voice is soft, accented with an older Lisbon. Jorge nods. He does not trust his own voice.

**Chapter 8: The Ghost Stop**
The tram stops at a station that does not exist on any modern map. Three more passengers board, all in period clothes. Jorge watches them in the rearview mirror.

**Chapter 9: Return**
At 12:47 AM the tram lurches and the city snaps back to the present. Jorge is alone. The old coins are still in his pocket.

**Chapter 10: The Museum**
Jorge takes the coins to the Museu da Cidade. An archivist confirms they are genuine 1955 escudos. Mint condition. Never circulated.

**Chapter 11: Doubt**
Jorge lies awake. He tells himself it was a dream. But he knows the route by heart. He knows what he saw.

**Chapter 12: The Second Crossing**
A week later. Same time. Same place. The glow returns. The tram crosses over. The man in the brown suit is waiting.

**Chapter 13: Maria Again**
She boards at Chiado. This time she sits near the front. She asks Jorge if he remembers the old cinema on Rua Garrett.

**Chapter 14: The Cinema Memory**
She describes a film she saw: "Um Homem do Rio." It was released in 1955. Jorge has heard of it. He was not born yet.

**Chapter 15: The Return of Doubt**
Back in the present, Jorge checks the film's release date. 1955. Exactly as she said. He begins to accept the impossible.

### Part II: The Conductor's Rules

**Chapter 16: The First Warning**
A tall man in an antique conductor's uniform appears at the rear of the tram. He does not collect fares. He only watches Jorge.

**Chapter 17: No Names**
The Night Conductor approaches Jorge. "You do not ask their names," he says. "You do not follow them when they leave."

**Chapter 18: Jorge Disobeys**
Maria gets off at Belem. Jorge watches her walk toward the Torre de Belem. She looks back once.

**Chapter 19: The Cafe**
The next Saturday, Jorge goes to Belem in daylight. The cafe she walked toward is a tourist shop. There is no trace of her. No record of a Maria dos Santos in any modern directory.

**Chapter 20: The Archivist Again**
Jorge returns to the museum. He asks about 1955 census records. The archivist helps. Maria dos Santos, born 1930, died 1957. Tuberculosis.

**Chapter 21: Grief**
Jorge reads the death certificate. She was twenty-seven. She died in the same year she boarded his tram. The dates do not make sense.

**Chapter 22: The Conductor's Laws**
The Night Conductor appears again. He explains the rules of the crossing. The midnight route is a seam between two Lisbons. The dead do not know they are dead.

**Chapter 23: The Burden**
Jorge now carries the weight of Maria's fate. He knows she will die in two years of her time. He cannot warn her. The Conductor forbids it.

**Chapter 24: A Night Off**
Jorge switches shifts with a colleague. He does not drive the midnight route. He sits at home, staring at the wall.

**Chapter 25: The Pull**
He cannot stay away. The next Sunday, he takes his shift back. He needs to see her again.

**Chapter 26: The Confession**
Jorge tells Maria that he is not from her time. She does not believe him. She laughs. Then she looks at his shoes, his watch, and stops laughing.

**Chapter 27: The Aftermath**
Maria is quiet for the rest of the ride. She gets off at Belem without saying goodbye. Jorge feels he has broken something.

**Chapter 28: The Archive**
Jorge digs deeper into 1955 Lisbon. He learns about the black market, the political tensions, the hope that ran beneath the poverty. Maria's world becomes vivid to him.

**Chapter 29: The Conductor's Story**
The Night Conductor reveals that he, too, was once a driver. He stayed too long in the other side. Now he is bound to the seam forever.

**Chapter 30: The Warning Deepens**
"You cannot save her," the Conductor says. "You can only ride alongside her until the track ends."

**Chapter 31: Maria's Gift**
Maria brings Jorge a small thing from her time — a lottery ticket from 1955. She says it is for luck. He keeps it pressed in a book.

**Chapter 32: The Trap**
Jorge considers staying in 1955. He researches whether it is possible. The Conductor explains: the seam is not a door. It is a crack. A body cannot live on both sides.

**Chapter 33: December 1955**
Maria mentions that it is almost Christmas. Jorge realizes: her death is approaching in her timeline. The realization is a physical blow.

**Chapter 34: The Plan**
Jorge decides to tell Maria everything. The Conductor cannot stop him. The worst that can happen is that she does not believe him.

**Chapter 35: The Truth**
At the Belem stop, Jorge steps off the tram. He follows Maria into the 1955 night. The Conductor watches from the window.

### Part III: The Last Fare

**Chapter 36: Belem 1955**
Jorge walks through the streets of old Lisbon. The smells are different. Coal smoke. Salt. Unfiltered tobacco. He finds Maria at a cafe.

**Chapter 37: The Cafe Imperial**
They sit at a small table. Maria is frightened. Jorge tells her everything. The year. Her death. The tram. The seam.

**Chapter 38: Maria's Choice**
She does not run away. She asks questions. She touches his face and feels the truth in the way his skin does not match the light of her world.

**Chapter 39: The Night Conductor Arrives**
The Conductor appears at the cafe. He does not enter. He stands on the street, watching. Jorge feels the seam beginning to close.

**Chapter 40: The Laws of the Seam**
The Conductor speaks through the window. "Every crossing has a cost. You have spent too much time here. The return will hurt."

**Chapter 41: The Return**
Jorge walks back through the streets of 1955. The city begins to flicker. Buildings fade. He reaches the tram just as the lights go out.

**Chapter 42: The Cost**
Back in the present, Jorge collapses. He is sick for three days. The seam has taken part of him.

**Chapter 43: January 1956**
The next crossing. Maria is still there. She is thinner. She has been waiting. She has not told anyone about Jorge.

**Chapter 44: The Final Crossing**
The Conductor appears one last time. He gives Jorge a choice. He can stay in 1955 and die with Maria. Or he can watch her fade from the safety of the present.

**Chapter 45: The Decision**
Jorge chooses to stay. He steps off the tram at Belem for the last time. He does not look back.

**Chapter 46: The Nightmare**
But being in 1955 is wrong. Jorge is a ghost himself. His body does not belong. He begins to unravel.

**Chapter 47: Maria Sees**
Maria sees him fading. She understands. She tells him to go back. She does not want him to die for a world that is already gone.

**Chapter 48: The Return, Broken**
Jorge drags himself back to the tram. The Conductor helps him aboard. The seam closes. Jorge is in the present, alive, but hollow.

**Chapter 49: The Green Coat**
The next crossing, Maria does not board. Jorge waits. The stop comes and goes. He knows she died in her time. He drives on.

**Chapter 50: The Route Continues**
Jorge still drives the number 28. The midnight crossings still happen. But now he understands. The tram carries memory. And memory, like Lisbon, is a city you can visit but never stay in.
