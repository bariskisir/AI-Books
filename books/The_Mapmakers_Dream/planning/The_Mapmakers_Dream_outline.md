# The Mapmaker's Dream — Outline

## Premise

Arthur Finch, a meticulous cartographer for the British East India Company, is sent to map the uncharted interior of Central Africa. Local rumors speak of a great river that leads to a city of stone, a place that appears on no European map. With his native guide Kibo, Arthur traces the river through jungle, rapids, and highlands. At its end he finds a walled city — ancient, inhabited, and deliberately omitted from every chart. The city's Guardian tests Arthur's intentions: will he draw the truth and destroy the city's protection, or leave it unwritten and betray his life's calling?

## Core Cast

- **Arthur Finch** — A London cartographer, precise and methodical. Believes the world can be known through measurement. The journey forces him to question what cannot be mapped.
- **Kibo** — A guide from a coastal trading village. Speaks three languages, reads no maps. He knows the river by memory and the city by legend.
- **The Guardian** — An elder woman of the stone city. She keeps the archive and determines who may know of the city's existence.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Commission (Chapters 1-8)
1: Arthur at his desk in London, receives the commission for the African survey.
2: The voyage to Zanzibar. Arthur studies blank spaces on existing maps.
3: Hiring Kibo in a coastal market. Kibo's refusal to look at a map.
4: The first days inland. Heat, insect swarms, the weight of equipment.
5: The river appears. Kibo says it is called the Ulima, meaning "vein."
6: Rapids and a capsized canoe. Arthur nearly drowns, Kibo saves him.
7: Night in the jungle. Arthur hears drums. Kibo tells him the stories the drums carry.
8: They cross the first set of falls. Arthur sees the river bends in a way no cartographer would draw.

### Part II: The River (Chapters 9-18)
9: Deeper into the interior. The river widens, the trees darken.
10: A village where no one speaks Swahili. Kibo trades with gestures.
11: The fever takes Arthur. Kibo nurses him for three days.
12: Fever dreams: Arthur sees a white city on a hill of green.
13: Recovery. The river turns north through a gorge.
14: Stone ruins along the bank. Pillars carved with animals.
15: Kibo explains the ruins belong to "the old ones who drew the land."
16: A hippopotamus attack. The canoe is destroyed.
17: Building a new vessel from reeds. Arthur learns to work with his hands.
18: The river opens into a broad lake. On the far shore: a wall.

### Part III: The City (Chapters 19-30)
19: The first sight of the wall. Cut stone, fifty feet high.
20: Finding the gate. A woman stands in the entrance.
21: The Guardian greets them. She speaks English.
22: Inside the city. Grid streets, two-story houses, a central market.
23: The archive. Rooms of maps on parchment, hide, and bark.
24: The Guardian shows Arthur the oldest map: the city at the center of the world.
25: Arthur realizes no outside map includes this city. The omission is deliberate.
26: He asks why. The Guardian tells him of the burning of Timbuktu.
27: Kibo explores the city's lower quarters. He finds people who know his grandmother's village.
28: Arthur is invited to copy the city's maps. He begins.
29: The Guardian questions his purpose. "What will you do with what you find?"
30: Arthur cannot answer.

### Part IV: The Trial (Chapters 31-40)
31: The Guardian reveals the archive's law: no visitor may draw the city.
32: Arthur argues for knowledge. The Guardian argues for protection.
33: Kibo mediates. He understands both sides.
34: The Guardian offers a choice: map the river but omit the city, or leave with nothing.
35: Arthur stops drawing. He wanders the city alone.
36: He meets a child who shows him a garden irrigated by the river.
37: He understands the city is alive, not a specimen.
38: Arthur asks to see the city's sky-view. The Guardian takes him to a tower.
39: From the tower he can see the river's full course. He memorizes it.
40: Arthur decides. He will not draw the city.

### Part V: The Return (Chapters 41-50)
41: Arthur makes a final copy of the river maps — the city a blank.
42: The Guardian gives him a small map on hide. A gift, not a record.
43: Leaving the city. The gate closes behind them.
44: The journey back. The river feels different, smaller.
45: Kibo tells Arthur the city will move in the stories now.
46: They reach the coast. The Company agent asks for the maps.
47: Arthur hands over the river maps. He says nothing of the city.
48: The agent is satisfied. The blank space on the Company's chart remains blank.
49: Arthur returns to London. His desk. His instruments. The small hide map in his drawer.
50: Arthur resigns from the Company. He becomes a teacher. He never publishes his final map.
