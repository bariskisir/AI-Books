The old account of the moment would have been brief: Mara Venn read the docket twice before she put down her coffee. "Start at the beginning," Mara said.

"The beginning is usually older than the file," came the answer.

"Then start where the paper starts lying."

That was enough. They worked from the object outward: date, access, material, motive, and the human convenience that joined them. Each piece had to answer a narrow question before it could be allowed to join the larger accusation.

That detail did not prove the whole scheme, but it changed the direction of the next question. Mara did not need anyone to confess to see causality taking shape. A forged or altered record required a person who wanted the land unburdened, a person who understood the archive well enough to wound it, and a chain of helpers who could call haste professionalism. The ridge itself waited outside those rooms, indifferent to the vocabulary used against it.

There were disagreements, and they mattered. Niko wanted every irregularity to have an innocent explanation because his own work had passed too near it. Priya wanted the archive defended without making the archive look fragile. Elias wanted the ground to be consulted before anyone trusted a line. Ruth wanted the practical life of the ridge named accurately. Leda wanted plants treated as evidence, not decoration. Mara wanted all of that translated into something a judge could use before the sale hardened.

Niko kept looking for a place to put his hands. Priya noticed and said nothing. Elias would have said something too severe, and Ruth would have improved on it. The opposition was not faceless. Vale had lawyers, lenders, consultants, messengers, and a model with clean roofs. That made him look organized, not right. Mara had learned that organization could carry a falsehood a long way if no one stopped to ask why every convenient fact pointed in the same profitable direction.

By the end of the chapter's turn, the next consequence was unavoidable. The clue had to move from conversation into custody, from memory into affidavit, from suspicion into a form the court would accept. Mara labeled the page, checked the time, and made one more note in the margin. When Mara left, she carried less certainty than burden, but it was the kind of burden that could be filed.

She also noted the weather, because later a witness might remember rain more honestly than a time.
