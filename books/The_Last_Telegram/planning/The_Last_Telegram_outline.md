# The Last Telegram

## Premise

In November 1944, Rose Clayton, a twenty-two-year-old Wren decoder at a remote Royal Navy signals station on the south coast of England, decodes the daily stream of encrypted telegrams that track every ship, submarine, and casualty of the Atlantic war. When she begins piecing together the fragmentary signals concerning one damaged destroyer, HMS Archer, she grows obsessed with the crew's fate, particularly that of a junior officer whose name recurs in the signals. Before she ever lays eyes on Lieutenant David Davies, she reads the telegram that announces his destroyer has been sunk with all hands. Weeks later, when a man named Davies arrives at the station as the new signals officer, Rose must reconcile the dead man she read about with the living one who stands before her, and face what the machines cannot encode: survival, grief, and the chance to love someone she already mourned.

## Core Cast

- **Rose Clayton** — Wren decoder, twenty-two, from a Somerset village, sharp-eyed and quiet, prone to thinking in patterns
- **Lieutenant David Davies** — Signals officer, twenty-eight, recently promoted, scarred from a depth-charge attack, carries a slight limp
- **Commander Phillip Bryce** — Station commander, fifty-two, career navy, gruff but not unkind, lost a son at sea
- **Mabel Finch** — Senior Wren, thirty-five, Rose's supervisor, practical and maternal
- **Alice Weaver** — Fellow Wren, twenty, gregarious, from London
- **Captain Tom Harris** — Davies's former captain on HMS Archer, mentioned in telegrams but never seen

## Chapter Plan

1. **The Station** — Rose steps off the train at Shingle Cove station, salt wind in her face, and sees the concrete blockhouse where she will work.

2. **The Wrens** — Mabel Finch assigns Rose to the afternoon watch and introduces the cramped decoding room with its smell of ink and damp wool.

3. **First Signal** — Rose decodes her first live telegram: a routine position report from a merchant convoy eastbound from Halifax.

4. **Commander Bryce** — Bryce summons Rose to his office and warns her that the work requires silence, discretion, and a strong stomach.

5. **The Routine** — Two weeks pass. Rose learns the rhythms of the watch rotation, the clatter of the Type-X, the weight of the blackout curtains.

6. **HMS Archer** — A garbled signal arrives. Rose pieces together that destroyer HMS Archer has sustained damage from a U-boat attack and is limping toward port.

7. **Davies** — A name surfaces in the Archer signal: Lieutenant D. Davies, gunnery officer, listed among the wounded.

8. **The Casualty List** — Rose decodes a follow-up signal: three confirmed dead, seven wounded, the Archer making slow headway.

9. **Fascination** — Rose finds herself checking every new signal for mention of the Archer. Alice teases her about her intensity.

10. **Bryce's Warning** — Commander Bryce tells the Wrens not to get attached to names. Rose cannot let it go.

11. **Silence** — Four days pass with no news of the Archer. Rose imagines the ship sinking in cold water.

12. **News at Last** — A telegram confirms the Archer has reached Londonderry for repairs. Rose exhales.

13. **The Medals Signal** — A list of commendations includes Davies mentioned for gallantry under fire. Rose clips the signal flimsy from the waste bin.

14. **Mabel Notices** — Mabel sees Rose reading the clipping at breakfast and warns her that names on paper are not men in the world.

15. **The Archer Sails** — A telegram reports the Archer has been repaired and is returning to active duty.

16. **Davies's Voice** — Rose decodes a rare personal signal addressed to Davies from his mother in Cardiff. She feels she knows him.

17. **The Attack** — A frantic series of signals describes a wolfpack engagement. The Archer is in the thick of it.

18. **Distress** — A distress signal from the Archer: engines failed, taking on water, position broadcast.

19. **All Hands** — The final signal from the Archer: "Abandoning ship. God save the King." Rose decodes it with shaking hands.

20. **Grief** — Rose leaves the decoding room and vomits behind the coal shed. Alice follows and holds her hair back.

21. **The Telegram** — The Admiralty confirms HMS Archer sunk with all hands. No survivors expected. Rose crosses Davies's name off her mental list.

22. **Winter** — December 1944. The station settles into a gray routine. Rose works mechanically, the war losing its romance.

23. **Transfer Orders** — Bryce announces a new signals officer will arrive from Londonderry. Rose does not care.

24. **Arrival** — Lieutenant David Davies steps out of a staff car. Rose recognizes the name from the signals. But he is alive.

25. **Shock** — Rose cannot speak to him. She avoids the mess hall. Alice demands to know what is wrong.

26. **Confrontation** — Davies introduces himself to the Wrens. Rose shakes his hand. His fingers are warm, not dead.

27. **The Story** — Over tea, Davies tells Rose he was transferred off the Archer before its final voyage because of his wound. Rose realizes the telegrams had no room for that detail.

28. **Fragments** — Rose shows Davies the clipping about his medal. He is touched and unsettled.

29. **Tension** — Commander Bryce notices Rose paying too much attention to Davies and reassigns her to the night watch.

30. **Night Shift** — Rose and Davies work the midnight-to-dawn shift together. The station is quiet. They talk.

31. **Cardiff** — Davies describes growing up in Cardiff, his father a dockworker, his mother a nurse. Rose tells him about Somerset.

32. **The Scar** — Rose notices Davies's limp and asks. He shows her the shrapnel scar along his ribs from the Archer attack.

33. **Proximity** — Rose begins volunteering for night shifts. Alice says she is being obvious.

34. **The Raid** — A Luftwaffe reconnaissance plane appears. The station goes to battle stations. Davies pushes Rose into a shelter.

35. **Aftermath** — Shaken, Rose admits to herself she cares whether he lives or dies. She already knows what that costs.

36. **The Archive** — Rose digs through old signal flimsies in the archive room, searching for every mention of Davies.

37. **Davies Finds Her** — Davies discovers Rose surrounded by papers. She confesses she read his death before she met him.

38. **Understanding** — Davies sits beside her and says the telegrams only carry facts, not truth. He never died. She is the first person to mourn him.

39. **Christmas Eve** — A small party in the mess. Davies gives Rose a book of poetry. She gives him a knitted scarf.

40. **The Kiss** — After midnight, behind the blackout curtains, Davies kisses Rose. She does not pull away.

41. **Bryce Warns Again** — Bryce tells Rose that fraternization is forbidden and could see Davies reassigned.

42. **Secrecy** — Rose and Davies agree to keep their distance during working hours. The secrecy makes every glance charged.

43. **The Last Convoy** — News arrives that the war in Europe is nearing its end. The last Atlantic convoy is being assembled.

44. **Davies's Request** — Davies requests transfer to the convoy as a liaison officer. Bryce approves before Rose knows.

45. **Anger** — Rose confronts Davies. He says he cannot hide behind a desk while others sail. She says she cannot decode another death telegram about him.

46. **Departure** — Davies leaves for the convoy. Rose does not say goodbye properly.

47. **Waiting** — Five days of silence. Rose cannot eat. Alice sits with her through the night watches.

48. **The Signal** — A telegram arrives from the convoy: minor damage, no casualties. Davies is alive. Rose cries at her desk.

49. **Return** — Davies comes back thin and exhausted. Rose meets him at the gate. They stand in the rain without speaking.

50. **The Last Telegram** — In March 1945, the station receives its final signal before decommissioning. Rose decodes it: the Archer's surviving crew list, officially updated to reflect Davies's transfer. She keeps the flimsy. Then she walks outside where Davies waits in the spring light, and she does not look back.
