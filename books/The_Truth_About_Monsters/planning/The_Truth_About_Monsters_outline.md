# The Truth About Monsters — Outline

## Premise

Ten-year-old Elodie has a secret: she can see the monsters that live under other people's beds. These creatures are not imaginary — they are living embodiments of human fears, and each one needs a host to survive. When people outgrow their fears, their monsters begin to fade, and Elodie must help them find new hosts before they vanish forever. But a sinister figure called The Collector is hunting the same monsters, trapping them in glass jars to feed his own dark purpose. With the help of her own gentle monster Grumble, Elodie must navigate a hidden world of fear and courage to save the creatures that everyone is afraid of.

## Core Cast

- **Elodie** — A ten-year-old girl who can see other people's monster fears. She is brave, kind, and carries the weight of a secret no adult would believe.
- **Grumble** — Elodie's own monster, who lives under her bed. He is large, furry, soft-spoken, and deeply loyal. He fears losing Elodie as she grows up.
- **The Collector** — A man in a long gray coat who hunts monsters and traps them in glass jars. He believes fear is a resource to be harvested and hoarded.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I: The World Beneath (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Thing Under the Bed** — Elodie wakes in the night and talks to Grumble, her monster. She has always been able to see him.
2. **The Rules of Monsters** — Grumble explains that every monster is born from a human fear, and each must have a host.
3. **The Neighbor's Monster** — Elodie sees Mrs. Pasternak's monster — a small quivering thing — and realizes Mrs. Pasternak is no longer afraid of it.
4. **Fading** — Grumble tells Elodie that when a human outgrows a fear, the monster begins to fade away.
5. **The First Rescue** — Elodie helps a fading spider-monster find a new host: a boy afraid of the dark.
6. **Grumble's Secret** — Elodie learns that Grumble himself is fading — she is growing up and outgrowing her childhood fears.
7. **The Collector Appears** — Elodie glimpses a man in a long gray coat watching her from across the street.
8. **The Jar** — She finds an empty glass jar on her windowsill with a note: "I know what you see."
9. **The Trap** — The Collector captures a monster from the old bakery, trapping it in glass.
10. **Elodie's Choice** — Elodie decides she must save the monsters before The Collector takes them all.

### Part II: The Hunt (Chapters 11–20)

11. **Following the Collector** — Elodie trails The Collector through the evening streets.
12. **The Monster Market** — She discovers a hidden market where monsters trade fears among themselves.
13. **The Whisperer** — An old woman at the market tells Elodie about the history of monsters and the Collector's origin.
14. **Grumble's Strength** — Grumble shows Elodie that even fading monsters have power.
15. **The Library Monster** — A massive creature lives in the town library, feeding on the fear of being lost.
16. **The Bargain** — The library monster agrees to help if Elodie finds it a new host.
17. **The Collector's Workshop** — Elodie finds where The Collector keeps his jarred monsters.
18. **The Caged Ones** — She sees the trapped monsters, dim and desperate inside their glass prisons.
19. **The Alarm** — The Collector nearly catches her; she escapes with a single jar.
20. **A Small Victory** — Elodie frees the jarred monster and finds it a new home.

### Part III: The Hunters and the Hunted (Chapters 21–30)

21. **The Collector's Past** — The Whisperer tells Elodie that The Collector was once a boy who lost his own monster.
22. **The Lonely Fear** — Elodie begins to understand that The Collector is driven by loneliness, not cruelty.
23. **Grumble Weakens** — Grumble is fading faster now. Elodie is terrified of losing him.
24. **The Storm** — A fierce storm traps Elodie in the library with Grumble and the library monster.
25. **The Pact** — The monsters of the town agree to help Elodie stop The Collector.
26. **The Plan** — Elodie devises a plan to break all the jars at once.
27. **The Decoy** — Grumble volunteers to be the decoy, knowing it will drain his remaining strength.
28. **The Night of the Rescue** — Elodie and the monsters move through the town in the dark.
29. **The Break-In** — They enter The Collector's workshop; the jars glow on the shelves.
30. **The Confrontation** — The Collector catches them. He and Elodie face each other.

### Part IV: The Truth (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The Collector's Monster** — The Collector shows Elodie his own monster, trapped long ago in a jar.
32. **The Story** — He explains that his monster abandoned him when he stopped being afraid, and he has been trying to keep all monsters from fading ever since.
33. **A Different Way** — Elodie tells The Collector that trapping monsters is not the answer — they need to find new hosts, not be preserved.
34. **The Doubt** — The Collector wavers. He has never considered another way.
35. **The Fading Monster** — The Collector's own monster is barely visible in its jar — almost gone.
36. **Grumble's Gift** — Grumble offers some of his own fading strength to sustain The Collector's monster.
37. **The Alliance** — The Collector agrees to help Elodie instead of hunting monsters.
38. **Freeing the Jars** — Together, they release the trapped monsters one by one.
39. **Finding Homes** — Elodie and The Collector work as a team to match each monster with a new host.
40. **The New Purpose** — The Collector finds meaning in helping monsters transition rather than trapping them.

### Part V: Letting Go (Chapters 41–50)

41. **Grumble's Goodbye** — Grumble tells Elodie it is almost time for him to go.
42. **The Last Night** — Elodie spends a final night talking with Grumble under her bed.
43. **The Other Side** — Grumble shows Elodie where fading monsters go — they become dreams.
44. **The Transformation** — Grumble begins to change, growing lighter and less solid.
45. **The Collector's Gift** — The Collector gives Elodie an empty jar, telling her it can hold memories.
46. **The Final Host** — Grumble's last act is to find a tiny fear in a newborn baby — a seed of a monster.
47. **The Dawn** — Elodie wakes to find Grumble gone. Her room feels larger, emptier.
48. **The Memory Jar** — She places a piece of Grumble's fur in the jar — not to trap him, but to remember.
49. **The New Monster** — Elodie sees a new, small monster stirring under her baby cousin's crib.
50. **The Cycle** — Elodie understands that monsters never truly die — they just move on. She is not afraid anymore. She is ready to help the next generation see what she sees.
