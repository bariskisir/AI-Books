## Summary

The Truth About Monsters is a warm, imaginative children's fantasy about a ten-year-old girl named Elodie who can see other people's monster fears. With the help of her own gentle monster Grumble, she navigates a hidden world of fear and courage, helping fading monsters find new hosts while confronting The Collector — a man who traps monsters in glass jars. The story explores themes of empathy, growing up, letting go, and the beautiful cycle of fear and courage that connects all living things.

## Characters

- **Elodie** — A ten-year-old girl who can see monsters. Brave, kind, and learning to let go.
- **Grumble** — Elodie's gentle monster. Large, furry, and fading as Elodie outgrows her childhood fears.
- **The Collector** — A man in a gray coat who traps monsters in jars. Driven by his own fear of loss.
