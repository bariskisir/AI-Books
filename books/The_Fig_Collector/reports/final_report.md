# The Fig Collector — Final Report

## Summary

The Fig Collector is a standalone literary travel narrative set in 1938. Greek botanist Alekos Kappas, born in Smyrna before the 1923 population exchange, returns to the eastern Mediterranean to document heritage fig varieties before modern agriculture erases them. His journey through Turkey, Lesbos, and Lebanon becomes a meditation on displacement, memory, and what endures.

## Plot Synopsis

Alekos Kappas, a 52-year-old botanist at the University of Athens, receives a Swiss grant to document heritage fig varieties. He travels to Turkey (İzmir/Kuşadası/Aydın), where he meets Mustafa Yılmaz, custodian of a 300-year-old tree his father once studied; to Lesbos, where a beekeeping monk maintains a Byzantine-era orchard; and to Lebanon, where his former student Samia helps him document varieties mentioned in medieval Arabic texts. Through the journey, he collects not just specimens but the stories embedded in the trees — and begins to heal the wound of displacement he has carried since 1923.
