# The Fig Collector

**by Barış Kısır**

A literary travel narrative set in 1938, following Greek botanist Alekos Kappas as he journeys through Turkey, Lesbos, and Lebanon documenting heritage fig varieties — and the stories embedded in the trees.

## About

Spring 1938. Alekos Kappas is a Greek botanist born in Smyrna — a city he was forced to flee in 1923. Now a professor in Athens, he receives a grant to document the heritage fig varieties of the eastern Mediterranean before modern agriculture erases them forever.

Traveling through Turkish villages, a monastery orchard on Lesbos, and the Bekaa Valley of Lebanon, Alekos discovers that each tree carries a story. The great fig near Kuşadası, planted by a dervish three centuries ago and tended by a Turkish farmer whose father once shared tea with Alekos's own father. The Bride's Blush in Molyvos, where seven generations of women have married under its branches. The Damascus Pearl, so delicate it cannot travel — it must be eaten where it grows.

Through quiet encounters with farmers, monks, schoolteachers, and fellow botanists, Alekos learns that he is not just collecting figs. He is collecting memory. The fig tree — ancient, resilient, capable of growing from a cutting carried across the sea — becomes a metaphor for what endures when a homeland is lost.
