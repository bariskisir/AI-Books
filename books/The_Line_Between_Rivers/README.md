## Synopsis

In the drought-parched border town of La Cienega, the drowning death of eight-year-old Emiliano Mendoza in the nearly dry Rio Grande is ruled an accident. But the tragedy ripples through a community already frayed by the border wall, cartel violence, and the slow disappearance of the river itself. Dolores Reyes, the boy's grandmother, begins to suspect the death is connected to her husband's disappearance twenty years earlier. Mateo Cruz, a journalist, discovers his own father vanished in the same region at the same time. Elena Vasquez, a CBP officer, finds evidence pointing to her training officer. Santiago Rojas, a deported young man, is forced into the cartel's coyote trade. Four characters converge on the truth about a smuggling ring, a cover-up, and the invisible line that divides the living from the dead.
