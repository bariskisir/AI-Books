# The Garden of Unearthly Delights — Outline

## Premise

In a near future where genetic modification is outlawed, Iris Vasquez works in shadow as a bio-artist, crafting living sculptures from illegally spliced DNA. Her creations — glowing flora, symbiotic insect-plant hybrids, self-sustaining micro-ecosystems — are sought by underground collectors and hunted by the Bio-Regulation Bureau. When Agent Elara Cross is assigned to destroy her latest garden, she finds herself caught between the beauty of the work and the letter of the law. Meanwhile, a shadowy patron known only as the Collector wants Iris's art for himself, and he will burn everything she loves to own it.

## Core Cast

- **Iris Vesper** — Bio-artist. Former genetic researcher. She creates living sculptures in secret, believing beauty justifies the risk.
- **Agent Marcus Cross** — Senior regulator, Bioethics Compliance Authority. Methodical, principled, increasingly conflicted.
- **The Collector** — Wealthy patron of forbidden art. Real name unknown. He acquires bio-art by any means necessary.

## 50-Chapter Plan

1. Iris checks her hidden garden at midnight, tending a bioluminescent orchid that should not exist.
2. The BCA raids a warehouse across town — Iris's former studio — and destroys everything inside.
3. Agent Cross arrives at the raided studio and finds a dying creature that still reaches for light.
4. The Collector watches the raid from a distance, already planning his acquisition.
5. Iris burns her remaining notes and goes to ground, erasing her digital footprint.
6. Cross interrogates a captured courier who carried Iris's genetic material.
7. Iris rents a derelict greenhouse under a false name and begins again.
8. The Collector sends an encrypted message: an invitation, no strings attached.
9. Iris refuses the invitation and doubles down on her work, creating something new.
10. Cross pieces together Iris's identity from fragments: a former research geneticist.
11. The Collector's emissary visits Iris's new location, offering safety and resources.
12. Iris creates a symbiotic vine that glows in patterns — her most complex work yet.
13. Cross arrests Renko, Iris's seed-supplier, in a dawn raid.
14. The Collector offers Iris a genuine greenhouse — climate-controlled, hidden, safe.
15. Iris begins work on a full garden: a self-sustaining ecosystem of modified life.
16. Cross extracts information from Renko through hours of interrogation.
17. The Collector visits Iris in person — charming, dangerous, insistent.
18. Iris realizes she is squeezed between the law and the Collector's desire.
19. Her garden grows: phosphorescent moss, chimeric ferns, singing blossoms.
20. Cross finds the trace — a soil shipment to the derelict greenhouse address.
21. Iris packs her garden into crates and moves at two in the morning.
22. The Collector offers protection that Iris cannot trust.
23. The garden's central piece — a tree of glowing fruit — shows signs of awareness.
24. Cross stands in the abandoned greenhouse and feels the residual warmth of life.
25. The Collector reveals his plan: patent Iris's designs, mass-produce, sell.
26. Iris realizes her tree is not just art — it is a new form of life, thinking.
27. Cross and Iris meet. He comes to arrest her. She shows him the tree.
28. Cross makes a deal: let me document this, prove its scientific value.
29. The Collector's men raid the new location while Cross is there.
30. Cross takes a bullet meant for Iris and helps her escape.
31. Iris tends Cross's wound in a safe house neither of them trusts.
32. They plan together: expose the Collector, save the garden.
33. The Collector uses his political connections to expand the warrant.
34. Iris's tree begins to wilt without the proper environment.
35. Cross sees the tree, touches its fruit, and his worldview cracks open.
36. They decide to release the garden into a public space — undeniable.
37. Iris works frantically to stabilize the tree while Cross runs interference.
38. The Collector corners them in the safe house.
39. Iris triggers her garden's defense mechanisms — spore clouds, thorn walls.
40. Cross is shot covering their escape; Iris drags him through the garden.
41. Iris nurses Cross in a basement lab, using her genetic knowledge to heal him.
42. They plan to release the garden in the city's central botanical garden.
43. The Collector uses a court order to freeze Iris's assets and alias accounts.
44. Iris must choose: destroy her work or let the Collector own it.
45. Cross burns his badge and goes rogue, buying them time.
46. The final confrontation in the botanical garden at midnight.
47. Iris releases the garden — the plants spread, glow, sing in public view.
48. Public outcry saves the garden from immediate destruction.
49. Iris is arrested but the trial becomes a referendum on bio-art.
50. The garden thrives under court protection. Iris works from a prison lab. Cross visits on Sundays.
