# The Iron Whale — Outline

## Premise

In the spring of 1968, the American diesel-electric submarine USS *Cuttlefish* runs a covert listening patrol off the edge of Soviet waters, recording the acoustic signatures of a new class of warship. When a fire breaks out near the auxiliary machinery space — close enough to the reactor compartment to terrify a crew that does not fully trust their boat — the *Cuttlefish* loses her radio mast, her trim, and her contact with command. Trapped deep with thinning air, a possible saboteur aboard, and hostile surface ships overhead, Executive Officer Daniel Reyes must hold a frightened crew together while the captain weakens and the choice narrows to one impossible question: stay silent and suffocate, or surface into the guns of an enemy that must never know they were here.

## Core Cast

- **Lt. Cmdr. Daniel Reyes** — Executive officer. Methodical, tired, the son of a merchant sailor. The man who must decide.
- **Cmdr. Walter Hale** — Captain. Burned and concussed early; his judgment becomes a question the boat must answer.
- **Chief of the Boat Tom Garrity** — Senior enlisted man. Gruff, loyal, the deck plates made human.
- **Lt. Avery Cole** — Engineering officer. Young, brilliant, blames himself for the fire.
- **Sonarman First Class Eddie Mahon** — Ears of the boat. Hears what no one else can.
- **Doc Pruitt** — Independent-duty corpsman. The only medicine aboard.
- **Petty Officer Karl Brandt** — Quiet electrician with a forged past; the man the suspicion settles on.
- **Lt. (jg) Sam Okafor** — Navigator and communications officer.
- **Seaman Lewis Tran** — Youngest aboard, a torpedoman striker.
- **Chief Auxiliaryman Joe Salas** — Keeps the air, the water, the cold machinery alive.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Patrol (1–12)
1. The *Cuttlefish* rigs for ultra-quiet; Reyes logs the contact they are not supposed to have.
2. Captain Hale explains the mission's terms: hear everything, be heard by nothing.
3. Mahon catches the new warship's screws on sonar; the recorders turn.
4. The daily grind of a hidden boat — heat, sweat, the smell of men and oil.
5. Cole frets over the failing auxiliary machinery and a corner he was told to cut.
6. Brandt keeps to himself; Garrity notes it without saying so.
7. A near-detection by a passing trawler tightens every nerve.
8. Reyes and Hale weigh how long to stay; orders say three more days.
9. Tran's first real fear; Reyes teaches him to breathe through it.
10. A small electrical fault, fixed and forgotten — or not.
11. The boat goes deep to avoid a patrol; the hull speaks.
12. Fire. Smoke in the after spaces, and the lights go.

### Act II — The Casualty (13–26)
13. Damage control in the dark; the fire is fought hand to hand.
14. Hale is hurt — burned arm, a blow to the head — and Reyes takes the conn.
15. The fire is out, but the radio mast is dead and the boat hangs heavy.
16. Air begins to foul; CO2 climbs while scrubbers fight to keep up.
17. Cole maps the damage and finds the fire's start was no accident.
18. Suspicion enters the boat like a second smoke.
19. Doc Pruitt fights to keep Hale lucid; the captain drifts.
20. Surface contacts multiply overhead — they are being hunted, or watched.
21. Reyes inventories the air, the battery, the time they have left.
22. A second small failure; Salas suspects a hand, not chance.
23. The crew learns the truth they were spared: how close the reactor is.
24. Brandt's record won't add up; Garrity brings it to Reyes.
25. Hale wakes long enough to give an order Reyes cannot obey.
26. The battery falters; the boat begins to grow cold.

### Act III — Pressure (27–38)
27. Reyes confronts Brandt; the man's answer is not what anyone expected.
28. The real saboteur question splits the wardroom.
29. Mahon hears a sonar pinging — they have been found, or nearly.
30. Rationing air: half the crew lies still to breathe slower.
31. Cole and Salas attempt a jury-rig to clear the foul air.
32. A man panics in the dark; Reyes talks him down.
33. The captain worsens; Doc admits what he cannot do.
34. Tran proves himself in the flooded bilge.
35. Garrity and Reyes settle the matter of command between them.
36. The truth about the fire surfaces — fear, not treason.
37. A vote that is not a vote: the crew's will to surface or hold.
38. The jury-rig buys hours, not days; the math still ends one way.

### Act IV — The Surface (39–50)
39. Reyes commits to a plan: rise slow, listen, choose at the last moment.
40. Trim by hand, blowing tanks they can barely afford to blow.
41. The ascent — the hull groaning back through the layers.
42. Mahon counts the screws above and names the danger.
43. A destroyer dead overhead; the boat hangs beneath it, holding breath.
44. Hale, lucid for a moment, gives Reyes the only order that matters: live.
45. The choice — periscope depth into a gray dawn and a foreign flag.
46. Surfaced, dead in the water, signaling the only thing they can.
47. The standoff on the surface — guns trained, no one firing.
48. A line, a tow, a salvaged silence; what is said and what is buried.
49. The long passage home and the report that can never be written true.
50. Reyes ashore, the boat behind him, the weight of the men who breathed.
