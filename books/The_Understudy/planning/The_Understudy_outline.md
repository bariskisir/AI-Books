# The Understudy — Outline

## Premise

Corina Vance has spent three years as the understudy for Lena Voss, Broadway's reigning star, in a sold-out psychological thriller called *The Hollow Hour*. Night after night she watches from the wings as Lena receives standing ovations for a role Corina knows she could play better. When a mysterious backstage accident leaves Lena hospitalized with a fractured skull, Corina finally gets her curtain call. But as she steps into the spotlight, she begins to suspect that the attack was meant for her — and that someone is still watching from the shadows, waiting for the understudy to fall.

## Core Cast

- **Corina Vance** — The understudy. Thirty-two, talented, overlooked. She has waited her whole life for this chance and will not let it go without a fight.
- **Lena Voss** — The lead. Forty-one, magnetic, ruthless. She built her career on brilliance and backroom deals. Now she lies in a hospital bed, unable to speak.
- **Director Vance** — Corina's father and the show's director. A Broadway legend who built his reputation on perfection. He chose Lena over his own daughter, and that choice haunts them both.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Act I — The Understudy Waits

1. Corina watches from the wings as Lena commands the stage — the role that should have been hers.
2. Corina finds a threatening note tucked into her dressing room mirror: "Step back or step out."
3. During a dress rehearsal, a sandbag counterweight nearly crushes Lena onstage.
4. Director Vance reviews the incident with stage management, ruling it an accident.
5. Corina visits her father's office and sees a photo of her mother, a dancer who died mid-performance.
6. Lena invites Corina for a drink, probing for weakness with surgical precision.
7. Corina meets Rourke, the lighting tech who lingers too long near her station.
8. The opening night of *The Hollow Hour* — the curtain rises on a full house.
9. Lena performs flawlessly; Corina watches from the wings, mouthing every line.
10. After the show, Corina discovers another note: "She dies tonight. You're next."

### Act II — The Fall

11. Lena collapses during the second act of the fourth performance — a scream, then silence.
12. Backstage chaos: paramedics, whispers, the stage manager's white face.
13. Corina is told to finish the show. She steps onstage to a stunned audience.
14. The performance is raw, electric — Corina finds something in the role Lena never did.
15. Director Vance watches from the house, seeing his daughter for the first time.
16. At the hospital, Lena's condition is critical — fractured skull, subdural hematoma.
17. Detective Maris arrives to investigate. She interviews the cast and crew.
18. Corina learns that the sandbag counterweight had a cut rope — not a frayed one.
19. The police classify Lena's fall as suspicious. Corina becomes a person of interest.
20. Rourke tells Corina someone was in the lighting booth during the show, unauthorized.

### Act III — The Spotlight

21. Corina takes over as the new lead. The marquee changes: "Starring Corina Vance."
22. Her first full show as Lena's replacement draws raves from the critics.
23. Corina finds Rourke's dead body in the lighting booth — a syringe on the floor.
24. Detective Maris tightens her focus on Corina: she had motive, means, opportunity.
25. Director Vance tells Corina he believes in her — but his eyes say something else.
26. Corina searches Lena's dressing room and finds a journal full of paranoid scribbles.
27. The journal mentions a "benefactor" who financed Lena's career from the beginning.
28. Corina is questioned at the precinct for six hours. She gives nothing.
29. The stage manager, Delia, quits, saying the theater is cursed.
30. A third note appears in Corina's costume: "You were never supposed to go on."

### Act IV — The Unraveling

31. Corina realizes the handwriting on the notes matches Lena's journal.
32. Detective Maris shares a detail: the rope cut on the sandbag was left-handed.
33. Corina recalls that Lena signs autographs with her left hand — but performs right-handed.
34. Corina confronts her father about what he knows. He deflects.
35. Corina discovers that Lena's "benefactor" is Director Vance — her father funded Lena's career.
36. The revelation hits: her father chose Lena over her, paid for Lena's rise.
37. Corina digs deeper and finds that her mother's death was ruled an accident too.
38. She finds old newspaper clippings — her mother was also an understudy who replaced the lead.
39. The pattern emerges: a lead falls, the understudy rises, then the understudy dies.
40. Corina realizes she is not the target. She is the bait.

### Act V — The Curtain Call

41. Corina sets a trap: she announces she is leaving the show, giving a final performance.
42. She arranges a private meeting with Detective Maris, sharing all her evidence.
43. On the night of her "final" performance, the theater is wired with surveillance.
44. During the curtain call, the lights go out. A scuffle in the dark.
45. Corina is grabbed from behind — a needle presses against her neck.
46. The lights come up. Director Vance stands with a syringe, his face unreadable.
47. Corina speaks the line she wrote for this moment — a confession from the stage.
48. Police move in. Director Vance is arrested for the murders of Lena's predecessors.
49. Lena wakes from her coma. She cannot remember the fall — but she remembers the fear.
50. Corina takes her bow. The house rises. She owns the stage.
