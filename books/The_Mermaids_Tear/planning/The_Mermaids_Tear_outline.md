# The Mermaid's Tear — Outline

## Premise

In 1924, deep in the Flores Sea of the Dutch East Indies, a young pearl diver named Suli brings up a pearl that glows with its own light. Word spreads across the archipelago, drawing a ruthless German treasure hunter, a conflicted local Raja caught between tradition and colonial power, and a band of Celebes pirates. As white sails appear on the horizon and competing claims collide, Suli must navigate treachery, legend, and the truth behind the Mermaid's Tear — a pearl said to be the frozen grief of a sea spirit who drowned for love.

## Core Cast

- **Suli** — A pearl diver from a small fishing village on Komodo Island. Quiet, observant, haunted by his father's disappearance at sea. He does not seek the pearl's power, but he will not let it be stolen.
- **Captain Joachim Brandt** — A German seaman turned treasure hunter, scarred by the war, commanding the schooner *Seeschlange*. He wants the Mermaid's Tear to buy his way back to respectability.
- **The Raja Alauddin** — The Dutch-backed ruler of the local sultanate. Educated in Batavia, he serves two masters and trusts neither. He believes the pearl holds the key to his people's independence.
- **Ibrahim** — Suli's elder brother, a fisherman who wants nothing to do with the pearl. Protective, pragmatic, afraid.
- **De Groot** — The Dutch colonial agent on Flores. Cold, bureaucratic, patient. He represents the unseen hand of empire.
- **Rajah Laut** — The pirate queen of the eastern passages. Her real name is Melati. She captains the *Naga Hitam* and answers to no flag.
- **Tuan Khalid** — An old pearl merchant from Makassar. He knows the old stories. He warns Suli.

## 50 Chapter Plan

### Part I — The Pearl Bed (Chapters 1–10)

1. **The Dive** — Suli descends forty feet through green water to the oyster beds. The bottom is litter with the bones of old wrecks.
2. **Light in the Shallows** — Among the oysters, a pulse of light. Suli pries open a shell and finds a pearl that burns cold in his palm.
3. **Return** — Suli surfaces to the morning sun. His boat is empty. He hides the pearl in his sarong.
4. **The Village** — Suli walks home through Kampung Laut. The elders sit in the shade. He says nothing about the pearl.
5. **Ibrahim's Worry** — Suli's brother sees something in his face. "What did you find?" Suli lies.
6. **The Glow at Night** — Suli cannot sleep. The pearl throbs with light under his sleeping mat. He takes it to the water's edge.
7. **Tuan Khalid's Visit** — The old merchant arrives by prahu. He sees the light in Suli's hut before Suli speaks of it.
8. **The Story** — Khalid tells the legend of the Mermaid's Tear — a spirit bride who wept for her drowned lover, her tears turning to pearls.
9. **The Warning** — Khalid warns Suli: "There are men who would kill for this. Hide it. Or throw it back."
10. **The Schooner** — A white sail appears on the horizon. Captain Brandt has heard the rumor already.

### Part II — The Hunters (Chapters 11–20)

11. **Brandt's Landing** — The *Seeschlange* anchors off Kampung Laut. Brandt comes ashore in a launch, a revolver at his belt, a map in his hand.
12. **The Offer** — Brandt finds Suli in the market. He offers guilders, then threats. Suli says he has no pearl.
13. **Search** — Brandt's men tear apart Suli's hut. They find nothing. Suli has hidden the pearl in the reef.
14. **The Raja's Summons** — A runner arrives from the palace. The Raja Alauddin demands Suli appear before him.
15. **The Palace** — Suli travels inland to the Raja's wooden palace. Dutch flags fly alongside the Sultanate banner.
16. **The Raja's Question** — Alauddin is polite, educated. He asks about the pearl. Suli again denies.
17. **De Groot's Shadow** — The Dutch agent is present at the audience, silent. Suli feels his gaze like a weight.
18. **Brandt and the Raja** — Brandt visits the palace. The two men circle each other. Both want the pearl. Neither trusts the other.
19. **Ibrahim's Plea** — Ibrahim finds Suli at the river. "Give it to them. It will destroy us."
20. **The Pearl's Song** — Suli retrieves the pearl at night. Under the moonlight, it pulses in rhythm with the tide. He feels it calling.

### Part III — The Chase (Chapters 21–30)

21. **Pirates** — Smoke on the water. The *Naga Hitam* appears, black hull, red sails. Rajah Laut has come for the pearl.
22. **The Village Burns** — Rajah Laut's men raid Kampung Laut. They burn huts, question fishermen. Ibrahim is beaten.
23. **Suli Flees** — Suli takes a fishing prahu and heads east, the pearl wrapped in oilcloth. He leaves the village burning behind him.
24. **The Reef Passage** — Suli navigates the inner reef, knowing the channels. The *Naga Hitam* gives chase but cannot follow.
25. **Rajah Laut's Rage** — Melati stands at the prow, watching Suli escape. She orders a course for the eastern islands. She will find him.
26. **Brandt Pursues** — The *Seeschlange* weighs anchor. Brandt has his own charts. He knows where Suli is heading.
27. **De Groot's Letter** — In the Dutch residency, De Groot composes a coded message to Batavia. The Mermaid's Tear is more than a legend. It must be secured.
28. **Alone at Sea** — Suli drifts for two days. He is out of water. The sun burns. He talks to the pearl.
29. **The Prahu** — A fishing boat finds Suli half-dead. An old Bugis couple takes him aboard.
30. **The Old Sailor's Tale** — The Bugis husband tells Suli of a cave on an island to the east, sacred to the sea spirits. "If the pearl came from the sea, that is where it must return."

### Part IV — The Island (Chapters 31–40)

31. **The Lost Island** — Suli sees it at dawn — a volcanic pinnacle ringed by white sand. A cave mouth gapes in the cliff face.
32. **The Cave** — Suli swims into the cave. Inside, the walls are lined with mother-of-pearl. The water glows.
33. **The Garden of Pearls** — The cave floor is carpeted with luminous pearls, hundreds of them. Suli understands: the Mermaid's Tear is not one pearl. It is a place.
34. **Brandt Arrives** — The *Seeschlange* drops anchor off the island. Brandt sees Suli's prahu beached on the sand.
35. **The Confrontation** — Brandt finds Suli in the cave. He sees the pearls. His eyes go wide with greed.
36. **The Pirate Queen** — The *Naga Hitam* appears, cutting off escape. Rajah Laut has followed Brandt.
37. **Three-Way Standoff** — Brandt, Suli, and Melati face each other in the cave of pearls. Guns drawn. Nobody moves.
38. **The Raja's Gambit** — The Raja's war prahu arrives, flying both the Dutch flag and the Sultanate standard. De Groot is with him.
39. **The Offer** — Alauddin offers a deal: the pearls belong to the Sultanate, a national treasure. Brandt can take a share. Melati can take a share. Suli will be protected.
40. **Suli's Choice** — Suli looks at the pearls, at the armed men, at the greed in every face. He makes a decision.

### Part V — The Tear (Chapters 41–50)

41. **The Legend Fulfilled** — Suli tells them the truth: the pearls glow because of a rare microorganism in the cave's water. They lose their light once taken from this place.
42. **The Test** — Brandt does not believe him. He snatches a handful of pearls and carries them into the sun. They dim and go dark within an hour.
43. **The Rage of Brandt** — Brandt turns on Suli, pistol raised. "You knew. You knew all along."
44. **Melati's Choice** — Rajah Laut steps between them. "He did not make the pearl. It was never meant to be sold."
45. **De Groot's Calculation** — De Groot sees the scientific value. He will send a report to Batavia. The cave can be studied.
46. **The Raja's Grief** — Alauddin realizes his dream of independence cannot be bought with dim stones. He orders the cave sealed.
47. **The Pact** — They agree: the cave will be a secret. Brandt will report empty waters. De Groot will claim the legend was false. Melati will take her payment in other goods. Suli will be left in peace.
48. **The Return** — Suli goes back to Kampung Laut. They are rebuilding. Ibrahim embraces him.
49. **The Last Pearl** — Suli kept one pearl. The first one. He holds it above the water. It does not glow. The light died when it left the cave.
50. **The Sea Takes It Back** — Suli rows out to the reef where it all began. He drops the pearl into the water. It sinks through green light, down to the oyster beds, to sleep among the bones of old wrecks. The circle closes.
