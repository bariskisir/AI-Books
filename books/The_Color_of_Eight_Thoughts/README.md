# The Color of Eight Thoughts

**Author:** Barış Kısır
**Genre:** Literary Fiction
**Subgenre:** Non-Human Point of View / Animal Consciousness
**Model:** sonnet-4.6
**Chapters:** 50

## Synopsis

The entire short, brilliant life of a single octopus on a Mediterranean reef, told from inside her wholly non-human mind — a creature who thinks in color, tastes the world through eight half-independent arms, and answers at last the one fatal imperative of her kind.

On a Mediterranean reef scattered with the amphorae of an ancient shipwreck lives a single common octopus, and this is the whole of her short, brilliant life, told from inside a mind that is wholly unlike ours. She has no language but a vivid, coherent intelligence. She thinks in color — her shifting chromatophores are her moods and her thoughts — and she tastes the world through the suckers of eight arms that each half-think for themselves. Her eye reads light and shadow but not color; color belongs to the inside, to feeling, to the self.
