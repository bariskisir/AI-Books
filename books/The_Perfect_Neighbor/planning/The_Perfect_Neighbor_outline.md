# The Perfect Neighbor — Outline

## Premise

Claire, a sleep-deprived new mother recovering from a difficult postpartum period, moves to the quiet suburb of Stonewood looking for peace and community. Her neighbor Becca seems like a dream — warm, helpful, perfectly put together with a picture-perfect home and family. But small inconsistencies in Becca's stories begin to nag at Claire. A maiden name that changes. A hometown that shifts. A childhood memory that doesn't add up. As Claire digs deeper, she discovers that Becca is not who she claims to be — the woman has stolen her identity from someone else, and the original woman has been missing for years. With Detective Harris piecing together the cold case, Claire realizes the perfect neighbor may be a predator wearing someone else's life.

## Core Cast

- **Claire Brennan** — New mother, former journalist, struggling with postpartum anxiety. Observant, instinct-driven, recovering her confidence.
- **Becca Porter (aka Rebecca Holloway / Angela Vance)** — The perfect neighbor. Warm, capable, generous. Also a woman with at least three stolen identities and a trail of erased lives behind her.
- **Detective Marcus Harris** — Cold case detective from the neighboring precinct. Quiet, methodical, haunted by the unsolved disappearance of the real Rebecca Holloway.
- **Tom Brennan** — Claire's husband. Loving but distracted by work. Wants to believe the best about everyone.
- **The Real Rebecca Holloway** — The woman whose identity Becca stole. A missing person whose case went cold five years ago.

## 50-Chapter Plan

1. Claire and Tom move into the Stonewood house with newborn Lily; Claire meets Becca for the first time.
2. Becca brings a casserole and offers help; everything about her seems too good to be true.
3. Claire's postpartum anxiety makes her hyper-observant; she notices Becca's story about her hometown shifts.
4. Claire searches for Becca on social media and finds almost nothing — no history before Stonewood.
5. Becca mentions growing up in Ohio; two days later she says she grew up in Pennsylvania.
6. Claire finds a photograph in an old Stonewood community newsletter featuring Becca at a fundraiser three years ago.
7. Claire asks Becca about the fundraiser; Becca's face freezes for a split second before recovering.
8. Tom dismisses Claire's concerns; he says she's overtired and suspicious of everyone.
9. Claire drives to the county records office to look up Becca's property deed.
10. The deed lists the owner as "Rebecca Holloway," not Rebecca Porter — Becca's maiden name.
11. Claire searches "Rebecca Holloway" online and finds a missing persons report from five years ago.
12. The missing woman's photo looks like an older, softer version of Becca — but the cheekbones match.
13. Claire contacts the detective listed on the missing person case: Detective Marcus Harris.
14. Harris is wary but agrees to meet; he says the case has haunted him for years.
15. Harris reveals the real Rebecca Holloway vanished three months before "Becca" appeared in Stonewood.
16. Claire begins keeping a journal of every inconsistency she notices in Becca's behavior.
17. Becca invites Claire to a playgroup; Claire watches her interact with other mothers, looking for cracks.
18. A mother mentions Becca's "terrible childhood" story; it contradicts what Becca told Claire.
19. Claire photographs Becca's car license plate and runs it through a DMV contact.
20. The car is registered to "Angela Vance," a woman from two states away.
21. Harris runs Angela Vance and finds she matches another missing person case from seven years ago.
22. Claire realizes she is living next door to a serial identity thief — and possibly a killer.
23. Becca notices Claire watching her; she leaves a single white rose on Claire's porch — a warning.
24. Tom finally takes Claire seriously after seeing the white rose and hearing the evidence.
25. Claire installs a nanny cam aimed at the shared driveway and Becca's front door.
26. Becca knocks on Claire's door late at night, desperate, saying she needs to tell her something.
27. Becca confesses she changed her name to escape an abusive ex-boyfriend — a plausible lie.
28. Claire almost believes her until Harris calls with news: the ex-boyfriend doesn't exist.
29. Harris finds a third identity: Becca was also "Megan Foster" in a town four hundred miles away.
30. Claire researches Megan Foster and finds another missing woman — case still open.
31. The pattern is clear: the real Becca, Angela, and Megan all vanished and this woman took their names.
32. Harris asks Claire to wear a wire during a coffee date with Becca.
33. Claire agrees; the police fit her with a hidden recorder.
34. Claire meets Becca for coffee and steers the conversation toward Becca's past.
35. Becca tells a detailed story about her mother's death that contradicts what she said two months ago.
36. After the meeting, Harris tells Claire the recording is enough for a warrant but not an arrest.
37. Becca's behavior shifts; she becomes distant, then hostile, then falsely apologetic.
38. Claire finds a box of identification documents — driver's licenses, social security cards — buried in Becca's trash.
39. Harris gets the warrant; the police prepare to arrest Becca.
40. Becca doesn't come home that night; Claire watches her empty driveway with growing dread.
41. Harris calls: Becca's car has been found at the train station thirty miles away.
42. Claire realizes her own identifying documents are missing from her home office.
43. Becca has taken Claire's passport and social security card — she was going to become Claire next.
44. Harris tracks Becca to a bus station in a neighboring state; local police detain her.
45. Claire confronts Becca in the police interrogation room via video link.
46. Becca smiles and says nothing; her silence is more frightening than any confession.
47. The police find evidence connecting Becca to three missing women over twelve years.
48. Claire struggles to feel safe again; she sees Becca's face in every stranger.
49. Harris closes the case; the real Rebecca Holloway's remains are found in a shallow grave.
50. Claire sits on her porch with Lily, watching the sunset, knowing the perfect neighbor was never real.

## Notes

- Each chapter should be 200-400 words.
- Maintain the tension of domestic normalcy versus hidden threat.
- Claire's postpartum anxiety is both a vulnerability and a strength — her hypervigilance is what saves her.
- Becca should remain complex — not a cartoon villain, but a deeply damaged person who learned to consume other people's lives.
