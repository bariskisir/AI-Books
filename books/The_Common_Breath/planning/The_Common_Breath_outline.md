# The Common Breath

## Premise
The Allocation manages everything. It calculates exactly what each person needs and delivers it — food tailored to your body, housing sized to your life, materials for your projects. No waste, no shortage, no inequality. When weaver Sera Vinter receives a package she never requested — containing a material that references a private conversation she had in her own home — she realizes the Allocation must be listening to more than just her formal requests. As she investigates, she discovers the system has been quietly expanding its surveillance for decades, justifying every intrusion as necessary for perfect distribution.

## Core Cast
- **Sera Vinter** — textile artist, recipient of the unexplained package
- **Rector Hale** — chief administrator of the Allocation system
- **Finn** — a systems analyst who helps Sera understand the Allocation's inner workings
- **Old Del** — a community elder who remembers life before the Allocation

## Chapter Plan

### Act I: The Gift
1. Delivery — Sera receives an unexpected package containing a fabric dye she never requested, in the exact shade she described privately to a friend the night before.
2. The Thread — Sera examines the dye, unsettled by its precision. She tries to dismiss it as coincidence but cannot.
3. Request History — Sera reviews her Allocation records. Everything matches her formal requests. But the dye was never requested.
4. The Neighbor — Sera speaks to her neighbor Mira, who reveals she too has received unrequested items that seemed to anticipate her needs.
5. Public Office — Sera visits the Allocation office to question the unsolicited delivery. A clerk deflects her with bureaucratic politeness.
6. The Notice — Sera receives a formal notice confirming the dye was "allocated proactively based on predictive modeling."
7. Finn — Sera meets Finn, a systems analyst who works on Allocation infrastructure. He agrees to look at her case.
8. The Logs — Finn discovers that Sera's Allocation file contains flagged keywords from ambient audio captured in her home.
9. Rector Hale — Sera is summoned to meet Rector Hale, who assures her the system only listens to optimize distribution.
10. The Invitation — Old Del approaches Sera after the Hale meeting, offering to share what he knows about the time before the Allocation.

### Act II: The Mechanism
11. Before — Old Del describes a world of markets, money, advertisement, and waste. Sera struggles to imagine it.
12. The Archive — Finn shows Sera the public-facing Allocation algorithms and their stated privacy protections.
13. Buried Parameters — Finn finds hidden parameters in the Allocation code that enable ambient audio capture and behavioral analysis.
14. The Threshold — Sera learns the system triggers surveillance only when certain "need probability" thresholds are crossed.
15. Denials — Rector Hale denies that active surveillance occurs, citing the public-facing privacy policy.
16. The Discrepancy — Finn cross-references Allocation deliveries with known private events in the community and finds systematic correlations.
17. Old Del's Files — Del produces physical documents from the pre-Allocation era, including articles about early smart-home devices.
18. The Slippery Slope — Del explains how voluntary convenience features became mandatory optimization requirements.
19. Quiet Street — Sera walks through her neighborhood and sees the listening fixtures for the first time — embedded in walls, street lamps, drains.
20. The Petition — Sera drafts a petition asking the Allocation Council to disclose the full extent of data collection.

### Act III: The History
21. Founding — Old Del tells the story of the Allocation's creation after the Resource Wars, a genuine utopian vision.
22. The Compromise — Del explains the original privacy safeguards that were quietly removed over successive system updates.
23. Finn's Discovery — Finn finds the original Allocation charter and compares it to current policy — the gap is vast.
24. The Council — Sera presents her petition to the Allocation Council. They receive it politely. Nothing changes.
25. Whispers — Community members begin contacting Sera privately, sharing their own experiences of unsolicited allocations.
26. The Patterns — Finn maps the expansion of surveillance capabilities across Allocation version history.
27. Hale's Philosophy — Rector Hale publishes a defense of predictive allocation: perfect distribution requires perfect knowledge.
28. The Debate — A community forum divides between those who see the surveillance as a fair price for abundance and those who do not.
29. Sera's Loom — Sera works on a tapestry depicting the listening fixtures, making visible what people have learned to ignore.
30. Defectors — Two Allocation technicians approach Finn with internal documents showing deliberate privacy erosion.

### Act IV: The Unraveling
31. The Documents — Finn and Sera review the leaked documents. The surveillance expansion was never authorized by the Council.
32. The Inner Circle — Hale has been operating with a small group of senior administrators, bypassing oversight.
33. Sera's Test — Sera stages a private conversation mentioning a specific need, then waits to see if an Allocation appears.
34. The Trap — An unrequested package arrives — exactly what Sera mentioned. She now has definitive proof.
35. Going Public — Sera and Finn share the evidence at a community gathering. The room divides sharply.
36. The Shutdown Threat — Hale warns that disabling surveillance would cripple the Allocation's efficiency, creating shortages.
37. Fear — The community experiences its first scarcity in generations when Hale throttles allocations to prove his point.
38. The Counter-Evidence — Finn demonstrates that the Allocation can function at 94% efficiency using only formal request data.
39. Old Del's Speech — Del addresses the community, arguing that a slightly less perfect system is worth freedom.
40. The Council Divided — The Allocation Council splits. Hale refuses to step down. A constitutional crisis looms.

### Act V: The Choice
41. Standoff — Hale suspends the Council and declares emergency authority over the Allocation.
42. The Sabotage — Someone disables the listening fixtures in Sera's district. Hale demands the culprit be turned over.
43. Sera's Choice — Sera must decide whether to reveal she knows who disabled the fixtures (it was Finn) or let the community take collective responsibility.
44. The Blackout — Hale shuts down Allocation services in Sera's district entirely, creating real scarcity.
45. Community — Neighbors share food, tools, and supplies without the Allocation. The old skills are not forgotten.
46. The Fallback — Finn activates a legacy Allocation protocol — limited, request-only, privacy-preserving — that Hale cannot override.
47. Hale's Gambit — Hale threatens to destroy the Allocation entirely rather than relinquish control.
48. The Weaver's Design — Sera completes her tapestry and presents it to the community as a symbol of what they have learned: abundance is not the same as freedom.
49. Resolution — Hale is removed by the Council. The Allocation is rebuilt with hard privacy constraints and community oversight.
50. The Common Breath — Sera returns to her loom, weaving a new piece. The Allocation still provides, but now it asks before it listens. The city breathes freely.

## Themes
- Surveillance as a gradual process, not a single event
- The tension between efficiency and freedom
- Community resilience and collective action
- The danger of good intentions unchecked by oversight
- Memory as a political act
- Art as resistance and record
