# The Jaguar Codex

**Genre:** Historical / Archaeological Thriller  
**Setting:** 1920s Yucatan Peninsula, Mexico, with flashbacks to the Postclassic Maya period (c. 1450-1520)  
**Author:** Barış Kısır  
**Model:** deepseek-v4-flash  
**Status:** Complete (TXT + EPUB)

## Premise

In 1924, British archaeologist Dr. Thomas Ashworth arrives in Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula driven by a single lead: a dying Jesuit archivist's letter describing a hidden Maya codex that was never destroyed by the conquistadors. Local Maya woman Ixchel Ku, descendant of the last priestly scribes of Chichen Itza, is pressed into service as his reluctant guide. Together they navigate treacherous jungle, hostile hacienda owners, and a rival archaeologist with his own secret agenda. When they unearth the legendary Codex of Kukulkan, they find not only astronomical tables and ritual calendars but a historical chronicle of the Spanish invasion written by Maya scribes as it happened — including revelations of internecine Maya warfare that made conquest possible.

## Characters

- **Dr. Thomas Ashworth** — 34-year-old British archaeologist from Cambridge
- **Ixchel Ku** — Maya guide and translator, descendant of the last priestly scribes
- **Professor Alistair Finch** — Ashworth's former mentor, a rival archaeologist
- **Don Rafael Mendoza** — Wealthy Yucatecan hacendado with his own agenda
- **Dr. Eliza Whitcombe** — British botanist specializing in organic preservation
