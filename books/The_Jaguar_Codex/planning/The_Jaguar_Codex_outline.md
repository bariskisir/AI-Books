# The Jaguar Codex — Outline

## Premise

In 1924, British archaeologist Dr. Thomas Ashworth arrives in Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula driven by a single lead: a dying Jesuit archivist's letter describing a hidden Maya codex that was never destroyed by the conquistadors. Local Maya woman Ixchel Ku, descendant of the last priestly scribes of Chichen Itza, is pressed into service as his reluctant guide. Together they navigate treacherous jungle, hostile hacienda owners, and a rival archaeologist with his own secret agenda. When they unearth the legendary Codex of Kukulkan, they find not only astronomical tables and ritual calendars but a historical chronicle of the Spanish invasion written by Maya scribes as it happened — including revelations of internecine Maya warfare that made conquest possible. As word of the discovery spreads, competing forces converge: a wealthy landowner who wants the codex for his private collection, a European museum scheming through Ashworth's former mentor, and the Mexican federal government asserting national patrimony. Ixchel emerges as the codex's true interpreter, challenging the academic establishment and confronting those who would suppress its uncomfortable truths about colonialism.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Thomas Ashworth** — 34-year-old British archaeologist from Cambridge, earnest and principled but naive about colonial dynamics. Specializes in Mesoamerican epigraphy.
- **Ixchel Ku** — Sharp-witted Maya woman in her late twenties, translator and guide. Raised by her grandfather, the last traditional priest in her village, who taught her to read Maya glyphs in secret.
- **Professor Alistair Finch** — Ashworth's former doctoral supervisor at Oxford, in his sixties. Charismatic and manipulative, sees the Maya as a dead civilization to be catalogued and owned by European institutions.
- **Don Rafael Mendoza** — Wealthy Yucatecan hacendado in his fifties, ruthless in protecting his interests. Wants the codex for personal prestige.
- **Dr. Eliza Whitcombe** — 32-year-old British botanist specializing in tropical plant resins and organic preservation. Pragmatic, resourceful, politically astute.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Dispatch (Chapters 1-10)
1. **The Lost Dispatch** — Thomas Ashworth in Cambridge receives a letter from a dying Jesuit archivist in Merida describing a hidden Maya codex.
2. **The Yucatan Shore** — Ashworth arrives at Progreso, meets the heat and chaos of the Yucatan coast, hires Ixchel Ku as translator.
3. **The Jesuit Archive** — Ashworth and Ixchel search the Jesuit library in Merida for clues to the codex location; Ixchel proves indispensable.
4. **The Hacienda San Felipe** — They travel to Don Rafael Mendoza's hacienda, where the codex is rumored to be hidden. Mendoza is charming but evasive.
5. **The Serpent's Gate** — Ixchel identifies a carved serpent portal in the jungle that matches descriptions from the Jesuit letter.
6. **The Witness of Blood** — Flashback to 1519: Maya scribes witness the first Spanish contact and begin a secret chronicle.
7. **Breaking the Seal** — Ashworth and Ixchel enter the hidden chamber beneath the serpent gate.
8. **The First Chamber** — They find an antechamber with murals and a broken ceramic vessel, but the codex is not there.
9. **The Rival's Approach** — Professor Finch arrives unexpectedly at the hacienda, feigning collegial support.
10. **The Fragment Speaks** — Ixchel deciphers a fragment of codex page left behind, pointing to a secondary location.

### Part II: The Serpent's Mirror (Chapters 11-20)
11. **Ixchel's Burden** — Ixchel's backstory: her grandfather's death, her secret knowledge of glyphs, her estrangement from her village.
12. **The Road to Cobá** — The group travels to the ruins of Cobá through mosquito-ridden jungle. Tensions rise between Ashworth and Finch.
13. **The Cenote of Mirrors** — They discover a cenote with reflective pools that was used for ritual scrying.
14. **The Ambush** — Armed men hired by a rival collector attack the camp; Eliza Whitcombe reveals her resourcefulness.
15. **The Curandera's Hut** — Wounded, they take shelter with an aging Maya healer who speaks of the old prophecies.
16. **The Serpent's Mirror** — Ixchel realizes the cenote itself is a map; the reflection at a certain hour reveals hidden glyphs on the cave walls.
17. **The Caracol Puzzle** — At the Caracol observatory in Chichen Itza, they decode an astronomical alignment that reveals a cache location.
18. **Finch Revealed** — Ashworth discovers Finch has been sending coded telegrams to a European museum about their progress.
19. **The Night of the Equinox** — The spring equinox: the serpent shadow descends Kukulkan's pyramid, and the alignment reveals a hidden door at the base.
20. **The Serpent Descends** — They enter the secret chamber beneath the pyramid where the codex has been hidden for four centuries.

### Part III: The Codex of Kukulkan (Chapters 21-30)
21. **The Archive of Shadows** — Inside the chamber: dozens of codices, ritual objects, and the preserved remains of the last scribes.
22. **The Codex of Kukulkan** — Ashworth holds the codex — bark paper covered in precise glyphs and brilliant illustrations.
23. **The First Reading** — Ixchel begins to read the codex: astronomical tables, ritual calendars — and then the invasion chronicle.
24. **Mendoza's Demand** — Don Rafael Mendoza arrives and claims ownership of the codex as his ancestral property.
25. **The Journey to Merida** — A tense journey back to Merida with the codex, pursued by Mendoza's men.
26. **The Conservator's Alarm** — Eliza examines the codex and discovers it is deteriorating rapidly — the wrong handling has activated old resins.
27. **The Missing Folia** — Pages are missing from the invasion chronicle; the codex is incomplete.
28. **The Botanical Clue** — Eliza identifies a plant resin used in the codex's binding that is native only to the Puuc region.
29. **The Cambridge Telegram** — Ashworth's university pressures him to ship the codex to England for "safekeeping."
30. **The Split** — Ixchel confronts Ashworth about his intentions; she refuses to help further unless the codex stays in Mexico.

### Part IV: The Complete History (Chapters 31-40)
31. **The Puuc Hills** — Ashworth, Ixchel, and Eliza travel to the Puuc region seeking the missing pages.
32. **The Village of Singing Stones** — They find a remote Maya village where oral traditions still speak of the codex scribes.
33. **The Scribe's Testament** — Flashback: the last scribe hides the missing pages in a cave shrine, knowing the Spanish are coming.
34. **The Cave of Whispers** — They discover the cave with the missing folia, including a scribe's personal testament.
35. **The Complete History** — The missing pages reveal the full chronicle: Maya civil wars, alliances with Cortes, and the prophecy of the codex's return.
36. **The Return to Merida** — They return with the complete codex; a federal agent awaits them.
37. **The Reunion of the Codex** — The full codex is reunited; Ixchel reads the complete chronicle aloud.
38. **The Lion's Den** — Ashworth meets with the Mexican federal authorities, who assert that the codex belongs to the nation.
39. **Ixchel's Testimony** — Ixchel testifies before a panel about the codex's meaning and her people's history.
40. **The Verdict Postponed** — The legal proceedings are suspended; threats against Ixchel begin to surface.

### Part V: The Living Codex (Chapters 41-50)
41. **The Death Threats** — Anonymous threats arrive; Ixchel is offered money to "lose" the codex.
42. **The Public Lecture** — Ashworth gives a lecture at Merida's university about the discovery, but Ixchel corrects his colonial framing.
43. **The Shots in the Dark** — An assassination attempt against Ixchel fails; Eliza is injured protecting her.
44. **The Investigation** — The authorities investigate; evidence points to Mendoza and his network.
45. **The Exhibition** — A public exhibition of the codex at Merida's Museo Yucateco draws international attention.
46. **The Legal Resolution** — The Mexican government declares the codex national patrimony; Mendoza's claims are dismissed.
47. **The Prophecy** — Ixchel translates the final pages: a prophecy that the codex would one day return to light to guide the Maya people.
48. **The Ceremony at Chichen Itza** — A traditional Maya ceremony blesses the codex and its journey; Ixchel is recognized by her community.
49. **The Divided Paths** — Ashworth returns to England changed; Ixchel stays to become the codex's custodian. Finch is disgraced.
50. **The Living Codex** — Epilogue: the codex is housed in a new museum in Merida, curated by Ixchel, with digital copies sent to Maya communities.
