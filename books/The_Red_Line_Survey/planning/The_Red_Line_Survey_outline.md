# The Red Line Survey - Outline

## Premise

When a mountain county's new boundary survey moves a red jurisdiction line across a cemetery, a spring, and a dormant lithium claim, cadastral surveyor Elian Marr is hired to verify whether the shift is a harmless correction or a theft drawn in coordinates. The official map is clean, but the field monuments are not: one stone has been reset, a GPS base log is missing, and an old railroad plat points to a different ridge. As Elian works with a county clerk and a retired chainman, the survey becomes a fight over who gets to turn land, water, and the dead into assets.

## Core Cast

- Elian Marr - Adult cadastral surveyor known for treating old monuments as witnesses, not obstacles.
- Nora Vale - Adult county clerk whose land records are being overwritten by a state modernization project.
- Silas Quern - Adult retired chainman who helped survey the ridge forty years earlier and remembers the old line by scars on trees.
- Mara Dace - Adult tribal land officer whose community's spring is shifted outside protection by the new map.
- Hollis Pike - Adult mining investor whose dormant claim becomes valuable if the line moves.
- Alma Rusk - Adult cemetery caretaker whose family graves become subject to transfer.
- Bennet Shaw - Adult state GIS director defending the digital survey as objective.
- Kira Venn - Adult drone mapper whose flight data contradicts the official model.
- Owen Tarl - Adult field technician who vanished after logging the disputed base station.
- Judge Irena Holt - Adult circuit judge asked to freeze the red line before deeds change hands.

## 50 Chapter Plan

1. Elian arrives at the cemetery and sees the new red line crossing graves no old map ever divided.
2. Nora shows him the state survey and the deeds already queued for automatic update.
3. Alma points out a boundary stone that has fresh soil around its base.
4. Elian compares the stone to old field notes and finds the chisel mark rotated.
5. Mara Dace explains that the new line removes a protected spring from tribal stewardship.
6. Bennet Shaw insists the GIS correction is based on satellite-grade accuracy.
7. Silas Quern takes Elian to a scarred pine that marked the original chain line.
8. Kira Venn gives Elian drone images showing a service truck at the monument at night.
9. Elian discovers the GPS base station log for that night is missing.
10. Hollis Pike files a transfer request for the lithium claim under the new line.
11. Nora finds that the digital parcel update was scheduled before public notice.
12. Elian surveys the ridge and finds three old monuments still agreeing with the old plat.
13. Alma receives notice that cemetery access will become a private easement.
14. Mara produces an 1890 railroad plat that references the spring and ridge saddle.
15. Bennet discredits the plat as obsolete but refuses to release raw correction files.
16. Silas remembers Owen Tarl asking about "redrawing without moving dirt."
17. Elian finds a replacement brass cap ordered under a false county account.
18. Kira's drone metadata places Hollis Pike's survey consultant near the stone.
19. Nora traces the false account to a land-services contractor tied to Hollis.
20. Elian realizes the digital line was made to fit the moved stone, not the terrain.
21. The team builds a board of monuments, coordinates, deeds, water, and mining money.
22. Judge Holt grants a temporary freeze but demands physical proof by Friday.
23. Hollis offers to fund cemetery preservation if the new line is accepted.
24. Mara and Alma refuse separate settlements and force a joint record claim.
25. Elian reopens Silas's old field book and finds a missing page shadow.
26. Nora discovers the missing page scanned into the state archive under the wrong township.
27. The page proves the red line was measured from a boulder that still stands uphill.
28. Bennet admits a contractor supplied the correction model but denies tampering.
29. Owen Tarl's abandoned truck is found with a smashed receiver case.
30. Kira recovers partial base station data from a backup drone sync.
31. The recovered data shows the official correction used a spoofed base point.
32. Elian matches brass filings at the moved stone to the replacement cap order.
33. Silas confronts the contractor and is threatened with trespass charges.
34. Hollis accelerates deed transfers before the freeze can be extended.
35. Nora locks the record queue and risks her job.
36. Mara files a water protection affidavit using the old ridge evidence.
37. Alma opens burial ledgers showing the cemetery predates both county maps.
38. Bennet privately gives Elian raw model files after seeing the spoofed base.
39. Elian finds the model's red line was hand-snapped to the mining parcel.
40. Owen returns and says he was paid to reset the stone, then panicked.
41. Owen identifies Hollis's consultant as the person who supplied the cap.
42. The consultant claims Bennet authorized the correction under modernization rules.
43. Elian proves the authorization timestamp was forged after the night truck visit.
44. Judge Holt holds a field hearing at the disputed stone.
45. Elian reconstructs the original line with chain, compass, GPS, and railroad plat.
46. The spring, cemetery, and claim fall back on the old side of the boundary.
47. Hollis tries to argue economic reliance, but Nora shows the pre-notice deed queue.
48. Bennet withdraws the digital correction and refers the contractor for fraud.
49. Mara and Alma record a joint protection easement across the spring and cemetery road.
50. Elian leaves a new witness monument beside the old stone, with the red line corrected in public.
