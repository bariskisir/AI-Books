# The Red Line Survey

## Overview

When a new digital county line cuts through a cemetery, a protected spring, and a dormant lithium claim, surveyor Elian Marr is hired to determine whether the change is correction or theft. The state map looks precise, but the ground tells another story: fresh soil around a boundary stone, a rotated chisel mark, a missing base station log, and old monuments that still agree with the ridge. Working with county clerk Nora Vale, tribal land officer Mara Dace, caretaker Alma Rusk, and retired chainman Silas Quern, Elian follows the red line through deeds, water records, drone metadata, and contractor files.

**Genre:** Cartographic Crime / Border Survey Suspense
**Model:** gpt-5
**Chapters:** 50

## Story Engines

A boundary correction becomes a land theft when a reset monument and spoofed GPS base point pull a red line across graves, water, and mineral rights. The investigation joins field surveying, county records, drone metadata, and old plats into one public correction.

## Key Themes

- A map is only honest when its evidence can be walked.
- Public records can turn fraud into reality if no one stops the queue.
- Land disputes are also disputes over water, memory, and the dead.
- Old monuments and local records remain witnesses when digital systems are misused.

## Characters

**Elian Marr** - Adult cadastral surveyor known for treating old monuments as witnesses, not obstacles.

**Nora Vale** - Adult county clerk whose land records are being overwritten by a state modernization project.

**Silas Quern** - Adult retired chainman who remembers the old line by scars on trees.

**Mara Dace** - Adult tribal land officer whose community's spring is shifted outside protection by the new map.

**Hollis Pike** - Adult mining investor whose dormant claim becomes valuable if the line moves.

**Alma Rusk** - Adult cemetery caretaker whose family graves become subject to transfer.

**Bennet Shaw** - Adult state GIS director defending the digital survey as objective.

**Kira Venn** - Adult drone mapper whose flight data contradicts the official model.
