# The Mermaid of Monhegan — Outline

## Premise

Dr. Isabel Wells, a marine biologist grieving a drowned colleague, takes a summer position on Monhegan Island, Maine. She discovers a relict population of aquatic humanoids — creatures of legend made flesh. When a biotech corporation learns of the discovery, Wells must choose between scientific revelation and the protection of a species that trusts her.

## Core Cast

- **Dr. Isabel Wells** — 38, marine biologist, widowed by the sea, sharp and guarded
- **Ezra Pridham** — 62, Monhegan fisherman, quiet, knows what the island hides
- **Marcus Killian** — VP of Aethelon Biosciences, polished, ruthless
- **Dr. Lena Orloff** — Aethelon's staff scientist, uneasy conscience
- **The Creature** — young female mermaid, never named, last of her kind

## Chapter Plan

### Part I: Arrivals (Chapters 1-10)

1. **The Ferry** — Wells arrives on Monhegan, studies the gray Atlantic, feels the weight of her failed marriage and drowned colleague David Shaw.
2. **The Island** — She walks the island. Granite ledges. Spruce bent by wind. Ezra watches her from his boat.
3. **The Station** — The field station is cold, poorly equipped. She inventories gear. Something has been in the shed.
4. **First Night** — Restless sleep. She dreams of David's face underwater. A sound wakes her — not quite human.
5. **The Sound** — She follows the noise to a cove. Tidal pools. A disturbance in the water. Footprints that are not feet.
6. **Ezra** — Morning. Ezra finds her at the cove. He knows more than he says. "Some things belong to the island."
7. **Gathering Data** — She deploys hydrophones, water samplers. Ezra brings her fresh cod. They talk in fragments.
8. **The Song** — The hydrophones record a low, complex vocalization. Not whale. Not seal. She runs the spectrogram all night.
9. **Searching** — She takes the skiff out, follows the signal. Fog rolls in. She gets lost, hears the song close.
10. **The Glimpse** — The fog parts. A figure on a rock — human shape, dark eyes, webbed hands. Then gone.

### Part II: Contact (Chapters 11-25)

11. **Doubt** — She questions what she saw. The spectrogram is proof. She calls a colleague, gets voicemail.
12. **Return** — She goes back every day. Leaves fish on the rock. The creature watches from the water.
13. **Gift** — A scallop shell left on the station steps. Perfect. Unbroken. Wells begins to believe.
14. **Trust** — The creature surfaces twenty yards out. They look at each other for a full minute.
15. **Approach** — Wells sits in the shallows, talks softly. The creature circles, comes closer.
16. **Touch** — The creature lets Wells touch its arm. Skin like wet slate. Gills. Real.
17. **Data** — Wells photographs, records, measures. The creature tolerates it. She names no one.
18. **Ezra's Secret** — Ezra admits his grandfather knew of "the deep people." He shows her a carved bone in his shed.
19. **The Colony** — Wells asks where the others are. The creature — young, alone — has no answer she can understand.
20. **History** — Island records mention "sea people" in 1873, 1911, 1942. Each sighting smaller. A dying population.
21. **Bonding** — The creature brings stones, shells. Wells brings books, reads aloud. A strange friendship forms.
22. **The Leak** — Wells emails a cautious query to a genetics lab. The email is flagged, forwarded to Aethelon Biosciences.
23. **Arrival** — Marcus Killian arrives on the ferry, polished suit, corporate boat. He says he's here for "environmental impact research."
24. **The Pitch** — Killian charms, bullies. He knows about the creature. Offers funding, partnership. Wells refuses.
25. **Pressure** — Killian stays. His men fan out with drones, boats, sampling equipment. The island closes in.

### Part III: Siege (Chapters 26-40)

26. **Surveillance** — Wells finds a tracking device on her skiff. She smashes it, feels the walls tighten.
27. **The Creature Hides** — The creature stops coming. The cove is empty. The hydrophones are silent.
28. **Lena** — Dr. Lena Orloff, Aethelon's scientist, visits Wells. She is troubled. "He'll destroy it for a patent."
29. **Alliance** — Wells and Lena begin sharing information. Lena reveals Aethelon wants live capture, genetic sequencing, commercial breeding.
30. **Ezra's Network** — Ezra calls in favors. Old fishermen who remember. A network of silence forms around the island.
31. **The Trap** — Killian's men set acoustic nets in the deeper channel. Wells finds them at dusk, cuts them loose.
32. **Confrontation** — Killian confronts Wells on the dock. "You can't stop progress." She tells him progress can go to hell.
33. **Storm** — A nor'easter batters the island. The creature surfaces in the harbor, injured. Wells tends to a wound on its arm.
34. **Shelter** — Wells hides the creature in the station's tide pool room. Keeps it alive with seawater pumps.
35. **The Search** — Killian's men search the island. Ezra misdirects them. The island closes ranks.
36. **The Deal** — Killian offers Wells half a million dollars for exclusive access. She throws him off the property.
37. **Lena's Choice** — Lena copies Aethelon's files, gives them to Wells. "Burn it. Burn all of it."
38. **Evidence** — Wells sends the data to three universities, a journalist, the NOAA. She creates a digital trail.
39. **The Raid** — Killian attempts a forced entry at night. Ezra and the fishermen block the road with trucks. Confrontation.
40. **Retreat** — Killian leaves, promising legal action. The island breathes. But for how long?

### Part IV: The Deep (Chapters 41-50)

41. **Aftermath** — Silence. The creature heals. Wells knows the world will come now. There is no hiding.
42. **The Question** — What does the creature want? Wells realizes she has never asked. She has only taken data, given protection.
43. **The Answer** — The creature leads Wells to a cave, underwater, accessible only at low tide. Inside: bones. A burial ground.
44. **The Last** — The creature is the last. The others died. Pollution. Warming water. Ship strikes. She stayed because Wells came.
45. **Release** — Wells decides. No publication. No specimen. The creature deserves to live, not be studied.
46. **Goodbye** — Wells takes the creature to the outer ledges, where the water is deep and clean. Lets her go.
47. **The Cover-Up** — Wells burns her notes. Destroys the photographs. Lena does the same. Ezra burns the carved bone.
48. **Departure** — Killian's lawsuit fails. No evidence. Wells packs the station. The ferry waits.
49. **The Island** — Ezra stays. The island remembers. Wells stands on the deck, watching Monhegan shrink.
50. **Song** — On the mainland, late at night, Wells hears the sound of the creature on a voicemail. One last song. Proof she exists. She deletes it. Goodbye.
