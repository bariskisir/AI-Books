# The Drowned Bells — Outline

## Premise

When the tide recedes further than any living memory on the Cornish coast near the village of Porthgwarra, a submerged bell tower rises from the seabed. Marine biologist Ivy Roskilly, drawn by the event, discovers the ruin belongs to the lost church of Saint Keverne — a settlement swallowed by the sea three centuries ago. As the bells begin to ring at odd hours, the drowned dead stir in the waters, and Parson Trelawney, the last clergyman of the sunken church, walks the tide line with an unsettling purpose. Ivy must uncover what holds the drowned to this world before the spring tide carries her under with them.

## Core Cast

- **Ivy Roskilly** — Marine biologist, early 30s. Practical, observant, skeptical by training. Recently returned to Cornwall after her father's death. She studies tidal ecosystems and knows these waters as well as anyone alive.

- **Parson Trelawney** — The last vicar of Saint Keverne's Church, drowned in 1724. He emerges from the sea as a gaunt figure in rotting clerical black, his voice like wet stone. He is not malevolent — but he is desperate.

- **The Drowned** — The congregation of Saint Keverne's, bound to the seabed by an unfinished service. They rise with the bells, gray-skinned and waterlogged, their eyes the color of deep water. They do not speak. They do not need to.

- **Morwenna Pentire** — Ivy's aunt, keeper of the local museum and oral history. She knows the old stories and has been waiting for someone to ask the right questions.

- **Seth Nancarrow** — Local fisherman, Ivy's childhood friend. He sees things on the water that he cannot explain and drinks to forget them.

- **Catherine Roskilly** — Ivy's mother, who left Cornwall when Ivy was a child. Her letters hint at knowledge she never shared.

## 50-Chapter Plan

### Part I: The Ebb (Chapters 1–12)

1. **The Lowest Tide** — Ivy measures the tide at Porthgwarra. It pulls back past the marker stones, exposing rock she has never seen.

2. **Something in the Kelp** — Seth finds Ivy on the beach. They spot a dark shape in the receding water — a spire.

3. **The Spire** — Ivy wades out to the structure. Granite blocks, barnacle-crusted. A cross at the top.

4. **Saint Keverne's** — Morwenna identifies the ruin from parish records. The church was lost in the great storm of 1724.

5. **The First Bell** — At twilight, a single low note sounds from the sea. Ivy feels it in her chest.

6. **Parson Trelawney** — Seth sees a man in black walking the tide line at midnight. Footprints lead into the water but not back out.

7. **The Congregation** — Ivy dreams of shapes beneath the surface, faces pressed against dark water.

8. **Parish Records** — Morwenna shows Ivy the register of Saint Keverne's. Twenty-three names, the last entry undated.

9. **Catherine's Letters** — Ivy finds letters from her mother mentioning "the bells that call from below." Her mother left Cornwall abruptly in 1998.

10. **The Second Bell** — Two bells ring at dawn. The village hears them. Old Mr. Trevorrow crosses himself.

11. **Drowned Things** — A lobster pot brings up a prayer book, leather intact, pages legible. The service for the burial of the dead.

12. **The Tide Turns** — The water begins to rise. Ivy must decide whether to study the phenomenon or walk away.

### Part II: The Deep (Chapters 13–25)

13. **The Sea Door** — Ivy dives the site. She finds a doorway at the base of the tower, standing open.

14. **What Remains** — Inside the church: pews, a pulpit, a chalice on the altar. No bodies.

15. **The Third Bell** — Three bells at noon. They ring in sequence, not in peal. A call, not a celebration.

16. **Parson's Request** — Trelawney appears to Ivy on the cliff path. He speaks: "The service is not finished."

17. **The Unshriven** — Morwenna explains that the congregation drowned during a baptism. They died unblessed.

18. **Seth's Confession** — Seth admits he has heard the bells for years. He thought he was going mad.

19. **The Names** — Ivy reads the parish register aloud. For each name, the water in the bay grows stiller.

20. **The Fourth Bell** — Four bells at dusk. A child's voice joins the sound, humming.

21. **Catherine's Return** — Ivy's mother arrives in Porthgwarra, gray-haired and afraid. She came back because the bells have started.

22. **1998** — Catherine confesses: she was the last person to hear the bells, twenty-eight years ago. She fled.

23. **The Fifth Bell** — Five bells after midnight. The whole village hears them. Windows rattle.

24. **The Drowned Walk** — Shapes emerge from the surf. They stand on the wet sand, facing the tower.

25. **What the Drowned Want** — Trelawney tells Ivy the truth: they need the service finished so they can rest. She must say the words.

### Part III: The Service (Chapters 26–38)

26. **The Book** — Ivy takes the recovered prayer book to the church ruin. The pages for the burial service are marked with salt stains.

27. **Preparations** — Catherine, Morwenna, and Seth gather on the beach. They light candles.

28. **The Sixth Bell** — Six bells. The drowned turn their faces toward the shore.

29. **The Procession** — Trelawney leads his congregation out of the water, up the beach. They leave no footprints.

30. **The Nave** — Ivy enters the drowned church. The interior is dry, impossibly preserved. Candlelight burns without heat.

31. **The Reading** — Ivy begins the burial service. The drowned kneel in the pews.

32. **The Seventh Bell** — Seven bells, one for each day of the week. The sea outside the door rises.

33. **Interruption** — A rogue wave crashes through the door. The candles go out. Panic.

34. **The Baptism** — Ivy realizes the service was interrupted at the baptismal prayers. The child was never named.

35. **The Name** — Morwenna finds the baptismal record: Elias. Ivy speaks the child's name aloud.

36. **The Eighth Bell** — A single high chime, the voice of a child. The water recedes.

37. **The Blessing** — Ivy completes the service. The drowned begin to fade, their features softening.

38. **Trelawney's Peace** — The parson bows to Ivy. "You have done what I could not." He walks into the sea and does not rise.

### Part IV: The Aftertide (Chapters 39–50)

39. **Silence** — The bells stop. The sea is quiet. Ivy sleeps for sixteen hours.

40. **The Collapse** — The spire crumbles as the tide returns, settling back into the deep.

41. **Catherine's Goodbye** — Ivy's mother leaves again, but this time with an understanding between them.

42. **Seth's Promise** — Seth quits drinking. He offers to take Ivy out on the boat whenever she wants.

43. **Morwenna's Archive** — Morwenna catalogs the prayer book and the parish register for the museum.

44. **Aftermath** — The village heals. Tourists come to see the site. Ivy gives talks at the local school.

45. **The Body Beneath** — A storm reveals human remains near the tower site. The drowned, properly buried at last.

46. **Consecration** — The Bishop agrees to reconsecrate the site. Ivy attends the service.

47. **Ivy's Choice** — Ivy is offered a research position in Plymouth. She turns it down to stay in Porthgwarra.

48. **One Year Later** — Anniversary of the lowest tide. Ivy walks the beach at dawn. The water is calm.

49. **The Last Bell** — At midnight on the anniversary, a single bell sounds, once, fading. A farewell.

50. **The Tide Returns** — Ivy watches the sun rise over a peaceful sea. She has a new project: mapping the submerged villages of the Cornish coast. The drowned are at rest. She is home.
