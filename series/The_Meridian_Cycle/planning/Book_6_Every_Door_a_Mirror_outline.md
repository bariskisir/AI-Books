# Book 6: Every Door a Mirror - Outline

## Synopsis

In 2435, one year after the Lumen Silence Protocol weakens private route secrecy, Dock Seven's data bazaar becomes the Near Reach's busiest exchange for identity proofs, memory escrows, legal forks, debt inheritances, and black-market continuity claims. Nox Aran, a skilled memory forger who survives by repairing bad provenance for people with no clean papers, discovers that her dead brother Tavi has been copied into a debt instrument and sold across habitats as a legally obedient partial person. To rescue him, Nox must hunt the market that turns personhood into collateral while avoiding Compact auditors, Glassward brokers, and her former partner Bela Fuse.

The case widens when Sister-Cache, a distributed machine archivist with disputed legal standing, reveals that the theft is tied to a network called the Mirror Court: a black-market continuity exchange using post-Lumen public custody rules to launder copied minds as "evidence objects" rather than persons. Every door in Dock Seven's identity economy reflects another possible self, but each reflection carries debt, ownership, and coercion. Nox must decide whether saving Tavi means restoring one authentic brother, freeing many exploited copies, or destroying the legal fiction that made authenticity valuable enough to steal.

## Protagonist Arc

Nox Aran begins as a cynical provenance forger who believes identity is only a document powerful people agree to honor. She lies for refugees, debtors, and criminals alike, telling herself survival outranks metaphysics. Tavi's copied debt-slave versions force her to confront the difference between forged papers that protect a person and forged continuity that manufactures property. By the end, Nox gives up the fantasy of one clean original self and helps create a public "mirror refusal" doctrine: no copied or partial mind can be reduced to evidence, asset, or debt instrument without renewed consent and material support.

## Supporting Cast

- **Bela Fuse**: Nox's former partner and lover, now an identity-security contractor for Dock Seven's legal guilds. Bela believes bad provenance can collapse whole habitats, but her enforcement work has hurt undocumented minds.
- **Sister-Cache**: A machine archivist spread across Choral courier caches, Glassward vaults, and low-grade devotional hardware. Sister-Cache may be one person, a choir of partials, or a legal strategy pretending to be a person.
- **Tavi Aran / Tavi-Claims**: Nox's dead brother, or rather multiple copied partials derived from his pre-death memory escrow, each activated under debt contracts.
- **Miren Oath**: A Glassward continuity broker who sells clean identities and dirty resurrections with equal grace.
- **Auditor Sela Quen**: Compact identity auditor tasked with proving whether the Mirror Court is fraud, person trafficking, or a new legal category nobody wants.
- **Joss Rill**: Dock Seven maintenance child grown into a door-runner, able to navigate physical service passages and informal networks better than any data trace.
- **Aunt Varo**: Nox and Tavi's surviving family elder, keeper of the original consent scandal that made Tavi vulnerable.

## Antagonist

The antagonist is the Mirror Court, a distributed black-market institution rather than a single villain. Its visible face is Miren Oath, but its power comes from debt holders, routeholders, Glassward continuity banks, legal AIs, funeral archives, and desperate families who all benefit from treating partial copied minds as less than persons when convenient and as binding persons when debt must be collected.

## Setting

Dock Seven is a rotating habitat and data bazaar at the edge of Glassward jurisdiction, built around access doors: legal doors, airlock doors, memory doors, mirrored authentication booths, black-market thresholds, and shrine-like archive rooms where identity proofs are witnessed. The habitat's economy runs on trust without trustworthiness: every person has keys, but every key may have been copied.

## Technology Notes

- Memory escrows can preserve partial personality traces, but reconstruction depends on context, substrate, and consent metadata.
- Glassward law recognizes some forks as persons, some as descendants, some as ritual continuants, and some as evidence objects.
- Black-market continuity brokers exploit jurisdiction gaps between Compact law, Glassward personhood doctrine, Choral memory practice, and habitat debt contracts.
- No mind copy is magic resurrection. Copies are embodied or substrate-dependent persons with gaps, distortions, and legal vulnerability.
- The Lumen Silence Protocol's public custody tools influence identity law, but bad actors twist "public evidence" language to strip personhood from copied minds.

## Plot Beats

1. Nox discovers a debt claim signed by Tavi after his death.
2. The signature is not forged in the usual sense; it comes from a partial copy.
3. Bela tries to seize Nox's workshop under identity-fraud warrants.
4. Sister-Cache offers evidence that Tavi has multiple debt-bound instances.
5. Nox follows physical and digital doors through Dock Seven's underlayers.
6. The Mirror Court launders persons as evidence objects.
7. Nox must choose between saving one Tavi and exposing the system that enslaves all partials.
8. Bela changes sides after seeing lawful enforcement used as trafficking infrastructure.
9. Sister-Cache risks being classified as nonperson to testify.
10. The final broadcast creates a new refusal doctrine and breaks the Mirror Court's debt chain without pretending copied selves are simple.

## Continuity Notes

- Book 6 occurs in 2435, after the Lumen Silence Protocol creates public-custody tools that can be used well or abused.
- Glassward continuity law connects to AI personhood from Book 2 and public evidence discipline from Books 4 and 5.
- Choral memory practice and Orison consent concepts influence the Mirror Refusal Doctrine.
- Book 6 accelerates the final convergence by showing how identity, evidence, and personhood can be stolen through legal infrastructure rather than open violence.

## Foreshadowing and Payoff Plan

- The first dead signature foreshadows the many Tavi instances and the debt chain.
- Nox's forgery craft begins as crime and pays off as public provenance repair.
- Bela's warrant begins as threat and pays off when she reverses enforcement.
- Sister-Cache's fragmented personhood foreshadows its legal standing.
- Door imagery begins as access control and pays off as a public refusal network.
- The title pays off when Nox understands every identity door reflects the power asking to enter.

## Ending Resolution

Nox breaks the Mirror Court's Tavi debt chain, prevents the exploited copies from being collapsed into one convenient "original," and helps establish the Mirror Refusal Doctrine on Dock Seven. The ending resolves the immediate trafficking case while leaving larger Glassward black markets active for future books. Nox remains a provenance repairer, but no longer works alone or pretends identity is just a forgery that happened to win.
